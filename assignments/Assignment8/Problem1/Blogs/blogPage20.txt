<h2 class='date-header'><span>February 27, 2016</span></h2>

          <div class="date-posts">
        
<div class='post-outer'>
<div class='post hentry uncustomized-post-template' itemprop='blogPost' itemscope='itemscope' itemtype='http://schema.org/BlogPosting'>
<meta content='https://4.bp.blogspot.com/-3BS8C2LntpA/VtJRX4qdQ_I/AAAAAAAAB4E/YGLaq0KXBD4/s400/cover.jpg' itemprop='image_url'/>

<h3 class='post-title entry-title' itemprop='name'>
<a href='http://elijace.blogspot.com/2016/02/review-anti-by-rihanna.html'>REVIEW: "ANTI" by Rihanna</a>
</h3>
<div class='post-header'>
<div class='post-header-line-1'></div>
</div>
<div class='post-body entry-content' id='post-body-3248544454097263889' itemprop='description articleBody'>
<div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
<a href="https://4.bp.blogspot.com/-3BS8C2LntpA/VtJRX4qdQ_I/AAAAAAAAB4E/YGLaq0KXBD4/s1600/cover.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;"><img border="0" height="520" src="https://4.bp.blogspot.com/-3BS8C2LntpA/VtJRX4qdQ_I/AAAAAAAAB4E/YGLaq0KXBD4/s400/cover.jpg" width="520" /></a></div>
<div dir="ltr" style="line-height: 1.38; margin-bottom: 0pt; margin-top: 0pt; text-align: center;">
<span style="background-color: transparent; color: black; font-family: Georgia; font-style: normal; font-variant: normal; text-decoration: none; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre-wrap;"><span style="font-size: x-large;"><b>Rihanna Turns in a</b></span></span></div>
<div dir="ltr" style="line-height: 1.38; margin-bottom: 0pt; margin-top: 0pt; text-align: center;">
<span style="background-color: transparent; color: black; font-family: Georgia; font-variant: normal; text-decoration: none; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre-wrap;"><span style="font-size: x-large;"><b>Near Classic With </b><i><b>ANTI</b></i></span></span></div>
<br />
<div style="text-align: justify;">
<span style="font-family: Georgia; font-size: 16px; line-height: 1.38; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre-wrap;">  Meanwhile, in another part of the pop stratosphere, RiRi season has begun. The eighth album from Rihanna, </span><span style="font-family: Georgia; font-size: 16px; font-style: italic; line-height: 1.38; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre-wrap;">ANTI</span><span style="font-family: Georgia; font-size: 16px; line-height: 1.38; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre-wrap;">, arrived quietly in late January. There was more written about the nauseating album cover with a Chloe Mitchell poem printed in braille. The music&#8217;s pretty good, too.</span></div>
<div style="text-align: justify;">
&nbsp; &nbsp;<span style="font-family: Georgia; font-size: 16px; font-style: italic; line-height: 1.38; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre-wrap;">ANTI</span><span style="font-family: Georgia; font-size: 16px; line-height: 1.38; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre-wrap;"> opens with "Consideration," a loose neck-swiveler that sets the tone for the album's first half. It's nice to hear Rihanna give the assist to SZA, a sister-in-arms and a singer equally deserving to reach the same heights. Their voices move around each other in an uneven orbit. SZA bellowing beautifully bent notes. Rihanna soaring in an upward swing.</span></div>
<div style="text-align: justify;">
&nbsp; &nbsp;<span style="font-family: Georgia; font-size: 16px; line-height: 1.38; white-space: pre-wrap;">A soft muted organ clambers on "James Joint," a loosie before things get serious. "Kiss It Better" chisels through the speakers. I can picture Rihanna walking down a black staircase in black heels and black skirt to the opening. Fireworks sparkle in the background as stunted Eighties rock ballad lead guitar burrows into your head. Rihanna sings about a relationship where the bad gets swept under the bed. Emotion pumps from her voice no how many times it's processed and layered.&nbsp;</span></div>
<div style="text-align: justify;">
&nbsp; &nbsp;<span style="font-family: Georgia; font-size: 16px; line-height: 1.38; white-space: pre-wrap;">The first single, "Work," has made its way across the oceans with two different videos and a saucy performance on the BRIT Awards. Love the song. Though, I barely know what Rihanna's singing, or if she's singing anything in any language. Not sure, but it gets catchier as time goes on. It's one of her best singles. Drake is usually good for a guest verse and here he gives the necessary phrases. "Work" is their third single together following "Take Care" and "What&#8217;s My Name?"</span></div>
<div style="text-align: justify;">
&nbsp; &nbsp;<span style="font-family: Georgia; font-size: 16px; line-height: 1.38; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre-wrap;">"Woo" reminds me of the Bjork song "Pluto" off </span><span style="font-family: Georgia; font-size: 16px; font-style: italic; line-height: 1.38; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre-wrap;">Homogenic</span><span style="font-family: Georgia; font-size: 16px; line-height: 1.38; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre-wrap;">. Rihanna sings against a coarse electronic tremble that does not let up the entire song. I'd love to hear her move more in this direction. Maybe let Trent Reznor put her voice through the greater. Then, just when you think it can't any more vicious, Rihanna screams, "I don't mean to really luh you / I don't mean to really care about you no more."&nbsp;</span></div>
<div style="text-align: justify;">
&nbsp; &nbsp;<span style="font-family: Georgia; font-size: 16px; line-height: 1.38; white-space: pre-wrap;">Next, on "Needed Me," she gets cold with an ex-lover over a simple beat and an expanding wah-wah. When Rihanna sing, it's in a downward spiral. "Didn't they tell you that I was a savage? / fuck your white horse and your carriage." Somewhere an ex is crying in his beer in a dark bar. "Yeah, I Said It" resets the tone. The breath of Rihanna's vocals cloud both speakers in daydream tripping fantasy.&nbsp;</span></div>
<div style="text-align: justify;">
&nbsp; &nbsp;<span style="font-family: Georgia; font-size: 16px; line-height: 1.38; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre-wrap;">Then comes the curveball: a painfully straight-forward version of Tame Impala's "New Person, Same Old Mistakes," off 2015's </span><span style="font-family: Georgia; font-size: 16px; font-style: italic; line-height: 1.38; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre-wrap;">Currents</span><span style="font-family: Georgia; font-size: 16px; line-height: 1.38; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre-wrap;">. Retitled here as, "Same Ol' Mistakes," this version is so similar I wonder if Rihanna didn't just kareoke over the instrumental version of the original. The band gets production credits, so who knows. The song's decent enough so, fine, but somehow, it&#8217;s even longer than the original.&nbsp;</span></div>
<div style="text-align: justify;">
&nbsp; &nbsp;<span style="font-family: Georgia; font-size: 16px; line-height: 1.38; white-space: pre-wrap;">Coupled with the next song, "Never Ending," the album begins to drag a little. The final few songs are big syrupy ballads. Her vocals soar to such great, soul-baring heights and while, there's nothing wrong with that, it's just not my mug of wine.&nbsp;</span></div>
<div style="text-align: justify;">
&nbsp; &nbsp;<span style="font-family: Georgia; font-size: 16px; font-style: italic; line-height: 1.38; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre-wrap;">ANTI</span><span style="font-family: Georgia; font-size: 16px; line-height: 1.38; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre-wrap;"> is an almost-classic from Rihanna. From song-to-song it dips and crashes through many different styles, some all her own, some borrowed. It's been billed as a break from </span><span style="font-family: Georgia; font-size: 16px; line-height: 1.38; white-space: pre-wrap;">form for the Barbadian singer and it mostly is, but the second half of the album plays it </span><span style="font-family: Georgia; font-size: 16px; white-space: pre-wrap;">safe. I long for her powerful voice to find its way into the very outer extensions of where R&amp;B can go. </span></div>
<br />
<div style="text-align: justify;">
<b style="font-family: Georgia; font-size: 16px; white-space: pre-wrap;"><br /></b></div>
<div style="text-align: justify;">
<b style="font-family: Georgia; font-size: 16px; white-space: pre-wrap;">Key Tracks: </b><span style="font-family: Georgia; font-size: 16px; white-space: pre-wrap;">"Work," "Woo," "Needed Me," "Consideration," "Kiss It Better"</span></div>

<div class='post hentry uncustomized-post-template' itemprop='blogPost' itemscope='itemscope' itemtype='http://schema.org/BlogPosting'>
<meta content='https://1.bp.blogspot.com/-1Xh2RTCkXNQ/Vs4khCKeUUI/AAAAAAAAB30/YQbWuCMUWHg/s400/which%2Bone.jpg' itemprop='image_url'/>

<h3 class='post-title entry-title' itemprop='name'>
<a href='http://elijace.blogspot.com/2016/02/kanye-gives-tutorial-on-how-not-to.html'>Kanye Gives Tutorial on How NOT to Release an Album</a>
</h3>
<div class='post-header'>
<div class='post-header-line-1'></div>
</div>
<div class='post-body entry-content' id='post-body-7526038496327773991' itemprop='description articleBody'>
<div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
<a href="https://1.bp.blogspot.com/-1Xh2RTCkXNQ/Vs4khCKeUUI/AAAAAAAAB30/YQbWuCMUWHg/s1600/which%2Bone.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;"><img border="0" height="520" src="https://1.bp.blogspot.com/-1Xh2RTCkXNQ/Vs4khCKeUUI/AAAAAAAAB30/YQbWuCMUWHg/s400/which%2Bone.jpg" width="520" /></a></div>
<div style="color: #333333; font-family: georgia, 'times new roman', 'bitstream charter', times, serif; line-height: 24px; text-align: justify;">
<span data-mce-style="font-weight: 400;">&nbsp; &nbsp;Over the years, since appearing in a pink Polo and a backpack with sunglasses like shutter blinds, Kanye West has taught us all a lot about where hip-hop can go and what fame can do to a man. With the release of his seventh album,&nbsp;</span><i>The Life of Pablo,</i><span data-mce-style="font-weight: 400;">&nbsp;Mr. West has offered another lesson. This time it&#8217;s on how&nbsp;</span><i>not</i><span data-mce-style="font-weight: 400;">&nbsp;to release your new batch of material. It's a lesson that musicians should take note of.</span></div>
<div style="color: #333333; font-family: georgia, 'times new roman', 'bitstream charter', times, serif; line-height: 24px; text-align: justify;">
<span data-mce-style="font-weight: 400;">&nbsp; &nbsp;From the moment the cow jumped over the Jumpman,&nbsp;</span><i>The Life of Pablo</i><span data-mce-style="font-weight: 400;">&nbsp;has been a botched delivery. Since the very tip of 2015, when West dropped the singles "Only One," &#8220;FourFiveSeconds," and "All Day," it was clear that Yeezy season was upon us. The leaves were starting to turn black gold. He performed on award shows, runways, festivals, &nbsp;gave a few rants, shadowboxed with Paul McCartney. He had a title for number seven:&nbsp;</span><i>So Help Me God</i><span data-mce-style="font-weight: 400;">. Then that became&nbsp;</span><i>SWISH,</i><span data-mce-style="font-weight: 400;">&nbsp;or,&nbsp;</span><i>SWiSH,</i><span data-mce-style="font-weight: 400;">&nbsp;or, maybe just&nbsp;</span><i>Swish,</i><span data-mce-style="font-weight: 400;">&nbsp;we won&#8217;t ever know.</span></div>
<div style="color: #333333; font-family: georgia, 'times new roman', 'bitstream charter', times, serif; line-height: 24px; text-align: justify;">
<span data-mce-style="font-weight: 400;">&nbsp; &nbsp;This past New Year's Eve West let slip out into the world the spasmodic, fucking-goofy track, "FACTS." Something was near. Then came the tweet revealing a release date of February 11, a Thursday. Not the usual day for new releases, but that hardly matters online. The title changed again to&nbsp;</span><i>Waves</i><span data-mce-style="font-weight: 400;">. He shared a picture of a handwritten tracklist of 10 songs. Kylie was there. Then that blew up to 18 songs a few nights before. Who knows how many times he ended up polishing "Wolves." Oh, and the title changed again to its current state (or is it?).</span></div>
<div style="color: #333333; font-family: georgia, 'times new roman', 'bitstream charter', times, serif; line-height: 24px; text-align: justify;">
<span data-mce-style="font-weight: 400;">&nbsp; &nbsp;February 11 came. West held back-to-back listening parties at Madison Square Garden while debuting his new fashion line with animatronic models. The Kardashians&nbsp;<em>and</em>&nbsp;the&nbsp;</span><span data-mce-style="font-weight: 400;">Jenners were there in white feathered robes. Shit, Lamar Odom even came out to play. But, there was nothing online, no stream, no payment plan offered. There was a broken link on KanyeWest.com and tweets about still being in the studio. Chance the Rapper apparently held up the final mastering or maybe he was thrown under the bus. You set this date, Kanye! What are you doing? This was your schedule. We would&#8217;ve waited until it was right.</span></div>
<div style="color: #333333; font-family: georgia, 'times new roman', 'bitstream charter', times, serif; line-height: 24px; text-align: justify;">
<span data-mce-style="font-weight: 400;">&nbsp; &nbsp;Nights later West performed on&nbsp;</span><i>Saturday Night Live</i><span data-mce-style="font-weight: 400;">&nbsp;spewing some inaudibles about the album being available online right now. &#8220;Right now! Aaaagggggrggrrggghhgrh!&#8221; It wasn't. The next day he offered it on his website, but only in thirty-second previews. The full stream was available only to Tidal subscribers. Personally, I&#8217;m against streaming music. That just wasn&#8217;t gonna happen.</span></div>
<div style="color: #333333; font-family: georgia, 'times new roman', 'bitstream charter', times, serif; line-height: 24px; text-align: justify;">
<span data-mce-style="font-weight: 400;">&nbsp; &nbsp;Week and a half later and it's still unclear how this album can be widely purchased and which version it will be. Kanye&#8217;s saying it&#8217;ll only be available on Tidal. The whole thing has been one Kanye Thunderclap of Confusion for such a long build-up to what was an inevitable album.&nbsp;</span><span data-mce-style="font-weight: 400;">The 18-track version that has been circulated feels overstuffed and maybe the original 10-track version might&#8217;ve been a more succinct listening experience. I&#8217;m reserving my review here, but that thing is&nbsp;</span><i>bloated.</i></div>
<div style="color: #333333; font-family: georgia, 'times new roman', 'bitstream charter', times, serif; line-height: 24px; text-align: justify;">
<span data-mce-style="font-weight: 400;">&nbsp; &nbsp;So, bands, musicians, artists take note. If you and your band go through the long haul gruel of creating a cohesive unit of songs to be played as an Album, it should be delivered with authority. Don't be like this season's Yeezus. Be committed to the final product. Let some mystery build behind it. Know for certain when a song is complete. When music makes it to the Internet today, it travels fast. If you're not putting the most complete version out there it&#8217;ll get thrown in the whirlpool and now people are hearing something incomplete.</span></div>
<div style="color: #333333; font-family: georgia, 'times new roman', 'bitstream charter', times, serif; line-height: 24px; text-align: justify;">
<i>&nbsp; &nbsp;The Life of Pablo&nbsp;</i><span data-mce-style="font-weight: 400;">had huge hype. A handy tool for sales. But given that there was no clear path for purchase and that streaming services through Tidal aren&#8217;t counted by&nbsp;</span><i>Billboard,&nbsp;</i><span data-mce-style="font-weight: 400;">the album has no official standing of its total output effectively ruining all that built-in hype. That&nbsp;can't be&nbsp;good for your $53 million debt, fam.</span></div>
<div style="color: #333333; font-family: georgia, 'times new roman', 'bitstream charter', times, serif; line-height: 24px; text-align: justify;">
<span data-mce-style="font-weight: 400;"><br /></span></div>

          <div class="date-posts">
        
<div class='post-outer'>
<div class='post hentry uncustomized-post-template' itemprop='blogPost' itemscope='itemscope' itemtype='http://schema.org/BlogPosting'>
<meta content='http://1.bp.blogspot.com/-Y6CO9IhNEQ8/VqExfDIV2tI/AAAAAAAAB3g/7Dsjd17papE/s400/Blackstar%2Bcover.jpg' itemprop='image_url'/>

<h3 class='post-title entry-title' itemprop='name'>
<a href='http://elijace.blogspot.com/2016/01/review-blackstar-by-david-bowie.html'>REVIEW: "Blackstar" by David Bowie</a>
</h3>
<div class='post-header'>
<div class='post-header-line-1'></div>
</div>
<div class='post-body entry-content' id='post-body-5418456101293072322' itemprop='description articleBody'>
<div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
<a href="http://1.bp.blogspot.com/-Y6CO9IhNEQ8/VqExfDIV2tI/AAAAAAAAB3g/7Dsjd17papE/s1600/Blackstar%2Bcover.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;"><img border="0" height="520" src="http://1.bp.blogspot.com/-Y6CO9IhNEQ8/VqExfDIV2tI/AAAAAAAAB3g/7Dsjd17papE/s400/Blackstar%2Bcover.jpg" width="520" /></a></div>
<div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
<span style="font-family: &quot;georgia&quot; , &quot;times new roman&quot; , serif; font-size: x-large;"><i>Blackstar </i><b>is Bowie's everlasting</b></span></div>
<div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
<span style="font-family: &quot;georgia&quot; , &quot;times new roman&quot; , serif; font-size: x-large;"><b>wink from heaven</b></span></div>
<div dir="ltr" style="line-height: 1.38; margin-bottom: 0pt; margin-top: 0pt; text-align: justify;">
<span style="font-family: &quot;georgia&quot; , &quot;times new roman&quot; , serif;"><span style="background-color: transparent; color: black; font-size: 14.6667px; font-style: normal; font-variant: normal; font-weight: 400; text-decoration: none; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre-wrap;">   As I listened to David Bowie's new album </span><span style="background-color: transparent; color: black; font-size: 14.6667px; font-style: italic; font-variant: normal; font-weight: 400; text-decoration: none; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre-wrap;">Blackstar</span><span style="background-color: transparent; color: black; font-size: 14.6667px; font-style: normal; font-variant: normal; font-weight: 400; text-decoration: none; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre-wrap;"> for the first time, the artist himself, unbeknownst to me at the time, was ascending into eternity. Bowie&#8217;s 25th album, and now his last, arrived January 8th on the icon's 69th birthday. Two days later it would be announced in the night that Bowie was dead. He juked us all again. A surprise album. A surprise death. A true artist through his life&#8217;s final breath. </span></span></div>
<div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: justify;">
<span style="font-family: &quot;georgia&quot; , &quot;times new roman&quot; , serif;"><b id="docs-internal-guid-75e52e42-65aa-0796-8f18-211258491ab2" style="font-weight: normal;">&nbsp; &nbsp;</b><span style="font-size: 14.6667px; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre-wrap;">Instantly the dynamic of the music on the album changed. </span><span style="font-size: 14.6667px; font-style: italic; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre-wrap;">Blackstar</span><span style="font-size: 14.6667px; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre-wrap;"> couldn't be more fitting in tone and timing. It's extravagant, ghostly, teetering on the outskirts of what is considered to be a traditional rock and roll album. It swivels and sinks into the poorly lit backroom of the musical mansion Bowie built over his fifty-plus-year-career.</span></span></div>
<div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: justify;">
<span style="font-family: &quot;georgia&quot; , &quot;times new roman&quot; , serif;"><b style="font-weight: normal;">&nbsp; &nbsp;</b><span style="font-size: 14.6667px; font-style: italic; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre-wrap;">Blackstar </span><span style="font-size: 14.6667px; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre-wrap;">is a seven-song voyage, a trek through the panicked headspace of someone too aware of their mortality. It shifts in moods and tempos, wandering, but never too far. </span></span></div>
<div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: justify;">
<span style="font-family: &quot;georgia&quot; , &quot;times new roman&quot; , serif;"><b style="font-weight: normal;">&nbsp; &nbsp;</b><span style="font-size: 14.6667px; line-height: 1.38; white-space: pre-wrap;">The title track is a near ten-minute ride alongside the Thin White Duke as he passes through the layers of Heaven. The song wears many faces, turning inside out and evolving with the minutes. At the midpoint of the song the instruments start to lose their beat, pacing around one another in odd rhythms slowing down. It's the sound of our artist exiting earth life and throttling into the next. &#8220;I&#8217;m a black star,&#8221; he sings, catatonically. </span></span></div>
<div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: justify;">
<span style="font-family: &quot;georgia&quot; , &quot;times new roman&quot; , serif;"><b style="font-weight: normal;">&nbsp; &nbsp;</b><span style="font-size: 14.6667px; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre-wrap;">After slowly being peeled during the first track, </span><span style="font-size: 14.6667px; font-style: italic; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre-wrap;">Blackstar </span><span style="font-size: 14.6667px; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre-wrap;">picks up with &#8220;&#8216;Tis A Pity She Was A Whore.&#8221; A heavyset drum and bass union churns through the song as it progresses into a perpetual Coleman swirl, horns gone akimbo. Bowie hits the high notes with a twisted sadness, a hidden anarchy while singing the title line. You can see his chiseled grin slowly rise with each word.</span></span></div>
<div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: justify;">
<span style="font-family: &quot;georgia&quot; , &quot;times new roman&quot; , serif;"><b style="font-weight: normal;">&nbsp; &nbsp;</b><span style="font-size: 14.6667px; line-height: 1.38; white-space: pre-wrap;">&#8220;Lazarus&#8221; is when the listener begins to really ache. The song, backed up with an authentically creepy video, saunters in with a clean, melancholic guitar scale and steady drums. But, then these soft devious horns slither in just slightly offbeat. When Bowie enters he sings, &#8220;Look up here / I&#8217;m in heaven,&#8221; and your heart skips a beat. In his slow drift outward he&#8217;s catching the wind currents like the bluebird without misgiving.</span></span></div>
<div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: justify;">
<span style="font-family: &quot;georgia&quot; , &quot;times new roman&quot; , serif;"><b style="font-weight: normal;">&nbsp; &nbsp;</b><span style="font-size: 14.6667px; line-height: 1.38; white-space: pre-wrap;">The album continues its seesaw with &#8220;Sue (Or In A Season Of Crime).&#8221; Opening with a scattershot drumbeat, the song flails wildly. Genres are taken apart, thrown onto the floor and resculpted. Everything falls on the offbeat with Bowie weaving vocals in a posthumus quiver. &#8220;Sue, the clinic called / The x-ray&#8217;s fine,&#8221; he sings. The listener&#8217;s head ping pongs into full force headbanging by the finish.</span></span></div>
<div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: justify;">
<span style="font-family: &quot;georgia&quot; , &quot;times new roman&quot; , serif;"><b style="font-weight: normal;">&nbsp; &nbsp;</b><span style="font-size: 14.6667px; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre-wrap;">Bowie gives some of his strongest, most outlandish, hair-raising vocals on </span><span style="font-size: 14.6667px; font-style: italic; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre-wrap;">Blackstar </span><span style="font-size: 14.6667px; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre-wrap;">and &#8220;Girl Loves Me&#8221; represents that best. Lost on the calendar, Bowie sings, "Where the fuck did Monday go?" The song works on hypnosis, pulling the listener into a trance. The final song, &#8220;I Can&#8217;t Give Everything Away,&#8221; finishes the album on an upbeat, celebratory note.</span></span></div>
<div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: justify;">
<span style="font-family: &quot;georgia&quot; , &quot;times new roman&quot; , serif;">&nbsp; &nbsp;<span style="font-size: 14.6667px; font-style: italic; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre-wrap;">Blackstar </span><span style="font-size: 14.6667px; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre-wrap;">is a lasting statement to not only David Bowie&#8217;s artistry, but to how he lived his life through that artistry. He worked hard through the end of his days to give us a product he&#8217;d be remembered by, a final soundtrack to the epilogue of a life lived in constant creation. Your man did us proud, Iman.</span></span></div>
<div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: justify;">
<span style="font-family: &quot;georgia&quot; , &quot;times new roman&quot; , serif;"><span style="font-size: 14.6667px; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre-wrap;"><br /></span></span></div>
<div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: justify;">
<span style="font-family: &quot;georgia&quot; , &quot;times new roman&quot; , serif;"><span style="font-size: 14.6667px; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre-wrap;"><b>Key Tracks: </b><i>all</i></span></span></div>

<div class='post-outer'>
<div class='post hentry uncustomized-post-template' itemprop='blogPost' itemscope='itemscope' itemtype='http://schema.org/BlogPosting'>
<meta content='http://3.bp.blogspot.com/-Kt13ZO_HaUQ/VpHXwj1PboI/AAAAAAAAB2s/T7YkX18X1Vw/s400/stuck.gif' itemprop='image_url'/>

<h3 class='post-title entry-title' itemprop='name'>
<a href='http://elijace.blogspot.com/2016/01/storm-in-and-hail-the.html'> storm in and hail , the </a>
</h3>
<div class='post-header'>
<div class='post-header-line-1'></div>
</div>

<h3 class='post-title entry-title' itemprop='name'>
<a href='http://elijace.blogspot.com/2016/01/i-was-teenage-music-snob.html'>I Was A Teenage Music Snob</a>
</h3>
<div class='post-header'>
<div class='post-header-line-1'></div>
</div>
<div class='post-body entry-content' id='post-body-2834609370137855692' itemprop='description articleBody'>
<table align="center" cellpadding="0" cellspacing="0" class="tr-caption-container" style="margin-left: auto; margin-right: auto; text-align: center;"><tbody>
<tr><td style="text-align: center;"><a href="http://1.bp.blogspot.com/-VVrHComw4Qs/VpHLnb52ZxI/AAAAAAAAB2I/c1V4-JlGVO4/s1600/shoe%2B2.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: auto; margin-right: auto;"><img border="0" height="500" src="http://1.bp.blogspot.com/-VVrHComw4Qs/VpHLnb52ZxI/AAAAAAAAB2I/c1V4-JlGVO4/s400/shoe%2B2.jpg" width="410" /></a></td></tr>
<tr><td class="tr-caption" style="text-align: center;">The writer during one night in high school.</td></tr>
</tbody></table>
<div style="font-family: Georgia, 'Times New Roman', 'Bitstream Charter', Times, serif; line-height: 24px; text-align: center;">
<span data-mce-style="font-weight: 400;"><i><span style="font-size: large;">I don't want to feel this way forever</span></i></span></div>
<div style="font-family: Georgia, 'Times New Roman', 'Bitstream Charter', Times, serif; font-size: 16px; line-height: 24px; text-align: justify;">
<span data-mce-style="font-weight: 400;">&nbsp; &nbsp;At the switch of the centuries I can remember the early days of downloadable music. Songs would take full days, two, three, four. Single songs. The computer would heat and hum all night long. Single tracks would only give a taste, forcing you&nbsp;to spend money to own the full, physical album. I can remember downloading songs from Glassjaw, My Chemical Romance, You &amp; I, Saves The Day, Bright Eyes, Racebannon and Thursday before knowing anything about them. It felt like I was given special access to this new music. I felt privileged. I was the prime discoverer and so, felt an exaggerated ownership.</span></div>
<div style="font-family: Georgia, 'Times New Roman', 'Bitstream Charter', Times, serif; font-size: 16px; line-height: 24px; text-align: justify;">
<span data-mce-style="font-weight: 400;">&nbsp; &nbsp;I can remember being especially taken by Thursday and buying&nbsp;</span><i>Full Collapse,&nbsp;</i><span data-mce-style="font-weight: 400;">their second album in the early months of 2001. The album became embedded into my psyche like a steak knife through a juicy, raw steak. The songs surrounded my bedroom and left echoing webs in the walls and in the ceiling. It was very special to little old defunct me. My punched and held down emotions came belting from Geoff Rickly's desperately panicked cries of pain. It felt like he was channeling my own isolation and gloom&nbsp;and that made it okay, tolerable.</span></div>
<div style="font-family: Georgia, 'Times New Roman', 'Bitstream Charter', Times, serif; font-size: 16px; line-height: 24px; text-align: justify;">
<span data-mce-style="font-weight: 400;">&nbsp; &nbsp;When something like that then crosses into the mainstream it becomes like an intrusion on that shared intensity, making it less so. It was difficult to handle in an over-the-top pathetic teenage way. When my favorite band, who's ill-fitting t-shirts I wore weekly, started to become known, it felt like betrayal, like my darkest secrets were being exposed. I can remember being snot-faced with a curled up frown at the lunch table with my friends and someone, smiling widely, saying that Thursday was&nbsp;</span><i>my</i><span data-mce-style="font-weight: 400;">&nbsp;band with a mocking tone. I could only laugh along.&nbsp;</span><span data-mce-style="font-weight: 400;">It was ridiculous to let a universal appeal ruin something I cherished, but it did.</span></div>
<div style="font-family: Georgia, 'Times New Roman', 'Bitstream Charter', Times, serif; font-size: 16px; line-height: 24px; text-align: justify;">
<span data-mce-style="font-weight: 400;">&nbsp; &nbsp;What most likely did me in was probably the debut of their music video for "Understanding in a Car Crash" on MTV. That was the white flag moment. The wall was coming down. I watched the blurred clip with wide gushing eyes knowing that this thing I had held so dearly was no longer going to be mine.</span></div>
<table align="center" cellpadding="0" cellspacing="0" class="tr-caption-container" style="margin-left: auto; margin-right: auto; text-align: center;"><tbody>
<tr><td style="text-align: center;"><img border="0" height="520" src="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/-4RanwVh1raQ/VpHL57JZfSI/AAAAAAAAB2Q/aHTFuN2-46c/s320/Full-Collapse-cover.jpg" style="margin-left: auto; margin-right: auto;" width="520" /></td></tr>
<tr><td class="tr-caption" style="text-align: center;">"Full Collapse" was released April 10, 2001 on Victory Records.</td></tr>
</tbody></table>
<div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
<a href="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/-4RanwVh1raQ/VpHL57JZfSI/AAAAAAAAB2Q/aHTFuN2-46c/s1600/Full-Collapse-cover.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;"><span style="color: black;"></span></a></div>
<div style="font-family: Georgia, 'Times New Roman', 'Bitstream Charter', Times, serif; font-size: 16px; line-height: 24px; text-align: justify;">
<span data-mce-style="font-weight: 400;">&nbsp; &nbsp;Early on I saw Thursday open for Saves The Day in a room crowded with Saves The Day admirers. Months later, after that video hit, they played the unwholesome basement of the Nile Theatre in Mesa, Arizona. The space was so jam packed with sweaty fans, dried out and thirsty,&nbsp;that I ended up pushed onto the small stage to the right of guitarist&nbsp;</span><span data-mce-style="font-weight: 400;">Steve Pedulla. Before the show started he</span><span data-mce-style="font-weight: 400;">&nbsp;politely reminded me, "just don't step on these pedals." I probably had no response, shocked into silence, and spent the set dodging the spear end of his guitar. Still one of the best shows I&#8217;ve witnessed. Top ten.</span></div>
<div style="font-family: Georgia, 'Times New Roman', 'Bitstream Charter', Times, serif; font-size: 16px; line-height: 24px; text-align: justify;">
<span data-mce-style="font-weight: 400;">&nbsp; &nbsp; As the years went on the bleeding heart poetry of Thursday came to be almost comical, an easy target, and the band never made another record quite as powerful. Emo and Screamo music blew up so much to the point that the all-American high school jocks who had once made fun of our ironic t-shirts, unkempt hair and glasses, were suddenly donning Thursday and Jimmy Eat World t-shirts trying to be our friends.&nbsp;But who knows? So, they kind of thought&nbsp;<i><span data-mce-style="font-weight: 400;">Full Collapse</span></i><span data-mce-style="font-weight: 400;">&nbsp;was cool. Maybe they, too, had some darkness lurking that the album could explain. Who was I to take that away from them?</span></span></div>
<div style="font-family: Georgia, 'Times New Roman', 'Bitstream Charter', Times, serif; font-size: 16px; line-height: 24px;">
<span data-mce-style="font-weight: 400;"></span></div>
<div style="font-family: Georgia, 'Times New Roman', 'Bitstream Charter', Times, serif; font-size: 16px; line-height: 24px; text-align: justify;">
<span data-mce-style="font-weight: 400;">&nbsp; &nbsp; Over a decade later and most of the bands I discovered on KaZaa early on have rose to prominence with a quicker fade out. I have grown a lot since then and so, too, has the culture. Now, we share things. We share songs and albums with total strangers. And that's okay. Or, at least there's no stopping it, so why bother griping? All music from all eras is now available to us streaming and downloadable. We can relive the crusty gutter punk past or shake our hips to rare disco b-sides like our living rooms are Studio 54. In my Recent Downloads folder I have, side-by-side, without attempting to achieve any irony, The Misfits'&nbsp;</span><i>12 Hits From Hell</i><span data-mce-style="font-weight: 400;">&nbsp;and Selena Gomez's&nbsp;</span><i>Revival</i><span data-mce-style="font-weight: 400;">. So, who really am I now?</span><br />
<span data-mce-style="font-weight: 400;"><br /></span>

<h2 class='date-header'><span>July 23, 2015</span></h2>

          <div class="date-posts">
        
<div class='post-outer'>
<div class='post hentry uncustomized-post-template' itemprop='blogPost' itemscope='itemscope' itemtype='http://schema.org/BlogPosting'>
<meta content='http://4.bp.blogspot.com/-DyD7cjiD8Xc/VbGF7fOtIeI/AAAAAAAAB0E/t-G7_jz6jcM/s400/numb%2Bbats%2Bbees%2Band%2Btrees%2Bcover.jpg' itemprop='image_url'/>
<meta content='4254836637618694860' itemprop='blogId'/>
<meta content='4714569965744712337' itemprop='postId'/>
<a name='4714569965744712337'></a>
<h3 class='post-title entry-title' itemprop='name'>
<a href='http://elijace.blogspot.com/2015/07/review-bees-trees-by-numb-bats.html'>REVIEW: "Bees & Trees" by Numb Bats</a>
</h3>
<div class='post-header'>
<div class='post-header-line-1'></div>
</div>
<div class='post-body entry-content' id='post-body-4714569965744712337' itemprop='description articleBody'>
<div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
<a href="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/-DyD7cjiD8Xc/VbGF7fOtIeI/AAAAAAAAB0E/t-G7_jz6jcM/s1600/numb%2Bbats%2Bbees%2Band%2Btrees%2Bcover.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;"><img border="0" height="520" src="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/-DyD7cjiD8Xc/VbGF7fOtIeI/AAAAAAAAB0E/t-G7_jz6jcM/s400/numb%2Bbats%2Bbees%2Band%2Btrees%2Bcover.jpg" width="520" /></a></div>
<div dir="ltr" style="line-height: 1.38; margin-bottom: 0pt; margin-top: 0pt; text-align: center;">
<span style="font-size: x-large;"><span style="font-family: Georgia; line-height: 1.38; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre-wrap;"><b>Numb Bats Play Through the</b></span></span></div>
<div dir="ltr" style="line-height: 1.38; margin-bottom: 0pt; margin-top: 0pt; text-align: center;">
<span style="font-size: x-large;"><span style="font-family: Georgia; line-height: 1.38; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre-wrap;"><b>Heatwave on</b> </span><span style="font-family: Georgia; line-height: 1.38; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre-wrap;"><i>Bees &amp; Trees</i></span></span></div>
<div dir="ltr" style="line-height: 1.38; margin-bottom: 0pt; margin-top: 0pt; text-align: justify;">
<span style="background-color: transparent; color: black; font-family: Georgia; font-size: 16px; font-style: normal; font-variant: normal; font-weight: normal; text-decoration: none; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre-wrap;">   The music of <a href="https://www.facebook.com/NumbBats" target="_blank">Numb Bats</a> is a little like surf rock, one could say, if the waves that were being surfed upon were made of grease and glue. It&#8217;s punk rock dumped in a Gatorade cooler of molasses. </span></div>
<div dir="ltr" style="line-height: 1.38; margin-bottom: 0pt; margin-top: 0pt; text-align: justify;">
<b id="docs-internal-guid-cff27b04-bd76-1e36-eef6-08dcf538463c" style="font-weight: normal;">&nbsp; &nbsp;</b><span style="font-family: Georgia; font-size: 16px; line-height: 1.38; white-space: pre-wrap;">Numb Bats is a three-piece all-girl group from Phoenix, Arizona where the sun shines harshly seven days a week. Mo Neuharth whacks on the kit, Sophie Opich makes the bass tremble and Emily Hobeheidar croons in the dark. All their voices are heard, though, piping up in the background. &nbsp;</span></div>
<div dir="ltr" style="line-height: 1.38; margin-bottom: 0pt; margin-top: 0pt; text-align: justify;">
<b style="font-weight: normal;">&nbsp; &nbsp;</b><span style="font-family: Georgia; font-size: 16px; line-height: 1.38; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre-wrap;">On July 14, on their <a href="http://www.numbbats.bandcamp.com/" target="_blank">Bandcamp</a> page, Numb Bats self-released their new 6-track EP, </span><span style="font-family: Georgia; font-size: 16px; font-style: italic; line-height: 1.38; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre-wrap;">Bees &amp; Trees. </span><span style="font-family: Georgia; font-size: 16px; line-height: 1.38; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre-wrap;">The collection opens with &#8220;Runnin,&#8221; a side-winding slog of sleepy-eyed punk with reverb that washes through the sewers and drains. The second song, "Rainbow," begins with dreamy arousal then wades into an ascending drum punch.</span></div>
<div dir="ltr" style="line-height: 1.38; margin-bottom: 0pt; margin-top: 0pt; text-align: justify;">
<b style="font-weight: normal;">&nbsp; &nbsp;</b><span style="font-family: Georgia; font-size: 16px; font-style: italic; line-height: 1.38; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre-wrap;">Bees &amp; Trees, </span><span style="font-family: Georgia; font-size: 16px; line-height: 1.38; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre-wrap;">their second EP, is the follow-up to last year's full-length album, </span><span style="font-family: Georgia; font-size: 16px; font-style: italic; line-height: 1.38; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre-wrap;">Gentle Horror</span><span style="font-family: Georgia; font-size: 16px; line-height: 1.38; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre-wrap;">. It's hot and it&#8217;s humid. The structure of these songs is very disorderly, thrown-against-the-wall and unpredictable. They fill in the spaces where the song loses steam with a heavy fog of languorous drone. This is what it feels like to live and breathe in hundred-plus degree heat.</span></div>
<div dir="ltr" style="line-height: 1.38; margin-bottom: 0pt; margin-top: 0pt; text-align: justify;">
<b style="font-weight: normal;">&nbsp; &nbsp;</b><span style="font-family: Georgia; font-size: 16px; line-height: 1.38; white-space: pre-wrap;">And the temperature continues to rise with "Then I Went To The Refrigerator." The strands of feedback come together slowly. It&#8217;s the score to a house without air conditioning, the sound of your heavy eyelids deflecting the sun. It picks up, rupturing into Ren &amp; Stimpy punk, before falling under again.</span></div>
<div dir="ltr" style="line-height: 1.38; margin-bottom: 0pt; margin-top: 0pt; text-align: justify;">
<b style="font-weight: normal;">&nbsp; &nbsp;</b><span style="font-family: Georgia; font-size: 16px; line-height: 1.38; white-space: pre-wrap;">Hobeheidar snarls and barks from the quicksand on &#8220;U R A WINNER.&#8221; &#8220;I feel so weird inside,&#8221; she intones again and again against a fire-pit pounding drum cycle. She sounds fed-up and bleary, caught in a downturn. But, all ends in fairness with a cheeky shout-out from all the girls singing and laughing, &#8220;You are a winner!&#8221;</span></div>
<div dir="ltr" style="line-height: 1.38; margin-bottom: 0pt; margin-top: 0pt; text-align: justify;">
<b style="font-weight: normal;">&nbsp; &nbsp;</b><span style="font-family: Georgia; font-size: 16px; line-height: 1.38; white-space: pre-wrap;">The most distinguished song here is the longest. "Dog Poncho," over five minutes, opens with voices in motion like a mirage on the distant highway. Steady thread of junkie guitar and effects that move from ear to ear like a wasp move the track along. It waltzes and loops through shifting bridges before crumbling into something that sounds like the B-52's after huffing a fair amount of rubber cement. </span></div>
<div dir="ltr" style="line-height: 1.38; margin-bottom: 0pt; margin-top: 0pt; text-align: justify;">
<b style="font-weight: normal;">&nbsp; &nbsp;</b><span style="font-family: Georgia; font-size: 16px; line-height: 1.38; white-space: pre-wrap;">Easy comparisons would be Dum Dum Girls or a less buzzy and distorted Raveonettes. But there is definitely a heavier DIY appeal and a bouncier bubblegum attitude. They&#8217;re definitely sculpting their own post-rock sound.</span></div>
<div dir="ltr" style="line-height: 1.38; margin-bottom: 0pt; margin-top: 0pt; text-align: justify;">
&nbsp; &nbsp;<span style="font-family: Georgia; font-size: 16px; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre-wrap;">The looseness and fragility on </span><span style="font-family: Georgia; font-size: 16px; font-style: italic; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre-wrap;">Bees &amp; Trees</span><span style="font-family: Georgia; font-size: 16px; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre-wrap;"> is part of its charm, but is also a reminder that Numb Bats&#8217; best work could be stuck up in the pipes somewhere, ready to spill out. The group are currently skinning the west half of the country on tour. Find them.</span></div>
<div dir="ltr" style="line-height: 1.38; margin-bottom: 0pt; margin-top: 0pt; text-align: justify;">
<span style="font-family: Georgia; font-size: 16px; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre-wrap;"><br /></span></div>
<div dir="ltr" style="line-height: 1.38; margin-bottom: 0pt; margin-top: 0pt; text-align: justify;">
<span style="font-family: Georgia; font-size: 16px; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre-wrap;"><b>Key Tracks: </b>"Dog Poncho," "Rainbow," "Then I Went To The Refrigerator"</span></div>
<div dir="ltr" style="line-height: 1.38; margin-bottom: 0pt; margin-top: 0pt; text-align: justify;">
<span style="font-family: Georgia; font-size: 16px; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre-wrap;"><br /></span></div>
<div dir="ltr" style="line-height: 1.38; margin-bottom: 0pt; margin-top: 0pt; text-align: right;">

<h3 class='post-title entry-title' itemprop='name'>
<a href='http://elijace.blogspot.com/2015/07/review-star-wars-by-wilco.html'>REVIEW: "Star Wars" by Wilco</a>
</h3>
<div class='post-header'>
<div class='post-header-line-1'></div>
</div>
<div class='post-body entry-content' id='post-body-5676352413281178458' itemprop='description articleBody'>
<div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
<a href="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/--6brvC-c6_E/Va_xgb1_RBI/AAAAAAAABzc/w4-IWfVzTSo/s1600/wilco-star-wars.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;"><img border="0" height="520" src="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/--6brvC-c6_E/Va_xgb1_RBI/AAAAAAAABzc/w4-IWfVzTSo/s400/wilco-star-wars.jpg" width="520" /></a></div>
<div style="font-family: Georgia, 'Times New Roman', 'Bitstream Charter', Times, serif; line-height: 24px; text-align: center;">
<span style="font-size: x-large;"><b>Wilco Casually Drop New Album,</b></span></div>
<div style="font-family: Georgia, 'Times New Roman', 'Bitstream Charter', Times, serif; line-height: 24px; text-align: center;">
<span style="font-size: x-large;"><i>Star Wars<b>, </b></i><b>into the Web (Lucky Us)</b></span></div>
<div style="font-family: Georgia, 'Times New Roman', 'Bitstream Charter', Times, serif; font-size: 16px; line-height: 24px; text-align: justify;">
&nbsp; &nbsp;They&#8217;ve been celebrating twenty years of making music this summer with deluxe releases and discography-browsing setlists during a special anniversary tour. New music from Wilco felt somewhere close, not quite in reach, but somewhere on the distant horizon. Then, by way of Internet surprise, the Chicago band released&nbsp;<i>Star Wars,&nbsp;</i>their ninth album, for free.</div>
<div style="font-family: Georgia, 'Times New Roman', 'Bitstream Charter', Times, serif; font-size: 16px; line-height: 24px; text-align: justify;">
&nbsp; &nbsp;Eh, here you go.</div>
<div style="font-family: Georgia, 'Times New Roman', 'Bitstream Charter', Times, serif; font-size: 16px; line-height: 24px; text-align: justify;">
&nbsp; &nbsp;Main songwriter Jeff Tweedy and crew give us something to remember the summer by.</div>
<div style="font-family: Georgia, 'Times New Roman', 'Bitstream Charter', Times, serif; font-size: 16px; line-height: 24px; text-align: justify;">
<i>Stars Wars</i>&nbsp;opens with a pile of rusty strings on the very loose, very cross-eyed, "EKG." The album quickly warps into "More..." a folk funk jam with oceans of noise settling onto the shore. By the third song, "Random Name Generator," a heel-hammering nugget of rock, it's clear that Wilco are back in the front seat as one of America's greatest bands. Ain&#8217;t no foolin&#8217;.</div>
<div style="font-family: Georgia, 'Times New Roman', 'Bitstream Charter', Times, serif; font-size: 16px; line-height: 24px; text-align: justify;">
&nbsp; &nbsp;In fact,&nbsp;<i>Star Wars&nbsp;</i>might join&nbsp;<i>Yankee Hotel Foxtrot&nbsp;</i>and&nbsp;<i>Sky Blue Sky&nbsp;</i>as some of the most perfectly sculpted albums in rock and roll. For now, maybe, too soon to tell, but it feels right.</div>
<div style="font-family: Georgia, 'Times New Roman', 'Bitstream Charter', Times, serif; font-size: 16px; line-height: 24px; text-align: justify;">
Nearly every song lasts right around the average length of the classic pop song--some two minutes and thirty seconds. Within the short time span the songs are just as evolutionary, tightly-wound and gusto-filled as Wilco's greatest tracks. The held-back restriction of&nbsp;<i>Sky Blue Sky&nbsp;</i>mixes with the full-range expansion of&nbsp;<i>A Ghost Is Born.</i></div>
<div style="font-family: Georgia, 'Times New Roman', 'Bitstream Charter', Times, serif; font-size: 16px; line-height: 24px; text-align: justify;">
&nbsp; &nbsp;Slip into the casual breeze of &#8220;You Satellite&#8221; as it rises to a rushing wind. Ride the range on wisps of steel guitar during &#8220;Taste the Ceiling.&#8221; Get your shoulders up and shimmy along to the egged-on guitar of &#8220;Cold Slope.&#8221; &#8220;I know, I know and you know that I know / It&#8217;s a powerplay,&#8221; Tweedy sings in perfect gyration with the notes.</div>
<div style="font-family: Georgia, 'Times New Roman', 'Bitstream Charter', Times, serif; font-size: 16px; line-height: 24px; text-align: justify;">
&nbsp; &nbsp;Tweedy&#8217;s streaming sense of lyrical weirdness is fully intact on &#8220;The Joke Explained.&#8221; &#8220;I stare at the eyes staring at my face / It always ends in a tie / There is no meeting the divine / I cry at the joke explained,&#8221; he sings over the whirling electric guitar.</div>
<div style="font-family: Georgia, 'Times New Roman', 'Bitstream Charter', Times, serif; font-size: 16px; line-height: 24px; text-align: justify;">
&nbsp; &nbsp;"Pickled Ginger" charges forward in electric calm like a palm-muted version of Wire with sudden groove outbreaks and keyboard fallout. Tweedy warns in a low mumble, &#8220;No one tells me how to behave.&#8221;&nbsp;<i>Star Wars&nbsp;</i>is wrapped up neatly with a sweet, straight-forward love song that slips around. "Magnetized," is an ode to the realization of the love that stands before you.</div>
<div style="font-family: Georgia, 'Times New Roman', 'Bitstream Charter', Times, serif; font-size: 16px; line-height: 24px; text-align: justify;">
&nbsp; &nbsp;Since releasing their debut album,&nbsp;<i>A.M.,&nbsp;</i>in 1995 this band has gone through one of the most exciting transformations in music, shedding old sounds, embracing new tones, letting the old sounds resurface in another genre, but always finding a way to make it&nbsp;sound cohesive, intentional.&nbsp;<i>Star Wars&nbsp;</i>continues the trend with a large arched step forward.</div>
<div style="font-family: Georgia, 'Times New Roman', 'Bitstream Charter', Times, serif; font-size: 16px; line-height: 24px; text-align: justify;">
&nbsp; &nbsp;As Wilco continues to tackle these new songs on tour through the fall, it ought to bring them even further out of whatever comfort zone they thought they had.&nbsp;<i>Star Wars&nbsp;</i>gets the physical CD release August 21 with a vinyl release on November 27 from Wilco&#8217;s own label, dBpm.</div>
<div style="font-family: Georgia, 'Times New Roman', 'Bitstream Charter', Times, serif; font-size: 16px; line-height: 24px; text-align: justify;">
<br /></div>
<div style="font-family: Georgia, 'Times New Roman', 'Bitstream Charter', Times, serif; font-size: 16px; line-height: 24px; text-align: justify;">
<b>Key Tracks: </b>"More...," "Random Name Generator," "Cold Slope," "Pickled Ginger"</div>
<div style="font-family: Georgia, 'Times New Roman', 'Bitstream Charter', Times, serif; font-size: 16px; line-height: 24px; text-align: justify;">

<h2 class='date-header'><span>July 21, 2015</span></h2>

          <div class="date-posts">
        
<div class='post-outer'>
<div class='post hentry uncustomized-post-template' itemprop='blogPost' itemscope='itemscope' itemtype='http://schema.org/BlogPosting'>
<meta content='http://4.bp.blogspot.com/-63As5d9XHS4/Va6-nhnhaCI/AAAAAAAABys/5aQWGzi3bDY/s400/Neil%2BYoung%2BPromise%2Bof%2Bthe%2BReal%2BThe%2BMonsanto%2BYears%2Bcver.jpg' itemprop='image_url'/>

<h3 class='post-title entry-title' itemprop='name'>
<a href='http://elijace.blogspot.com/2015/07/review-monsanto-years-by-neil-young.html'>REVIEW: "The Monsanto Years" by Neil Young + Promise of the Real</a>
</h3>
<div class='post-header'>
<div class='post-header-line-1'></div>
</div>
<div class='post-body entry-content' id='post-body-5938716416090697179' itemprop='description articleBody'>
<div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
<a href="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/-63As5d9XHS4/Va6-nhnhaCI/AAAAAAAABys/5aQWGzi3bDY/s1600/Neil%2BYoung%2BPromise%2Bof%2Bthe%2BReal%2BThe%2BMonsanto%2BYears%2Bcver.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;"><img border="0" height="520" src="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/-63As5d9XHS4/Va6-nhnhaCI/AAAAAAAABys/5aQWGzi3bDY/s400/Neil%2BYoung%2BPromise%2Bof%2Bthe%2BReal%2BThe%2BMonsanto%2BYears%2Bcver.jpg" width="520" /></a></div>
<div style="font-family: Georgia, 'Times New Roman', 'Bitstream Charter', Times, serif; line-height: 24px; text-align: center;">
<b><span style="font-size: x-large;">Neil Young Feeding on Corporate&nbsp;</span></b></div>
<div style="font-family: Georgia, 'Times New Roman', 'Bitstream Charter', Times, serif; line-height: 24px; text-align: center;">
<span style="font-size: x-large;"><b>America with </b><i>The Monsanto Years</i></span></div>
<div style="font-family: Georgia, 'Times New Roman', 'Bitstream Charter', Times, serif; font-size: 16px; line-height: 24px; text-align: justify;">
&nbsp; &nbsp;Neil Young has kept himself busy in this post-divorce era of his career. In 2014 he released two albums,&nbsp;<i>A Letter Home,&nbsp;</i>a lo-fi selection of covers, and&nbsp;<i>Storytone,&nbsp;</i>a solo album with an extra orchestral version attached<i>.</i></div>
<div style="font-family: Georgia, 'Times New Roman', 'Bitstream Charter', Times, serif; font-size: 16px; line-height: 24px; text-align: justify;">
&nbsp; For Young&#8217;s thirty-sixth (<i>gasp</i>) album,&nbsp;<i>The Monsanto Years,&nbsp;</i>he aims his guitar and pen at Monsanto, the company genetically engineering seeds (or GMOs) that are found in plenty of American products. But that&#8217;s not the only major company caught in his flame. He chips away at the facades of Wal-Mart, Starbucks, Safeway and Chevron, a list that reads like most teenagers&#8217; first places of employment.</div>
<div style="font-family: Georgia, 'Times New Roman', 'Bitstream Charter', Times, serif; font-size: 16px; line-height: 24px; text-align: justify;">
&nbsp; &nbsp;Unlike Young&#8217;s more recent statement of protest albums--<i>Greendale</i>,&nbsp;<i>Fork in the Road</i>&nbsp;and&nbsp;<i>Living With War</i>--this one sounds more put together, less slapdash and hurried than the others. In Young&#8217;s last five-year or so late career dash, it&#8217;s his best album.</div>
<div style="font-family: Georgia, 'Times New Roman', 'Bitstream Charter', Times, serif; font-size: 16px; line-height: 24px; text-align: justify;">
&nbsp; &nbsp;California band, Promise of the Real, were enlisted as Young&#8217;s backing band for the album after the two played at last year&#8217;s Farm Aid. The group features Willie Nelson's boys, Lukas and Micah. They bring a wall of rumbling sound, not too dissimilar from Young&#8217;s main band of grizzled beasts in Crazy Horse. &nbsp;</div>
<div style="font-family: Georgia, 'Times New Roman', 'Bitstream Charter', Times, serif; font-size: 16px; line-height: 24px; text-align: justify;">
<i>&nbsp; &nbsp;The Monsanto Years&nbsp;</i>opens on a hopeful note with the revitalizing &#8220;The New Day.&#8221; Young comes right out proclaiming, &#8220;It&#8217;s a bad day to do nothing,&#8221; setting the progressive tone of the album. A beer mug-swinging chorus follows with everybody&#8217;s arms around one another, swaying to and fro. Young closes with a stunted guitar solo that fades out with the song.</div>
<div style="font-family: Georgia, 'Times New Roman', 'Bitstream Charter', Times, serif; font-size: 16px; line-height: 24px; text-align: justify;">
&nbsp; &nbsp;"Wolf Moon" is a sweet serenade pulled from Young's classic&nbsp;<i>Harvest</i>-<i>Harvest Moon</i>&nbsp;era. He sings with a vulnerable quiver to our tortured atmosphere over an acoustic strum that moves along calmly like dripping rain.</div>
<div style="font-family: Georgia, 'Times New Roman', 'Bitstream Charter', Times, serif; font-size: 16px; line-height: 24px; text-align: justify;">
"Big Box" rolls in like a hurricane with mists of Crazy Horse feedback. In the fog are Young's red eyes as he spews down on corporate America and its unjust strangulation of the American worker. "From the capital to the boarded-up main streets / Big Business is there at every turn," he sings, painting a gloomy vision of impossible odds. The rhythm feels like being chased through streets that never cease.</div>
<div style="font-family: Georgia, 'Times New Roman', 'Bitstream Charter', Times, serif; font-size: 16px; line-height: 24px; text-align: justify;">
&nbsp; &nbsp;Young doesn't mince his words. The lyrics could be the frustrated screed of a part-time worker written on a napkin during a fifteen-minute break. "People working part-time at Walmart / Never get the benefits for sure / Why not make it to full-time at Walmart? / Still standing by for the call to work," sung as matter-of-factly as can be.</div>
<div style="font-family: Georgia, 'Times New Roman', 'Bitstream Charter', Times, serif; font-size: 16px; line-height: 24px; text-align: justify;">
&nbsp; &nbsp;On "Rock Star Bucks A Coffee Shop" Young pierces Monsanto all the way through and pokes into Starbucks with the spearhead on the other side. He uses straight-speak lyrics to discuss the intertangling of the two companies sounding as though he&#8217;s singing words from a pamphlet handed out at a rally.</div>
<div style="font-family: Georgia, 'Times New Roman', 'Bitstream Charter', Times, serif; font-size: 16px; line-height: 24px; text-align: justify;">
&nbsp; &nbsp;Surely, the jumble of &#8220;preachy&#8221; words will turn off a lot of casual fans, but the song still has a pulse. It&#8217;s a whistle-while-you-work jingle that rails against the mislabeling practices of Monsanto. The chorus is a heartbreaking helpless plea to the GMO giant. Young ends the song with two simple lines that cut out all political pretense. "Mothers want to know what they feed their children," he implores. "Let our farmers grow what they want to grow."</div>
<div style="font-family: Georgia, 'Times New Roman', 'Bitstream Charter', Times, serif; font-size: 16px; line-height: 24px; text-align: justify;">
&nbsp; &nbsp;Neil Young continues to be the amplified bullhorn for the people, using his artistic merit to sing songs about the many discouragements of modern life. Long may he run.</div>
<div style="font-family: Georgia, 'Times New Roman', 'Bitstream Charter', Times, serif; font-size: 16px; line-height: 24px; text-align: justify;">
<br /></div>
<div style="font-family: Georgia, 'Times New Roman', 'Bitstream Charter', Times, serif; font-size: 16px; line-height: 24px; text-align: justify;">
<b>Key Words: </b>"Wolf Moon," "Big Box," "Workin' Man"</div>
<div style="color: #333333; font-family: Georgia, 'Times New Roman', 'Bitstream Charter', Times, serif; font-size: 16px; line-height: 24px; text-align: justify;">
