<h2 class='date-header'><span>Wednesday, December 30, 2015</span></h2>

          <div class="date-posts">
        
<div class='post-outer'>
<div class='post hentry uncustomized-post-template' itemprop='blogPost' itemscope='itemscope' itemtype='http://schema.org/BlogPosting'>

<h3 class='post-title entry-title' itemprop='name'>
<a href='http://turnitupjack.blogspot.com/2015/12/best-albums-of-2015.html'>Best Albums of 2015</a>
</h3>
<div class='post-header'>
<div class='post-header-line-1'></div>
</div>
<div class='post-body entry-content' id='post-body-4255358291458452352' itemprop='description articleBody'>
In a year of some strikingly original music, the choices seemed somehow easier this year. &nbsp;My musical DNA is on display in all these albums: &nbsp;the long shadow of Prince and Sly; the pulse of early electronic sounds by Kraftwerk and Eno; the pounding noise of the Clash and the Ramones; &nbsp;and the California pop of the Beach Boys and mid-period Fleetwood Mac. &nbsp;It's all in the mix, made endlessly new to make the world understandable -- and danceable.<br />
<div>
<br /></div>
<div>
In no particular order:<br />
<br />
Tame Impala,&nbsp;<em>Currents</em>&nbsp;- An irresistible blend of&nbsp;R&amp;B and&nbsp;electronica proving once again that&nbsp;we're all from somewhere else originally.<br />
<br />
<div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
<iframe allowfullscreen="" class="YOUTUBE-iframe-video" data-thumbnail-src="https://i.ytimg.com/vi/QhlPXa6g4C8/0.jpg" frameborder="0" height="266" src="https://www.youtube.com/embed/QhlPXa6g4C8?feature=player_embedded" width="320"></iframe></div>
<br />
Deerhunter, <em>Fading Frontier</em> - The perfect melancholy of the Beach Boys "In My Room," except that now there's weed and the internet in the room. <br />
<br />
<div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
<iframe allowfullscreen="" class="YOUTUBE-iframe-video" data-thumbnail-src="https://i.ytimg.com/vi/UCVWrqxyt3Y/0.jpg" frameborder="0" height="266" src="https://www.youtube.com/embed/UCVWrqxyt3Y?feature=player_embedded" width="320"></iframe></div>
<br />
<div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
<br /></div>
Sleater-Kinney, <em>No Cities to Love</em> - Hunger makes them modern girls.<br />
<br />
<br />
<div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
<iframe allowfullscreen="" class="YOUTUBE-iframe-video" data-thumbnail-src="https://i.ytimg.com/vi/_jiX-Svw7KQ/0.jpg" frameborder="0" height="266" src="https://www.youtube.com/embed/_jiX-Svw7KQ?feature=player_embedded" width="320"></iframe></div>
<div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
<br /></div>
<br />
Bob Moses, <em>Days Gone By</em> - Like floating on a raft in a swimming pool, McCarron Pool in Brooklyn that is.<br />
<br />
<br />
<div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
<iframe allowfullscreen="" class="YOUTUBE-iframe-video" data-thumbnail-src="https://i.ytimg.com/vi/wT8J8FK5U8Y/0.jpg" frameborder="0" height="266" src="https://www.youtube.com/embed/wT8J8FK5U8Y?feature=player_embedded" width="320"></iframe></div>
<div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
<br /></div>
<br />
Father John Misty, <em>I Love You Honeybear</em> - If this generation has a James Taylor, it might be him.<br />
<br />
<br />
<div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
<iframe allowfullscreen="" class="YOUTUBE-iframe-video" data-thumbnail-src="https://i.ytimg.com/vi/PfjrzZc3bAI/0.jpg" frameborder="0" height="266" src="https://www.youtube.com/embed/PfjrzZc3bAI?feature=player_embedded" width="320"></iframe></div>
<div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
<br /></div>
<br />
Lizz Wright, <em>Freedom and Surrender</em>&nbsp;- If this generation has a Roberta Flack, it might be her. <br />
<br />
<br />
<div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
<iframe allowfullscreen="" class="YOUTUBE-iframe-video" data-thumbnail-src="https://i.ytimg.com/vi/C_yoHLi3BGk/0.jpg" frameborder="0" height="266" src="https://www.youtube.com/embed/C_yoHLi3BGk?feature=player_embedded" width="320"></iframe></div>
<div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
<br /></div>
Thundercat, <em>The Beyond/Where the Giants Roam</em> - Sixteen minutes of perfect trip-hop, from the Flying Lotus branch of the tree. <br />
<br />
<div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
<iframe allowfullscreen="" class="YOUTUBE-iframe-video" data-thumbnail-src="https://i.ytimg.com/vi/GNCd_ERZvZM/0.jpg" frameborder="0" height="266" src="https://www.youtube.com/embed/GNCd_ERZvZM?feature=player_embedded" width="320"></iframe></div>
<div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
<br /></div>
<br />
D'Angelo and the Vanguard, <em>Black Messiah</em> - Searing and salving, D'Angelo came blazing back. <br />
<br />
<div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
<iframe allowfullscreen="" class="YOUTUBE-iframe-video" data-thumbnail-src="https://i.ytimg.com/vi/vo3RAH0zLlU/0.jpg" frameborder="0" height="266" src="https://www.youtube.com/embed/vo3RAH0zLlU?feature=player_embedded" width="320"></iframe></div>
<div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
<br /></div>
<br />
Courtney Barnett, <em>Sometimes I Sit and Think, Sometimes I Just Sit</em> - Because the best rock has a sense of humor.&nbsp; <br />
<div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
<iframe allowfullscreen="" class="YOUTUBE-iframe-video" data-thumbnail-src="https://i.ytimg.com/vi/1NVOawOXxSA/0.jpg" frameborder="0" height="266" src="https://www.youtube.com/embed/1NVOawOXxSA?feature=player_embedded" width="320"></iframe></div>
<div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
<br /></div>
Jaime XX, <em>Colours</em> - The DJ/producer/percussionist&nbsp;as auteur, visionary&nbsp;as Jimi Hendrix at&nbsp;his peak.&nbsp; No shit, really.<br />
<br />
<div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
<iframe allowfullscreen="" class="YOUTUBE-iframe-video" data-thumbnail-src="https://i.ytimg.com/vi/pFf3fNAwJsY/0.jpg" frameborder="0" height="266" src="https://www.youtube.com/embed/pFf3fNAwJsY?feature=player_embedded" width="320"></iframe></div>
<br />
Stay tuned: best singles of 2015 coming right up. <br />

<h3 class='post-title entry-title' itemprop='name'>
<a href='http://turnitupjack.blogspot.com/2015/11/sitting-in.html'>Sitting In</a>
</h3>
<div class='post-header'>
<div class='post-header-line-1'></div>
</div>
<div class='post-body entry-content' id='post-body-634987488274898243' itemprop='description articleBody'>
<div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
<a href="http://www.slate.com/content/dam/slate/articles/arts/books/2015/07/SBR/COVER/150709_SBR_Coates-COVER.jpg.CROP.original-original.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="clear: left; float: left; margin-bottom: 1em; margin-right: 1em;"><img border="0" src="http://www.slate.com/content/dam/slate/articles/arts/books/2015/07/SBR/COVER/150709_SBR_Coates-COVER.jpg.CROP.original-original.jpg" height="200" width="131" /></a></div>
Ta-Nehisi Coates, <i>Between the World and Me</i><br />
<br />
After it won the National Book Award in November, it can't be news to you that Coates has written a sobering book about what it means to be a Black American in 2015. &nbsp;It's time to read it.<br />
<br />
I devoured it in August in a five hour fever dream and was at a loss. &nbsp;It's a complex work full of anger and pessimism, a tale of promising lives cut short even when you play by the rules. &nbsp;Composed as a letter to his 15 year old son, there's not much light and even less hope. &nbsp;What could I do with this? &nbsp;"Live with it," turned about to be the answer. <br />
<br />
Now with campuses across the country having a noisy conversation about race, Coates' missive offers some much needed perspective about the institutionalized inequality we have yet to address. Don't be making that face. &nbsp;Listen for a minute.

<div class='post-header'>
<div class='post-header-line-1'></div>
</div>
<div class='post-body entry-content' id='post-body-5719839423132520501' itemprop='description articleBody'>
December. &nbsp;Can it be? &nbsp;Before the songs of Christmas both ancient and new start to fill your ears, here's one more dose of the Zeitgeist -- new sounds full of the confusion and promise of 2015. &nbsp; No Adele here folks; you get enough of that in your spin class. <br />

Blood Orange, <i>Sandra's Smile</i> - Dev Hynes just keeps topping himself and "The Gloved One."<br />

<br />
<div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">

<br />
<div style="text-align: left;">

<div style="text-align: left;">

<div style="text-align: center;">

<h2 class='date-header'><span>Friday, November 20, 2015</span></h2>

          <div class="date-posts">
        
<div class='post-outer'>
<div class='post hentry uncustomized-post-template' itemprop='blogPost' itemscope='itemscope' itemtype='http://schema.org/BlogPosting'>
<meta content='http://cdn4.pitchfork.com/albums/21842/homepage_large.04192b63.jpg' itemprop='image_url'/>
<meta content='6672529139534779011' itemprop='blogId'/>
<meta content='2242324609429687782' itemprop='postId'/>
<a name='2242324609429687782'></a>
<h3 class='post-title entry-title' itemprop='name'>
<a href='http://turnitupjack.blogspot.com/2015/11/cross-currents_20.html'>Cross Currents</a>
</h3>
<div class='post-header'>
<div class='post-header-line-1'></div>
</div>
<div class='post-body entry-content' id='post-body-2242324609429687782' itemprop='description articleBody'>
<div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
<a href="http://cdn4.pitchfork.com/albums/21842/homepage_large.04192b63.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="clear: left; float: left; margin-bottom: 1em; margin-right: 1em;"><img border="0" src="http://cdn4.pitchfork.com/albums/21842/homepage_large.04192b63.jpg" height="200" width="200" /></a></div>
Tame Impala, <i>Currents</i><br />
<em><br /></em>
<div>
<br /></div>
<div>
Tame Impala first came my way with their 2011 single, "Feels Like We Only Go Backwards," a swirly ballad full of synthesizers that could have come straight off John Lennon's <i>Mind Games</i> in 1973. &nbsp;It was at once a big wet kiss to psychedelia and at the same time a brilliant re-imagination of the genre. &nbsp;The album was <i>Lonerism</i>. Critics swooned. &nbsp;Early '70's psychedelia was back from the dead. <br />
</div>
<div>
<br /></div>
<div>
Cut to 2015 and pivot: &nbsp;TI frontman Kevin Parker surprises us again with a fresh take on another old-school sound. &nbsp;The synth-y wash is still present, as is the moody obsessiveness. &nbsp;But this time it's R&amp;B &nbsp;that gets a re-boot. &nbsp;Not the gritty, Stax soul that Elvis Costello repurposed so memorably on <i>Get Happy</i>. &nbsp;What Parker has in his mind is R&amp;B as the lovechild of the Chi-lites and the Electric Light Orchestra. Todd Rundgren tried this kind of mash-up years ago but nobody paid much attention. &nbsp;But when you hear "Yes, I'm Changing," you will take some serious notice:</div>
<div>
<br /></div>
<div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
<iframe allowfullscreen="" class="YOUTUBE-iframe-video" data-thumbnail-src="https://i.ytimg.com/vi/0uts-_rsxPM/0.jpg" frameborder="0" height="266" src="https://www.youtube.com/embed/0uts-_rsxPM?feature=player_embedded" width="320"></iframe></div>
<div>
<br /></div>
<div>
Then there's the finger-popping, blue-eyed soul of "The Less I Know the Better." &nbsp;Operator, get me Hall &amp; Oates:</div>
<div>
<br /></div>
<div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
<iframe allowfullscreen="" class="YOUTUBE-iframe-video" data-thumbnail-src="https://i.ytimg.com/vi/6lHzySwhUac/0.jpg" frameborder="0" height="266" src="https://www.youtube.com/embed/6lHzySwhUac?feature=player_embedded" width="320"></iframe></div>
<div>
<br /></div>
<div>
But the piece de resistance here is, "Cause I"m A Man," with Parker channeling Michael Jackson. Hoo-rah.&nbsp;</div>
<div>
<br /></div>
<div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
<iframe allowfullscreen="" class="YOUTUBE-iframe-video" data-thumbnail-src="https://i.ytimg.com/vi/EyEB2AEqHxc/0.jpg" frameborder="0" height="266" src="https://www.youtube.com/embed/EyEB2AEqHxc?feature=player_embedded" width="320"></iframe></div>
<div>
<br /></div>
<div>
This post-millennial time warp can be so confusing, but so much fun.&nbsp;</div>
<div style='clear: both;'></div>

<h2 class='date-header'><span>Friday, November 6, 2015</span></h2>

          <div class="date-posts">
        
<div class='post-outer'>
<div class='post hentry uncustomized-post-template' itemprop='blogPost' itemscope='itemscope' itemtype='http://schema.org/BlogPosting'>
<meta content='http://images.huffingtonpost.com/2015-08-29-1440844791-5154395-LizzWright_Photo_by_Jesse_Kitt.jpg' itemprop='image_url'/>
<meta content='6672529139534779011' itemprop='blogId'/>
<meta content='5591887979729028116' itemprop='postId'/>
<a name='5591887979729028116'></a>
<h3 class='post-title entry-title' itemprop='name'>
<a href='http://turnitupjack.blogspot.com/2015/11/take-me-to-church.html'>Take Me To Church</a>
</h3>
<div class='post-header'>
<div class='post-header-line-1'></div>
</div>
<div class='post-body entry-content' id='post-body-5591887979729028116' itemprop='description articleBody'>
<em><br /></em><br />
<br />
<a href="http://images.huffingtonpost.com/2015-08-29-1440844791-5154395-LizzWright_Photo_by_Jesse_Kitt.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="clear: left; float: left; margin-bottom: 1em; margin-right: 1em;"><img border="0" src="http://images.huffingtonpost.com/2015-08-29-1440844791-5154395-LizzWright_Photo_by_Jesse_Kitt.jpg" height="212" width="320" /></a><br />
Lizz Wright, &nbsp;<i>Freedom and Surrender</i><em></em><br />
<br /><br />
In the classic 60's soul era, you could hear the sound of gospel music in most every R&amp;B hit: the note-bending vocals, the call and response, and the longing for transcendent love: think Aretha, Wilson, Otis, Marvin, Sam, Solomon, Mavis. <br />
<br />
<br />
But as the R&amp;B form aged, the church influences waned and its own secular traditions -- first funk, then disco and later hip-hop --&nbsp;exerted a more powerful gravitational pull.<br />
<br />
<br />
<br />
<br />
<br />
Lizz Wright comes from that earlier church-y place but with a distinctive jazz style.&nbsp; On <em>Freedom and Surrender</em>, she brings a gospel foundation to a repertoire that runs from Nick Drake to the brothers Gibb to contemporary neo-soul.&nbsp;&nbsp;With a voice recalling Paula Cole, phrasing to rival Cassandra Wilson, and a glossy production matching Joni Mitchell's classic, <i>Court and Spark</i>, she has produced the year's best grownup album of vocal performance. <br />
<br />
<br />
<br />
Here's her take on the Bee Gee's nearly forgotten, "To Love Somebody," which her aching longing rescues from cheesiness forever:<br />
<br />
<br />
<br />
<br />
<div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
<iframe allowfullscreen="" class="YOUTUBE-iframe-video" data-thumbnail-src="https://i.ytimg.com/vi/boQE_7ax71Y/0.jpg" frameborder="0" height="266" src="https://www.youtube.com/embed/boQE_7ax71Y?feature=player_embedded" width="320"></iframe></div>
<br />
<div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: left;">
<br /></div>
<div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: left;">
"The New Game," shows she can bring us out into the aisles, too. &nbsp;</div>
<div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: left;">
<br /></div>
<div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
<br /></div>
<div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
<iframe allowfullscreen="" class="YOUTUBE-iframe-video" data-thumbnail-src="https://i.ytimg.com/vi/Ye19MlCiEPg/0.jpg" frameborder="0" height="266" src="https://www.youtube.com/embed/Ye19MlCiEPg?feature=player_embedded" width="320"></iframe></div>
<br />
<br />
<br />
Somebody give me an "Amen."
