<h3 class='post-title entry-title' itemprop='name'>
<a href='http://seveninchesisenough.blogspot.com/2016/04/charlie-dold.html'>Charlie Dold</a>
</h3>
<div class='post-header'>
<div class='post-header-line-1'></div>
</div>
<div class='post-body entry-content' id='post-body-6423609060999652763' itemprop='description articleBody'>
A few pretty early releases for the Bus Stop label.<br>
<br>
<br>
<br>
These sound like home 4-track recordings so at 1991 they&#39;d be really early to that party which wouldn&#39;t really start being a thing for at least a couple more years, if my powers of recall are anything. I don&#39;t even think the concept of Lo-fi even existed. But then I wouldn&#39;t reall consider these to be Lo-fi. Maybe Mid-fi.<br>
<br>
<br>
<div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
<a href="http://1.bp.blogspot.com/-f0qi-bTnL3g/VmThA1WTtzI/AAAAAAAABOw/R1YXAxJ-Qs8/s1600/dold%2B-%2Bsins%2Bb.JPG" imageanchor="1" style="clear: left; float: left; margin-bottom: 1em; margin-right: 1em;"><img border="0" height="200" src="http://1.bp.blogspot.com/-f0qi-bTnL3g/VmThA1WTtzI/AAAAAAAABOw/R1YXAxJ-Qs8/s200/dold%2B-%2Bsins%2Bb.JPG" width="200"></a><a href="http://1.bp.blogspot.com/-RCZIKCQNT2k/VmThBNWA5xI/AAAAAAAABO8/GiPAHnWNi1k/s1600/dold%2B-%2Bsins.JPG" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;"><img border="0" height="200" src="http://1.bp.blogspot.com/-RCZIKCQNT2k/VmThBNWA5xI/AAAAAAAABO8/GiPAHnWNi1k/s200/dold%2B-%2Bsins.JPG" width="200"></a></div>
<br>
<div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
</div>
<div style="text-align: center;">
<a href="http://www.mediafire.com/download/sk6f7jkbp1yd8ku/1991__Sins_on_my_Shoulder.zip" target="_blank">1991 - Sins on my Shoulders</a></div>
<div style="text-align: center;">
<br></div>
<div style="text-align: left;">
I&#39;m not totally sure how I feel about Charlie Dold. I like the songs for the most part, but his voice isn&#39;t really my cup of tea/poison. It seems like he&#39;s trying just a bit too hard and pushing his voice into a range and keys that don&#39;t quite suit it. But he sounds like he means it. I don&#39;t doubt that he was a copy shop dude.</div>
<div style="text-align: left;">
<br></div>
<br>
<div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
<a href="http://1.bp.blogspot.com/-kfTtt7_5EBA/VmThBDI_xFI/AAAAAAAABPI/VFKRW2eVcQs/s1600/dold%2B-%2Bany%2Bpoet%2527s.JPG" imageanchor="1" style="clear: left; float: left; margin-bottom: 1em; margin-right: 1em;"><img border="0" height="200" src="http://1.bp.blogspot.com/-kfTtt7_5EBA/VmThBDI_xFI/AAAAAAAABPI/VFKRW2eVcQs/s200/dold%2B-%2Bany%2Bpoet%2527s.JPG" width="200"></a><a href="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/-EFWvl_77fs0/VmThBADFymI/AAAAAAAABPM/Rdh-wQRy5ao/s1600/dold%2B-%2Bany%2Bpoet%2527s%2Bb.JPG" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;"><img border="0" height="200" src="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/-EFWvl_77fs0/VmThBADFymI/AAAAAAAABPM/Rdh-wQRy5ao/s200/dold%2B-%2Bany%2Bpoet%2527s%2Bb.JPG" width="200">&#160;</a></div>
<div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
<a href="http://www.mediafire.com/download/kq8qhmdaudl86wb/1991_-_Any_Poet%27s_Dream.zip" target="_blank">1991 - Any Poet&#39;s Dream</a></div>
<div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: left;">
<br></div>
<div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: left;">
I think the title track here is one of those love songs that likely didn&#39;t get him laid. It&#39;s a bit much. But the other two tracks settle in for some good times. He&#39;s learned to work the 4-track better. We&#39;re on the right track.</div>
<div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
<br></div>
<br>
<div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
<a href="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/-GQfLg1dyhHA/VmThBnpua0I/AAAAAAAABPQ/OX9etsC1ns0/s1600/dold%2B-%2Bwaiting.JPG" imageanchor="1" style="clear: left; float: left; margin-bottom: 1em; margin-right: 1em;"><img border="0" height="200" src="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/-GQfLg1dyhHA/VmThBnpua0I/AAAAAAAABPQ/OX9etsC1ns0/s200/dold%2B-%2Bwaiting.JPG" width="200"></a><a href="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/-rYbDeBjYRBQ/VmThBYdRrXI/AAAAAAAABPU/BUskwpeg_as/s1600/dold%2B-%2Bwaiting%2Bb.JPG" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;"><img border="0" height="200" src="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/-rYbDeBjYRBQ/VmThBYdRrXI/AAAAAAAABPU/BUskwpeg_as/s200/dold%2B-%2Bwaiting%2Bb.JPG" width="200">&#160;</a></div>
<div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
<a href="http://www.mediafire.com/download/d4826b7dm52hvuh/1992_-_Waiting_on_Mercury.zip" target="_blank">1992 - Waiting on Mercury</a></div>
<div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
<br></div>
<div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: left;">
An actual band at last.&#160; Earnest Midwest pop rocking.</div>

<h2 class='date-header'><span>Friday, April 1, 2016</span></h2>

          <div class="date-posts">
        
<div class='post-outer'>
<div class='post hentry uncustomized-post-template' itemprop='blogPost' itemscope='itemscope' itemtype='http://schema.org/BlogPosting'>
<meta content='http://1.bp.blogspot.com/-k0RLkN7Nxjg/VmTagJB_oRI/AAAAAAAABOE/BYgNh_4cAlk/s200/bill%2Bdireen%2Bsm.jpg' itemprop='image_url'/>
<meta content='8678696658649723366' itemprop='blogId'/>
<meta content='9049212703530954299' itemprop='postId'/>

<h3 class='post-title entry-title' itemprop='name'>
<a href='http://seveninchesisenough.blogspot.com/2016/04/april-fools.html'>April Fools</a>
</h3>
<div class='post-header'>
<div class='post-header-line-1'></div>
</div>
<div class='post-body entry-content' id='post-body-9049212703530954299' itemprop='description articleBody'>
<br />
Meh. I got nothing.<br />
<br />
Have a couple from the great state of New Zealand<br />
<br />
<div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
</div>
<div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
</div>
<div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
</div>
<div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
</div>
<div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
</div>
<div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
</div>
<div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
</div>
<div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
<a href="http://1.bp.blogspot.com/-k0RLkN7Nxjg/VmTagJB_oRI/AAAAAAAABOE/BYgNh_4cAlk/s1600/bill%2Bdireen%2Bsm.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="clear: left; float: left; margin-bottom: 1em; margin-right: 1em;"><img border="0" height="200" src="http://1.bp.blogspot.com/-k0RLkN7Nxjg/VmTagJB_oRI/AAAAAAAABOE/BYgNh_4cAlk/s200/bill%2Bdireen%2Bsm.jpg" width="200" /></a><a href="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/-n8yHl0fBi5o/VmTeJRJU7iI/AAAAAAAABOc/88jI7NZ7Xs8/s1600/bill%2Bdireen%2Bb%2Bsm.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;"><img border="0" height="200" src="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/-n8yHl0fBi5o/VmTeJRJU7iI/AAAAAAAABOc/88jI7NZ7Xs8/s200/bill%2Bdireen%2Bb%2Bsm.jpg" width="200" /></a></div>
<br />
<div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
</div>
<div style="text-align: center;">
&nbsp;<a href="http://www.mediafire.com/download/uab0jq0dcqg74qd/Bill_Direen_%26_the_Builders.zip" target="_blank">1991 - Bill Direen &amp; the Builders</a></div>
<div style="text-align: center;">
<br /></div>
<div style="text-align: left;">
<a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Bilders" target="_blank">Bill Direen</a> is hard to describe. He's been making interesting music since the dawn of time along with theater and writing and god knows what else. He's one stop art shopping. And he seems like the kind of guy who just does it all and doesn't give a fuck if anybody pays attention or not. He's just going to do it anyway.<br />On this one he does a redux of "Alien" from his "Six Impossible Things" 7" (of which 100 were pressed I have been told) with Chris Knox and Mike Dooley who were together in the seminal NZ bands Toy Love &amp; the Enemy. The band trees of all three participants are long and complicated and I'm hungry. You can investigate that shit on your own dime. It's worth it.</div>
<div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
</div>
<div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
</div>
<br />
On the other side the song is "Skulls" and he's joined by Alan Meek who was in a few incarnations of the Bilders/Builders as well as the Victor Dimisich Band and the Vacuum. (You have some googling to do, my friend.)<br />
<br />
<br />
<div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
<a href="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/-c-VRqSi8FX8/VmTagd-uj3I/AAAAAAAABOQ/6WGSu7fF9KI/s1600/dimmer%2Bsm.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="clear: left; float: left; margin-bottom: 1em; margin-right: 1em;"><img border="0" height="200" src="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/-c-VRqSi8FX8/VmTagd-uj3I/AAAAAAAABOQ/6WGSu7fF9KI/s200/dimmer%2Bsm.jpg" width="200" /></a><a href="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/-Jpjty2vDsCE/VmTeJaYZybI/AAAAAAAABOg/tYdLjeJ5JLo/s1600/dimmer%2Bbs.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;"><img border="0" height="200" src="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/-Jpjty2vDsCE/VmTeJaYZybI/AAAAAAAABOg/tYdLjeJ5JLo/s200/dimmer%2Bbs.jpg" width="200" />&nbsp;</a></div>
<div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
<a href="http://www.mediafire.com/download/rsc28o6x0heoyoc/Dimmer.zip" target="_blank">1995 - Dimmer</a></div>
<div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
<br /></div>
<div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: left;">
This is Shayne Carter post-Straitjacket Fits and in his youth a member of the Doublehappys. It's instrumental. It's on SubPop. Let that be your guide. </div>

</h3>
<div class='post-header'>
<div class='post-header-line-1'></div>
</div>
<div class='post-body entry-content' id='post-body-966268020340967896' itemprop='description articleBody'>
Volume Two<br />
<br />
Because I can and it's my blog and I'll cry if I want to.<br />
<br />
<div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
<a href="http://1.bp.blogspot.com/-fDralnn3OVY/VmTJiYHjukI/AAAAAAAABNw/7oVKkDQI1mM/s1600/VA_WickedGoodTime%2B2.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;"><img border="0" height="317" src="http://1.bp.blogspot.com/-fDralnn3OVY/VmTJiYHjukI/AAAAAAAABNw/7oVKkDQI1mM/s320/VA_WickedGoodTime%2B2.jpg" width="320" /></a></div>
<div style="text-align: center;">
<a href="http://www.mediafire.com/download/9rb8ro6zm1slisy/A_Wicked_Good_Time_vol_2.zip" target="_blank">1982 - A Wicked Good Time Vol 2</a></div>
<div style="text-align: center;">
<br /></div>
<div style="text-align: left;">
When last we convened we had the first venerable lp of a small percentage of Boston's alternative music scene to consider. It was followed up a while later with this second volume of more of the same. Somebody still thought Boy's Life and Someone and the Somebodies were worth recording but balanced it out with the Freeze and the first appearance of cold wave dance music by November Group. In some ways this one is both more and less successful than the previous volume, but I find less chaff overall. They didn't make another one.</div>

<h2 class='date-header'><span>Saturday, March 26, 2016</span></h2>

          <div class="date-posts">
        
<div class='post-outer'>
<div class='post hentry uncustomized-post-template' itemprop='blogPost' itemscope='itemscope' itemtype='http://schema.org/BlogPosting'>
<meta content='http://4.bp.blogspot.com/-lLn5_BuKzjo/VmTCvSWO1nI/AAAAAAAABNc/dr4dNOTcVro/s320/VA_WickedGoodTime.jpg' itemprop='image_url'/>
<meta content='8678696658649723366' itemprop='blogId'/>
<meta content='6028036324972756587' itemprop='postId'/>
<a name='6028036324972756587'></a>
<h3 class='post-title entry-title' itemprop='name'>
<a href='http://seveninchesisenough.blogspot.com/2016/03/a-wicked-good-time.html'>A Wicked Good Time</a>
</h3>
<div class='post-header'>
<div class='post-header-line-1'></div>
</div>
<div class='post-body entry-content' id='post-body-6028036324972756587' itemprop='description articleBody'>
After a marathon session slaving over a hot turntable and a pair of laptops I'm ready for a fresh round of homemade postings with oodles of sarcastic gravy. Loosen your belt, and strap on your feedbag.<br />
<br />
Compilation Lps. Can't live with 'em. Can't do without 'em. Always a mixed bag. But the good ones balance out the duff tracks with stuff of such wonder you tend to forget that you wasted four minutes of your life on some terrible crap waiting for the good stuff to happen again.<br />
<br />
Case in point:<br />
<br />
<div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
<a href="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/-lLn5_BuKzjo/VmTCvSWO1nI/AAAAAAAABNc/dr4dNOTcVro/s1600/VA_WickedGoodTime.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;"><img border="0" height="320" src="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/-lLn5_BuKzjo/VmTCvSWO1nI/AAAAAAAABNc/dr4dNOTcVro/s320/VA_WickedGoodTime.jpg" width="320" /></a></div>
<div style="text-align: center;">
<a href="http://www.mediafire.com/download/q3ot6mbic6a3nig/A_Wicked_Good_Time.zip" target="_blank">1981 - A Wicked Good Time</a></div>
<div style="text-align: center;">
<br /></div>
<div style="text-align: left;">
In the heady days before Newbury Comics was the beloved Boston institution that it has become it was a scrappy little hole in the wall on Newbury st that had a selection of otherwise hard to find punk and new wave records in the front and comic books in the back. Either of&nbsp; which, if you'd been a more savvy consumer at the time could be worth a small fortune now. I wasn't, but I did buy records from a young Aimee Mann. I have that going for me.</div>
<div style="text-align: left;">
<br /></div>
<div style="text-align: left;">
&nbsp;So here Newbury Comics teamed up with WBCN (in those heady pre-Clear Channel days when radio stations could play stuff not chosen by middle managers) to put out a compilation of local alternative bands (from when "alternative" meant an actual alternative to mainstream AOR rather than a marketing term used by mainstream AOR to sell you other shitty music).&nbsp;</div>
<div style="text-align: left;">
<br /></div>
<div style="text-align: left;">
This was the result.</div>
<div style="text-align: left;">
<br /></div>
<div style="text-align: left;">
It's not perfect. It does have some chaff and an inexplicable double dose of Bound &amp; Gagged, but I defy you&nbsp; not to come away from a listen without humming "Psycho Blonde" at some point in your day.&nbsp; Other highlights are the Outlets good and fast punk rocking, the Vacuumheads snarky "Preppie Girls" which my friend Jim and I thought was hilarious in high school. And the recorded debut of Young Snakes featuring the previously mentioned Aimee Mann being all angular and new wavey. (The actual ep they'd release a bit later is fucking genius). All in all a solid compilation of a small fraction of what was actually going on in town. Omissions galore but they were starting a record label an had an agenda. I'm ok with it.</div>
<div style='clear: both;'></div>

<h2 class='date-header'><span>Monday, March 21, 2016</span></h2>

          <div class="date-posts">
        
<div class='post-outer'>
<div class='post hentry uncustomized-post-template' itemprop='blogPost' itemscope='itemscope' itemtype='http://schema.org/BlogPosting'>
<meta content='http://4.bp.blogspot.com/-7cM6S3RXfdc/VluFH4qdHxI/AAAAAAAABMk/70yDJxeP3NM/s200/del%2Bfuegos.jpg' itemprop='image_url'/>
<meta content='8678696658649723366' itemprop='blogId'/>
<meta content='5593605144801650689' itemprop='postId'/>
<a name='5593605144801650689'></a>
<h3 class='post-title entry-title' itemprop='name'>
<a href='http://seveninchesisenough.blogspot.com/2016/03/ahh-youth.html'>Ahh Youth</a>
</h3>
<div class='post-header'>
<div class='post-header-line-1'></div>
</div>
<div class='post-body entry-content' id='post-body-5593605144801650689' itemprop='description articleBody'>
A couple from a misspent young adulthood in the Bay State.<br />
<br />
<br />
<br />
<div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
<a href="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/-7cM6S3RXfdc/VluFH4qdHxI/AAAAAAAABMk/70yDJxeP3NM/s1600/del%2Bfuegos.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="clear: left; float: left; margin-bottom: 1em; margin-right: 1em;"><img border="0" height="196" src="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/-7cM6S3RXfdc/VluFH4qdHxI/AAAAAAAABMk/70yDJxeP3NM/s200/del%2Bfuegos.jpg" width="200" /></a><a href="http://1.bp.blogspot.com/-I8N08ZlYmHk/VluFHmBCafI/AAAAAAAABMY/LLz797-r9cc/s1600/del%2Bfuegos%2Bb.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;"><img border="0" height="196" src="http://1.bp.blogspot.com/-I8N08ZlYmHk/VluFHmBCafI/AAAAAAAABMY/LLz797-r9cc/s200/del%2Bfuegos%2Bb.jpg" width="200" /></a></div>
<div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
<br /></div>
<div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
1982 - Del Fuegos</div>
<div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
<br /></div>
<div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
There was a point in the early part of the 80's when if you were in Boston and pulled up a random rock you might find the Del Fuegos playing under it. I didn't actually ever go out specifically to see them, but ended up seeing them quite frequently anyway playing with other bands I intended to see, They were doing well for a while too. Got signed to a major label, had a minor hit and managed to make it all the way to appearing in a <a href="https://youtu.be/88cewhasU74" target="_blank">beer commercial</a> which I'm still not sure they've ever quite lived down.</div>
<div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
This primitive slice is their debut single. You can almost smell the bathroom at the Rat in it.</div>
<div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
<br /></div>
<div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
<br /></div>
<div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
<br /></div>
<div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
<a href="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/-Hm5emnteM5Q/VluFHpjgpzI/AAAAAAAABMc/luEr_uHA-yk/s1600/dark.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="clear: left; float: left; margin-bottom: 1em; margin-right: 1em;"><img border="0" height="200" src="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/-Hm5emnteM5Q/VluFHpjgpzI/AAAAAAAABMc/luEr_uHA-yk/s200/dark.jpg" width="189" /></a><a href="http://1.bp.blogspot.com/-UHxNjyJVf2E/VluFHmwxAeI/AAAAAAAABMg/E4uAWBZguOM/s1600/dark%2Bb.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;"><img border="0" height="191" src="http://1.bp.blogspot.com/-UHxNjyJVf2E/VluFHmwxAeI/AAAAAAAABMg/E4uAWBZguOM/s200/dark%2Bb.jpg" width="200" /></a></div>
<br />
<br />
<div style="text-align: center;">
1981 - You Are in No Danger</div>
<div style="text-align: center;">
<br /></div>
<div style="text-align: center;">
Side one of this features a song called "Judy" the song is about a stalker level obsession with Judy Grunwald who at the time was in a band called the Maps (whose single "I'm Talking to You" is referenced in the intro and which I'll get around to posting probably a couple years from now.) She was also formed the band Salem 66 after leaving the Maps (who continued on as Art Yard who recorded but didn't get to release what would have been a <a href="https://youtu.be/jui-MEz-6Uo" target="_blank">monumental single on Propeller Records</a>) &nbsp;"Judy" however was a minor local hit for the Dark in Boston and I listened to it a shit ton in high school without ever actually seeing the band play live.</div>
<div style="text-align: center;">
The line "I'm really glad you dumped your boyfriend/He looks like an East German Border Guard" is alone enough to make it a classic for anybody except conceivably Judy's ex-boyfriend.</div>
<div style="text-align: center;">
All that being said there are also two other songs on the seven inch. You can interpret that how you wish.</div>
<br />
<div style='clear: both;'></div>

<a href='http://seveninchesisenough.blogspot.com/2016/03/another-compilation-lp.html'>Another compilation lp.</a>
</h3>
<div class='post-header'>
<div class='post-header-line-1'></div>
</div>
<div class='post-body entry-content' id='post-body-2661118477901618998' itemprop='description articleBody'>
Because I can and it's my blog and I can cry if I want to.<br />
<br />
<div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
<a href="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/-t7vCwLxLr5k/VluAsKEANjI/AAAAAAAABMI/feIMWjhoeYM/s1600/LastPogo-Front.jpeg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;"><img border="0" src="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/-t7vCwLxLr5k/VluAsKEANjI/AAAAAAAABMI/feIMWjhoeYM/s1600/LastPogo-Front.jpeg" /></a></div>
