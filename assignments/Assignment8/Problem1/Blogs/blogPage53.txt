'Feed the blog' &nbsp;he says. &nbsp;What should I feed a blog with? &nbsp;Bread and raw carrots won't be any use. Not sure a banana and strawberry smoothie will make its way to the belly of a blog either. &nbsp;Money? &nbsp;well even if I had some, I don't think that's what is required. &nbsp;Words.. I think he means words. &nbsp;hmmm &nbsp;what words to use to satisfy a hungry ( &nbsp;oooh &nbsp;now hold on.. &nbsp;is it hungry? It doesn't grumble &nbsp;or whinge or hover round the kitchen asking the usual questions.. 'when's tea? what is for tea?.. eeeuw' &nbsp;) &nbsp; So what words should I give to an empty blog? &nbsp;Pointless ones? &nbsp; &nbsp;That is something I probably could provide. &nbsp; Blunt, futile and &nbsp;my very &nbsp;favourite...inconsequential.<br />

          <div class="date-posts">
        
<div class='post-outer'>

<h3 class='post-title entry-title' itemprop='name'>
<a href='http://mo-forgetaboutit.blogspot.com/2013/01/3rd-bit.html'> 3rd bit?</a>
</h3>
<div class='post-header'>
<div class='post-header-line-1'></div>
</div>
<div class='post-body entry-content' id='post-body-627616607285936538' itemprop='description articleBody'>
She screwed up her face in confusion. Obviously not the card speaking so did she think that out loud? Was it something she thought? Was that really what was written on the card? &nbsp;She turned the card over... &nbsp;no ,it wasn't what was written on the card. Hmm &nbsp;so did she think it or hear it? &nbsp; 'Aaaargh,' a quick look at the clock meant standing here contemplating hearing voices wasn't an option, she was going to be late for work if she didn't move.<br />
She grabbed her jacket , threw the postcard into her bag and headed off.<br />
<br />
She admired the postcard up on the wall, on the cork board above her desk. It covered half a painting that was created about 2 years ago by the &nbsp;youngest child.. &nbsp;it was a colourful one &nbsp;but lacked.. &nbsp;hmm, definition? She couldn't remember now what it was supposed to be she guessed she did know once. &nbsp;But now the card obliterated half the colours. She wondered about the voice again.. &nbsp;but she knew it must have just been her thoughts given a 3D look.

<h3 class='post-title entry-title' itemprop='name'>
<a href='http://mo-forgetaboutit.blogspot.com/2013/01/paragraph-2.html'>paragraph 2</a>
</h3>
<div class='post-header'>
<div class='post-header-line-1'></div>
</div>
<div class='post-body entry-content' id='post-body-1833651355626999914' itemprop='description articleBody'>
<span style="background-color: #f5e4e9; color: #632035; font-family: Helvetica, Arial, Verdana, 'Trebuchet MS', sans-serif; font-size: 13px; text-align: justify;">With a deep sigh, she wandered through to the kitchen where she put the card down on the work surface. That was a slight exaggeration, she put it down on top of an old newspaper which in turn was on top of a dish towel which was on top of the work surface. She battered on with the general morning melee, boiling the kettle, reaching for breakfast cereals, loading dishes into the dishwasher, ushering children awake, or chasing them out the door and every time she passed the pretty postcard she wondered about the person who had sent it. Tall? no, this wasn't from a giant. Not blonde either. More than likely male,about her age, (probably younger, the odds were in that favour) Wow! she was good she thought, 'Look at all the information I have gleamed from a small piece of card.' She grinned,(luckily no one else was there to see it... a locked room would have been on the cards or at the very least a 6 hour psychotherapy session on Dr Very Expensive,('but I will make you feel so much better darling')'s couch.) She was being silly. She did know this information, just not from the card but from other means, she just didn't know what they were.&nbsp;</span><br style="background-color: #f5e4e9; color: #632035; font-family: Helvetica, Arial, Verdana, 'Trebuchet MS', sans-serif; font-size: 13px; text-align: justify;" /><span style="background-color: #f5e4e9; color: #632035; font-family: Helvetica, Arial, Verdana, 'Trebuchet MS', sans-serif; font-size: 13px; text-align: justify;">A bang of a door somewhere in the house sent her rushing for the card and without knowing quite why she deftly pushed the it under the newspaper. She raised her eyes as he came in and caught that glower that he always wore now, or at least while he was in her company. She checked out the floor again. He said something. "Grunt grunt mumble"? but she wasn't sure, nor was she sure of the correct answer. Turned out one wasn't needed. He lifted his car keys and turned and left. The card, now back in her hand, said "Civility would help".</span>

<h2 class='date-header'><span>Friday, 28 December 2012</span></h2>

          <div class="date-posts">
        
<div class='post-outer'>
<div class='post hentry uncustomized-post-template' itemprop='blogPost' itemscope='itemscope' itemtype='http://schema.org/BlogPosting'>

<h3 class='post-title entry-title' itemprop='name'>
<a href='http://mo-forgetaboutit.blogspot.com/2012/12/paragraph-1.html'>paragraph 1</a>
</h3>
<div class='post-header'>
<div class='post-header-line-1'></div>
</div>
<div class='post-body entry-content' id='post-body-2124334380218091456' itemprop='description articleBody'>
When the first one arrived she stared for a long time at it lying on the floor amongst the other post. It looked out of place amongst the brown and white envelopes of the bills and statements and the garish glossy junk mail. She watched it for a long while, &nbsp;maybe it would do tricks? But it didn't seem to do anything, &nbsp;just stayed where it was nestling amongst its minions. &nbsp;She stooped to pick it up, just the card ,everything else would wait, and she heard her knee click, (this old age thing is no big shakes). &nbsp;It wasn't heavy, this came as no surprise but it did take her a while to straighten back up and luckily there was the table by the door to use as a lever. As she looked at it ,her fingers were caressing it, feeling it in her fingers. Wondering if just by touching it she would get a sense of where it came from and more importantly from who(m?). &nbsp; The best and quickest way to find that out would be to read the thing, and so she did. It didn't help much. &nbsp;A foreign and exotic location and no clues with the writing.. What was she hoping for? &nbsp;A signed passport photograph of the sender attached in the bottom right hand corner? &nbsp;She held it closer to her face and sniffed it but the only scent she could detect were the faint traces of rain and paper together and the 'real lemon' smell on her hand that had come from the washing up liquid.
