<h3 class='post-title entry-title' itemprop='name'>
<a href='http://ilovetotaldestruction.blogspot.com/2016/04/the-monochrome-set-john-peel-sessions.html'>THE MONOCHROME SET : John Peel sessions 1979-80 (Radio broadcast).</a>
</h3>
<div class='post-header'>
<div class='post-header-line-1'></div>

<div class='post-body entry-content' id='post-body-6534108900475289973' itemprop='description articleBody'>

<a href="http://s262.photobucket.com/albums/ii111/diskochimp/?action=view&amp;current=SET3-1.jpg" target="_blank"><img alt="SET3" border="0" src="http://i262.photobucket.com/albums/ii111/diskochimp/SET3-1.jpg" /></a></div>

<a href="http://s262.photobucket.com/albums/ii111/diskochimp/?action=view&amp;current=SET4.jpg" target="_blank"><img alt="SET4" border="0" src="http://i262.photobucket.com/albums/ii111/diskochimp/SET4.jpg" /></a></div>

<div>
<a href="http://s262.photobucket.com/albums/ii111/diskochimp/?action=view&amp;current=SET1-1.jpg" target="_blank"><img alt="SET1" border="0" src="http://i262.photobucket.com/albums/ii111/diskochimp/SET1-1.jpg" /></a></div>
No explanation necessary...&nbsp;<a href="http://www.themonochromeset.co.uk/"><u>The Monochrome Set</u>'</a>s 3 John Peel sessions, recorded and broadcast 1979-80 during the band's bedeviled Dindisc era.&nbsp;<i>PTOOFF!</i><br />
<div>
<br /></div>
<div>
A couple of songs herein appear (sonically spruced up) on Cherry Red's&nbsp;<i>absolutely mandatory</i>&nbsp;<u><a href="http://www.amazon.co.uk/Contrast-Brilliance-Sessions-Singles-VINYL/dp/B00ETA7VLS/ref=sr_1_2?ie=UTF8&amp;qid=1459530495&amp;sr=8-2&amp;keywords=monochrome+set+volume+contrast"><i>Volume, Contrast, Brilliance</i></a></u>&nbsp;compilation in 1983 but, for the most part, these performances have remained unavailable and unheard since the BBC last aired them. I was an avid listener of Peel's show throughout the 1980s and certainly don't ever remember him repeating them. Sound quality is inevitably a little patchy in places - the abiding curse of&nbsp;<u><a href="http://www.rickmaybury.com/bootpages/other/tv4.jpg">early '80s Amstrad technology</a></u>&nbsp;I'm afraid - but if you're an admirer I doubt you'll be anything less than thrilled to see them here and, anyway, that crepitating Medium Wave reception merely adds to their&nbsp;<i>mythic allure...</i></div>
<div>
<br />
Prompted, perhaps, by frontman Bid's recent brush with serious ill health, and with original members Lester Square and Andy Warren both back on board, the band reformed in 2010 and have subsequently released a couple of equitable LPs via their own reactivated&nbsp;<u><a href="https://s-media-cache-ak0.pinimg.com/736x/3d/77/7a/3d777aaa190e0debd7c6f99684750d98.jpg">Disque Bleu</a></u>&nbsp;label.&nbsp;<i>Spaces Everywhere</i>, their 15th depending on what you do and don't count, appeared early last year on Hamburg's&nbsp;<u><a href="http://www.tapeterecords.de/artists/the-monochrome-set/">Tapete Records</a></u>&nbsp;(followed by the long-awaited second volume of&nbsp;<i>Volume, Contrast, Brilliance</i>). They've also made a welcome return to live performance, broader of waist and slacker of jowl (<i>well, aren't we all darling</i>) perhaps,&nbsp;but still insidiously catchy and frequently laugh out loud funny.</div>
<div>
<br /></div>
<div>
As the sleeve notes to Love Zombies genially advocated: "<i>Roll back the carpet, switch out the light, and dance in the glow of the firelight as The Monochrome Set provide your very own music, far from the maddening crowd of the dance halls</i>".</div>
<div>

<a href="http://s1381.photobucket.com/user/rooksby1/media/66501422-4ac5-4061-a940-8012a7d637eb_zpskg6y95e5.jpg.html" target="_blank"><img alt="1crispy photo 66501422-4ac5-4061-a940-8012a7d637eb_zpskg6y95e5.jpg" border="0" src="http://i1381.photobucket.com/albums/ah228/rooksby1/66501422-4ac5-4061-a940-8012a7d637eb_zpskg6y95e5.jpg" /></a><br />
<a href="http://s1381.photobucket.com/user/rooksby1/media/35aad2b0-8041-4d0b-a919-61f51e85a0ca_zpsbeasxok4.jpg.html" target="_blank"><img alt="2crispy photo 35aad2b0-8041-4d0b-a919-61f51e85a0ca_zpsbeasxok4.jpg" border="0" src="http://i1381.photobucket.com/albums/ah228/rooksby1/35aad2b0-8041-4d0b-a919-61f51e85a0ca_zpsbeasxok4.jpg" /></a><br />
<a href="http://s1381.photobucket.com/user/rooksby1/media/fc39525a-2e31-4610-8874-5271e0e5c960_zpsuq0q23xt.jpg.html" target="_blank"><img alt="3crispy photo fc39525a-2e31-4610-8874-5271e0e5c960_zpsuq0q23xt.jpg" border="0" src="http://i1381.photobucket.com/albums/ah228/rooksby1/fc39525a-2e31-4610-8874-5271e0e5c960_zpsuq0q23xt.jpg" /></a><br />
<a href="http://s1381.photobucket.com/user/rooksby1/media/dbcb57ae-b481-4ba2-84f9-be1034bc4049_zps3psrv8tz.jpg.html" target="_blank"><img alt="4crispy photo dbcb57ae-b481-4ba2-84f9-be1034bc4049_zps3psrv8tz.jpg" border="0" src="http://i1381.photobucket.com/albums/ah228/rooksby1/dbcb57ae-b481-4ba2-84f9-be1034bc4049_zps3psrv8tz.jpg" /></a><br />
Like many of Manchester's foremost punk-catalysed musicians, lifelong friends Alan Hempsall (vocals) and Robert Davenport (guitar) originally formed&nbsp;<u><a href="http://www.crispyambulance.com/">Crispy Ambulance</a></u>&nbsp;after witnessing the Sex Pistol's Manchester debut Lesser Free Trade Hall performance in 1976. Cryptically (and brilliantly) Christened by close friend Graham Massey (Biting Tongues / 808 State), the duo played their first gig on 1st January 1978 at a youth club in Stockport, with Massey stepping up to add electric violin on a couple of songs. Keith Derbyshire (bass) and Gary Madeley (drums) were recruited shortly thereafter - thereby establishing a line-up that would remain steadfastly in place for the duration of the band's existence, even after their confusing change of monicker (to Ram Ram Kino, taken from a German sex cinema) and defection to Psychic TV's Temple label (following a swan-song Crispy Ambulance performance at Nottingham's long-forgotten Ad Lib club in October '82). The same four core members reformed 17 years later at Manchester's Band On The Wall and continue to tour and release the occasional album, often-as-not with Massey at the controls.<br />
<br />
"<i>The motivation for formation for me was a combination if seeing the Sex Pistols at their first Manchester gig in July 1976 in front of an audience of about 40, made up mainly of Bowie clones and hippies, and seeing Magazine's first gig. The latter had a more immediate effect, with me forming Crispy Ambulance a mere six weeks after seeing Magazine. None of our early tunes passed the test of time, mainly because it took about 18 months to find an identity. People asked about (the name) and how it originated every time we did an interview. At the time every other band was called "The..." - fill in the blank space - whereas our name gave nothing away with regard to image, musical style, etc, but at the same time captured the imagination.</i><br />
<br />
<i>Joy Division stumbled upon us in July 1978 at a gig with played in Manchester and they liked our approach, even if the material was a little weak - to say the least. They dragged Rob Gretton, their new manager, down to see us some months later and, as a result, we did a gig with them at The Factory around the time that Unknown Pleasures was released. Tony (Wilson) never liked us, but suffered us because Rob liked what we did. &nbsp;Since he had become an equal shareholder (in Factory Records, following Ian Curtis's death) Tony had no choice but to bite his lip. Tony craftily got us off his back by depositing us on Factory Benelux, which we didn't object to because Tony was only making things difficult for us on Factory, whereas Michel Duval, boss of Factory's Belgian counterpart, genuinely liked us and had an enthusiasm for the records almost as strong as our own</i>." (Excerpts from an interview with Alan Hempsall by James Nice of&nbsp;<u><a href="http://www.ltmrecordings.com/">Les Temps Modernes</a></u>).<br />
<br />
Crispy Ambulance were mocked from the out-set as artless provincial Joy Division plagiarists, and took a lot of flack for their unfashionable beards and (gasp) flares, though musically they had as much in common with the dystopian prog of&nbsp;<i>Obscured By Clouds</i>&nbsp;or&nbsp;<i>Space Ritual</i>&nbsp;than&nbsp;<i>Unknown Pleasures</i>. Their only Factory-era long-player, 1982's unfairly neglected&nbsp;<i><u><a href="http://factorybenelux.com/the_plateau_phase_fbn12cd.html">The Plateau Phase</a></u></i>, is one of the label's finest "lost" releases: mystical, elemental and meditative, but never as relentlessly despairing or apocalyptic as Joy Division. Regrettably, much like The Wake's&nbsp;<i>Harmony</i>&nbsp;and Minny Pop's&nbsp;<i>Sparks In A Dark Room</i>, it's been written out of most histories of the label in favour of its obstreperous (and remunerative) Madchester era. Their storming January '81 appearance at the ICA's annual Rock Week (supporting The Passage and in the company of Cabaret Voltaire, This Heat, The Blue Orchids, the Soft Boys, The Cravats, Lemon Kittens, Altered Images, and most of the Postcard bands) was scathingly dismissed in the following week's NME as "<i>so uninspiring and uninspired that they do not deserve to waste any more of this space</i>". No doubt they were too busy grooming Kid Creole &amp; The Coconuts for success instead?<br />
<b><br /></b><b>Set-list:</b>&nbsp;Egypt / Come On / Drug User, Drug Pusher / New Violence / Batman - Dracula / Hell's Bells / Deaf / The Presence / From the Cradle to the Grave / October 1st.<br />
<br />
(Sound quality = a cautious B, i.e. crude in places, but surprisingly listenable considering the recordist's Walkman was concealed amongst the ensconcing folds of a dead man's overcoat.)<br />
<br />

          <div class="date-posts">
        
<div class='post-outer'>
<div class='post hentry uncustomized-post-template' itemprop='blogPost' itemscope='itemscope' itemtype='http://schema.org/BlogPosting'>
<meta content='http://i1381.photobucket.com/albums/ah228/rooksby1/a8b8f41c-e498-4cc3-94f9-b76dd80f4414_zpsbkldypbm.jpg' itemprop='image_url'/>

<a name='645856286191581190'></a>
<h3 class='post-title entry-title' itemprop='name'>
<a href='http://ilovetotaldestruction.blogspot.com/2016/01/the-teardrop-explodes-zoology-head.html'>THE TEARDROP EXPLODES : Zoology (To Rhyme With Eulogy) (Head Heritage CD, 2004).</a>
</h3>
<div class='post-header'>
<div class='post-header-line-1'></div>
</div>
<div class='post-body entry-content' id='post-body-645856286191581190' itemprop='description articleBody'>
<a href="http://s1381.photobucket.com/user/rooksby1/media/a8b8f41c-e498-4cc3-94f9-b76dd80f4414_zpsbkldypbm.jpg.html" target="_blank"><img alt=" photo a8b8f41c-e498-4cc3-94f9-b76dd80f4414_zpsbkldypbm.jpg" border="0" src="http://i1381.photobucket.com/albums/ah228/rooksby1/a8b8f41c-e498-4cc3-94f9-b76dd80f4414_zpsbkldypbm.jpg" /></a><br />
<a href="http://s1381.photobucket.com/user/rooksby1/media/1df4bb68-a6e6-4c10-8a83-d1fbdccbc628_zpsfplgf2dl.jpg.html" target="_blank"><img alt=" photo 1df4bb68-a6e6-4c10-8a83-d1fbdccbc628_zpsfplgf2dl.jpg" border="0" src="http://i1381.photobucket.com/albums/ah228/rooksby1/1df4bb68-a6e6-4c10-8a83-d1fbdccbc628_zpsfplgf2dl.jpg" /></a><br />
Last time I checked, <i>Zoology</i> was commanding in excess of &#163;100 on Amazon (and only marginally less on Discogs). Though several of its most interesting tracks have since been officially licensed for release on Mercury's various "historical" (and, frankly, better quality) collections - many of the "demos" included here are actually poorly recorded "off-air" captures of BBC session songs for instance - <i>Zoology</i>&nbsp;still harbours a slender depository of anachronistic cassette-sourced curiosities that you'd be hard-pressed to find anywhere else. So, as it's been summarily deleted from the <u><a href="https://www.headheritage.co.uk/">Head Heritage</a></u> catalogue, and is far superior to Document's earlier/similar <u><a href="http://www.discogs.com/Teardrop-Explodes-Piano/release/431839"><i>Piano</i></a></u> collection, I suggest you dig in (before shelling out for those bonus-packed "<u><a href="http://www.amazon.co.uk/s/ref=nb_sb_noss_2?url=search-alias%3Dpopular&amp;field-keywords=teardrop+explodes+deluxe&amp;rh=n%3A229816%2Ck%3Ateardrop+explodes+deluxe">deluxe</a></u>" editions of <i>Kilimanjaro</i> and <i>Wilder</i> obviously).<br />
<br />
Originally assembled by The Teardrop Explodes' pissed-off ex-<i>obergruppenf√ºhrer</i>&nbsp;in response to his former managers' promiscuous re-repackaging of the band's antecedent back catalogue, <i>Zoology</i> contains a handful of esoteric nuggets which may still blow the mind of any unsuspecting Cope afficianado: the <i>ridiculously</i> early rehearsal room recording of "(Read It In) Books" by the Arch Drude's pre-TX outfit A Shallow Madness featuring the Bunnymen's Ian McCulloch on vocals; Cope's funereal&nbsp;<i>as-nature-intended</i> pre-ensemble demo of "You Disappear From View"; and the psalmic rendition of John Cale's "I'm Not The Loving Kind" (from a <i>Wilder</i>-era Richard Skinner session) are my personal favourites.<br />
<br />
Meanwhile, anybody in search of further primitive Drude-ness would do well to step <u><a href="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=swQ0_OHEaJw">this way</a></u>.<br />
<br />
<b>&#9679;&nbsp;<a href="http://www.mediafire.com/download/pp8ebzomsylq3dw/TXzoo.zip">Nobody knows this is everywhere</a></b>

<h2 class='date-header'><span>4.11.15</span></h2>

          <div class="date-posts">
        
<div class='post-outer'>
<div class='post hentry uncustomized-post-template' itemprop='blogPost' itemscope='itemscope' itemtype='http://schema.org/BlogPosting'>
<meta content='http://i1381.photobucket.com/albums/ah228/rooksby1/e2fdbe1c-a45e-454a-ac74-219b893ff029_zpsskhdrtbo.jpg' itemprop='image_url'/>
<meta content='7870294340192514974' itemprop='blogId'/>
<meta content='6077250001485491709' itemprop='postId'/>
<a name='6077250001485491709'></a>
<h3 class='post-title entry-title' itemprop='name'>
<a href='http://ilovetotaldestruction.blogspot.com/2015/11/ut-1st-john-peel-session-15th-may-1984.html'>UT : 1st John Peel session - 15th May 1984 (Radio broadcast).</a>
</h3>
<div class='post-header'>
<div class='post-header-line-1'></div>
</div>
<div class='post-body entry-content' id='post-body-6077250001485491709' itemprop='description articleBody'>
<a href="http://s1381.photobucket.com/user/rooksby1/media/e2fdbe1c-a45e-454a-ac74-219b893ff029_zpsskhdrtbo.jpg.html" target="_blank"><img alt="1ut photo e2fdbe1c-a45e-454a-ac74-219b893ff029_zpsskhdrtbo.jpg" border="0" src="http://i1381.photobucket.com/albums/ah228/rooksby1/e2fdbe1c-a45e-454a-ac74-219b893ff029_zpsskhdrtbo.jpg" /></a><br />
<a href="http://s1381.photobucket.com/user/rooksby1/media/4f07cb61-dbcf-4c0d-8c99-97072cfbdbc4_zpsgp4lgv21.png.html" target="_blank"><img alt="3ut photo 4f07cb61-dbcf-4c0d-8c99-97072cfbdbc4_zpsgp4lgv21.png" border="0" src="http://i1381.photobucket.com/albums/ah228/rooksby1/4f07cb61-dbcf-4c0d-8c99-97072cfbdbc4_zpsgp4lgv21.png" /></a><br />
<a href="http://s1381.photobucket.com/user/rooksby1/media/f924c746-2bf3-4a2d-9825-5c078474705a_zpseuhsy100.png.html" target="_blank"><img alt="2ut photo f924c746-2bf3-4a2d-9825-5c078474705a_zpseuhsy100.png" border="0" src="http://i1381.photobucket.com/albums/ah228/rooksby1/f924c746-2bf3-4a2d-9825-5c078474705a_zpseuhsy100.png" /></a><br />
Though I was already a regular listener to Peel's show by the time <u><a href="http://www.utmusic.net/">UT</a></u> recorded their first session for him, I've no recollection of him broadcasting it &amp; was probably in the pub arguing about Echo &amp; The Bunnymen when I should've been at home listening to it. That said, I expect I may have still considered their aloof abstractions slightly too challenging at that point - &amp; too great a stylistic leap from my (then) beloved New Order, Sisters Of Mercy, &amp; Three Johns. Even The Fall would've sounded unusually orthodox by comparison I thnk? It would be another year or so, following my discovery of Sonic Youth's Bad Moon Rising LP &amp; its radical re-wiring of the electric guitar, before UT &amp; I finally crossed paths.<br />
<br />
<div>
It appears that a lot of other ardent tapers were similarly nonchalant as I've never managed to find a complete recording of UT's set - I've pieced this one together from 3 different sources, so you can expect some minor sonic turbulence, but the important thing is that it's complete.<br />
<div>
<br /></div>
<div>
Amusingly, the session was produced by Radio 2's Mark Radcliffe, who you're more likely to hear pontificating about archaic Manchester punk or extolling the (questionable) virtues of Elbow from Salford's garish MediaCity - an imperious glass citadel for corporate luvvies riding the BBC's licence fee gravy train - nowadays.</div>
<div>
<br /></div>
<div>
<b>Track-list:</b> Confidential / Absent Farmer / Tell It (Atomic Energy Pattern) / Phoenix.</div>
<div>
<br /></div>
<div>
