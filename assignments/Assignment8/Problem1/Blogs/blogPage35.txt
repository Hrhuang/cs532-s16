<h2 class='date-header'><span>Tuesday, December 1, 2015</span></h2>

          <div class="date-posts">
        
<div class='post-outer'>
<div class='post hentry uncustomized-post-template' itemprop='blogPost' itemscope='itemscope' itemtype='http://schema.org/BlogPosting'>
<meta content='http://cdn2.pitchfork.com/news/60538/6a5cb4c4.jpg' itemprop='image_url'/>
<meta content='746853886426466381' itemprop='blogId'/>

<h3 class='post-title entry-title' itemprop='name'>
<a href='http://jakobclapensteam.blogspot.com/2015/12/three-great-metal-albums-from-2015.html'>Three Great Metal Albums From 2015</a>
</h3>
<div class='post-header'>
<div class='post-header-line-1'></div>
</div>
<div class='post-body entry-content' id='post-body-8307169413422174845' itemprop='description articleBody'>
This year will hereafter be known to me as "The Year I Got Into Metal." Winter happened and I guess this year I just wanted heavier, louder music to go with the season. Credit should go to the folks who write Stereogum's <a href="http://www.stereogum.com/category/franchises/the-black-market/">"The Black Market"</a> columns, because that column is how I found out about most of these albums.<br />
<br />
DISCLAIMER: There's an absolutely massive amount of metal music out there with countless sub-genres. I haven't even begun to scrape the surface of the music. As such, this post should not be taken as a "best of" list, because I haven't listened to the thousands of other albums that probably would be in contention for that designation. I won't pretend to be some aficionado of metal; these are simply three metal albums I've heard from this year that I think are good and some thoughts I have about them. These three would probably make my list of top albums in any genre for this year, but I've decided to do metal separately. My top ten list for all the rest is coming soon.<br />
<br />
<b>"New Bermuda" </b><b>&#8212;  Deafheaven</b><br />
genres: black metal, post-metal, not really metal at all, post-progressive thrash, pop-rock, etc.<br />
<br />
<div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
<a href="http://cdn2.pitchfork.com/news/60538/6a5cb4c4.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="clear: left; float: left; margin-bottom: 1em; margin-right: 1em;"><img border="0" src="http://cdn2.pitchfork.com/news/60538/6a5cb4c4.jpg" height="200" width="200" /></a></div>
Metal albums often suffer because of their bloated run times. Too many albums have unnecessary interludes or needlessly repetitive, overlong songs. Even though it's a monumental piece of work and my <a href="http://jakobclapensteam.blogspot.com/2013/12/2013-music-roundup.html">top album of 2013 in any genre</a>, Deafheaven's "Sunbather" has several interlude tracks that don't need to be there and push the album's run time close to the hour mark. That's not to say the San Francisco band's latest is any better than "Sunbather," but at a taut 46 minutes, "New Bermuda" is better sequenced than its predecessor. The precise production flows so well mostly because of the band's brilliance as songwriters. Instead of resorting to cheap repetition or filler, the extended pieces here are through-composed and say only what they need to say.&nbsp;After purists cried that the band's previous album wasn't metal enough (or even metal at all), "New Bermuda" is heavier but somehow more progressive than "Sunbather" at the same time. At times, Kerry McCoy strums his guitar with the ferocity of black metal, but other times he trudges with it through thrashy chords ("Luna," "Come Back"), flies with it through classic rock solos ("Baby Blue") and sneaks it through the cracks with the nuances of post-rock ("Brought To The Water"). George Clarke's vocals are more cohesive and commanding, especially on closer "Gifts For The Earth," where you can almost tell what words he's screaming. "Gifts" is a perfect album closer, being both heavy as hell and the riskiest, prettiest and most progressive track on the album. If that's the shape of things to come, so be it. Deafheaven will no doubt continue to push the boundaries of metal and continue knocking it out of the park with every album they release.<br />
<br />
<b>"Anareta" </b><b>&#8212;  Horrendous</b><br />
genres: classic death metal, melodic (?) death metal, old school ripping death metal, nihilistic death metal<br />
<br />
<div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
<a href="http://static.stereogum.com/uploads/2015/10/horrendous.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="clear: left; float: left; margin-bottom: 1em; margin-right: 1em;"><img border="0" src="http://static.stereogum.com/uploads/2015/10/horrendous.jpg" height="200" width="200" /></a></div>
No single second of the third album by Philadelphia death trio Horrendous is boring. They don't play any uninteresting notes. Opener "The Nihilist" is balls-to-the-wall from the beginning, taking a catchy yet blistering riff and cleverly using repetition to turn it every which way. Horrendous strike me as a band that would sound just as polished live as they do on record &#8212; they're totally locked in, operating on the same otherworldly wavelength on this album. The guitar work is stunning. Every song has a great riff and is bursting with ideas and creativity. That's all the more impressive because "Anareta" came just a year after the band's previous &#8212;  and highly acclaimed &#8212;  release "Ecdysis." The songs on this album have you thinking they couldn't possibly go farther but then they pulverize your face into the ground over and over. Standout "Acolytes" starts with a thrash-death tidal wave of manic, twisting guitar riffs. Then it backs off to a math-rock groove before shattering your ears in a cacophonous conclusion. It just keeps getting better and better until it ends. And then you'll want to start it over, wishing that your speakers weren't already at max volume.<br />
<br />
<b>"The Children of the Night" </b><b>&#8212;  Tribulation</b><br />
genres: heavy rock, classic rock, <a href="http://pitchfork.com/features/show-no-mercy/9633-feeling-of-horror-the-dark-aura-of-swedens-tribulation/">"death metal for stadiums"</a><br />
<b> </b><br />
<div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
<a href="http://static.stereogum.com/uploads/2015/04/tribulation1-558x560.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="clear: left; float: left; margin-bottom: 1em; margin-right: 1em;"><img border="0" src="http://static.stereogum.com/uploads/2015/04/tribulation1-558x560.jpg" height="200" width="198" /></a></div>
A descending organ line invites you in with the first notes of Swedish band Tribulation's latest album. A piano joins the organ, mirroring the same theme of "Strange Gateways Beckon." The beginning riff seems to come from the opposite side of the metaphorical gateway the title alludes to, taunting and daring the listener to join Tribulation for a ride in the dark, theatrical masterpiece that is "The Children of the Night." The Swedes are classicists, embracing the theater elements that have been a part of metal for decades. Take for example the <a href="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=TNZgqxAxaj4">title track's music video</a>, which is so cliche I can't imagine them being serious while filming it. The music is decidedly classic, too, with larger-than-life, clean guitar sounds and pounding drums that seem to be clear nods to Led Zeppelin. I'm not the first to compare the band to those London rock titans &#8212; or to point out the drummer Jakob Ljungberg's <a href="http://pitchfork-cdn.s3.amazonaws.com/longform/150/tribulation18652.jpg">Bonham-esque mustache</a>. And like Zep, Tribulation make every beat feel like a cannon blast. But the crisp guitar work from Adam Zaars and Jonathan Hultén balances the heaviness &#8212; the riffs are light and catchy as hell. Most songs establish a central guitar melody or theme and vary it, like "Melancholia," where the riff loops into a solo eventually toward the end. On standout "The Motherhood of God," all that comes together in a blistering banger anchored by Ljungberg's drums. In the following song, "Strains of Horror," an ominous organ ushers in a great guitar solo from Zaars that's tasteful, catchy and commanding of the listener's attention. From the album's opening notes, Tribulation drag you down into their cavern of horror and you can't get out until they stop playing.<br />
<br />
Three honorable mentions:<br />
<br />
<b><a href="https://candlelightrecordsusa.bandcamp.com/album/the-accuser">"The Accuser" &#8212; Abigail Williams</a>: </b>An extreme metal surprise from some veterans of the genre that's as bizarre as it is interesting.<br />
<br />
<b><a href="https://thetruepanopticon.bandcamp.com/">"Autumn Eternal" &#8212; Panopticon</a>: </b>A black metal epic from the eclectic Austin Lunn, who moved to Minnesota and started a craft brewery. In all his music Lunn does an uncanny job of capturing the sounds of the environment around him.<br />
<br />
Baroness's <a href="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=DnYO7iQfQDQ">"Chlorine and Wine"</a> deserves a mention here. It's one of my favorite songs of the year, but I haven't heard their new album yet.<br />
<br />
Thanks for reading. My list of favorite albums in other genres is forthcoming.<br />
<br />

<h2 class='date-header'><span>Friday, September 26, 2014</span></h2>

          <div class="date-posts">
        
<div class='post-outer'>
<div class='post hentry uncustomized-post-template' itemprop='blogPost' itemscope='itemscope' itemtype='http://schema.org/BlogPosting'>
<meta content='http://consequenceofsound.files.wordpress.com/2014/02/kendrick-jppg.jpg' itemprop='image_url'/>
<meta content='746853886426466381' itemprop='blogId'/>
<meta content='8060584628201027914' itemprop='postId'/>
<a name='8060584628201027914'></a>
<h3 class='post-title entry-title' itemprop='name'>
<a href='http://jakobclapensteam.blogspot.com/2014/09/kendrick-lamars-new-single-should-be.html'>Kendrick Lamar's New Single Should Be The Theme Song For #HeForShe</a>
</h3>
<div class='post-header'>
<div class='post-header-line-1'></div>
</div>
<div class='post-body entry-content' id='post-body-8060584628201027914' itemprop='description articleBody'>
<div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
<a href="http://consequenceofsound.files.wordpress.com/2014/02/kendrick-jppg.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;"><img border="0" src="http://consequenceofsound.files.wordpress.com/2014/02/kendrick-jppg.jpg" height="266" width="400" /></a></div>
Unfortunately for his much-maligned melancholy rival Drake, Kendrick Lamar can do no wrong in the eyes of most of the hip-hop community. I won't waste words by writing about how well-loved his first two albums are. This week, he released the first single from his forthcoming album.<br />
<br />
"i" has been divisive. The <a href="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=q1DDgNCLD84">Isley Brothers</a>-sampling beat has been called too light. The lyrics have been called too sappy. Lamar's nasally voice on the song has put off some listeners. People say this song isn't the "real Kendrick." They want the darkness of "good kid, m.A.A.d city" instead of a pop tune.<br />
<br />
There's some truth to that. It's not his best song. I'm reminded of when <a href="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=EZW7et3tPuQ">"Backseat Freestyle"</a> came out and&nbsp; people were taken aback by how he went from a <a href="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Z5yb2H7Fqi8">conscious Reagan-hater on "Section.80"</a> to a kid wanting a bigger penis. But the song made perfect sense in the context of "good kid, m.A.A.d city" and now it's one of his biggest songs.<br />
<br />
"i" will surely make sense in the context of Lamar's new album. The outro lyrics are different from the rest of the song<span style="font-family: Times,&quot;Times New Roman&quot;,serif;"><span class="st">&#8212;"I lost my head," "Woes keep me / It's a jungle inside"</span></span><span style="font-family: Times,&quot;Times New Roman&quot;,serif;"><span class="st">&#8212;</span></span>and could be part of a narrative setting up a darker song after "i" on the album.<br />
<br />
<div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
<object class="BLOGGER-youtube-video" classid="clsid:D27CDB6E-AE6D-11cf-96B8-444553540000" codebase="http://download.macromedia.com/pub/shockwave/cabs/flash/swflash.cab#version=6,0,40,0" data-thumbnail-src="https://ytimg.googleusercontent.com/vi/jltN3fLFmTQ/0.jpg" height="420" width="315"><param name="movie" value="https://youtube.googleapis.com/v/jltN3fLFmTQ&source=uds" /><param name="bgcolor" value="#FFFFFF" /><param name="allowFullScreen" value="true" /><embed width="420" height="315"  src="https://youtube.googleapis.com/v/jltN3fLFmTQ&source=uds" type="application/x-shockwave-flash" allowfullscreen="true"></embed></object></div>
<br />
The biggest thing I take away from "i," though, is its positivity. It's a fun, poppy song. The funky sample is a throwback to the soul-charged pop <a href="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Ce7j9aVStwQ">Kanye West</a> and <a href="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=PWgvGjAhvIw">OutKast</a> gave us a decade ago. The jazzy bass solo at the end could fit right in to "The Love Below."<br />
<br />
Lamar touched on the song's subject matter on GKMC's <a href="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=w4wcfMZwi4E">"Real"</a> two years ago. "i" is far more direct and gets to its straightforward hook in less than 30 seconds. Lamar hasn't hidden his obstacle-strewn Compton upbringing, but on this song he says "fuck it, let's just keep on going."<br />
<br />
This is a song you just can't hate. It's too much of a feel-good number to say it's truly awful. Letting "paranoia haunt you," as Lamar puts it, has been a theme of pop culture and society for the last decade, but his answer to it is "lift up your head and keep moving." It's not an original point, but it's a hard one to disagree with.<br />
<br />
Emma Watson's recent speech on the United Nations' <a href="http://www.heforshe.org/">#HeForShe</a> campaign had some of the same points. "Fighting for women's rights," she says, "has too often become synonymous with man-hating." It's alienated men from feminism and it's got to stop "for certain," she says.<br />
<br />
<div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
<object class="BLOGGER-youtube-video" classid="clsid:D27CDB6E-AE6D-11cf-96B8-444553540000" codebase="http://download.macromedia.com/pub/shockwave/cabs/flash/swflash.cab#version=6,0,40,0" data-thumbnail-src="https://ytimg.googleusercontent.com/vi/p-iFl4qhBsE/0.jpg" height="420" width="315"><param name="movie" value="https://youtube.googleapis.com/v/p-iFl4qhBsE&source=uds" /><param name="bgcolor" value="#FFFFFF" /><param name="allowFullScreen" value="true" /><embed width="420" height="315"  src="https://youtube.googleapis.com/v/p-iFl4qhBsE&source=uds" type="application/x-shockwave-flash" allowfullscreen="true"></embed></object></div>
<br />
Feminism is not just about women, she says. By definition, it's the theory of the equality of the sexes. In school, as her female friends dropped out of their favorite sports to avoid looking too masculine, Watson's male friends were unable to express their feelings for fear of looking too feminine.<br />
<br />
Suicide is the leading killer of men in the U.K. ages 20 to 49, Watson points out. That's more than car accidents, cancer and heart disease. A stigma still surrounds mental health worldwide. Too many people make jokes about suicide while too few know how to recognize the signs of it.<br />
<br />
Men feel immense pressure from society, and it's hard for them to love themselves. Not often do we talk about men being confined by gender stereotypes, but neither sex has the benefit of gender equality, Watson says.<br />
<br />
Gender equality should be about celebrating and loving oneself. We should embrace our identities as men and women instead of confining them. We should celebrate our sexualities and our feelings and we should be able to express them however we want. We should be happy about who we are.<br />
<br />
It will take decades, perhaps even centuries for gender equality to be realized. But it won't take that long for some things to change. Along with our identities, we should celebrate the little differences.<br />
<br />
Superstars like Kendrick Lamar and Emma Watson have the power to change minds and mentalities. Watson's speech and Lamar's single couldn't have surfaced at a better time and they complement each other beautifully. With their words, maybe the distorted building blocks with which we've created these gender stereotypes will start to crumble. Maybe men and women will start to appreciate and respect one another as equal, beautiful beings. Maybe we will finally start to love each other.<br />
<br />
But that starts with loving ourselves.

<h2 class='date-header'><span>Friday, August 15, 2014</span></h2>

          <div class="date-posts">
        
<div class='post-outer'>
<div class='post hentry uncustomized-post-template' itemprop='blogPost' itemscope='itemscope' itemtype='http://schema.org/BlogPosting'>
<meta content='http://www.highsnobiety.com/files/2014/06/fka-twigs-two-weeks-1.jpg' itemprop='image_url'/>
<meta content='746853886426466381' itemprop='blogId'/>
<meta content='3805712786109828667' itemprop='postId'/>
<a name='3805712786109828667'></a>
<h3 class='post-title entry-title' itemprop='name'>
<a href='http://jakobclapensteam.blogspot.com/2014/08/fka-twigs-lp1.html'>FKA twigs - "LP1"</a>
</h3>
<div class='post-header'>
<div class='post-header-line-1'></div>
</div>
<div class='post-body entry-content' id='post-body-3805712786109828667' itemprop='description articleBody'>
<table align="center" cellpadding="0" cellspacing="0" class="tr-caption-container" style="margin-left: auto; margin-right: auto; text-align: center;"><tbody>
<tr><td style="text-align: center;"><a href="http://www.highsnobiety.com/files/2014/06/fka-twigs-two-weeks-1.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: auto; margin-right: auto;"><img border="0" src="http://www.highsnobiety.com/files/2014/06/fka-twigs-two-weeks-1.jpg" height="225" width="400" /></a></td></tr>
<tr><td class="tr-caption" style="text-align: center;">Listen to FKA twigs' album "LP1" at the bottom of this post.</td></tr>
</tbody></table>
<span class="Apple-style-span">The internet bought in to the hype surrounding Gloucestershire, England singer Tahliah Barnett last year due to her generally brilliant "EP2." Featuring generally brilliant production from <a href="https://soundcloud.com/arca1000000">Arca</a>, the project took risks and about-faced more than most full-length albums. This all turned out brilliant, generally. On top of that, her eclectic visual presence, especially the vaguely pornographic and generally brilliant video to <a href="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=OydK91JjFOw">"Papi Pacify,"</a> convinced most internet music nerds that FKA twigs was generally brilliant and something they had never seen before. The music drew comparisons to Bj</span><span class="Apple-style-span" style="line-height: 16px;"><span class="Apple-style-span" style="font-family: inherit;">ö</span></span><span class="Apple-style-span">rk and Aaliyah.</span><br />
<br />
Barnett's debut full-length came out this week to general acclaim. Critics usually&nbsp;froth at the mouth after&nbsp;a record this hyped comes out, but the universal praise for "LP1" is perplexing.<br />
<br />
What is perhaps the record's biggest problem comes at its beginning. The only redeeming quality of "Prelude" is a brief, slick hip-hop breakdown in the middle, but it does little to showcase Barnett's talent. The only reason it appears to be there is to push the album's run time over 40 minutes so people can better justify spending 10 bucks on it. It's clear filler.<br />
<br />
That's a shame, because the opener proper, "Lights On," might be the best of the album's four good songs&#8212;yes, four; you read that correctly. The honking synth, acoustic bass, and panflute bring to mind jazz and world music, not too far from the eclectic (and admittedly goofy) sounds of&nbsp;<a href="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=kPqtpY5VgYo">Pat Metheny</a>. The chords in the chorus switch from minor to major in a manner similar to <a href="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=a_OEJ0wqt2g">"Spain."</a><br />
<br />
From there the record goes in to sinister, brooding lead single "Two Weeks." While the excellent <a href="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=3yDP9MKVhZc">video</a>&nbsp;for the track is less sexual than that of "Papi Pacify," the lyrics are not. "You know I'd quench that thirst," "I can fuck you better than her," "My thighs are apart for when you're ready to breathe in," etc. Barnett delivers these bristly lyrics in her deadpan way, slinking in and out of the accompaniment and moving with the song like a wisp of smoke. Her voice tensely pushes higher and higher, nearing its limit as it explodes in a cathartic release in the bridge, which is one of the best musical moments on record so far in 2014.<br />
<br />
But with the brilliant tandem of the album's opening two songs, it's already peaked. The album never reaches the musical or lyrical intensity after those two songs, going into the boring "Hours." The song feels like it wasn't finished. Its verse is disappointingly undeveloped. The song leans on the crutch of its lackluster hook and becomes redundant in the process.<br />
<br />
<div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
<a href="http://www.xlrecordings.com/uploads/news/633.jpeg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;"><img border="0" src="http://www.xlrecordings.com/uploads/news/633.jpeg" height="177" width="400" /></a></div>
<br />
Barnett previously found work as a backup dancer for pop videos, including Jessie J's <a href="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=pOf3kYtwASo">"Do It Like A Dude."</a>&nbsp;That's the thematic material for "Video Girl." The song feels forced and whiny. Why should anyone care that Barnett gets annoyed by people asking her if she's "the girl from the video?"<br />
<br />
<span class="Apple-style-span">The second half of the record is mostly disposable. The production on "Numbers" is quirky and catchy in the way that&nbsp;</span>Bj<span class="Apple-style-span" style="line-height: 16px;"><span class="Apple-style-span" style="font-family: inherit;">ö</span></span><span class="Apple-style-span">rk</span><span class="Apple-style-span">'s album <a href="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=B5Dc1LEn4AM">"Debut"</a> is, but again, the songwriting leaves much to be desired. "Closer" and "Give Up" are both lethargic, their hooks too generic and poppy.</span><br />
<br />
Closer "Kicks," however, has the same glitchy minimalism that trip-hop acts like <a href="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=00PdHIPjaWQ">Portishead</a> explored a decade ago. As the longest track on the album, "Kicks" fills all of its five minutes and 25 seconds meaningfully, swirling through various sounds and structures. The closer is Barnett's best vocal performance of the album. Barnett dives in to her Knowles-esque lower register on the song in addition to stabbing the melody with her piercing falsetto.<br />
<br />
The deliberate ballad "Pendulum" builds ever so slowly. Palm-muted guitar and echoing keyboard evoke post-punk. A clicking sound in the beat sounds like a knock on a door as Barnett tentatively delivers the song's longing lyrics: "So lonely trying to be yours."<br />
<br />
The album has some great moments and Barnett has loads of potential. But most of it is mediocre. It gets boring quickly, and then gets even more boring. It's a short album by comparison, with only ten tracks, but most of the songs end up sounding twice as long as they actually are. "EP2" sounded consistent because Barnett stuck with one producer. On her debut LP, she uses a slew of hip-hop, pop, and avant-garde producers and it sounds disjointed. Barnett isn't a good enough songwriter yet to cook up a good album out of the diverse and challenging ingredients she has here from the producers.<br />
<br />
"LP1" has loads more personality than other recent efforts by <a href="https://itunes.apple.com/us/album/in-the-lonely-hour-deluxe/id826860709">British R&amp;B singers</a>. That being said, it doesn't deserve to be put in a category with the best albums of the year. It has very little lasting power besides those few choice singles. And it's light-years away from work that the genre greats to whom she's drawing comparisons have done. Barnett has a lot of work to do if she wants to avoid being forgotten in a year.<br />
<br />
Grade: C<br />
<br />

<h2 class='date-header'><span>Sunday, August 10, 2014</span></h2>

          <div class="date-posts">
        
<div class='post-outer'>
<div class='post hentry uncustomized-post-template' itemprop='blogPost' itemscope='itemscope' itemtype='http://schema.org/BlogPosting'>
<meta content='http://images.starpulse.com/Photos/Previews/Spoon-band-15.jpg' itemprop='image_url'/>

<h3 class='post-title entry-title' itemprop='name'>
<a href='http://jakobclapensteam.blogspot.com/2014/08/spoon-they-want-my-soul.html'>Spoon - "They Want My Soul"</a>
</h3>
<div class='post-header'>
<div class='post-header-line-1'></div>
</div>
<div class='post-body entry-content' id='post-body-7779615628810925969' itemprop='description articleBody'>
<table cellpadding="0" cellspacing="0" class="tr-caption-container" style="margin-left: auto; margin-right: auto; text-align: center;"><tbody>
<tr><td style="text-align: center;"><a href="http://images.starpulse.com/Photos/Previews/Spoon-band-15.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: auto; margin-right: auto;"><img border="0" src="http://images.starpulse.com/Photos/Previews/Spoon-band-15.jpg" /></a></td></tr>
<tr><td class="tr-caption" style="text-align: center;">Listen to Spoon's new album "They Want My Soul" at the bottom of this post.</td></tr>
</tbody></table>
<div dir="ltr" style="line-height: 1.15; margin-bottom: 0pt; margin-top: 0pt;">
<div style="text-align: justify;">
<span style="background-color: transparent; color: black; font-family: Arial; font-size: 15px; font-style: normal; font-variant: normal; font-weight: normal; text-decoration: none; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre-wrap;">Back in late May, Spoon used the <a href="http://www.stereogum.com/1683618/spoons-r-i-p-coming-610/news/">cryptic abbreviation &#8220;R.I.P.&#8221;</a> to tease their new project. It was the first sign of new music from the Austin, Texas band since &#8220;Transference&#8221; in 2010.</span></div>
</div>
<div dir="ltr" style="line-height: 1.15; margin-bottom: 0pt; margin-top: 0pt;">
<div style="text-align: justify;">
<span style="background-color: transparent; color: black; font-family: Arial; font-size: 15px; font-style: normal; font-variant: normal; font-weight: normal; text-decoration: none; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre-wrap;"><span class="Apple-tab-span" style="white-space: pre;"> </span></span></div>
</div>
<div dir="ltr" style="line-height: 1.15; margin-bottom: 0pt; margin-top: 0pt;">
<div style="text-align: justify;">
<span style="background-color: transparent; color: black; font-family: Arial; font-size: 15px; font-style: normal; font-variant: normal; font-weight: normal; text-decoration: none; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre-wrap;">The internet wondered. Britt Daniel and Jim Eno formed the band in 1993. Were they calling it quits after 21 years? It was a morbid sign from a band that has become almost universally loved over its tenure.</span></div>
</div>
<div dir="ltr" style="line-height: 1.15; margin-bottom: 0pt; margin-top: 0pt;">
<div style="text-align: justify;">
<span style="background-color: transparent; color: black; font-family: Arial; font-size: 15px; font-style: normal; font-variant: normal; font-weight: normal; text-decoration: none; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre-wrap;"><span class="Apple-tab-span" style="white-space: pre;"> </span></span></div>
</div>
<div dir="ltr" style="line-height: 1.15; margin-bottom: 0pt; margin-top: 0pt;">
<div style="text-align: justify;">
<span style="background-color: transparent; color: black; font-family: Arial; font-size: 15px; font-style: normal; font-variant: normal; font-weight: normal; text-decoration: none; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre-wrap;">Thankfully, the letters stood for the title of the brazen lead single <a href="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=NOPfmfRVWmk">&#8220;Rent I Pay&#8221;</a> from their eighth LP, <a href="http://spoon.kungfustore.com/they-want-my-soul/twms-digital-download.html">&#8220;They Want My Soul.&#8221;</a> Eno&#8217;s drums come thumping in and seize control of the song. Daniel sings cynical lyrics. He asks for peace that he &#8220;ought to be owed,&#8221; but realizes that&#8217;s what he signed up for two decades ago when he decided to start a rock and roll band.</span></div>
</div>
<div dir="ltr" style="line-height: 1.15; margin-bottom: 0pt; margin-top: 0pt;">
<div style="text-align: justify;">
<br /></div>
<div style="text-align: justify;">
<span style="font-family: Arial; font-size: 15px; line-height: 1.15; white-space: pre-wrap;">Along with the commanding stop-and-start bassline and the fuzzy guitars, the opening track previews what the rest of the album will sound like. The rock-solid rhythm section trucks through the record while Daniel sneers.</span></div>
</div>
<div dir="ltr" style="line-height: 1.15; margin-bottom: 0pt; margin-top: 0pt;">
<div style="text-align: justify;">
<span style="background-color: transparent; color: black; font-family: Arial; font-size: 15px; font-style: normal; font-variant: normal; font-weight: normal; text-decoration: none; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre-wrap;"><span class="Apple-tab-span" style="white-space: pre;"> </span></span></div>
</div>
<div dir="ltr" style="line-height: 1.15; margin-bottom: 0pt; margin-top: 0pt;">
<div style="text-align: justify;">
<span style="background-color: transparent; color: black; font-family: Arial; font-size: 15px; font-style: normal; font-variant: normal; font-weight: normal; text-decoration: none; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre-wrap;">The spacey &#8220;Inside Out&#8221; reflects the mystical orb of the cover. Daniel&#8217;s lyrics contradict themselves as keyboard, guitar, and harp create a sound without edges</span><span style="font-family: Arial; font-size: 15px; line-height: 17.25px; white-space: pre-wrap;">&#8212;much like </span><span style="font-family: Arial; font-size: 15px; line-height: 1.15; white-space: pre-wrap;">the cover&#8217;s shapeless entity of light.</span></div>
</div>
<div dir="ltr" style="line-height: 1.15; margin-bottom: 0pt; margin-top: 0pt;">
<div style="text-align: justify;">
<span style="background-color: transparent; color: black; font-family: Arial; font-size: 15px; font-style: normal; font-variant: normal; font-weight: normal; text-decoration: none; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre-wrap;"><span class="Apple-tab-span" style="white-space: pre;"> </span></span></div>
</div>
<div dir="ltr" style="line-height: 1.15; margin-bottom: 0pt; margin-top: 0pt;">
<div style="text-align: justify;">
<span style="background-color: transparent; color: black; font-family: Arial; font-size: 15px; font-style: normal; font-variant: normal; font-weight: normal; text-decoration: none; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre-wrap;">&#8220;Rainy Taxi&#8221; shows the same quiet reserve Spoon&#8217;s fellow rockers Wilco deployed on an entire album. Like much of <a href="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=FqDWVErme40">&#8220;A Ghost Is Born,&#8221;</a> the song uses tremolo-guitar sparingly to make way for a light groove from the piano, bass, and drums. But the song explodes in its brilliant bridge as the bassline pushes and Daniel laments, &#8220;I may run with you / I&#8217;ve got nothin&#8217; else / I&#8217;ve got nowhere else.&#8221;</span></div>
</div>
<div dir="ltr" style="line-height: 1.15; margin-bottom: 0pt; margin-top: 0pt;">
<div style="text-align: justify;">
<span style="background-color: transparent; color: black; font-family: Arial; font-size: 15px; font-style: normal; font-variant: normal; font-weight: normal; text-decoration: none; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre-wrap;"><span class="Apple-tab-span" style="white-space: pre;"> </span></span></div>
</div>
<div dir="ltr" style="line-height: 1.15; margin-bottom: 0pt; margin-top: 0pt;">
<div style="text-align: justify;">
<span style="background-color: transparent; color: black; font-family: Arial; font-size: 15px; font-style: normal; font-variant: normal; font-weight: normal; text-decoration: none; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre-wrap;">Standout &#8220;Do You&#8221; is a classic Spoon tune. Daniel&#8217;s sing-song &#8220;doot-doos&#8221; bend around a jazzy major-7 chord as the rhythm section (once again) chugs along. Daniel&#8217;s lyrics address a lover he&#8217;s calling to action: &#8220;Do you wanna get understood? / Do you run when it&#8217;s just getting good? / Do you, do you do you?&#8221;</span></div>
</div>
<div dir="ltr" style="line-height: 1.15; margin-bottom: 0pt; margin-top: 0pt;">
<div style="text-align: justify;">
<span style="background-color: transparent; color: black; font-family: Arial; font-size: 15px; font-style: normal; font-variant: normal; font-weight: normal; text-decoration: none; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre-wrap;"><span class="Apple-tab-span" style="white-space: pre;"> </span></span></div>
</div>
<div dir="ltr" style="line-height: 1.15; margin-bottom: 0pt; margin-top: 0pt;">
<div style="text-align: justify;">
<span style="background-color: transparent; color: black; font-family: Arial; font-size: 15px; font-style: normal; font-variant: normal; font-weight: normal; text-decoration: none; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre-wrap;">&#8220;They Want My Soul&#8221; gets dark in its midsection. In the ominous "Knock Knock Knock," Daniel alludes to a recurring visitor in the chorus who might be dealing with addiction: </span><span style="line-height: 1.15;">&nbsp;</span></div>
<div style="text-align: justify;">
<span style="background-color: transparent; color: black; font-family: Arial; font-size: 15px; font-style: normal; font-variant: normal; font-weight: normal; text-decoration: none; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre-wrap;"><br /></span></div>
<div style="text-align: justify;">
<span style="background-color: transparent; color: black; font-family: Arial; font-size: 15px; font-style: normal; font-variant: normal; font-weight: normal; text-decoration: none; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre-wrap;">"Every day I hear &#8216;knock knock knock'</span><br />
<span style="font-family: Arial; font-size: 15px; line-height: 1.15; white-space: pre-wrap;">Then I see you, and you&#8217;re shaking</span><br />
<span style="font-family: Arial; font-size: 15px; line-height: 1.15; white-space: pre-wrap;">And you&#8217;re breaking, and you tell me I&#8217;m you&#8217;re only friend</span><br />
<span style="font-family: Arial; font-size: 15px; line-height: 1.15; white-space: pre-wrap;">Then it starts all over again."</span></div>
<div style="text-align: justify;">
<br /></div>
<div style="text-align: justify;">
<span style="background-color: transparent; color: black; font-family: Arial; font-size: 15px; font-style: normal; font-variant: normal; font-weight: normal; text-decoration: none; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre-wrap;">He&#8217;s fed up with someone or something for this entire album.</span></div>
</div>
<div dir="ltr" style="line-height: 1.15; margin-bottom: 0pt; margin-top: 0pt;">
<div style="text-align: justify;">
<span style="font-family: Arial; font-size: 15px; line-height: 1.15; text-indent: 36pt; white-space: pre-wrap;"><br /></span></div>
</div>
<div dir="ltr" style="line-height: 1.15; margin-bottom: 0pt; margin-top: 0pt;">
<div style="text-align: justify;">
<span style="font-family: Arial; font-size: 15px; line-height: 1.15; text-indent: 36pt; white-space: pre-wrap;">The ghoulish funk of the drums and tentative bassline call to mind <a href="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=2Okd3Oyii7E">&#8220;Animals&#8221;-era Pink Floyd</a> and make it one of the record&#8217;s most intriguing moments. Daniel even puts his voice through a Floyd-like filter towards the end.</span></div>
</div>
<div dir="ltr" style="line-height: 1.15; margin-bottom: 0pt; margin-top: 0pt;">
<div style="text-align: justify;">
<span style="background-color: transparent; color: black; font-family: Arial; font-size: 15px; font-style: normal; font-variant: normal; font-weight: normal; text-decoration: none; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre-wrap;"><span class="Apple-tab-span" style="white-space: pre;"> </span></span></div>
</div>
<div dir="ltr" style="line-height: 1.15; margin-bottom: 0pt; margin-top: 0pt;">
<div style="text-align: justify;">
<span style="background-color: transparent; color: black; font-family: Arial; font-size: 15px; font-style: normal; font-variant: normal; font-weight: normal; text-decoration: none; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre-wrap;">&#8220;Outlier&#8221; segues right in with a faster, urgent drum beat and an organ chord that echoes itself. Daniel is vaguely judgmental in the lyrics, especially in the &#8220;Garden State&#8221; line that many have noticed. The post-punk and new wave influences on the album are becoming more obvious. </span></div>
</div>
<div dir="ltr" style="line-height: 1.15; margin-bottom: 0pt; margin-top: 0pt;">
<div style="text-align: justify;">
<span style="background-color: transparent; color: black; font-family: Arial; font-size: 15px; font-style: normal; font-variant: normal; font-weight: normal; text-decoration: none; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre-wrap;"><span class="Apple-tab-span" style="white-space: pre;"> </span></span></div>
</div>
<div dir="ltr" style="line-height: 1.15; margin-bottom: 0pt; margin-top: 0pt;">
<span style="background-color: transparent; color: black; font-family: Arial; font-size: 15px; font-style: normal; font-variant: normal; font-weight: normal; text-decoration: none; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre-wrap;"></span><br />
<div style="text-align: justify;">
<span style="background-color: transparent; color: black; font-family: Arial; font-size: 15px; font-style: normal; font-variant: normal; font-weight: normal; text-decoration: none; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre-wrap;"><span style="line-height: 1.15;">Despite its foreboding title, the title track is one of the album&#8217;s poppiest songs. Daniel&#8217;s lyrics point out everyone who wants to get at him, but he seems to have accepted it and moved on. It&#8217;s Rob Pope&#8217;s leapfrogging bassline that completes the song as Daniel yells the chorus over the top of the band.</span></span></div>
</div>
<div dir="ltr" style="line-height: 1.15; margin-bottom: 0pt; margin-top: 0pt;">
<div style="text-align: justify;">
<span style="background-color: transparent; color: black; font-family: Arial; font-size: 15px; font-style: normal; font-variant: normal; font-weight: normal; text-decoration: none; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre-wrap;"><span class="Apple-tab-span" style="white-space: pre;"> </span></span></div>
</div>
<div dir="ltr" style="line-height: 1.15; margin-bottom: 0pt; margin-top: 0pt;">
<div style="text-align: justify;">
<a href="http://www.spoontheband.com/img/TWMS850.png" imageanchor="1" style="clear: right; float: right; margin-bottom: 1em; margin-left: 1em; text-align: justify;"><img border="0" src="http://www.spoontheband.com/img/TWMS850.png" height="200" width="200" /></a><span style="background-color: transparent; color: black; font-family: Arial; font-size: 15px; font-style: normal; font-variant: normal; font-weight: normal; text-decoration: none; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre-wrap;">&#8220;I Just Don&#8217;t Understand&#8221; follows in the footsteps of <a href="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=sSu6ixOlzmc">&#8220;Don&#8217;t You Evah&#8221;</a> by being better than the original, though it could be more fun by getting rowdier towards the end. &#8220;Let Me Be Mine&#8221; is another poppy song; a fun deep cut that fits well in the album&#8217;s second half.</span></div>
</div>
<div dir="ltr" style="line-height: 1.15; margin-bottom: 0pt; margin-top: 0pt;">
<div style="text-align: justify;">
<span style="background-color: transparent; color: black; font-family: Arial; font-size: 15px; font-style: normal; font-variant: normal; font-weight: normal; text-decoration: none; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre-wrap;"><span class="Apple-tab-span" style="white-space: pre;"> </span></span></div>
</div>
<div dir="ltr" style="line-height: 1.15; margin-bottom: 0pt; margin-top: 0pt;">
<div style="text-align: justify;">
<span style="background-color: transparent; color: black; font-family: Arial; font-size: 15px; font-style: normal; font-variant: normal; font-weight: normal; text-decoration: none; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre-wrap;">&#8220;New York Kiss&#8221; closes out the album with brilliance from the band. The eclectic instrumentation&#8212;with vintage keyboard sounds, vibraphone, and extra percussion&#8212;augments Daniel&#8217;s understated vocal. The bassline, calling to mind Radiohead&#8217;s <a href="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=NvTNWWL4CW4">&#8220;Airbag,&#8221;</a> dips in and out of the second verse before storming in with a purely post-punk rhythm in the chorus. The album even has a built-in encore as the band comes back in with another chorus after the stop-time bridge. It&#8217;s one of the band&#8217;s best songs ever and it couldn&#8217;t be much better as a closer.</span></div>
</div>
<div dir="ltr" style="line-height: 1.15; margin-bottom: 0pt; margin-top: 0pt;">
<div style="text-align: justify;">
<span style="font-family: Arial; font-size: 15px; line-height: 1.15; text-indent: 36pt; white-space: pre-wrap;"><br /></span></div>
</div>
<div dir="ltr" style="line-height: 1.15; margin-bottom: 0pt; margin-top: 0pt;">
<div style="text-align: justify;">
<span style="font-family: Arial; font-size: 15px; line-height: 1.15; text-indent: 36pt; white-space: pre-wrap;">With &#8220;They Want My Soul,&#8221; Spoon gives us the poppiness of <a href="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=7v0KCoPMTdU">&#8220;Ga Ga Ga Ga Ga,&#8221;</a> the Pixies-induced angst of <a href="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=t9bIq-LIb5I">&#8220;A Series of Sneaks,&#8221;</a> the non-conventional sound fans fell in love with on <a href="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=uA7uEW56rCE">&#8220;Kill The Moonlight,&#8221;</a> and a little extra polish and finesse we haven&#8217;t heard before from the band.</span></div>
</div>
<div dir="ltr" style="line-height: 1.15; margin-bottom: 0pt; margin-top: 0pt;">
<div style="text-align: justify;">
<span style="font-family: Arial; font-size: 15px; line-height: 1.15; text-indent: 36pt; white-space: pre-wrap;"><br /></span></div>
</div>
<div dir="ltr" style="line-height: 1.15; margin-bottom: 0pt; margin-top: 0pt;">
<div style="text-align: justify;">
<span style="font-family: Arial; font-size: 15px; line-height: 1.15; text-indent: 36pt; white-space: pre-wrap;">Daniel sings with the confidence and cockiness that only a 20-year veteran of the business could have. He might be jaded, but he&#8217;s not washed-up. Once again, he proves he's one of the best songwriters of the 21st century here.</span></div>
</div>
<div dir="ltr" style="line-height: 1.15; margin-bottom: 0pt; margin-top: 0pt;">
<div style="text-align: justify;">
<span style="font-family: Arial; font-size: 15px; line-height: 1.15; text-indent: 36pt; white-space: pre-wrap;"><br /></span></div>
</div>
<div dir="ltr" style="line-height: 1.15; margin-bottom: 0pt; margin-top: 0pt;">
<div style="text-align: justify;">
<span style="font-family: Arial; font-size: 15px; line-height: 1.15; text-indent: 36pt; white-space: pre-wrap;">It might not have as much personality or be as much fun as those three other Spoon albums, but &#8220;They Want My Soul&#8221; is solid as hell. Rock-production gods Joe Chiccarelli and Dave Fridmann make it sleek and cool with near-flawless production.</span></div>
</div>
<div dir="ltr" style="line-height: 1.15; margin-bottom: 0pt; margin-top: 0pt;">
<div style="text-align: justify;">
<span style="font-family: Arial; font-size: 15px; line-height: 1.15; text-indent: 36pt; white-space: pre-wrap;"><br /></span></div>
</div>
<div dir="ltr" style="line-height: 1.15; margin-bottom: 0pt; margin-top: 0pt;">
<div style="text-align: justify;">
<span style="font-family: Arial; font-size: 15px; line-height: 1.15; text-indent: 36pt; white-space: pre-wrap;">It&#8217;s not new wave enough to be post punk, but it&#8217;s not innocent or light enough to be indie rock. At the risk of coining a new dumb music category, I might call it post-indie? </span><span style="font-family: Arial; font-size: 15px; line-height: 1.15; text-indent: 36pt; white-space: pre-wrap;">In an era where more and more kids are <a href="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=HmrOHxS_cK0">throwing out their guitars and buying turntables</a>, bands like Spoon need to exist so that the legacy of the guitar will continue.</span></div>
</div>
<div dir="ltr" style="line-height: 1.15; margin-bottom: 0pt; margin-top: 0pt;">
<div style="text-align: justify;">
<span style="font-family: Arial; font-size: 15px; line-height: 1.15; text-indent: 36pt; white-space: pre-wrap;"><br /></span></div>
</div>
<div dir="ltr" style="line-height: 1.15; margin-bottom: 0pt; margin-top: 0pt;">
<div style="text-align: justify;">
<span style="font-family: Arial; font-size: 15px; line-height: 1.15; text-indent: 36pt; white-space: pre-wrap;"><a href="http://prettymuchamazing.com/reviews/spoon-they-want-my-soul">Others have pointed out</a> that &#8220;They Want My Soul&#8221; seems like it will be the last Spoon album. Listening to Daniel&#8217;s lyrics and looking at the title, you get the sense that his return to music is a somewhat reluctant one.</span></div>
</div>
<div dir="ltr" style="line-height: 1.15; margin-bottom: 0pt; margin-top: 0pt;">
<div style="text-align: justify;">
<span style="font-family: Arial; font-size: 15px; line-height: 1.15; text-indent: 36pt; white-space: pre-wrap;"><br /></span></div>
</div>
<div dir="ltr" style="line-height: 1.15; margin-bottom: 0pt; margin-top: 0pt;">
<div style="text-align: justify;">
<span style="font-family: Arial; font-size: 15px; line-height: 1.15; text-indent: 36pt; white-space: pre-wrap;">But what a swan song this would be. Spoon have heard everything about how rock music is dying, and they came back to show everyone that, dammit, they can still make a fucking good rock album.</span></div>
</div>
<div dir="ltr" style="line-height: 1.15; margin-bottom: 0pt; margin-top: 0pt;">
<div style="text-align: justify;">
<span style="font-family: Arial; font-size: 15px; line-height: 1.15; text-indent: 36pt; white-space: pre-wrap;"><br /></span></div>
</div>
<div dir="ltr" style="line-height: 1.15; margin-bottom: 0pt; margin-top: 0pt;">
<div style="text-align: justify;">
<span style="font-family: Arial; font-size: 15px; line-height: 1.15; text-indent: 36pt; white-space: pre-wrap;">Far from what that teaser seemed to suggest, Spoon is nowhere near dead.</span></div>
<div style="text-align: justify;">
<span style="font-family: Arial; font-size: 15px; line-height: 1.15; text-indent: 36pt; white-space: pre-wrap;"><br /></span></div>

<h2 class='date-header'><span>Friday, December 27, 2013</span></h2>

          <div class="date-posts">
        
<div class='post-outer'>
<div class='post hentry uncustomized-post-template' itemprop='blogPost' itemscope='itemscope' itemtype='http://schema.org/BlogPosting'>
<meta content='http://assets-s3.rollingstone.com/assets/images/album_review/deafheaven-sunbather-1376335512.jpg' itemprop='image_url'/>
<meta content='746853886426466381' itemprop='blogId'/>

<h3 class='post-title entry-title' itemprop='name'>
<a href='http://jakobclapensteam.blogspot.com/2013/12/2013-music-roundup.html'>2013 Music Roundup</a>
</h3>
<div class='post-header'>
<div class='post-header-line-1'></div>
</div>
<div class='post-body entry-content' id='post-body-6987783330580202772' itemprop='description articleBody'>
<span style="font-family: Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif;">Hey folks, here's my annual list of my favorite music things of the year. You'll find my top ten favorite albums of the year, along with a slew of honorable mentions, as well as a few of my favorite songs. Enjoy!</span><br />
<span style="font-family: Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif;"><br /></span>
<b><span style="font-family: Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif;">Albums</span></b><br />
<b><span style="font-family: Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif;"><br /></span></b>
<span style="font-family: Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif;">Honorable mentions (in alphabetical order by artist):</span><br />
<span style="font-family: Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif;"><br /></span>
<span style="font-family: Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif;"><span style="background-color: white; line-height: 18px; white-space: pre-wrap;"><i>LongLiveA$AP</i> - A$AP Rocky</span></span><br />
<span style="font-family: Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif;"><i style="line-height: 18px; white-space: pre-wrap;">Old </i><span style="background-color: white; line-height: 18px; white-space: pre-wrap;">- Danny Brown</span></span><br />
<span id="docs-internal-guid-5edc944c-3689-dbb2-936c-2034bebd24a3"><span style="font-family: Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif;"><span style="background-color: white; font-style: italic; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre-wrap;">Nothing Was The Same</span><span style="background-color: white; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre-wrap;"> - Drake</span></span></span><br />
<span style="font-family: Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif;"><i style="line-height: 18px; white-space: pre-wrap;">Days Are Gone</i><span style="background-color: white; line-height: 18px; white-space: pre-wrap;"> - Haim</span></span><br />
<span style="font-family: Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif;"><span style="background-color: white; line-height: 18px; white-space: pre-wrap;"><i>Excavation</i> - The Haxan Cloak</span></span><br />
<span style="font-family: Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif;"><i style="line-height: 18px; white-space: pre-wrap;">Immunity</i><span style="background-color: white; line-height: 18px; white-space: pre-wrap;"> - Jon Hopkins</span></span><br />
<span style="font-family: Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif;"><i style="background-color: white; white-space: pre-wrap;">Matangi</i><span style="background-color: white; white-space: pre-wrap;"> - M.I.A.</span></span><br />
<span style="font-family: Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif;"><span style="background-color: white; font-style: italic; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre-wrap;">mbv</span><span style="background-color: white; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre-wrap;"> - My Bloody Valentine</span></span><br />
<span style="font-family: Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif;"><i style="background-color: white; white-space: pre-wrap;">My Name Is My Name</i><span style="background-color: white; white-space: pre-wrap;"> - Pusha T</span></span><br />
<span><span style="font-family: Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif;"><span style="background-color: white; font-style: italic; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre-wrap;">Major Arcana</span><span style="background-color: white; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre-wrap;"> - Speedy Ortiz</span></span></span><br />
<span style="font-family: Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif;"><i style="background-color: white; white-space: pre-wrap;">The 20/20 Experience </i><span style="background-color: white; white-space: pre-wrap;">- Justin Timberlake</span></span><br />
<span style="background-color: white; white-space: pre-wrap;"><span style="font-family: Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif;"><i>Anything In Return</i> - Toro Y Moi</span></span><br />
<span style="font-family: Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif;"><span><span style="background-color: white; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre-wrap;"><i>Is Survived By</i> - Touch</span></span><span style="background-color: white; line-height: 18px; white-space: pre-wrap;">é Amor</span><span style="background-color: white; line-height: 18px; white-space: pre-wrap;">é</span></span><br />
<span style="font-family: Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif;"><i style="line-height: 18px; white-space: pre-wrap;">1017 Thug</i><span style="background-color: white; line-height: 18px; white-space: pre-wrap;"> - Young Thug</span></span><br />
<span style="background-color: white; line-height: 18px; white-space: pre-wrap;"><span style="font-family: Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif;"><br /></span></span>
<div dir="ltr" style="line-height: 1.15; margin-bottom: 0pt; margin-top: 0pt;">
<span style="font-family: Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif;"><span style="background-color: transparent; color: black; font-style: normal; font-variant: normal; font-weight: normal; text-decoration: none; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre-wrap;">1) </span><span style="background-color: transparent; color: black; font-style: italic; font-variant: normal; font-weight: normal; text-decoration: none; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre-wrap;">Sunbather</span><span style="background-color: transparent; color: black; font-style: normal; font-variant: normal; font-weight: normal; text-decoration: none; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre-wrap;"> - Deafheaven</span></span></div>
<div dir="ltr" style="line-height: 1.15; margin-bottom: 0pt; margin-top: 0pt;">
<span style="font-family: Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif;"><span style="background-color: transparent; color: black; font-style: normal; font-variant: normal; font-weight: normal; text-decoration: none; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre-wrap;"><br /></span></span></div>
<div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
<a href="http://assets-s3.rollingstone.com/assets/images/album_review/deafheaven-sunbather-1376335512.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="clear: left; float: left; margin-bottom: 1em; margin-right: 1em;"><img border="0" height="200" src="http://assets-s3.rollingstone.com/assets/images/album_review/deafheaven-sunbather-1376335512.jpg" width="200" /></a></div>
<div dir="ltr" style="line-height: 1.15; margin-bottom: 0pt; margin-top: 0pt;">
<span style="font-family: Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif; line-height: 1.15; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre-wrap;">The music on both of these top two albums is excellent and thoroughly captivating. But </span><span style="font-family: Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif; font-style: italic; line-height: 1.15; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre-wrap;">Sunbather</span><span style="font-family: Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif; line-height: 1.15; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre-wrap;"> deserves this year&#8217;s crown because it will be much more important to the genre of metal than </span><span style="font-family: Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif; font-style: italic; line-height: 1.15; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre-wrap;">Yeezus </span><span style="font-family: Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif; line-height: 1.15; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre-wrap;">will be to the genre of hip-hop. Metal has long been associated with darkness and ugliness, but </span><span style="font-family: Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif; font-style: italic; line-height: 1.15; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre-wrap;">Sunbather</span><span style="font-family: Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif; line-height: 1.15; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre-wrap;"> is the exact opposite. Its bright pink cover is an accurate representation of the bright and shining music within. The booming, dominant guitars cascade through the dramatic, epic chord progressions. The blastbeats and frantic basslines in the rhythm section drive the point home emphatically on every song. And George Clarke&#8217;s screamed vocals are an acquired taste, but they&#8217;re never overbearing and they contribute necessary emotion to the songs. </span><span style="background-color: white; font-family: Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif; font-style: italic; line-height: 1.15; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre-wrap;">Sunbather</span><span style="font-family: Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif; line-height: 1.15; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre-wrap;"> is a record that immediately blossoms to great heights and then pushes itself further and further. It&#8217;s a gorgeous, monumental, brilliant composition that&#8217;s arrived at a time when rock and roll music has hit a brick wall. </span><span style="font-family: Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif; font-style: italic; line-height: 1.15; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre-wrap;">Sunbather </span><span style="font-family: Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif; line-height: 1.15; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre-wrap;">bridges the gap between rock fan and metal fan and raises the stakes for both genres. The music world needed a record like this, and it&#8217;s a miracle that the music on it turned out to be so wonderful.</span></div>
<b style="font-weight: normal;"><span style="font-family: Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif;"><br /><span style="background-color: transparent; color: black; font-style: normal; font-variant: normal; font-weight: normal; text-decoration: none; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre-wrap;"></span></span></b>
<div dir="ltr" style="line-height: 1.15; margin-bottom: 0pt; margin-top: 0pt;">
<span style="font-family: Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif;"><span style="background-color: transparent; color: black; font-style: normal; font-variant: normal; font-weight: normal; text-decoration: none; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre-wrap;">2) </span><span style="background-color: transparent; color: black; font-style: italic; font-variant: normal; font-weight: normal; text-decoration: none; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre-wrap;">Yeezus</span><span style="background-color: transparent; color: black; font-style: normal; font-variant: normal; font-weight: normal; text-decoration: none; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre-wrap;"> - Kanye West</span></span></div>
<div dir="ltr" style="line-height: 1.15; margin-bottom: 0pt; margin-top: 0pt;">
<span style="font-family: Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif;"><span style="background-color: transparent; color: black; font-style: normal; font-variant: normal; font-weight: normal; text-decoration: none; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre-wrap;"><br /></span></span></div>
<div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">

<span style="font-family: Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif; line-height: 1.15; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre-wrap;">Probably everything Kanye West does from here on out is going to be wildly divisive, and in that respect this album is a perfect representation of The Era of the Angry Yeezy. Within the first five seconds of &#8220;On Sight,&#8221; people decided they hated everything about this album. The mangy, visceral beats, the seemingly inappropriate sampling, the disgusting sexual lyrics. But those people also failed to realize that the music on </span><span style="font-family: Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif; font-style: italic; line-height: 1.15; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre-wrap;">Yeezus</span><span style="font-family: Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif; line-height: 1.15; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre-wrap;"> is undeniably brilliant; the work of a modern-day musical genius. Kanye deliberately throws everyone off course with this album as he conjures convoluted songs from a crazy conglomeration of genres. He&#8217;s not afraid to take risks, but somehow he pulls them all off. When the Jamaican dancehall vocals at the end of &#8220;Send It Up&#8221; segue abruptly into the throwback soul of &#8220;Bound 2,&#8221; it feels effortless. It made me think &#8220;how can he come up with stuff like this?&#8221; There&#8217;s very few musical minds more mesmerizing than Kanye&#8217;s at the moment, and with </span><span style="font-family: Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif; font-style: italic; line-height: 1.15; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre-wrap;">Yeezus</span><span style="font-family: Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif; line-height: 1.15; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre-wrap;"> he&#8217;s once again proved his astounding brilliance.</span><br />

Yoncé already seriously upped her game with 2011&#8217;s </span><span style="background-color: white; color: black; font-style: italic; font-variant: normal; font-weight: normal; text-decoration: none; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre-wrap;">4</span><span style="background-color: white; color: black; font-style: normal; font-variant: normal; font-weight: normal; text-decoration: none; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre-wrap;">, where she proved that pop can be experimental, too. With so many great music makers working on the album, </span><span style="background-color: transparent; color: black; font-style: italic; font-variant: normal; font-weight: normal; text-decoration: none; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre-wrap;">BEYONC</span><span style="background-color: white; color: black; font-style: italic; font-variant: normal; font-weight: normal; text-decoration: none; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre-wrap;">É </span><span style="background-color: white; color: black; font-style: normal; font-variant: normal; font-weight: normal; text-decoration: none; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre-wrap;">is one of the most diverse albums of any genre this year. It&#8217;s a fast-paced and brief whirlwind of lavish lyrics, fierce flows, and brazen beats. The songs go by so fast that on your tenth listen you&#8217;ll still discover new things. Mike and El have been around too long to let slip something on a record that they don&#8217;t have complete confidence in. There&#8217;s never an insincere moment here. </span><span style="background-color: white; color: black; font-style: italic; font-variant: normal; font-weight: normal; text-decoration: none; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre-wrap;">Run The Jewels</span><span style="background-color: white; color: black; font-style: normal; font-variant: normal; font-weight: normal; text-decoration: none; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre-wrap;"> will blow you away.</span></span></div>
<b style="font-weight: normal;"><span style="font-family: Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif;"><br /><span style="background-color: white; color: black; font-style: normal; font-variant: normal; font-weight: normal; text-decoration: none; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre-wrap;"></span></span></b>
<div dir="ltr" style="line-height: 1.15; margin-bottom: 0pt; margin-top: 0pt;">
<span style="font-family: Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif;"><span style="background-color: white; color: black; font-style: normal; font-variant: normal; font-weight: normal; text-decoration: none; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre-wrap;"><br /></span></span></div>
<div dir="ltr" style="line-height: 1.15; margin-bottom: 0pt; margin-top: 0pt;">
<span style="font-family: Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif;"><span style="background-color: white; color: black; font-style: normal; font-variant: normal; font-weight: normal; text-decoration: none; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre-wrap;"><br /></span></span></div>
<div dir="ltr" style="line-height: 1.15; margin-bottom: 0pt; margin-top: 0pt;">
<span style="font-family: Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif;"><span style="background-color: white; color: black; font-style: normal; font-variant: normal; font-weight: normal; text-decoration: none; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre-wrap;"><br /></span></span></div>
<div dir="ltr" style="line-height: 1.15; margin-bottom: 0pt; margin-top: 0pt;">
<span style="font-family: Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif;"><span style="background-color: white; color: black; font-style: normal; font-variant: normal; font-weight: normal; text-decoration: none; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre-wrap;"><br /></span></span></div>
<div dir="ltr" style="line-height: 1.15; margin-bottom: 0pt; margin-top: 0pt;">
<span style="font-family: Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif;"><span style="background-color: white; color: black; font-style: normal; font-variant: normal; font-weight: normal; text-decoration: none; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre-wrap;"><br /></span></span></div>
<div dir="ltr" style="line-height: 1.15; margin-bottom: 0pt; margin-top: 0pt;">
<span style="font-family: Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif;"><span style="background-color: white; color: black; font-style: normal; font-variant: normal; font-weight: normal; text-decoration: none; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre-wrap;">9) </span><span style="background-color: white; color: black; font-style: italic; font-variant: normal; font-weight: normal; text-decoration: none; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre-wrap;">Woman</span><span style="background-color: white; color: black; font-style: normal; font-variant: normal; font-weight: normal; text-decoration: none; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre-wrap;"> - Rhye</span></span></div>
<b style="font-weight: normal;"><span style="font-family: Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif;"><br /><span style="background-color: white; color: black; font-style: normal; font-variant: normal; font-weight: normal; text-decoration: none; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre-wrap;"></span></span></b>
<div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
<a href="http://media.npr.org/assets/img/2013/03/06/rhye_woman_5x5_phys-29f00bf_custom-01679020529c267c46cfe4b4f6fb3ab08b37dc7b-s6-c30.jpeg" imageanchor="1" style="clear: left; float: left; margin-bottom: 1em; margin-right: 1em;"><img border="0" height="200" src="http://media.npr.org/assets/img/2013/03/06/rhye_woman_5x5_phys-29f00bf_custom-01679020529c267c46cfe4b4f6fb3ab08b37dc7b-s6-c30.jpeg" width="200" /></a></div>
<div dir="ltr" style="line-height: 1.15; margin-bottom: 0pt; margin-top: 0pt;">
<span style="font-family: Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif;"><span style="background-color: white; color: black; font-style: normal; font-variant: normal; font-weight: normal; text-decoration: none; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre-wrap;">It&#8217;s hard to make a sexy record that doesn&#8217;t sound pornographic. Rhye don&#8217;t keep anything to themselves on </span><span style="background-color: white; color: black; font-style: italic; font-variant: normal; font-weight: normal; text-decoration: none; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre-wrap;">Woman</span><span style="background-color: white; color: black; font-style: normal; font-variant: normal; font-weight: normal; text-decoration: none; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre-wrap;">. &#8220;I&#8217;m a fool for that shake in your thighs&#8221; Mike Milosh croons on the album&#8217;s first line (just think about it for a second&#8230;). But </span><span style="background-color: white; color: black; font-style: italic; font-variant: normal; font-weight: normal; text-decoration: none; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre-wrap;">Woman</span><span style="background-color: white; color: black; font-style: normal; font-variant: normal; font-weight: normal; text-decoration: none; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre-wrap;"> avoids sleaziness with its sleek, minimal production. Milosh&#8217;s androgynous voice keeps the album cryptic, as well. Explicit lyrics aside, the songs are cool, groovy, and unconventional. Most of the songs feature a string quartet, which gives a welcome retro air to the otherwise futuristic music. Rhye&#8217;s music evokes images of dimly lit, hip nightclubs. They even perform all their shows in the shadows, allowing only candlelight into venues. Listening to this record will make you feel hip, like you&#8217;re in on something that most people don&#8217;t understand.</span></span></div>
<b style="font-weight: normal;"><span style="font-family: Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif;"><br /><span style="background-color: white; color: black; font-style: normal; font-variant: normal; font-weight: normal; text-decoration: none; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre-wrap;"></span></span></b>
<div dir="ltr" style="line-height: 1.15; margin-bottom: 0pt; margin-top: 0pt;">
<span style="font-family: Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif;"><span style="background-color: white; color: black; font-style: normal; font-variant: normal; font-weight: normal; text-decoration: none; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre-wrap;">10) </span><span style="background-color: white; color: black; font-style: italic; font-variant: normal; font-weight: normal; text-decoration: none; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre-wrap;">The Electric Lady</span><span style="background-color: white; color: black; font-style: normal; font-variant: normal; font-weight: normal; text-decoration: none; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre-wrap;"> - Janelle Monaé</span></span></div>
<div dir="ltr" style="line-height: 1.15; margin-bottom: 0pt; margin-top: 0pt;">
<span style="font-family: Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif;"><span style="background-color: white; color: black; font-style: normal; font-variant: normal; font-weight: normal; text-decoration: none; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre-wrap;"><br /></span></span></div>
<div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
<a href="http://prettymuchamazing.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/08/JANELLE-MONAE-ELECTRIC-LADY-e1376434058729.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="clear: left; float: left; margin-bottom: 1em; margin-right: 1em;"><img border="0" height="200" src="http://prettymuchamazing.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/08/JANELLE-MONAE-ELECTRIC-LADY-e1376434058729.jpg" width="200" /></a></div>
<span style="font-family: Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif;"><span style="background-color: white; vertical-align: baseline; white-space: pre-wrap;">Janelle Monaé&#8217;s sophomore effort is just as diverse, theatrical, and captivating as her debut. The two halves of the record are very different, as they follow the high concept of fugitive android Cindi Mayweather. But even with Monaé&#8217;s dedication to the concept, the music is great. She employs a slew of collaborators, including the alchemist Miguel on &#8220;Primetime,&#8221; who turns everything he works on into gold. Monaé has one of the best pure singing voices in all of pop today. We&#8217;re lucky that she does, or else the ambitious feats she attempts wouldn&#8217;t succeed the way they have for her first two albums. She will continue exploring on her future releases.</span></span><br />
<div dir="ltr" style="line-height: 1.15; margin-bottom: 0pt; margin-top: 0pt;">
<br /></div>

<div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
<a href="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/-GzSzLc3TXKA/Uml2MXroENI/AAAAAAAAAIE/gqZ5BemRj-A/s1600/built-to-spill-dec-2012-640.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;"><img border="0" height="250" src="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/-GzSzLc3TXKA/Uml2MXroENI/AAAAAAAAAIE/gqZ5BemRj-A/s400/built-to-spill-dec-2012-640.jpg" width="400" />&nbsp;</a></div>
<div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
<br /></div>
<div class="MsoNormal" style="margin-bottom: .0001pt; margin-bottom: 0in;">
<span style="font-family: inherit;"><span style="font-size: 12pt; line-height: 115%;">This
past Tuesday, I had the pleasure of seeing Built to Spill in concert.<span style="mso-spacerun: yes;">&nbsp; </span>The Idaho rockers had a hand in the creation
of alternative rock back in the 1990s along with Pavement, Nirvana, and Sonic
Youth.</span></span></div>
<div class="MsoNormal" style="margin-bottom: .0001pt; margin-bottom: 0in;">
<br /></div>
<div class="MsoNormal" style="margin-bottom: .0001pt; margin-bottom: 0in;">
<span style="font-family: inherit;"><span style="font-size: 12pt; line-height: 115%;">Frontman
Doug Martsch experimented with layering noisy guitar chords and alternative
tunings to create 1990s classics like &#8220;Perfect From Now On&#8221; and &#8220;Keep It Like A
Secret.&#8221;<span style="mso-spacerun: yes;">&nbsp; </span>Martsch is 44 now, but he and
the rest of the band proved they still know how to rock after all these years.</span></span></div>
<div class="MsoNormal" style="margin-bottom: .0001pt; margin-bottom: 0in;">
<br /></div>
<div class="MsoNormal" style="margin-bottom: .0001pt; margin-bottom: 0in;">
<span style="font-family: inherit;"><span style="font-size: 12pt; line-height: 115%;">They
opened with the beautiful &#8220;Velvet Waltz,&#8221; a song I didn&#8217;t even expect them to
play, much less open with.<span style="mso-spacerun: yes;">&nbsp; </span>That song
showcases the sound layering that made Built to Spill so famous in a sublime
way.<span style="mso-spacerun: yes;">&nbsp; </span>They continued with the punky &#8220;You
Were Right,&#8221; an ode of sorts to the classic rock Martsch grew up on.</span></span></div>
<div class="MsoNormal" style="margin-bottom: .0001pt; margin-bottom: 0in;">
<br /></div>
<div class="MsoNormal" style="margin-bottom: .0001pt; margin-bottom: 0in;">
<span style="font-family: inherit;"><span style="font-size: 12pt; line-height: 115%;">The
band did an excellent job of mixing their early pop cuts like &#8220;Reasons&#8221; and &#8220;Joyride&#8221;
with longer, more experimental songs like &#8220;Untrustable.&#8221;<span style="mso-spacerun: yes;">&nbsp; </span>Martsch sounded as polished as ever on
guitar, and his distinctive vocal whine hasn&#8217;t lost much intensity as he has
aged.<span style="mso-spacerun: yes;">&nbsp; </span>It was a great rock show.</span></span></div>
<div class="MsoNormal" style="margin-bottom: .0001pt; margin-bottom: 0in;">
<br /></div>
<div class="MsoNormal" style="margin-bottom: .0001pt; margin-bottom: 0in;">
<span style="font-family: inherit;"><span style="font-size: 12pt; line-height: 115%;">It
got me to thinking about the present state of rock and roll.<span style="mso-spacerun: yes;">&nbsp; </span>The seminal genre that kickstarted popular
music as we know it is struggling right now.</span></span></div>
<div class="MsoNormal" style="margin-bottom: .0001pt; margin-bottom: 0in;">
<br /></div>
<div class="MsoNormal" style="margin-bottom: .0001pt; margin-bottom: 0in;">
<span style="font-family: inherit;"><span style="font-size: 12pt; line-height: 115%;">Before
I dive in to this, I need to define what I call &#8220;rock and roll.&#8221;<span style="mso-spacerun: yes;">&nbsp; </span>It&#8217;s literally impossible, as is categorizing
any kind of music, but I&#8217;ve come up with a very loose, very broad definition.</span></span></div>
<div class="MsoNormal" style="margin-bottom: .0001pt; margin-bottom: 0in;">
<br /></div>
<div class="MsoNormal" style="margin-bottom: .0001pt; margin-bottom: 0in;">
<span style="font-family: inherit;"><span style="font-size: 12pt; line-height: 115%;">Rock
and roll started with the guitar, bass and drums.<span style="mso-spacerun: yes;">&nbsp; </span>That&#8217;s still the core group of instruments
that, along with vocals, we can call rock and roll.<span style="mso-spacerun: yes;">&nbsp; </span>Other instruments, like piano, strings and
horns, are often featured.<span style="mso-spacerun: yes;">&nbsp; </span>But for me,
rock and roll should be based on those three core instruments, especially the
guitar.</span></span></div>
<div class="MsoNormal" style="margin-bottom: .0001pt; margin-bottom: 0in;">
<br /></div>
<div class="MsoNormal" style="margin-bottom: .0001pt; margin-bottom: 0in;">
<a href="http://1.bp.blogspot.com/-mOK6xsIDIzM/Uml4qYxbAWI/AAAAAAAAAIY/cm_MNinxt9Q/s1600/IMG_1644.JPG" imageanchor="1" style="clear: right; float: right; margin-bottom: 1em; margin-left: 1em;"><img border="0" height="320" src="http://1.bp.blogspot.com/-mOK6xsIDIzM/Uml4qYxbAWI/AAAAAAAAAIY/cm_MNinxt9Q/s320/IMG_1644.JPG" width="241" /></a><span style="font-family: inherit;"><span style="font-size: 12pt; line-height: 115%;">With
the exception of two brief uses of a cowbell, Built to Spill only used those
three instruments on Tuesday.<span style="mso-spacerun: yes;">&nbsp; </span>But let&#8217;s
remember that they mostly played songs that are 15-plus years old.<span style="mso-spacerun: yes;">&nbsp; </span>I love the band, and I loved the show, but
they haven&#8217;t really demonstrated any innovation since 1999&#8217;s masterful &#8220;Perfect
From Now On.&#8221;</span></span></div>
<div class="MsoNormal" style="margin-bottom: .0001pt; margin-bottom: 0in;">
<br /></div>
<div class="MsoNormal" style="margin-bottom: .0001pt; margin-bottom: 0in;">
<span style="font-family: inherit;"><span style="font-size: 12pt; line-height: 115%;">It
seems to me that Martsch and the band know this.<span style="mso-spacerun: yes;">&nbsp; </span>Out of the 15 songs they played (excluding two
covers), only four were from their albums after 1999.<span style="mso-spacerun: yes;">&nbsp; </span>They played two each from 2006&#8217;s &#8220;You In
Reverse&#8221; and 2009&#8217;s &#8220;There Is No Enemy.&#8221;<span style="mso-spacerun: yes;">&nbsp;
</span>These two albums, while they have their highlights, are generally
considered to be two of the weakest in Built to Spill&#8217;s seven-album catalogue.</span></span></div>
<div class="MsoNormal" style="margin-bottom: .0001pt; margin-bottom: 0in;">
<br /></div>
<div class="MsoNormal" style="margin-bottom: .0001pt; margin-bottom: 0in;">
<span style="font-family: inherit;"><span style="font-size: 12pt; line-height: 115%;">You&#8217;d
think a band would play mostly songs from their latest album on a tour.<span style="mso-spacerun: yes;">&nbsp; </span>I saw Bruce Springsteen last year after he
released &#8220;Wrecking Ball,&#8221; and even though the Boss has an incredible body of
work, he played a lot of songs from his newest album.</span></span></div>
<div class="MsoNormal" style="margin-bottom: .0001pt; margin-bottom: 0in;">
<br /></div>
<div class="MsoNormal" style="margin-bottom: .0001pt; margin-bottom: 0in;">
<span style="font-family: inherit;"><span style="font-size: 12pt; line-height: 115%;">But
not Built to Spill.<span style="mso-spacerun: yes;">&nbsp; </span>Martsch has always
seemed like an insecure rock star kind of guy, but it was clear from Tuesday&#8217;s
setlist that he doesn&#8217;t have much confidence in his latest two releases.<span style="mso-spacerun: yes;">&nbsp; </span>Could it be he thinks his band&#8217;s conventional
rock sound has run its course?<span style="mso-spacerun: yes;">&nbsp; </span>After
all, what all can you possibly do with just guitar, bass, and drums?</span></span></div>
<div class="MsoNormal" style="margin-bottom: .0001pt; margin-bottom: 0in;">
<br /></div>
<div class="MsoNormal" style="margin-bottom: .0001pt; margin-bottom: 0in;">
<span style="font-family: inherit;"><span style="font-size: 12pt; line-height: 115%;">The
two openers also only used the three core instruments.<span style="mso-spacerun: yes;">&nbsp; </span>The first, Genders, had some nice songs that
were well-written.<span style="mso-spacerun: yes;">&nbsp; </span>But they heavily
borrowed sounds from other bands.<span style="mso-spacerun: yes;">&nbsp; </span>I
thought their rhythm section sounded like Yuck, their guitars sounded like Real
Estate&#8217;s and their singer sounded like Victoria Legrand from Beach House.</span></span></div>
<div class="MsoNormal" style="margin-bottom: .0001pt; margin-bottom: 0in;">
<br /></div>
<div class="MsoNormal" style="margin-bottom: .0001pt; margin-bottom: 0in;">
<span style="font-family: inherit;"><span style="font-size: 12pt; line-height: 115%;">The
second opener, Slam Dunk, was a lot of fun.<span style="mso-spacerun: yes;">&nbsp;
</span>Again, they used only the core group.<span style="mso-spacerun: yes;">&nbsp;
</span>They had a goofy onstage persona and yelled a lot, which was really entertaining.<span style="mso-spacerun: yes;">&nbsp; </span>But none of their songs were all that
interesting.<span style="mso-spacerun: yes;">&nbsp; </span>I got bored in the middle
and looked at my phone for a while.</span></span></div>
<div class="MsoNormal" style="margin-bottom: .0001pt; margin-bottom: 0in;">
<br /></div>
<div class="MsoNormal" style="margin-bottom: .0001pt; margin-bottom: 0in;">
<span style="font-family: inherit;"><span style="font-size: 12pt; line-height: 115%;">The
truth is rock and roll has hit a brick wall.<span style="mso-spacerun: yes;">&nbsp;
</span>In indie music, its presence is catastrophically limited.<span style="mso-spacerun: yes;">&nbsp; </span>There are some groups you could consider rock
and roll.<span style="mso-spacerun: yes;">&nbsp; </span>Arcade Fire have the driving
rhythms and guitar riffs that characterize the music, but they are also heavily
orchestral.<span style="mso-spacerun: yes;">&nbsp; </span>Their new,
electronic-influenced music would have Chuck Berry rolling in his grave if I
called it rock and roll.</span></span></div>
<div class="MsoNormal" style="margin-bottom: .0001pt; margin-bottom: 0in;">
<br /></div>
<div class="MsoNormal" style="margin-bottom: .0001pt; margin-bottom: 0in;">
<span style="font-family: inherit;"><span style="font-size: 12pt; line-height: 115%;">People
call bands that have banjos and saxophones rather than electric guitars &#8220;indie
rock,&#8221; but that&#8217;s not the type of music the genre was founded on.<span style="mso-spacerun: yes;">&nbsp; </span>Bands like the Black Keys and the White
Stripes are definitely rock, but their music is largely a throwback to blues
sounds.</span></span></div>
<div class="MsoNormal" style="margin-bottom: .0001pt; margin-bottom: 0in;">
<br /></div>
<div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
<a href="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/-4sB6HiwzAAc/Uml2fG1mN9I/AAAAAAAAAIM/wqYjY9wgFnM/s1600/speedy+ortiz.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="clear: left; float: left; margin-bottom: 1em; margin-right: 1em;"><img border="0" height="200" src="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/-4sB6HiwzAAc/Uml2fG1mN9I/AAAAAAAAAIM/wqYjY9wgFnM/s200/speedy+ortiz.jpg" width="200" /></a></div>
<div class="MsoNormal" style="margin-bottom: .0001pt; margin-bottom: 0in;">
<span style="font-family: inherit;"><span style="font-size: 12pt; line-height: 115%;">Speedy
Ortiz released a wonderful album this year.<span style="mso-spacerun: yes;">&nbsp;
</span>Its sound is harder to pinpoint, but it again borrows from the alt
rockers of the 1990s.<span style="mso-spacerun: yes;">&nbsp; </span>Torres released a
raw, emotionally powerful rock album at the very beginning of this year, but
she&#8217;s from Nashville and borrowed songwriting techniques from the country
singer-songwriters of that city.</span></span></div>
<div class="MsoNormal" style="margin-bottom: .0001pt; margin-bottom: 0in;">
<br /></div>
<div class="MsoNormal" style="margin-bottom: .0001pt; margin-bottom: 0in;">
<span style="font-family: inherit;"><span style="font-size: 12pt; line-height: 115%;">Deerhunter
are pretty clearly rock and roll, and their frontman Bradford Cox has a fresh
style of songwriting.<span style="mso-spacerun: yes;">&nbsp; </span>But they borrow
heavily from the garage-rock sound of the Strokes.<span style="mso-spacerun: yes;">&nbsp; </span>Emo/screamo revival bands like Touche Amore and
The World Is A Beautiful place are trying to bridge the gap between mainstream
and alternative, but it&#8217;s slow-going and has little potential for success.</span></span></div>
<div class="MsoNormal" style="margin-bottom: .0001pt; margin-bottom: 0in;">
<span style="font-family: inherit;"><span style="font-size: 12pt; line-height: 115%;">The
freshest rock release in recent memory came in the form of Deafheaven&#8217;s &#8220;Sunbather.&#8221;<span style="mso-spacerun: yes;">&nbsp; </span>Their mesmerizing mix of shoegaze guitars,
black metal drums and screaming vocals make a great concoction that is excellently
executed and produced.<span style="mso-spacerun: yes;">&nbsp; </span>It&#8217;s about the
only rock release I can think of from the last five years that has lasting
potential.</span></span></div>
<div class="MsoNormal" style="margin-bottom: .0001pt; margin-bottom: 0in;">
<br /></div>
<div class="MsoNormal" style="margin-bottom: .0001pt; margin-bottom: 0in;">
<span style="font-family: inherit;"><span style="font-size: 12pt; line-height: 115%;">There
are lots of bands that have decent songwriters who write nice melodies, but
most of them borrow from earlier bands and sounds.<span style="mso-spacerun: yes;">&nbsp; </span>That&#8217;s not enough to create really good music
with lasting power.<span style="mso-spacerun: yes;">&nbsp; </span>It&#8217;s not enough to
be catchy, there has to be something new and different.<span style="mso-spacerun: yes;">&nbsp; </span>And that&#8217;s something for which the rock and
roll genre is at a loss right now.</span></span></div>
<div class="MsoNormal" style="margin-bottom: .0001pt; margin-bottom: 0in;">
<br /></div>
<div class="MsoNormal" style="margin-bottom: .0001pt; margin-bottom: 0in;">
<span style="font-family: inherit;"><span style="font-size: 12pt; line-height: 115%;">Of
course, rock and roll is based on borrowing aspects of music from other
people.<span style="mso-spacerun: yes;">&nbsp; </span>But for as long as the genre has
existed, we&#8217;ve seen new, innovative sounds.</span></span></div>
<div class="MsoNormal" style="margin-bottom: .0001pt; margin-bottom: 0in;">
<br /></div>
<div class="MsoNormal" style="margin-bottom: .0001pt; margin-bottom: 0in;">
<span style="font-family: inherit;"><span style="font-size: 12pt; line-height: 115%;">Bo
Diddley was an experimental master of guitar.<span style="mso-spacerun: yes;">&nbsp;
</span>George Harrison, Jimi Hendrix, and Jimmy Page all had polished,
perfected, and unpredictable guitar skills.<span style="mso-spacerun: yes;">&nbsp;
</span>Then Thurston Moore and Lee Ranaldo came along in the 80s and made the
guitar sound like a totally different instrument.<span style="mso-spacerun: yes;">&nbsp; </span>Only a few short years later Kevin Shields
reinvents the instrument again.</span></span></div>
<div class="MsoNormal" style="margin-bottom: .0001pt; margin-bottom: 0in;">
<br /></div>
<div class="MsoNormal" style="margin-bottom: .0001pt; margin-bottom: 0in;">
<span style="font-family: inherit;"><span style="font-size: 12pt; line-height: 115%;">Radiohead
and Wilco both made strides in experimental music at the beginning of the
2000s, but they didn&#8217;t totally flip the genre on its head like those other guys
did.<span style="mso-spacerun: yes;">&nbsp; </span>It&#8217;s been a long time since we&#8217;ve
seen anything truly new in rock and roll.<span style="mso-spacerun: yes;">&nbsp;
</span>And if Doug Martsch can&#8217;t figure it out, it will be very difficult for
anyone else to get out of the rut.</span></span></div>
<div class="MsoNormal" style="margin-bottom: .0001pt; margin-bottom: 0in;">
<br /></div>
<div class="MsoNormal" style="margin-bottom: .0001pt; margin-bottom: 0in;">
<span style="font-family: inherit;"><span style="font-size: 12pt; line-height: 115%;">I
don&#8217;t consider myself a classicist by any means.<span style="mso-spacerun: yes;">&nbsp; </span>I like music new and old.<span style="mso-spacerun: yes;">&nbsp; </span>But I&#8217;ve realized that for at least the past
year of my life, most of the rock and roll I&#8217;ve listened to has been from
before 2000.<span style="mso-spacerun: yes;">&nbsp; </span>There just isn&#8217;t
interesting stuff happening right now.</span></span></div>
<div class="MsoNormal" style="margin-bottom: .0001pt; margin-bottom: 0in;">
<br /></div>
<div class="MsoNormal" style="margin-bottom: .0001pt; margin-bottom: 0in;">
<span style="font-family: inherit;"><span style="font-size: 12pt; line-height: 115%;">I
don&#8217;t know what to tell people to do with their guitars to sound new.<span style="mso-spacerun: yes;">&nbsp; </span>I don&#8217;t have the answers.<span style="mso-spacerun: yes;">&nbsp; </span>But I do know that the situation is dire and
only getting worse.<span style="mso-spacerun: yes;">&nbsp; </span>It would be a shame
to see this great music go away, but maybe it has run its course.<span style="mso-spacerun: yes;">&nbsp; </span>It seems to be the beginning of the end.</span></span></div>
<div style='clear: both;'></div>
