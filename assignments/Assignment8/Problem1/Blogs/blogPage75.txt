<h3 class='post-title entry-title' itemprop='name'>
<a href='http://sixtyat60.blogspot.com/2016/03/strange-magic.html'>Strange Magic - Songs 1975</a>
</h3>
<div class='post-header'>
<div class='post-header-line-1'></div>
</div>
<div class='post-body entry-content' id='post-body-1049676212834685205' itemprop='description articleBody'>
<div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
<a href="https://1.bp.blogspot.com/-Zp-OoscVBww/VvvUXLoPjgI/AAAAAAAACKA/MgIseTYCHAQVDtFogxgbncdAdcAqLMgbw/s1600/sm75.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="clear: left; float: left; margin-bottom: 1em; margin-right: 1em;"><img border="0" height="234" src="https://1.bp.blogspot.com/-Zp-OoscVBww/VvvUXLoPjgI/AAAAAAAACKA/MgIseTYCHAQVDtFogxgbncdAdcAqLMgbw/s320/sm75.jpg" width="320" /></a></div>
Songs 1975. A collection of songs from albums released in 1975.<br />
<br />
This is the first mix of the Songs 1975 series and sets the tone for much of the year. There were certainly many great albums released in 1975 but overall I thought it was a weaker year in music. Looking back it feels like a transition year from the highs of the late sixties and early seventies classic rock to the soon to be happening punk, new wave and disco eras. Let's not forget progressive rock too.<br />
<br />
This was also a year that I personally spent a lot of time exploring jazz, reggae and blues of many different eras.<br />
<br />
<br />

<li>The Band - It Makes No Difference <i>(Northern Lights Southern Cross)</i></li>

<li>Joan Baez - Diamonds And Rust<i> (Diamonds &amp; Rust)</i></li>
<li>Joan Armatrading - Cool Blue Stole My Heart <i>(Back To The Night)</i></li>
<li>Hall &amp; Oates - Camellia <i>(Hall &amp; Oates)</i></li>
<li>Jefferson Starship - Miracles <i>(Red Octopus)</i></li>
<li>Eagles - Take It To The Limit <i>(One Of These Nights)</i></li>
<li>Earth Wind &amp; Fire - Reasons <i>(That's The Way Of The World)</i></li>
<li>Todd Rundgren - Real Man <i>(Initiation)</i></li>
<li>Electric Light Orchestra - Strange Magic <i>(Face The Music)</i></li>
<li>Steely Dan - Everyone's Gone To The Movies <i>(Katy Lied)</i></li>
