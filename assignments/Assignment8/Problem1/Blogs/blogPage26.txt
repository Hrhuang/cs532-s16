<h3 class='post-title entry-title'>
<a href='http://f-measure.blogspot.com/2016/03/inxs-dont-change-song-remains-same.html'>INXS - "Don't Change" (the song remains the same) </a>
</h3>
<div class='post-header-line-1'></div>
<div class='post-body entry-content'>
<div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
<a href="https://3.bp.blogspot.com/-aJkBvnRpYHo/Vud4uquX0wI/AAAAAAAABy8/hcSGb4IZI6ADaiCiv68NJ27mDohcLlNzA/s1600/inxs-dont-change.jpeg" imageanchor="1" style="clear: left; float: left; margin-bottom: 1em; margin-right: 1em;"><img border="0" height="200" src="https://3.bp.blogspot.com/-aJkBvnRpYHo/Vud4uquX0wI/AAAAAAAABy8/hcSGb4IZI6ADaiCiv68NJ27mDohcLlNzA/s200/inxs-dont-change.jpeg" width="200" /></a></div>
Most people's don't realize <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/INXS">INXS</a> had a full career before 1987's "<a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kick_(INXS_album)">Kick</a>", which was so popular when I was in college it effectively blocked everyone's memory of anything that came before it.&nbsp; One of my favorite songs is "Don't Change", a single from their 1982 LP "<a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Shabooh_Shoobah">Shabooh Shoobah</a>".&nbsp; It's a great song that still sounds fresh some 24+ years later.&nbsp; You also have to enjoy the low budget video as well -- over the top videos were apparently still a few years away.<br />
<br />
So imagine my delight when I recently uncovered <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bruce_Springsteen">Bruce Springsteen</a> doing a fun cover of it while in concert in Australia in 2014.&nbsp; <br />
<blockquote class="tr_bq">
I'm standing here on the ground<br />
The sky above won't fall down<br />
See no evil in all directions<br />
Resolution of happiness<br />
Things have been dark for too long</blockquote>
<br />

<h3 class='post-title entry-title'>
<a href='http://f-measure.blogspot.com/2016/02/the-time-jungle-love-forgotten-song.html'>The Time - "Jungle Love" (forgotten song)</a>
</h3>
<div class='post-header-line-1'></div>
<div class='post-body entry-content'>
<div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
<a href="https://1.bp.blogspot.com/-PHzwAbLvPnk/VrlTl2JHmpI/AAAAAAAABxw/saFhqCbmgpY/s1600/time-jungle-love.jpeg" imageanchor="1" style="clear: left; float: left; margin-bottom: 1em; margin-right: 1em;"><img border="0" height="200" src="https://1.bp.blogspot.com/-PHzwAbLvPnk/VrlTl2JHmpI/AAAAAAAABxw/saFhqCbmgpY/s200/time-jungle-love.jpeg" width="200" /></a></div>
<a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Super_Bowl_50">Super Bowl 50</a> is now in the books.&nbsp; The game itself was pretty good (if you like defense), but as always there has a been a lot of talk about the <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Super_Bowl_50_halftime_show">halftime show</a>.&nbsp; <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Coldplay">Coldplay</a> was ... well, Coldplay.&nbsp; On paper I "should" like them but wow, I just can't.&nbsp; I can't even say I dislike them... they're just so boring that I feel nothing at all.&nbsp;<br />
<br />
(Full disclosure: I did buy "<a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Parachutes_%28album%29">Parachutes</a>" in 2000, but I honestly can't remember the last time I played it).&nbsp; Apparently it's <a href="http://www.theonion.com/article/super-bowl-halftime-show-marred-functioning-sound--52312">cool to hate on Coldplay</a>, so I won't waste any more time with them (although their <a href="http://www.cbsnews.com/news/coldplay-in-the-adventure-of-a-lifetime/">bit on CBS Sunday Morning</a> did make them seem like pretty decent guys...)<br />
<br />
<a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Beyonc%C3%A9">Beyonce</a> caused <a href="http://www.latimes.com/entertainment/music/posts/la-et-ms-beyonce-super-bowl-reaction-20160208-story.html">quite the stir</a> with her contribution (<a href="http://www.nydailynews.com/entertainment/beyonce-halftime-show-draws-ire-praise-article-1.2524039">Giuliani</a> inserted himself into the story without even mentioning 9/11!), but I don't have anything to offer there either.<br />
<br />
I want to talk about the uninspired inclusion of <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bruno_Mars">Bruno Mars</a> (ok, technically "<a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mark_Ronson">Mark Ronson</a> featuring Bruno Mars"). &nbsp; One, we just had him <a href="http://f-measure.blogspot.com/2014/02/stevie-wonder-higher-ground-forgotten.html">two years ago</a>.&nbsp; Two, "<a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Uptown_Funk">Uptown Funk</a>" is over a year old at this point.&nbsp; Three, doesn't it bother anyone that "Uptown Funk" sounds exactly like "<a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jungle_Love_%28The_Time_song%29">Jungle Love</a>"?&nbsp; There are a lot of articles that talk about <a href="http://radio.com/2015/03/11/8-artists-that-could-sue-uptown-funk-on-the-same-grounds-as-blurred-lines/">how it sounds like many different funk songs</a>, but to me it sounds identical to <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Time_(band)">The Time</a>'s 1984 single from "<a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ice_Cream_Castle">Ice Cream Castle</a>" and featured prominently in "<a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Purple_Rain_%28film%29">Purple Rain</a>".&nbsp; Side note: I didn't realize in 80s that The Time basically <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ice_Cream_Castle#Personnel"><i>was</i> Prince</a>.&nbsp; As Danette's college roommate said: "Prince <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Prince_associates">dresses him</a>" (or her, where appropriate).<br />
<br />
So I just wanted to take this opportunity to say that for the last year, every time we heard "Uptown Funk" (which was a lot!), Danette and I always said "<span class="st">Jerome bring me my mirror!</span>"<br />
<br />
The Time - "<a href="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=N2FPQvwhSDY">Jungle Love</a>"

<h3 class='post-title entry-title'>
<a href='http://f-measure.blogspot.com/2016/01/the-eagles-seven-bridges-road-spotlight.html'>The Eagles - "Seven Bridges Road" (spotlight)</a>
</h3>
<div class='post-header-line-1'></div>
<div class='post-body entry-content'>
<div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
<a href="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/-MXOque7eaOA/Vp5YtlW6G2I/AAAAAAAABww/4VVy5diZuiQ/s1600/eagles-seven-bridges-road.jpeg" imageanchor="1" style="clear: left; float: left; margin-bottom: 1em; margin-right: 1em;"><img border="0" height="198" src="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/-MXOque7eaOA/Vp5YtlW6G2I/AAAAAAAABww/4VVy5diZuiQ/s200/eagles-seven-bridges-road.jpeg" width="200" /></a></div>
I'm getting tired of having to acknowledge the passing of legends...&nbsp; Yesterday <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Glenn_Frey">Glenn Frey</a>, co-founder of the <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Eagles_%28band%29">Eagles</a>, <a href="http://www.bbc.com/news/world-us-canada-35349025">died yesterday</a>.&nbsp; The Eagles were so good at the entire Southwest, country-rock crossover thing, they experienced an inevitable backlash -- it became <a href="https://www.washingtonpost.com/news/morning-mix/wp/2016/01/19/how-glenn-frey-and-the-eagles-outlasted-everyone-who-loved-to-hate-them/">cool to hate them</a> because of their success (and the 70s-era excess they represented).&nbsp; But the truth is anyone who was raised on <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Album-oriented_rock">AOR</a> (now known as classic rock) stations, as this <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Generation_X">Gen Xer</a> was,&nbsp; knows most of their songs by heart.<br />
<br />
I'll choose to remember them not for any of the dozens of popular songs they wrote, but for a cover of a <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Steve_Young_(musician)">Steve Young</a> <a href="http://americansongwriter.com/2007/11/behind-the-song-seven-bridges-road-written-by-steve-young/">song</a>, "<a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Seven_Bridges_Road">Seven Bridges Road</a>",&nbsp; they released in 1980 (from "<a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Eagles_Live">Eagles Live</a>") as their last single before they <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Eagles_%28band%29#Reunion">reunited in 1994</a>.&nbsp; For a band that spent most of their time <a href="http://www.bbc.com/news/entertainment-arts-35347075">fighting each other</a>, the five part harmony of this mostly a cappella song was a nice metaphor for what they could achieve together.&nbsp; <br />
<br />
Eagles - "<a href="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=t7Li1ez-iQ4">Seven Bridges Road</a>"

<h3 class='post-title entry-title'>
<a href='http://f-measure.blogspot.com/2016/01/david-bowie-blackstar-spotlight.html'>David Bowie - "Blackstar" (spotlight)</a>
</h3>
<div class='post-header-line-1'></div>
<div class='post-body entry-content'>
<div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
<a href="http://1.bp.blogspot.com/-adPuQ1U1ARQ/VpPFczOykAI/AAAAAAAABwc/yikL_h-XdoA/s1600/david-bowie-blackstar-single.jpeg" imageanchor="1" style="clear: left; float: left; margin-bottom: 1em; margin-right: 1em;"><img border="0" height="199" src="http://1.bp.blogspot.com/-adPuQ1U1ARQ/VpPFczOykAI/AAAAAAAABwc/yikL_h-XdoA/s200/david-bowie-blackstar-single.jpeg" width="200" /></a></div>
<a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/David_Bowie">David Bowie</a> <a href="http://www.bbc.com/news/entertainment-arts-35278872">died yesterday</a>.&nbsp; Obviously when an icon of this stature dies it is tempting to recall any of his numerous <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/David_Bowie#Legacy_and_influence">popular and/or influential works</a> (with "<a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Low_%28David_Bowie_album%29">Low</a>" being an example of <i>influential</i> but not really <i>popular</i>, at least by comparison to other works in his canon).&nbsp; <br />
<br />
Instead, I prefer to draw your attention to "<a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Blackstar_%28David_Bowie_song%29">Blackstar</a>", which came out about six weeks ago, right before Thanksgiving.&nbsp; I heard it driving home one night on "<a href="http://whro.org/radio/personalities/paul-shugrue">Out of the Box</a>" and was struck by the fact that although it was clearly David Bowie, it sounded like classic Bowie but I couldn't identify the song.&nbsp; And there were modern influences as well, so maybe it wasn't a classic song.&nbsp; And "Out of the Box" focuses on new music -- was this a re-release?.&nbsp; Was it another artist ripping off Bowie?&nbsp; Eventually I was able to <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Shazam_%28service%29">shazam</a> the song and learn that it was the title track from his <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Blackstar_(David_Bowie_album)">forthcoming LP</a> (which was released just two days before his death).<br />
<br />
I texted &amp; emailed with all my music buddies saying "you need to check out this new Bowie song".&nbsp; The summary of those conversations was how effortlessly it was both modern &amp; classic, seamlessly incorporating the past &amp; present.&nbsp; And as a bonus, the video was mysterious &amp; freaky.&nbsp; I had planned to blog about it then, but with the holidays it got delayed.&nbsp; His illness had not been made public, so I did not realize there was much time left.<br />
<br />
So don't just remember <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/David_Bowie#1972.E2.80.9373:_Ziggy_Stardust">Ziggy Stardust</a> and other characters.&nbsp; Remember Bowie for making engaging, challenging music for his entire career, even right at the end.&nbsp; <br />
<br />
David Bowie - "<a href="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=kszLwBaC4Sw">Blackstar</a>".

<h2 class='date-header'><span>Wednesday, December 30, 2015</span></h2>

          <div class="date-posts">
        
<div class='post-outer'>
<div class='post hentry'>
<a name='1025072233201621984'></a>
<h3 class='post-title entry-title'>
<a href='http://f-measure.blogspot.com/2015/12/hawkwind-silver-machine-forgotten-song.html'>Hawkwind - "Silver Machine" (forgotten song)</a>
</h3>
<div class='post-header-line-1'></div>
<div class='post-body entry-content'>
<div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
<a href="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/-hfHmKz4Fvh4/VoP-k01isFI/AAAAAAAABvw/OCjusZP6zFU/s1600/hawkwind-lemmy.png" imageanchor="1" style="clear: left; float: left; margin-bottom: 1em; margin-right: 1em;"><img border="0" height="146" src="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/-hfHmKz4Fvh4/VoP-k01isFI/AAAAAAAABvw/OCjusZP6zFU/s200/hawkwind-lemmy.png" width="200" /></a></div>
Ian "<a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lemmy">Lemmy</a>" Kilmister <a href="http://www.bbc.com/news/entertainment-arts-35192060">died this week</a> at the age of 70.&nbsp; If you don't know what Lemmy means to metal, or just rock in general, then I'm not going to be able to explain it to you.&nbsp; Exhibit A: NPR did a story <a href="http://www.npr.org/sections/thetwo-way/2015/12/28/461327109/rock-icon-lemmy-of-mot-rhead-dies-at-age-70">about his passing</a>.&nbsp; NPR.&nbsp; Think about that.&nbsp; Words like "icon", "legend",&nbsp; and "pioneer" are often casually tossed around, but in this case they're appropriate.&nbsp; Quoting from a <a href="http://www.theguardian.com/music/2015/aug/13/lemmy-apparently-i-am-still-indestructible-motorhead-album-bad-magic">Guardian story</a> from this summer:<br />
<blockquote class="tr_bq">
Lemmy is as much a collection of myths and legends as a man. In the popular imagination, he&#8217;s made up of equal parts Jack Daniel&#8217;s, amphetamine sulphate, Nazi memorabilia and extreme-velocity noise. The myths and legends cloak him as surely as the black shirt, the black jeans, the custom-made boots, the cowboy hat with its &#8220;Death or Glory&#8221; insignia and the Iron Cross around his neck.</blockquote>
Of course, Lemmy is best known as the vocalist &amp; bassist -- and only permanent member -- for <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mot%C3%B6rhead">Motorhead</a>.&nbsp; Motorhead, of course, is the seminal band that first combined outlaw biker imagery with a <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mot%C3%B6rhead#Style">punk style / ethos &amp; metal heaviness</a>, although Lemmy always insisted they were simply a "rock-n-roll band".&nbsp; Furthermore, he was innovative in that he played bass like it was a lead instrument and not rhythm.&nbsp; But before there was Motorhead, there was <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hawkwind">Hawkwind</a> -- the seminal space rock group, featuring <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/List_of_Hawkwind_band_members">fluid membership</a>, <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Michael_Moorcock">Michael Moorcock</a> inspired imagery &amp; lyrics, and nearly limitless <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/This_Is_Spinal_Tap">Spinal Tap-esque</a> cliches (including one of the first appearances of the <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/In_Search_of_Space#Sleeve">heavy metal umlaut</a>).&nbsp; <br />
<br />
I could memorialize Lemmy with any of numerous excellent Motorhead songs, but instead I'll choose Hawkwind's 1972 single, "<a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Silver_Machine">Silver Machine</a>", which he didn't even write but I believe was the first song on which he sang lead (he was not the primary vocalist for Hawkwind), and turned out to be Hawkwind's only "hit" song.&nbsp; Although Lemmy would not be <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lemmy#1971.E2.80.931975:_Hawkwind">fired from Hawkwind</a> for another three years, this is probably the song that set him on the path from "band member" to "band leader".&nbsp; <br />
<br />
I believe <a href="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=MoZ_Lg21b14">this video</a> is the one shown on <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Silver_Machine#Top_of_the_Pops">BBC's Top of the Pops in 1972</a> (instead of having the band in the studio lip syncing).&nbsp; It features Lemmy in his pre-Motorhead, all black garb.&nbsp; The sound is early-70s groovy, the studio version is badly synced with the concert footage, which features bubbles, a <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nik_Turner">flautist</a> (!) and, of course, a <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Stacia">dancer</a>.&nbsp; You can't make this stuff up.&nbsp; Despite (because of?) all that, the song rawks and I love it.&nbsp; Danette hates it, for all of the same reasons listed above, though she does like Motorhead in general.<br />
<br />
A month ago, The Atlantic had an article featuring Lemmy and entitled "<a href="http://www.theatlantic.com/magazine/archive/2015/11/twilight-of-the-headbangers/407848/">Twilight of the Headbangers: How long can the legends of heavy metal keep on rocking?</a>".&nbsp; Not long enough, though I think we have to count ourselves lucky that he made it to 70.<br />
<br />
Hawkwind - "<a href="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=MoZ_Lg21b14">Silver Machine</a>"<br />

<h3 class='post-title entry-title'>
<a href='http://f-measure.blogspot.com/2015/12/waxahatchee-cerulean-salt-lp-review.html'>Waxahatchee - "Cerulean Salt" (LP Review)</a>
</h3>
<div class='post-header-line-1'></div>
<div class='post-body entry-content'>
<div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
<a href="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/-XiLYESL4QaQ/VnxS0B0W7CI/AAAAAAAABvc/HMqOyOdNUx8/s1600/cerulean-salt.jpeg" imageanchor="1" style="clear: left; float: left; margin-bottom: 1em; margin-right: 1em;"><img border="0" height="200" src="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/-XiLYESL4QaQ/VnxS0B0W7CI/AAAAAAAABvc/HMqOyOdNUx8/s200/cerulean-salt.jpeg" width="200" /></a></div>
Just over a year ago I reviewed "<a href="http://f-measure.blogspot.com/2014/10/waxahatchee-american-weekend-lp-review.html">American Weekend</a>", the debut LP from <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Waxahatchee">Waxahatchee</a>.&nbsp; Her second LP "<a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cerulean_Salt">Cerulean Salt</a>" (2013) finds her backed by a "real band" (actually members shared with her sister's band, <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Swearin'">Swearin'</a>).&nbsp;<br />
<br />
Whereas "American Weekend" was a solo, lo-fi, bedroom recording masterpiece, "Cerulean Salt" doesn't try to duplicate that success.&nbsp; The songs are still sparse and minimally produced, but are enriched by the fuller sound afforded by the presence of band members.&nbsp; Some of them even downright rawk -- such as "Coast to Coast" and especially "Misery Over Dispute".&nbsp; And while the comparison to <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Liz_Phair">Liz Phair</a> continues (exhibit A: "<a href="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=5nyLqJjCglU">Brother Bryan</a>" vs. "<a href="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=BiEwavLjpeo">Girls! Girls! Girls!</a>"; Exhibit B: "<a href="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=EmEaIveGJzs">Dixie Cups and Jars</a>" vs. "<a href="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=MkssHBwG6CY">Go West</a>"), a more thematic comparison would be to <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bruce_Springsteen#Lyrical_themes">Bruce Springsteen</a> in that the glossy, upbeat songs hide some pretty dark lyrics.&nbsp; Almost any song would do, but it's hard to beat "<a href="http://genius.com/Waxahatchee-dixie-cups-and-jars-lyrics">Dixie Cups and Jars</a>":<br />
<blockquote class="tr_bq">
I'm not a whipper in the wind<br />Or solace laying at the bottom of a bottle<br />Or your thick skin<br />Escape yells both our names out loud<br />We run like hell, I'll write a tragic epilogue and you'll act it out<br /><br />I watched your dad give you away<br />I watched him drink the bitter taste in his exertion away<br />Make-up sits on your face like tar<br />The champagne flutes poorly engineered<br />Employ dixie cups and jars</blockquote>
A more eloquent LP review is available at <a href="http://pitchfork.com/reviews/albums/17688-waxahatchee-cerulean-salt/">Pitchfork</a>.&nbsp; I will say that while it doesn't speak to me personally like "American Weekend", "Cerulean Salt" is arguably a better all around LP.&nbsp;<br />
<br />
Standout songs: "<a href="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=EmEaIveGJzs">Dixie Cups and Jars</a>" (<a href="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=I06fxqaMsSk">live</a>), "<a href="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=5nyLqJjCglU">Brother Bryan</a>" (<a href="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=2aSLTh-DwKE">live</a>), "<a href="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=HHivBJoFa_0">Coast to Coast</a>" (<a href="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=HlhFE8-KBDE">live</a>) (<a href="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=nfCRtyQ_omc">live w/ Mish Way</a>), "<a href="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=4NwIeK7-DjA">Tangled Envisioning</a>" (<a href="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=f-d4VWwNFxY">live</a>), "<a href="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=se2ksyP2XuU">Misery Over Dispute</a>" (<a href="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=GwH9lWmLRd8">live</a>), "<a href="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=PY9bqHD5Csw">Lively</a>" (<a href="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=qa-CnAbtntI">live</a>), "<a href="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=sQusUE8y3SA">Swan Dive</a>" (<a href="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=lA-77mdPJIA">live</a>), <br />
<br />
Skip 'em songs: none.<br />
<br />
Final score: 9/10.&nbsp;

<h3 class='post-title entry-title'>
<a href='http://f-measure.blogspot.com/2015/11/balam-acab-see-birds-lp-review.html'>Balam Acab - "See Birds" (LP Review)</a>
</h3>
<div class='post-header-line-1'></div>
<div class='post-body entry-content'>
<div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
<a href="http://1.bp.blogspot.com/-wzEZ3zB87YU/Vlih2v5oEOI/AAAAAAAABuc/Y5YSkZq1zXg/s1600/balam-acam-see-birds.jpeg" imageanchor="1" style="clear: left; float: left; margin-bottom: 1em; margin-right: 1em;"><img border="0" height="200" src="http://1.bp.blogspot.com/-wzEZ3zB87YU/Vlih2v5oEOI/AAAAAAAABuc/Y5YSkZq1zXg/s200/balam-acam-see-birds.jpeg" width="200" /></a></div>
<a href="http://pitchfork.com/artists/28640-balam-acab/">Balam Acab</a>, aka <a href="http://www.phoenixnewtimes.com/music/balam-acabs-alec-koone-lives-with-his-mom-quietly-reviving-randb-6451548">Alec Koone</a>, released his first EP, "<a href="http://www.discogs.com/Balam-Acab-See-Birds/master/301049">See Birds</a>", to critical acclaim in 2010 (for example, the <a href="http://pitchfork.com/reviews/albums/14548-see-birds-ep/">Pitchfork review</a>).&nbsp; Herbert turned me on to this a few years ago and I had to get my own copy.&nbsp; It's tempting to discount this EP because Koone wasn't an established artist (and arguably still isn't), but I have to give it my highest possible score because it was a shocking eye opener for me, sounding nothing like what I was used to.&nbsp; Created with "found" (on the Internet) music samples, Koone creates a rich, subtle, and surprisingly warm sound.&nbsp; I can't help but compare it to, say, "<a href="http://f-measure.blogspot.com/2009/05/dj-shadow-what-does-your-soul-look-like.html">What Does Your Soul Look Like</a>" ca. 1994.&nbsp; Another stylistic comparison would be The Caretaker's "<a href="http://f-measure.blogspot.com/2009/11/caretaker-persistent-repetition-of.html">Persistent Repetition of Phrases</a>", but with different source material.<br />
<br />
Apparently the "new" sound of which he was a part earned the label "<a href="http://pitchfork.com/features/articles/7806-ghosts-in-the-machine/">witch house</a>", which was applied at least partially in jest.&nbsp; I'm not going to argue for or against that label, but I will say this is a compelling new style of music that demands a listen. &nbsp; Unfortunately, while his 2011 full length LP "<a href="http://pitchfork.com/reviews/albums/15766-wander-wonder/">Wander / Wonder</a>" is good, it fails to continue the magic introduced on this EP.&nbsp; <br />
<br />
Standout songs: all:&nbsp; <a href="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=v3oa3zfKW2k&amp;index=1&amp;list=PLwXmcy0JQnx66ujCxoqBLhGOF61xU1kOa">See Birds (Moon)</a>, <a href="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=_tmAMPz_noE&amp;index=2&amp;list=PLwXmcy0JQnx66ujCxoqBLhGOF61xU1kOa">Regret Making Mistakes</a>, <a href="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=dxt3YdmHHpQ&amp;index=3&amp;list=PLwXmcy0JQnx66ujCxoqBLhGOF61xU1kOa">Big Boy</a>, <a href="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=5hxdefnF8f0&amp;index=4&amp;list=PLwXmcy0JQnx66ujCxoqBLhGOF61xU1kOa">Dream Out</a>, <a href="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=X-sbKNpshbw&amp;index=5&amp;list=PLwXmcy0JQnx66ujCxoqBLhGOF61xU1kOa">See Birds (Sun)</a>; all five songs as a <a href="https://www.youtube.com/playlist?list=PLwXmcy0JQnx66ujCxoqBLhGOF61xU1kOa">playlist</a>.&nbsp; <br />
<br />
Skip 'em songs: none<br />
<br />
Final score: 10/10
