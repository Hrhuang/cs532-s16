<h3 class='post-title entry-title'>
<a href='http://cherryarea.blogspot.com/2016/02/trockel-margiela-mcfetridge-bore.html'>Trockel : Margiela : McFetridge (: Boré)</a>
</h3>
<div class='post-header'>
<div class='post-header-line-1'></div>
</div>
<div class='post-body entry-content' id='post-body-8325160895670495383'>
<div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
<a href="https://2.bp.blogspot.com/-lmKtK_sJOBI/VsK7IHMSxfI/AAAAAAAACE4/5P14ipsscQo/s1600/Trockel%2BMargiela.JPG" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;"><img border="0" height="400" src="https://2.bp.blogspot.com/-lmKtK_sJOBI/VsK7IHMSxfI/AAAAAAAACE4/5P14ipsscQo/s400/Trockel%2BMargiela.JPG" width="335" /></a><a href="https://2.bp.blogspot.com/-iV2mfI_qa48/VsK7Q9kvSSI/AAAAAAAACE8/xl7quJ3w5sU/s1600/McFetridge%2BPoster.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;"><img border="0" height="400" src="https://2.bp.blogspot.com/-iV2mfI_qa48/VsK7Q9kvSSI/AAAAAAAACE8/xl7quJ3w5sU/s400/McFetridge%2BPoster.jpg" width="278" /></a></div>
<span style="color: #6a6a6a; font-family: arial, sans-serif; font-size: x-small;"><span style="background-color: white; line-height: 18.2px;"><b><br /></b></span></span>
I've <a href="http://cherryarea.blogspot.com/2015/08/found.html">mentioned before</a> that I think all of <a href="http://dandygum.blogspot.com/">Begüm Boré's work</a> is absolutely brilliant and savant-like, and this entry (above left) bridging an untitled Rosemarie Trockel piece from the 2000s with a shot from Maison Martin Margiela's A/W 2013 is no exception. If I may contribute a puny little addendum(b): the combined effect of the source material for Boré's post puts me in mind of a Geoff McFetridge solo exhibition from a couple of years ago, the brilliantly-titled and -themed&nbsp;<i>Meditallucination</i>.<div>
<br /></div>
<div>
Needless to say, my feeling is that Boré is as much a capital-A Artist here as Trockel or Margiela or McFetridge.</div>

<h3 class='post-title entry-title'>
<a href='http://cherryarea.blogspot.com/2016/02/seeing-cy-twombly.html'>Seeing Cy Twombly</a>
</h3>
<div class='post-header'>
<div class='post-header-line-1'></div>
</div>
<div class='post-body entry-content' id='post-body-7531813467579726340'>
everywhere, lately.<br />
<br />
Exhibit A: Imagined a KOM jersey designed by Twombly.<br />
<br />
<div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
<a href="https://2.bp.blogspot.com/-hOo98nvWOKs/VsKuVbWQxnI/AAAAAAAACD8/LkBY5tRRmSw/s1600/KOM.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;"><img border="0" height="426" src="https://2.bp.blogspot.com/-hOo98nvWOKs/VsKuVbWQxnI/AAAAAAAACD8/LkBY5tRRmSw/s640/KOM.jpg" width="640" /></a></div>
<br />
<div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
<a href="https://4.bp.blogspot.com/-dIX57RDG6oc/VsKuVVDqhJI/AAAAAAAACD4/bUluHIOlYWQ/s1600/Twombly%2BKOM.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;"><img border="0" src="https://4.bp.blogspot.com/-dIX57RDG6oc/VsKuVVDqhJI/AAAAAAAACD4/bUluHIOlYWQ/s1600/Twombly%2BKOM.jpg" /></a></div>
<br />
B: Saw Twombly in a postage stamp.<br />
<br />
<div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
<a href="https://3.bp.blogspot.com/-mAl9OVEP5fE/VsKv5L3rYCI/AAAAAAAACEQ/DhI9FdrDI80/s1600/Twombly%2BStamp.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em; text-align: center;"><img border="0" height="361" src="https://3.bp.blogspot.com/-mAl9OVEP5fE/VsKv5L3rYCI/AAAAAAAACEQ/DhI9FdrDI80/s400/Twombly%2BStamp.jpg" width="400" /></a></div>
<div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
(As what could be one of his calligraphic works layered overtop one of his floral works. Palimpsestual, perhaps in keeping with Twombly's aesthetic.)</div>
<div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
<br /></div>
<br />
<div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
<a href="https://2.bp.blogspot.com/-5inlC6u_rY0/VsKv5I4_h6I/AAAAAAAACEM/OcSYtheYxik/s1600/Twombly%2BSea.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;"><img border="0" src="https://2.bp.blogspot.com/-5inlC6u_rY0/VsKv5I4_h6I/AAAAAAAACEM/OcSYtheYxik/s1600/Twombly%2BSea.jpg" /></a></div>
<br />
It's one thing for the USPS to occasionally issue a commemorative stamp (I haven't yet encountered one for Twombly, FWIW), but it would've&nbsp;<i>really </i>been something if they could've gotten Twombly to void used stamps by hand in his lifetime, scribbling through postage from 9 to 5. Good way to kill two birds with one stone, providing publicly-funded stability for an artist (not that Twombly would've needed the help) while also fulfilling a practical need. I admit that it's not quite Bernie Sanders' <a href="http://www.theatlantic.com/business/archive/2015/10/bernie-sanders-lets-turn-post-offices-into-banks/411589/">proposal to convert post offices into banks</a>, but hey. I think it'd be nice to receive mail with something small and beautiful adorning it, even if just incidentally, or for practical purposes. Reminded of Lennon's defense of that Ono piece in which a tiny, indecipherable marking ("YES") is visible on the ceiling above a ladder and a magnifying glass--where annoyed critics argued that it was meaningless, Lennon asserted that the naysayers would have gotten quite a kick out if it if they saw it on the ceiling while they were on the john (no pun intended) (not that I agree, exactly). ANYWAY, why settle for a plain white envelope when you can have something like this?!:<br />
<div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
<a href="https://1.bp.blogspot.com/-z3B_5rv6TDY/VsK1unY_UdI/AAAAAAAACEg/VBwg4vdJbnk/s1600/Alla.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;"><img border="0" src="https://1.bp.blogspot.com/-z3B_5rv6TDY/VsK1unY_UdI/AAAAAAAACEg/VBwg4vdJbnk/s1600/Alla.jpg" /></a></div>
<br />
Koch--who famously hung out around quite a few painters, like the rest of his New York School posse--spent considerable time in Rome, which the expat Twombly made a second home. I wonder if they ever crossed paths. I think O'Hara has an entry in a book on writings about Twombly, IIRC.

<h3 class='post-title entry-title'>
<a href='http://cherryarea.blogspot.com/2016/02/the-classic-build-italians-do-do-it.html'>The Classic Build / Italians <i>Do</i> Do It Better</a>
</h3>
<div class='post-header'>
<div class='post-header-line-1'></div>
</div>
<div class='post-body entry-content' id='post-body-6831502162623557197'>
<blockquote class="tr_bq">
At 1.72 m (5 ft 8 in) and 57 kg (126 lbs), he had the classic build for a mountain climber.</blockquote>
Marco Pantani, duh. But also, you know...<i>me</i>.<br />
<br />
Have been thinking about this again lately, riding in the super-steep on my days off. I did six miles in the cold wet last Monday before turning around, scared and tired--it felt more like sixteen than six, I would've put money on it before I checked the map. My watch, as if to warn me, was fogged up with the haunting of that ride until yesterday. Stuck, too spooked, to a few practical (i.e. flat) miles today, to the ATM and back, no kit or anything.<br />
<br />
But back to Pantani: when I first started riding with racers, my co-workers got a kick out of the fact that I had the same measurements--and riding proclivities, unbeknownst to me--as Il Pirata. Except that's about all we have in common, I think, other than maybe a predisposition towards addictive/destructive behavior.<br />
<br />
Will pour one out for the dude this Valentine's Day, twelve years since his untimely passing.<br />
<br />
<div style="text-align: center;">
-------------------------------------</div>
<div style="text-align: center;">
<br /></div>
<div style="text-align: left;">
Here, as a tangent, is a really striking photograph from Fausto Coppi's funeral:</div>
<br />
<div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
<a href="https://1.bp.blogspot.com/-GsxxTQ4bHPk/VrmCvgviywI/AAAAAAAACDc/RwWxMxrhrw8/s1600/Coppi.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;"><img border="0" height="465" src="https://1.bp.blogspot.com/-GsxxTQ4bHPk/VrmCvgviywI/AAAAAAAACDc/RwWxMxrhrw8/s640/Coppi.jpg" width="640" /></a></div>
<br />
Coppi and Pantani, of course, are two of the six Tour de France champs to hail from Italy. Each is said, in his own way, to have dealt with the devil, and each died under strange circumstances. Even the lore is amazing. Italians <i>do&nbsp;</i>do it better.

The gradient is sometimes steeper than one-in-five. Of all athletes, cycling pros have the largest hearts and, in normal circumstances, a very low pulse rate--but on mountain climbs, this may rise as high as one-hundred and eight to two-hundred, which could lead to heart failure in a non-athlete. <h3 class='post-title entry-title'>
<a href='http://cherryarea.blogspot.com/2016/01/not-man-dying-of-shame.html'>Not a Man / Dying of Shame</a>
</h3>
<div class='post-header'>
<div class='post-header-line-1'></div>
</div>
<div class='post-body entry-content' id='post-body-632523610811268792'>
"If man had a sense of proportion, he would die of shame." &#8212; J.M. Ledgard,&nbsp;<i>Submergence</i><br />
<div>
<br /></div>
<div style="text-align: center;">
---------------------------------</div>
<div>
<br /></div>
At the natural foods grocer on the way downtown, a person can scoop from the bulk dry goods bins however much of whatever she pleases: raisins, for instance (organic or non-, domestic or imported), or whole almonds, or both. I think they go so well together; she can have her pick. She is charged by weight.<br />
<br />
The ideal ratio, roughly, is three raisins to one almond. This means one scoop to three (this is if she agrees with me).<br />
<br />
This is if she places, gently, a single almond onto the tip of her tongue, three raisins waiting in her open palm, her waiting with three raisins in her open palm. This is if she is one to wait, if she is one to open up her palm.<br />
<div style="text-align: center;">
<br /></div>
<div style="text-align: center;">
---------------------------------</div>
<div>
<br /></div>
<div>
Bill Frisell is playing over the stereo. Bill Frisell is playing at the Center for the Arts next month. Kenneth Gerber understands Frisell, understands that phrasing is of more importance than the sheer number of notes in a bar. Richard Thurston concurs: "It's not how many," he says, "it's which ones."</div>

</h3>
<div class='post-header'>
<div class='post-header-line-1'></div>
</div>
<div class='post-body entry-content' id='post-body-1277782695577996689'>
<blockquote class="tr_bq">
Two years [after his last release, a collaboration with Q-Tip], Busta Rhymes is back with a new tape of unheard material, this time without Q-Tip. <i>The Return of the Dragon (The Abstract Went on Vacation)</i> doesn&#8217;t exactly herald the return of Peak Busta or even Good Busta&#8212;in fact, it does the opposite. The raps are mostly uninspired and sometimes they don&#8217;t even make sense ("My money long, thicker than a waffle," on "God&#8217;s Plan"; "I&#8217;m Def Leppard and I ain&#8217;t tryna hear the noise," on "Choose a Side"). <blockquote class="tr_bq">
Many of these B-sides sound years old&#8212;songs recorded for alternate purposes that were at one point or another shelved and never should&#8217;ve seen the light of day. For starters, there&#8217;s a guest appearance from long-forgotten R&amp;B singer J. Holiday on a song ironically titled "Hits for Days." When Styles P and Jadakiss do their usual back-and-forth on "Respect My Conglomerate 2," a sequel to the 2009 Busta song of the same name, there&#8217;s a reference to the bank Wachovia, which was absorbed by Wells Fargo in 2008. Some other songs feel like relics from a bygone era, especially "Real Niggas," which reeks of Lex Luger "MC Hammer"-like trap opulence with big, dramatic chamber orchestration (and a Rick Ross feature to boot). "UFC (Tap-Out)" reunites Waka Flocka Flame and Gucci Mane, a dated pairing given their contentious recent history and Gucci&#8217;s overlapping prison stint.</blockquote>

<div class='post-header'>
<div class='post-header-line-1'></div>
</div>
<div class='post-body entry-content' id='post-body-2032217048711078114'>
Maybe I'm superstitious, but I feel like christening a snowstorm too far ahead of its arrival--even one as <i>historic</i>&nbsp;as the meteorologists keep reminding us that this weekend's expected weather event will be--is about as misguided as revealing the name of a baby boy before bris.<br />
<br />
<div style="text-align: center;">
-----------------------------------</div>
<br />
My Costa Rican roommate, whose first language is not English, often introduces me to her friends by saying, sort of formally, "He is Alex." This never fails to make me chuckle. She also once, in Spanish, introduced one of the house's cats, Jäger, to a friend, to which said friend replied, lighting up: "Cómo Jäger-<i>bomb</i>?" Had me in stitches.<br />
<br />
<div style="text-align: center;">
-----------------------------------<br />
<div>
<br /></div>
<div style="text-align: left;">

<div style="text-align: left;">
<br /></div>
<div style="text-align: left;">
<div style="text-align: center;">
-----------------------------------<br />
<div>
<br /></div>
<div style="text-align: left;">

<h3 class='post-title entry-title'>

<div class='post-body entry-content' id='post-body-6813085356469903633'>
<div class="separator tr_bq" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
<a href="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/-ZaZzN-mt3gI/Vp3PMd8pGJI/AAAAAAAACBM/kO-Czzsfjo0/s1600/Magritte.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;"><img border="0" height="480" src="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/-ZaZzN-mt3gI/Vp3PMd8pGJI/AAAAAAAACBM/kO-Czzsfjo0/s640/Magritte.jpg" width="640" /></a></div>
<br />
Magritte painted the second of his <i>Les Amants</i>&nbsp;in 1928. The MOMA, where it is held, <a href="http://www.moma.org/learn/moma_learning/rene-magritte-the-lovers-le-perreux-sur-marne-1928">says the following</a> about the piece:<br />
<blockquote class="tr_bq">
"Enshrouded faces were a common motif in Magritte&#8217;s art. The artist was 14 when his mother committed suicide by drowning. He witnessed her body being fished from the water, her wet nightgown wrapped around her face."</blockquote>
Another prominent motif in the Belgian surrealist's oeuvre, as the MOMA points out, is that of frustrated desire.&nbsp;<i>Les Amants II</i>&nbsp;"[transforms] an act of passion into one of isolation and frustration. Some have interpreted this work as a depiction of the inability to fully unveil the true nature of even our most intimate companions."<br />
<br />
<div style="text-align: center;">
----------------------------------------------------------------</div>
<div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
<a href="http://1.bp.blogspot.com/-ecWi0L2ZFlk/Vp3PMeWPhgI/AAAAAAAACBI/j0KfezwU_3Y/s1600/Shopsin.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;"><img border="0" src="http://1.bp.blogspot.com/-ecWi0L2ZFlk/Vp3PMeWPhgI/AAAAAAAACBI/j0KfezwU_3Y/s1600/Shopsin.jpg" /></a></div>
<div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: left;">
This is an illustration by Tamara Shopsin for a 2008&nbsp;<i>Walrus</i> article on "Skype Love," whose subtitle is "Are we drawn closer by being farther away?" (I've never read it.) The illustrator, of course, is of the same Shopsin clan whose storied&nbsp;grocery-turned-diner is a New York City institution. <a href="http://tamarashopsin.com/mumbaifaq/">Of her family and its business</a>:</div>
<blockquote>
We grew up wearing Salvation Army bargains and playing on the sidewalk. I have 3 brothers and a twin sister. We didn&#8217;t have bedtimes and could curse as much as we like. My parents are from the Bronx. [...]&nbsp;All my life we ran Shopsin&#8217;s&#8212;first as a corner store and then as a restaurant. It was and is the center of everything in my family. My parents loved what they did and where they did it. The customers were filtered by my dad&#8217;s temper and honesty, so it felt like a clubhouse and still does.</blockquote>
The <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Shopsins">Wiki page</a> on Shopsin's mentions this about its founding (and its founder, Tamara's father Kenny):<br />
<blockquote class="tr_bq">
Shopsin's first iteration was as a neighborhood grocery at the corner of Bedford and Morton streets in The Village. It, the stock and good will, but not the building, was purchased by Kenny Shopsin for $25,000 in 1973. He was undergoing intensive Freudian psychoanalysis at the time and lived in the neighborhood. His father put up the money. The previous owner had prepared and sold roast beef, a practice Shopsin continued.</blockquote>
