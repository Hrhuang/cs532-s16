But more often than not I see beards like Borchers (Go Portland Timbers!) roaming the streets, tossing this way and that in an effort to...escape. At least I'd try to escape if I were that kind of beard.&nbsp;</span></div>
<div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
<span style="color: #f3f3f3; margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;"><a href="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/-aIjPhLci2RQ/Vnx-N77A95I/AAAAAAAAAzA/nymbX2WhBAM/s1600/beards%2B4.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;"><img border="0" height="180" src="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/-aIjPhLci2RQ/Vnx-N77A95I/AAAAAAAAAzA/nymbX2WhBAM/s320/beards%2B4.jpg" width="320" /></a></span></div>

It makes everything better, even the terrifying swaths of bacterial sponges that these modern men grow on the lower half of their faces.&nbsp;</span></div>
<div style="box-sizing: border-box; font-family: proxima_nova, Helvetica, Arial, sans-serif; font-size: 15px; line-height: 23.25px; margin-bottom: 1em; margin-top: 1em;">
<span style="color: #f3f3f3;"><a href="http://www.esquire.com/style/news/a39982/glitter-beards/" target="_blank">Esquire magazine does not approve</a> of this #GlitterBeard trend. But I say, ignore the haters and glitter it up, fellas. <b>Why should women have all the adornment fun...?</b></span></div>

<div class='post-outer'>
<div class='post hentry uncustomized-post-template' itemprop='blogPost' itemscope='itemscope' itemtype='http://schema.org/BlogPosting'>

<h3 class='post-title entry-title' itemprop='name'>
<a href='http://mileinmine.blogspot.com/2014/08/womens-face-change-america.html'>Women's Face Change: America</a>
</h3>
<div class='post-header'>
<div class='post-header-line-1'></div>
</div>
<div class='post-body entry-content' id='post-body-5779384556031410972' itemprop='description articleBody'>
<br />
<span style="background-color: black;"><span style="color: #cccccc;">The other day, an awesome article entitled "Facts About the Changing Face of Women in America" by </span><a href="http://www.jamiedunham.com/"><span style="color: #b6d7a8;">Jamie Dunham</span></a><span style="color: #cccccc;"> popped up in my gmail via LinkedIn. Jamie is a brand strategist, cultural curator, and blogger who focuses on building cultural relevancy for brands, which in turn leads to stronger and more profitable consumer relationships. Her factual, kick ass article is below. Read it and be inspired, ladies! Then visit her blog </span><a href="http://www.lipstickeconomy.com/"><span style="color: #b6d7a8;">Lipstick Economy</span></a><span style="color: #cccccc;"> to keep up with the latest and greatest of this upward trend.</span></span><br />
<span style="background-color: black;"><span style="color: #cccccc;"><br /></span></span>
<span style="background-color: black;"><span style="color: #cccccc;">"Facts About the Changing Face of Women in America"</span><span style="color: #cccccc;"><br /></span><br /><span style="color: #cccccc;">You many instinctively know the facts about the changing face of women in society, but when you are exposed to the data, you will start to see the changes all around you.</span></span><br />
<div style="border: 0px; font-size: 15.199999809265137px; line-height: 20px; margin-bottom: 1em; margin-top: 1em; outline: 0px; padding: 0px; vertical-align: baseline;">
<img alt="" class="left" src="http://m.c.lnkd.licdn.com/mpr/mpr/p/6/005/079/0f6/3f6e06f.jpg" height="271" style="border: 0px; float: left; font-size: 15.199999809265137px; font-style: inherit; font-variant: inherit; line-height: inherit; margin: 0px 1em 1em 0px; max-width: 606px; outline: 0px; padding: 0px; vertical-align: baseline;" width="390" /><span style="background-color: black; color: #cccccc; font-family: Times, Times New Roman, serif;">Changes like more singles, more single Moms, Breadwinner Wives, and Grandmother Moms. Women are more independent and well-educated than ever before, and they are living their lives differently.</span></div>
<div style="border: 0px; font-size: 15.199999809265137px; line-height: 20px; margin-bottom: 1em; margin-top: 1em; outline: 0px; padding: 0px; vertical-align: baseline;">
<span style="background-color: black; color: #cccccc; font-family: Times, Times New Roman, serif;">In addition to recognizing that women control 85% of all consumer spending, here are my top ten facts to know before you market to women.</span></div>
<ol style="border: 0px; font-size: 15.199999809265137px; line-height: 20px; list-style-image: initial; list-style-position: initial; margin: 1em 0px; outline: 0px; padding: 0px 0px 0px 3em; vertical-align: baseline;">
<li style="border: 0px; font-size: 15.199999809265137px; font-style: inherit; font-variant: inherit; line-height: inherit; margin: 0px; outline: 0px; padding: 0px; vertical-align: baseline;"><span style="background-color: black; font-family: Times, Times New Roman, serif;"><strong style="border: 0px; color: #cccccc; font-size: 15.199999809265137px; font-style: inherit; font-variant: inherit; line-height: inherit; margin: 0px; outline: 0px; padding: 0px; vertical-align: baseline;">The Nuclear Family is Blown Up.</strong><span style="color: #cccccc;">&nbsp;A&nbsp;</span><a href="http://jamiedunham.wordpress.com/2014/04/30/marketing-demographics-nine-facts-about-the-new-face-of-america/" rel="nofollow" style="border: 0px; color: #cccccc; font-size: 15.199999809265137px; font-style: inherit; font-variant: inherit; line-height: inherit; margin: 0px; outline: 0px; padding: 0px; text-decoration: none; vertical-align: baseline;" target="_blank"><strong style="border: 0px; font-size: 15.199999809265137px; font-style: inherit; font-variant: inherit; line-height: inherit; margin: 0px; outline: 0px; padding: 0px; vertical-align: baseline;"><span style="color: #cccccc;">significant portion of women who are not marrying, marrying later, living single, living with a same-sex partner, divorced, or widowed.</span>&nbsp;</strong></a><span style="color: #cccccc;">And one-third of all women do not have children. Today&#8217;s woman wants a life of meaning and purpose on their own terms. Some&nbsp;</span><a href="http://jamiedunham.com/stats-on-women/" rel="nofollow" style="border: 0px; font-size: 15.199999809265137px; font-style: inherit; font-variant: inherit; line-height: inherit; margin: 0px; outline: 0px; padding: 0px; vertical-align: baseline;" target="_blank"><strong style="border: 0px; font-size: 15.199999809265137px; font-style: inherit; font-variant: inherit; line-height: inherit; margin: 0px; outline: 0px; padding: 0px; vertical-align: baseline;"><span style="color: #b6d7a8;">96% list &#8220;being independent&#8221;</span><span style="color: #cccccc; text-decoration: none;">&nbsp;</span></strong></a><span style="color: #cccccc;">as their single most important life goal. These women call themselves the single &#8220;Indies&#8221;, and they now surpass the number of married moms. Today only 20% of children live in households with a married stay-at-home mother with a working husband, compared to 41% of children in 1970.</span></span></li>
<li style="border: 0px; font-size: 15.199999809265137px; font-style: inherit; font-variant: inherit; line-height: inherit; margin: 0px; outline: 0px; padding: 0px; vertical-align: baseline;"><span style="background-color: black; color: #cccccc; font-family: Times, Times New Roman, serif;"><strong style="border: 0px; font-size: 15.199999809265137px; font-style: inherit; font-variant: inherit; line-height: inherit; margin: 0px; outline: 0px; padding: 0px; vertical-align: baseline;">Parenthood is More Important than Marriage.</strong>&nbsp;Pew Research shows that 41% of Millennials think marriage is obsolete. 51% of births to Millennial Moms are to single households. And 40% of all births are to single Moms. Definitely the stigma of out-of-wedlock births is fading as celebrities and non-celebrities have children with no consideration for marriage.</span></li>
<li style="border: 0px; font-size: 15.199999809265137px; font-style: inherit; font-variant: inherit; line-height: inherit; margin: 0px; outline: 0px; padding: 0px; vertical-align: baseline;"><span style="background-color: black; color: #cccccc; font-family: Times, Times New Roman, serif;"><strong style="border: 0px; font-size: 15.199999809265137px; font-style: inherit; font-variant: inherit; line-height: inherit; margin: 0px; outline: 0px; padding: 0px; vertical-align: baseline;">71% of Moms are Working Moms.</strong>&nbsp;Women are in equal number to men in the workforce today, and moms are no different. Most persons under 40 have never known a workplace without women. And this working mom has created whole industries for convenience and shopping. By the way, 84% of these moms are shopping online at work during the day.</span></li>
<li style="border: 0px; font-size: 15.199999809265137px; font-style: inherit; font-variant: inherit; line-height: inherit; margin: 0px; outline: 0px; padding: 0px; vertical-align: baseline;"><span style="background-color: black; font-family: Times, Times New Roman, serif;"><strong style="border: 0px; color: #cccccc; font-size: 15.199999809265137px; font-style: inherit; font-variant: inherit; line-height: inherit; margin: 0px; outline: 0px; padding: 0px; vertical-align: baseline;">Grandmothers are the Second Mother.</strong><span style="color: #cccccc;">&nbsp;With all these single and working moms, is it any surprise that one in ten children live with a grandparent and many are being cared for primarily by a grandparent.&nbsp;</span><strong style="border: 0px; font-size: 15.199999809265137px; font-style: inherit; font-variant: inherit; line-height: inherit; margin: 0px; outline: 0px; padding: 0px; vertical-align: baseline;"><a href="http://jamiedunham.wordpress.com/2011/10/24/marketing-to-moms-12-outrageous-facts-i-learned-at-m2moms/" rel="nofollow" style="border: 0px; font-size: 15.199999809265137px; font-style: inherit; font-variant: inherit; line-height: inherit; margin: 0px; outline: 0px; padding: 0px; vertical-align: baseline;" target="_blank"><span style="color: #b6d7a8;">Some 75% of Boomer grandparents</span></a><span style="color: #cccccc; text-decoration: none;">&nbsp;</span></strong><span style="color: #cccccc;">are involved in the raising of their grandchildren, and using that disposable income on Junior.</span></span></li>
<li style="border: 0px; font-size: 15.199999809265137px; font-style: inherit; font-variant: inherit; line-height: inherit; margin: 0px; outline: 0px; padding: 0px; vertical-align: baseline;"><span style="background-color: black; color: #cccccc; font-family: Times, Times New Roman, serif;"><strong style="border: 0px; font-size: 15.199999809265137px; font-style: inherit; font-variant: inherit; line-height: inherit; margin: 0px; outline: 0px; padding: 0px; vertical-align: baseline;">Close bonds between Millennial Women and their Moms</strong>. Okay, some of this bond may be due to &#8220;grandmothers as second moms&#8221; but there is a different type of bond between all millennial women and their mothers. The Millennial Woman rarely makes a large purchase or life decision without consulting her mom. This generation has grown up with a lot of parental support and still expect and respect it.</span></li>
<li style="border: 0px; font-size: 15.199999809265137px; font-style: inherit; font-variant: inherit; line-height: inherit; margin: 0px; outline: 0px; padding: 0px; vertical-align: baseline;"><span style="background-color: black; color: #cccccc; font-family: Times, Times New Roman, serif;"><strong style="border: 0px; font-size: 15.199999809265137px; font-style: inherit; font-variant: inherit; line-height: inherit; margin: 0px; outline: 0px; padding: 0px; vertical-align: baseline;">Young women are more educated than men.&nbsp;</strong>Thirty eight percent of women aged 25-32 have at least a four-year degree, compared with 31% of men. They are also beginning to be paid at near parity with men. Women&#8217;s earnings in this age group were 93% of their male counterparts in 2012. Another new change among married women was noted in 2012, 21% had spouses who were less educated than they were&#8212;a 3x increase from 1960.</span></li>
<li style="border: 0px; font-size: 15.199999809265137px; font-style: inherit; font-variant: inherit; line-height: inherit; margin: 0px; outline: 0px; padding: 0px; vertical-align: baseline;"><span style="background-color: black; font-family: Times, Times New Roman, serif;"><a href="http://jamiedunham.wordpress.com/2013/05/29/marketing-to-moms-four-in-ten-households-have-breadwinner-moms/" rel="nofollow" style="border: 0px; font-size: 15.199999809265137px; font-style: inherit; font-variant: inherit; line-height: inherit; margin: 0px; outline: 0px; padding: 0px; vertical-align: baseline;" target="_blank"><strong style="border: 0px; font-size: 15.199999809265137px; font-style: inherit; font-variant: inherit; line-height: inherit; margin: 0px; outline: 0px; padding: 0px; vertical-align: baseline;"><span style="color: #b6d7a8;">Breadwinner Moms</span><span style="color: #cccccc; text-decoration: none;">&nbsp;</span></strong></a><strong style="border: 0px; color: #cccccc; font-size: 15.199999809265137px; font-style: inherit; font-variant: inherit; line-height: inherit; margin: 0px; outline: 0px; padding: 0px; vertical-align: baseline;">are the highest wage earners in 40% of marriages.</strong><span style="color: #cccccc;">Today, four in ten households with children under 18 include mothers who are either the sole or primary source of income for the family. Some 50 years ago, that number was one in ten. The &#8220;breadwinner moms&#8221; are made up of two very different groups: 5.1 million (37%) are married mothers who have a higher income than their husbands, and 8.6 million (63%) are single mothers. This growth is tied to many trends including the fact that women make up some 47% of the today&#8217;s workforce.</span></span></li>
<li style="border: 0px; font-size: 15.199999809265137px; font-style: inherit; font-variant: inherit; line-height: inherit; margin: 0px; outline: 0px; padding: 0px; vertical-align: baseline;"><span style="background-color: black; color: #cccccc; font-family: Times, Times New Roman, serif;"><strong style="border: 0px; font-size: 15.199999809265137px; font-style: inherit; font-variant: inherit; line-height: inherit; margin: 0px; outline: 0px; padding: 0px; vertical-align: baseline;">Pets as family members.&nbsp;</strong>Fetch this fact: 81% of us think our pets are family. Millennials and Baby Boomers alike have replaced children with pets. Some 76% of pet parents bought their pets a holiday gift.</span></li>
<li style="border: 0px; font-size: 15.199999809265137px; font-style: inherit; font-variant: inherit; line-height: inherit; margin: 0px; outline: 0px; padding: 0px; vertical-align: baseline;"><span style="background-color: black; font-family: Times, Times New Roman, serif;"><strong style="border: 0px; color: #cccccc; font-size: 15.199999809265137px; font-style: inherit; font-variant: inherit; line-height: inherit; margin: 0px; outline: 0px; padding: 0px; vertical-align: baseline;">The Latina Woman rules.&nbsp;</strong><span style="color: #cccccc;">One in six of every American is Hispanic and they are power shoppers in many categories. Latina women are in the driver&#8217;s seat. An&nbsp;</span><a href="http://www.emarketer.com/Article/Hispanic-Shoppers-Bring-Social-Mobile-Habits-Grocery-Aisle/1009839" rel="nofollow" style="border: 0px; font-size: 15.199999809265137px; font-style: inherit; font-variant: inherit; line-height: inherit; margin: 0px; outline: 0px; padding: 0px; vertical-align: baseline;" target="_blank"><strong style="border: 0px; font-size: 15.199999809265137px; font-style: inherit; font-variant: inherit; line-height: inherit; margin: 0px; outline: 0px; padding: 0px; vertical-align: baseline;"><span style="color: #b6d7a8;">eMarketer study</span></strong></a><span style="color: #cccccc;">&nbsp;finds that Hispanics do more grocery shopping than the average US shopper and they spend 20% more during routine trips. Their use of mobile and smartphones while shopping is higher than any other ethnic group. They are more likely to compare product prices, call or text a family member about a product and look for a coupon.</span></span></li>
<li style="border: 0px; font-size: 15.199999809265137px; font-style: inherit; font-variant: inherit; line-height: inherit; margin: 0px; outline: 0px; padding: 0px; vertical-align: baseline;"><span style="background-color: black; color: #cccccc; font-family: Times, Times New Roman, serif;"><strong style="border: 0px; font-size: 15.199999809265137px; font-style: inherit; font-variant: inherit; line-height: inherit; margin: 0px; outline: 0px; padding: 0px; vertical-align: baseline;">91% of Women Don&#8217;t Think Marketers Understand Them.</strong>&nbsp;After reading facts 1-9, I think you understand why. Women don&#8217;t feel they are being accurately portrayed. Using the color pink is not marketing to women. Women respond to marketing in a more emotional level. Women place importance on personal and proactive customer communication. We want authenticity, relevance, honesty and an exchange of information. And we want to see ourselves portrayed as we really are. There are some great marketers who have captured this emotion &#8211; P&amp;G in their Mom Olympic spots, the interracial Cheerio family, the new Verizon spots about girls and engineering and the famous Dove spots. Some of us feel that advertising does not portray us well because only 3% of creative directors in agencies are women.</span></li>
</ol>

<h3 class='post-title entry-title' itemprop='name'>
<a href='http://mileinmine.blogspot.com/2014/04/its-all-about-wild-ones.html'>It's All About The Wild Ones</a>
</h3>
<div class='post-header'>
<div class='post-header-line-1'></div>
</div>
<div class='post-body entry-content' id='post-body-4799885763843827646' itemprop='description articleBody'>
<img src="https://ci4.googleusercontent.com/proxy/UTlKmO22ZUho7s1kSbLhzw93uM-r1qtKD6XEcAOZUqykyn4PS6_Ozrcp27glg9rC6Ya0sxdEwcX05xpvp3bruHJ6vJsrZp27BEkLKudxGKC59LsZRK-iegQa9JSxzjb7cC1l_kTbfFU3rugcgb0=s0-d-e1-ft#https://gallery.mailchimp.com/3052f4e66032a4a6a763c6a32/images/wildones_boston681467.jpg" /><br />
<br />
<span style="font-size: x-small;">photo by Gunnar MacCormick</span><br />
<br />
<u><i>Keep It Safe</i> re-released by <a href="http://tumblr.us4.list-manage1.com/track/click?u=3052f4e66032a4a6a763c6a32&amp;id=de2c428063&amp;e=db1cc28abe">Topshelf Records</a>!</u><br />
The Wild Ones debut album <i>Keep It Safe</i> is now officially available through Topshelf Records. You can download the deluxe version from iTunes <a href="http://tumblr.us4.list-manage.com/track/click?u=3052f4e66032a4a6a763c6a32&amp;id=5a6c1ce7c4&amp;e=db1cc28abe" target="_blank">HERE</a>. It features a b-side of "Yard" as well as the Trails and Ways' remix of "Rivals". Also, be sure to check out the new vinyl pressings!<br />
<br />
<img height="320" src="https://ci5.googleusercontent.com/proxy/oVuS30chHNvxC7WPF2bdx0FKFAIsiMwlvJ7sjmEpfVqidB1sacfrF3TMUlYoNg5ROw6bx1_ZoVkXF2Rku_6cQT3n0k_JQH-zMzuCk1Qw871-5WwX1vD1nxecGxH7qh5Wpw=s0-d-e1-ft#https://gallery.mailchimp.com/3052f4e66032a4a6a763c6a32/images/topshelf.jpg" width="320" /><br />
<br />
<u>"Paia" video</u><br />
Right before they left to tour, Wild Ones released a new music video for their song "Paia". Check it out!<br />
<div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
<object class="BLOGGER-youtube-video" classid="clsid:D27CDB6E-AE6D-11cf-96B8-444553540000" codebase="http://download.macromedia.com/pub/shockwave/cabs/flash/swflash.cab#version=6,0,40,0" data-thumbnail-src="https://ytimg.googleusercontent.com/vi/_2WnA4j7NTM/0.jpg" height="266" width="320"><param name="movie" value="https://youtube.googleapis.com/v/_2WnA4j7NTM&source=uds" /><param name="bgcolor" value="#FFFFFF" /><param name="allowFullScreen" value="true" /><embed width="320" height="266"  src="https://youtube.googleapis.com/v/_2WnA4j7NTM&source=uds" type="application/x-shockwave-flash" allowfullscreen="true"></embed></object></div>
<br />
<u>On tour with Typhoon now!</u><br />
After SXSW, Wild Ones joined up with their good buds Typhoon and have been on the road together these past few weeks. Some highlights so far include 930 Club in DC, Webster Hall in NYC, The Sinclair in Boston, and - of course - fresh lobster in Portland, Maine. They have a couple more weeks left on the road before returning to Portland, Oregon for their show at the Doug Fir Lounge on April 19th. All dates can be found <a href="http://tumblr.us4.list-manage.com/track/click?u=3052f4e66032a4a6a763c6a32&amp;id=258af2be73&amp;e=db1cc28abe">HERE!</a><br />
<div>
<img height="320" src="https://ci6.googleusercontent.com/proxy/7weLZHx_efJtYS4RWMfD8yYxOaK--hzo0KUosO9WGc_jhEyYuVtXsWj0-xFPjy4Kk_-5VScd0GU7j6-huxSVnbPSt_qZ71zvwIscIlhIeKkI2SG6208EpedQZp7Mn95a0ibWGKDseMk=s0-d-e1-ft#https://gallery.mailchimp.com/3052f4e66032a4a6a763c6a32/images/Portland_maine.jpeg" width="240" /> <br />
<br />
<u>Daytrotter session from SXSW</u><br />
SXSW was a whirlwind of good food, cheap beer, and little sleep&#8230;but Wild Ones got through 6 great shows AND found the time to record a Daytrotter session as well. It's now available <a href="http://tumblr.us4.list-manage.com/track/click?u=3052f4e66032a4a6a763c6a32&amp;id=4d8fac896b&amp;e=db1cc28abe">HERE</a> and features live studio versions of "Golden Twin" and "Paia" as well as new songs "Von Hell" and "Gleaming". If you're not a Daytrotter member, the 3 day free trial is very easy to sign up for and there are LOADS of other great content on their site.<br />
<br />
Stay Tuned. Visit Often. <br />

<h3 class='post-title entry-title' itemprop='name'>
<a href='http://mileinmine.blogspot.com/2014/02/the-art-of-fiction-no-161.html'>The Art Of Fiction No. 161</a>
</h3>
<div class='post-header'>
<div class='post-header-line-1'></div>
</div>
<div class='post-body entry-content' id='post-body-1372104612609020549' itemprop='description articleBody'>
<div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
<a href="http://1.bp.blogspot.com/-EIIV0D7qK6Q/Uv0HStlm53I/AAAAAAAAAiY/yFwqm1M88hk/s1600/TCBanana.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;"><img border="0" src="http://1.bp.blogspot.com/-EIIV0D7qK6Q/Uv0HStlm53I/AAAAAAAAAiY/yFwqm1M88hk/s1600/TCBanana.jpg" height="320" width="256" /></a></div>
<span style="background-color: black;"><span style="color: #cccccc; font-size: small;"><span style="font-family: georgia, verdana, sans-serif;"><span style="font-weight: normal;">Below is an excerpt from the&nbsp;</span></span><span style="font-family: georgia, verdana, sans-serif; font-weight: normal;">T. Coraghessan Boyle <a href="http://www.theparisreview.org/interviews/651/the-art-of-fiction-no-161-t-coraghessan-boyle" target="_blank">interview</a>&nbsp;</span><span style="font-family: georgia, verdana, sans-serif; font-weight: normal;">by Elizabeth E. Adams</span><span style="font-family: georgia, verdana, sans-serif; font-style: italic; font-weight: normal;"> </span><span style="font-family: georgia, verdana, sans-serif; font-weight: normal;">in the <i>Paris Review</i>:</span></span></span><br />
<span style="background-color: black;"><span style="color: #cccccc;"><span style="font-family: georgia, verdana, sans-serif; font-size: 14px; line-height: 21px; text-indent: 22px;"><br /></span>
<span style="font-family: georgia, verdana, sans-serif; font-size: 14px; line-height: 21px; text-indent: 22px;">"To me, a story is an exercise of the imagination, and most of my work comes out of that spirit of game-playing or puzzle-solving, as I said earlier. I was and still am very taken with the playful work of writers like Borges, Nabokov, Calvino. So the short answer is, very little is autobiographical. But because I try to keep myself open to all the possibilities, I exclude no form or mode. Some of my best-known stories have autobiographical elements&#8212;&#8220;Greasy Lake,&#8221; &#8220;If the River Was Whisky,&#8221; &#8220;Back in the Eocene&#8221;&#8212;but they are inventions in which the autobiographical elements have been radically transformed.&nbsp;</span></span></span><br />
<span style="background-color: black;"><span style="color: #cccccc;"><span style="font-family: georgia, verdana, sans-serif; font-size: 14px; line-height: 21px; text-indent: 22px;"><br /></span>
<span style="font-family: georgia, verdana, sans-serif; font-size: 14px; line-height: 21px; text-indent: 22px;">"Most writers are writers because they are introverts who have chosen a profession in which they don&#8217;t have to deal with anyone. I, on the other hand, don&#8217;t mind getting up onstage and doing everything I can to engage and entertain an audience, for which I&#8217;ve been misunderstood and even reviled in some quarters, as if literature is some sort of priesthood and by making people laugh and writhe I&#8217;m somehow an apostate. But to my mind literature is alive, a living, vital art form that needs to appeal to its audience&#8212;not pander to it, but appeal to it in the highest and lowest way."</span></span></span><br />
<span style="background-color: black;"><span style="color: #cccccc;"><span style="font-family: georgia, verdana, sans-serif; font-size: 14px; line-height: 21px; text-indent: 22px;"><br /></span>
<span style="font-family: georgia, verdana, sans-serif; font-size: 14px; line-height: 21px; text-indent: 22px;">Stay Tuned. Visit Often.</span></span></span>

<h2 class='title'>Pronunciation and Meaning</h2>
<div class='widget-content'>
For all you non-German speakers, Anreise is pronounced Un-rye-za. Or say it like Sunrise...minus the S...with a duplicate U at the end. Deena has no German ancestory, but decided years ago that having the word Anreise for her last name was simply too good to pass up. It can mean both Arrival (Anreise) and Departure/To Travel (An Reise), which made her think about life, about how each of us is always coming and going. Sounded just right. But, it was only recently that Deena came across the author <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/John_Gardner_%28American_writer%29">John Gardner</a>'s famous quote "There are only two plots in American fiction, either a stranger comes to town...or someone goes on a journey." Hmm...<br/><br/><br/><br/>
