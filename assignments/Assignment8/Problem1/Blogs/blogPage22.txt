<br />
Well hello again, everybody, and welcome to yet another installment of the most important running column on the internet: Flatbasset Radio's 25 Most Played Albums.<br />
<br />
Thank God it's April. No joke. I won't bore you with the details, but March of every year is far &amp; away my busiest month each year. I have a lot of work between my jobs and honestly, once I finish those my priorities are sleep, basset hound, food... and that's pretty much it. It's not just the blog/podcast that gets neglected, it's friends, family, personal appearance, nutrition, cleanliness... Pretty much everything.<br />
<br />
That's a roundabout way of apologizing for the lack of posts over the last month. However, with March behind me, it's time to get back to work. <h2 class='date-header'><span>Saturday, March 12, 2016</span></h2>

          <div class="date-posts">
        
<div class='post-outer'>
<div class='post hentry uncustomized-post-template' itemprop='blogPost' itemscope='itemscope' itemtype='http://schema.org/BlogPosting'>
<meta content='https://3.bp.blogspot.com/-fIORe6jmXTo/VuR1ukjFgSI/AAAAAAAACDU/FWgfeUTb22Q6hW-zITDfAyXqt9cAXD18Q/s320/The%2BSocial%2BDisaster%2B-%2BDark%2BSide%2BOf%2BThe%2BRoller%2BRink.jpg' itemprop='image_url'/>

<h3 class='post-title entry-title' itemprop='name'>
<a href='http://flatbasset.blogspot.com/2016/03/album-of-week-dark-side-of-roller-rink.html'>Album Of The Week: "Dark Side Of The Roller Rink" by The Social Disaster</a>
</h3>
<div class='post-header'>
<div class='post-header-line-1'></div>
</div>
<div class='post-body entry-content' id='post-body-819089888589578411' itemprop='description articleBody'>
<br />
Flatbasset Radio's Album Of The Week for the week of March 6th-12th, 2016:<br />
<br />
<div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
<a href="https://3.bp.blogspot.com/-fIORe6jmXTo/VuR1ukjFgSI/AAAAAAAACDU/FWgfeUTb22Q6hW-zITDfAyXqt9cAXD18Q/s1600/The%2BSocial%2BDisaster%2B-%2BDark%2BSide%2BOf%2BThe%2BRoller%2BRink.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;"><img border="0" height="320" src="https://3.bp.blogspot.com/-fIORe6jmXTo/VuR1ukjFgSI/AAAAAAAACDU/FWgfeUTb22Q6hW-zITDfAyXqt9cAXD18Q/s320/The%2BSocial%2BDisaster%2B-%2BDark%2BSide%2BOf%2BThe%2BRoller%2BRink.jpg" width="320" /></a></div>
<br />
<b>The Social Disaster - Dark Side Of The Roller Rink</b><br />
<br />
01. Queen Of Agency<br />
02. Machinery<br />
03. Gold<br />
04. Little Girl<br />
05. Bad Boys With Big Hearts<br />
06. God<br />
07. Want It All<br />
08. Battleground<br />
09. Quiet Savior<br />
10. Voyeur<br />
<br />
<table align="center" cellpadding="0" cellspacing="0" class="tr-caption-container" style="margin-left: auto; margin-right: auto; text-align: center;"><tbody>
<tr><td style="text-align: center;"><a href="https://2.bp.blogspot.com/-UqG5Ls6xnks/VuS1PvKQgBI/AAAAAAAACDk/GR44XJsbDOMveVlvJu6fzc0Q4wlxBfkiw/s1600/The%2BSocial%2BDisaster%2B-%2BProfile.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: auto; margin-right: auto;"><img border="0" height="266" src="https://2.bp.blogspot.com/-UqG5Ls6xnks/VuS1PvKQgBI/AAAAAAAACDk/GR44XJsbDOMveVlvJu6fzc0Q4wlxBfkiw/s400/The%2BSocial%2BDisaster%2B-%2BProfile.jpg" width="400" /></a></td></tr>
<tr><td class="tr-caption" style="text-align: center;">(The Social Disaster by Laramie Carlson)</td></tr>
</tbody></table>
<br />
Straight out of sunny Duluth we have <i>Dark Side Of The Roller Rink,</i> the debut LP from self-described "proto-pop, gloom pop, dance &amp; doo-wop" quintet The Social Disaster. While that may seem like a curious description for a band, it's appropriate. For better or worse, on <i>DSOTRR</i>, the band manages to cover those bases and and more.<br />
<br />
<iframe seamless="" src="https://bandcamp.com/EmbeddedPlayer/album=2391996892/size=large/bgcol=ffffff/linkcol=0687f5/tracklist=false/artwork=small/track=1774722725/transparent=true/" style="border: 0; height: 120px; width: 100%;"><a href="http://thesocialdisaster.bandcamp.com/album/dark-side-of-the-roller-rink">Dark Side of the Roller Rink by The Social Disaster</a></iframe>

<br />
<br />
The Social Disaster wisely aims to make their case on the opening track "Queen Of Agency." The band locks into a a decidedly indie-pop swing (complete with a bed of synthesizers) as singer Rachel Phoenix steps front and center to deliver no-less-than three different hooks. It's one of the real highlights on the album, a moment when everything the band is trying to accomplish comes together in the same song.<br />
<br />
The Social Disaster spends the next three tracks leaning toward the "dark side" aspect of the album title rather than the "roller rink" side. Flashing a harder-edged sound on songs "Machinery," "God," &amp; "Little Girl" Phoenix questions religion, sexism, technology, &amp; a woman's role in the world. Perhaps the band's crunch is meant to mirror the gravity of the subject matter, but unfortunately it causes these three songs to lump together, doing an injustice to all three.<br />
<br />
<iframe seamless="" src="https://bandcamp.com/EmbeddedPlayer/album=2391996892/size=large/bgcol=ffffff/linkcol=0687f5/tracklist=false/artwork=small/track=1295914592/transparent=true/" style="border: 0; height: 120px; width: 100%;"><a href="http://thesocialdisaster.bandcamp.com/album/dark-side-of-the-roller-rink">Dark Side of the Roller Rink by The Social Disaster</a></iframe>

<br />
<br />
High-water mark "Bad Boys With Big Hearts" functions as a mood-lightening reprieve at the end of the album's first half. The band steps back from the <i>rawk</i> to indulge in some straight-up doo-wop-pop, with Phoenix playing the role of pin-up charmer, lamenting her inability to avoid the same bad-boy mistakes that are just too much fun to repeat.<br />
<br />
<iframe seamless="" src="https://bandcamp.com/EmbeddedPlayer/album=2391996892/size=large/bgcol=ffffff/linkcol=0687f5/tracklist=false/artwork=small/track=952530954/transparent=true/" style="border: 0; height: 120px; width: 100%;"><a href="http://thesocialdisaster.bandcamp.com/album/dark-side-of-the-roller-rink">Dark Side of the Roller Rink by The Social Disaster</a></iframe>

<br />
<br />
The album's second half finds The Social Disaster moving away from the charm of the first half and doubling down on a sort-of post-grunge crunch. "God" is Phoenix's anthem to the modern woman, declaring "I am my own God!" as the band rumbles behind her. They continue in this direction for duration of <i>DSOTRR</i>'s second half. Unfortunately, as Phoenix forces the issue lyrically and the band works up the anger to match, the album loses the playful energy that makes the first half of the album a real joy.<br />
<br />
I hope that doesn't sound too harsh. I did enjoy <i>Dark Side Of The Roller Rink</i>. Like a lot of debut albums, though, it's a bit scattered stylistically and bit on-the-nose lyrically. The band seems to be trying to be everything all the time, which isn't tremendously surprising. It's completely understandable for a band to try to say <i>everything</i> on their first album. However, given how the band sounds when they loosen up, it's clear that they have the ability to be the kind of band that has a little something for everyone. I have very high hopes for The Social Disaster going forward. They're very close to striking the balance between their playful &amp; serious side on this album. Going forward I certainly hope they find that balance, as the two sides of this band compliment each other tremendously.<br />
<br />
If you'd like to check out <i>Dark Side Of The Roller Rink</i> for yourself, head over to <a href="https://thesocialdisaster.bandcamp.com/album/dark-side-of-the-roller-rink">The Social Disaster's Bandcamp page</a> and give it a listen.<br />

<br />
Well hello again, everybody. Welcome to another month's worth of instant nostalgia!<br />
<br />
Even with a bonus day February flew by. I'm sorry that the posts haven't been too routine. I was very busy and very tired, but I'm trying my best here. Of course, big thank you to everybody who did make the time to check out the couple of Flatbasset Radio episodes I put together this month as well as that P.O.S. MinneClassics show.<br />
<br />
The flipside of being so busy means that I likely spent a lot of time on public transit, which means I was able to listen to a ton of music. As you can see below, I covered a lot of ground.<br />
<br />
As always, please remember that one song = one play. If I listen to, say, Springsteen's <i>The River</i> once all the way through, it counts as 20 plays. These stats are all pulled from my <a href="http://www.last.fm/user/TCDroogsma">Last.fm page</a>, which you should definitely follow if you're certain you've checked out absolutely everything else on the internet.<br />
<br />
So, without further ado, here's Flatbasset Radio's 25 Most Played Albums Of February, 2016:<br />

*That blank spot you see is for an album called <i>Lollygag</i> by a local artist called Collage. Does anybody know anything about this band or album? I bought it on a whim over at Cheapo. There does not appear to be any information about it whatsoever on the internet. All I can gather from the album is that it<i> </i>was released in 1994 on Gypsy Gyrl records. It seems to be the project/brainchild of a woman named Terry Blessing. That's actually quite a bit of info, but if anybody knows anymore about it or maybe saw this band back in the 90's, please hit me up in the comment section or over on the <a href="http://www.facebook.com/flatbassetpodcast">Flatbasset Facebook page</a>. <br />

<a href="https://1.bp.blogspot.com/-jx9pdPoPHwY/VtS4UOLJiJI/AAAAAAAACCw/w19rjtHuR3Q/s1600/Glasset%2BHound.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;"><img border="0" src="https://1.bp.blogspot.com/-jx9pdPoPHwY/VtS4UOLJiJI/AAAAAAAACCw/w19rjtHuR3Q/s1600/Glasset%2BHound.jpg" /></a></div>
<br />
Well hello again, podcast fans! Welcome to Flatbasset Radio: Episode #74!<br />
<br />
Happy Leap Day, everybody! In honor of this rare, yet scheduled phenomena I figured I should get my shit together and make the most of this bonus day by sitting on my ass recording a new show. Mission: Accomplished.<br />
<br />
In this week's episode I celebrate some anniversaries, play some new local music, check in on some old Bay Area friends, lament the lack of respect for Owatonna's greatest songwriter, the sadness running through a classic beat album, revisit a single that was supposed to break one of America's great punk bands, struggle with my accents, fawn over my favorite rapper, and get unreasonably excited about my first trip to see The Boss live! All that plus the Cover Of The Week &amp; Flatbasset Flatclassic!<br />
<br />
As always, Flatbasset Radio is completely free. I've finally set up a decent downloading site, so if you'd like to download Flatbasset Radio: Episode #74 (with all the appropriate iTunes tagging), <a href="https://archive.org/compress/Episode74_201602">just click here</a>.<br />

<h3 class='post-title entry-title' itemprop='name'>
<a href='http://flatbasset.blogspot.com/2016/02/minneclassics-4-de-la-souls-by-pos.html'>MinneClassics #4: "De La Souls" by P.O.S.</a>
</h3>
<div class='post-header'>
<div class='post-header-line-1'></div>
</div>
<div class='post-body entry-content' id='post-body-6654647983676204202' itemprop='description articleBody'>
<br />
<i>Found some things I could relate with, I wore out the tape...</i><br />
<br />
<table align="center" cellpadding="0" cellspacing="0" class="tr-caption-container" style="margin-left: auto; margin-right: auto; text-align: center;"><tbody>
<tr><td style="text-align: center;"><a href="https://3.bp.blogspot.com/-TUg8orjmfLg/VrUBkkf-7RI/AAAAAAAACAQ/ky0SSp2CInw/s1600/P.O.S.%2B-%2B2006.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: auto; margin-right: auto;"><img border="0" height="320" src="https://3.bp.blogspot.com/-TUg8orjmfLg/VrUBkkf-7RI/AAAAAAAACAQ/ky0SSp2CInw/s320/P.O.S.%2B-%2B2006.jpg" width="313" /></a></td></tr>
<tr><td class="tr-caption" style="text-align: center;">(P.O.S. by Dan Monick)</td></tr>
</tbody></table>
Well hello again, podcast fans! Welcome to the fourth installment of my short-running MinneClassics series!<br />
<i> </i><br />
Folks, last week marked the 10 year anniversary of the release of P.O.S.'s second full-length album <i>Audition</i>. To mark the occasion I thought I'd devote a few minutes to discussing one of my favorite tracks from this classic album.<br />
<br />
<div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
<a href="https://3.bp.blogspot.com/-HdXcrb88SbQ/VrUBvlxEbaI/AAAAAAAACAU/nFRAxPLl5WI/s1600/P.O.S.%2B-%2BAudition.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="clear: left; float: left; margin-bottom: 1em; margin-right: 1em;"><img border="0" height="200" src="https://3.bp.blogspot.com/-HdXcrb88SbQ/VrUBvlxEbaI/AAAAAAAACAU/nFRAxPLl5WI/s200/P.O.S.%2B-%2BAudition.jpg" width="200" /></a></div>
&nbsp;Like a lot of people, <i>Audition</i> was just that, a first look at the rapper P.O.S. and the Doomtree collective. It was an introduction to a world that had equal love for both hardcore punk and underground hip-hop and decided that there was no point separating the two. The album is full of highlights, from the aggression of opener "Half-Cocked Concepts," the anger of "The Kill In Me," the straight-banging "Stand Up (Let's Get Murdered)," to spot-on guest spots from Slug, Craig Finn, Greg Attonito &amp; Mike Mictlan, the album more than meets the goal of opening the door to world of Doomtree (a door that, 10 years on, was quickly flooded with like-minded individuals).<br />
<br />
However, looking back, "De La Souls" is still the moment when it all comes together. Over a simple beat and autobiographical rhymes, P.O.S. invites us all in by opening up and telling his story. In doing so, he's asking that we respond by telling our own stories. He's inviting everybody to join in not by becoming an anonymous face in a larger movement, but by reaffirming that a group of like-minded individuals is still a group of individuals, with their own hopes, dreams, goals, &amp; disappointments.&nbsp; He's knocking down whatever was left of the wall between performer and audience and extending a hand.<br />
<br />
Anyway, give the show a listen to hear what I'm talking about. As always, Flatbasset Radio &amp; these MinneClassics are free. Just click the Mixcloud player below to give it a listen.<br />
