<h3 class='post-title entry-title' itemprop='name'>
<a href='http://thischarmingyan.blogspot.com/2016/04/you-should-always-keep-hand-me-downs.html'>You Should Always Keep Hand-Me-Downs</a>
</h3>
<div class='post-header'>
<div class='post-header-line-1'></div>
</div>
<div class='post-body entry-content' id='post-body-3261743818600536483' itemprop='description articleBody'>
<div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: left;">
<span style="font-family: &quot;trebuchet ms&quot; , sans-serif;">When you're young and obsessed with contemporary culture, you're oblivious to the plethora of gems awaiting your inheritance. But in the long run, it's in your best interest to store and protect your family relics because you never know, but they might actually accumulate worth and be lusted over by modern society; we see this again and again in what is considered to be 'hipster' fashion and pop culture trends, such as 70's flares and the vinyl revival. So it's not just about sentimental value, it's also about being ahead of the curve. Here are some of my personal hand-me-downs that I'm never letting go of.&nbsp;</span></div>
<div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: left;">
<span style="font-family: &quot;trebuchet ms&quot; , sans-serif;"><br /></span></div>
<div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: left;">
<span style="font-family: &quot;trebuchet ms&quot; , sans-serif;"><b>CLOTHES</b></span></div>
<div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: left;">
<span style="font-family: &quot;trebuchet ms&quot; , sans-serif;">Mum worked in the pub industry for years and managed to nab a limited edition Budweiser jersey, of which only 5000 were ever manufactured. Granted, I only wear it to bed, but at least I have lounge wear to boast about.</span></div>
<div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
<a href="https://2.bp.blogspot.com/-AHXKUE_9TfE/VwK9TU8HFfI/AAAAAAAAHwo/r73nA0U3R2gCwkE4N0GELhY0uTEGesYtA/s1600/FullSizeRender%2B%25283%2529.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;"><img border="0" height="320" src="https://2.bp.blogspot.com/-AHXKUE_9TfE/VwK9TU8HFfI/AAAAAAAAHwo/r73nA0U3R2gCwkE4N0GELhY0uTEGesYtA/s320/FullSizeRender%2B%25283%2529.jpg" width="320" /></a><a href="https://2.bp.blogspot.com/-NKjbr935b2c/VwK9TutqsyI/AAAAAAAAHws/Vlzr0OlXa24J12VwqegGBr4dKLOuNszgw/s1600/FullSizeRender%2B%25284%2529.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;"><img border="0" height="320" src="https://2.bp.blogspot.com/-NKjbr935b2c/VwK9TutqsyI/AAAAAAAAHws/Vlzr0OlXa24J12VwqegGBr4dKLOuNszgw/s320/FullSizeRender%2B%25284%2529.jpg" width="320" /></a></div>
<span style="font-family: &quot;trebuchet ms&quot; , sans-serif;">Some boyfriend fit Levi's 501s, also passed down from mum. They're sturdy, they're comfy, they're rather steep to buy new or vintage, they cup my ass perfectly and they'll never go out of fashion.</span><br />
<div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
<span style="font-family: &quot;trebuchet ms&quot; , sans-serif;"><a href="https://4.bp.blogspot.com/-04Z4mcDhmiE/VwVfRNa_PWI/AAAAAAAAHyA/A1WruyikfFM1NyqmYOfGapyGHu0kb6TVg/s1600/FullSizeRender%2B%25284%2529.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;"><img border="0" height="320" src="https://4.bp.blogspot.com/-04Z4mcDhmiE/VwVfRNa_PWI/AAAAAAAAHyA/A1WruyikfFM1NyqmYOfGapyGHu0kb6TVg/s320/FullSizeRender%2B%25284%2529.jpg" width="320" /></a><a href="https://2.bp.blogspot.com/-XvhfC0YNgHM/VwVfQ_oOFSI/AAAAAAAAHx8/po_abYaRMT0O6abwq1HnJLQlqz6vgICHg/s1600/FullSizeRender%2B%25283%2529.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;"><img border="0" height="320" src="https://2.bp.blogspot.com/-XvhfC0YNgHM/VwVfQ_oOFSI/AAAAAAAAHx8/po_abYaRMT0O6abwq1HnJLQlqz6vgICHg/s320/FullSizeRender%2B%25283%2529.jpg" width="320" /></a></span></div>
<br />
<span style="font-family: &quot;trebuchet ms&quot; , sans-serif;"><b>BOOKS</b></span><br />
<span style="font-family: &quot;trebuchet ms&quot; , sans-serif;"><i>The Complete Works of William Shakespeare</i>, a Christmas gift from my grandma to my grandpa in 1957. Inner message reads: "To Brian, with all my love for Christmas and always. Love, Jean." A true money-can't-buy family treasure.</span><br />
<div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
<a href="https://3.bp.blogspot.com/-rAJJUdxmlKU/VwK9UDRTK3I/AAAAAAAAHw0/dKOOir0CycwoFCqOwi6U9ZVkw9mI0XEmA/s1600/FullSizeRender%2B%25285%2529.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;"><img border="0" height="320" src="https://3.bp.blogspot.com/-rAJJUdxmlKU/VwK9UDRTK3I/AAAAAAAAHw0/dKOOir0CycwoFCqOwi6U9ZVkw9mI0XEmA/s320/FullSizeRender%2B%25285%2529.jpg" width="320" /></a><a href="https://2.bp.blogspot.com/-sOtuMGIh-5o/VwK9URhzHeI/AAAAAAAAHw8/KauQyxNSHyMDDKNG5dEOqf5NY7Mqr3c3Q/s1600/FullSizeRender%2B%25286%2529.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;"><img border="0" height="320" src="https://2.bp.blogspot.com/-sOtuMGIh-5o/VwK9URhzHeI/AAAAAAAAHw8/KauQyxNSHyMDDKNG5dEOqf5NY7Mqr3c3Q/s320/FullSizeRender%2B%25286%2529.jpg" width="320" /></a></div>
<div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: left;">
<span style="font-family: &quot;trebuchet ms&quot; , sans-serif;">Left; 1921 edition of Rudyard Kipling's classic novel, <i>The Jungle Book</i>. Right; First edition Reader's Digest featuring Ian Fleming's <i>The Man With the Golden Gun</i>. Our family has shelf upon shelf of these.</span></div>
<div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
<a href="https://1.bp.blogspot.com/-ZfnfuUUcGcM/VwK9Ua17IAI/AAAAAAAAHw4/bdEz9wNwD28VvGJqXq3-tdplEWzzH6Ajg/s1600/FullSizeRender%2B%25287%2529.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;"><img border="0" height="320" src="https://1.bp.blogspot.com/-ZfnfuUUcGcM/VwK9Ua17IAI/AAAAAAAAHw4/bdEz9wNwD28VvGJqXq3-tdplEWzzH6Ajg/s320/FullSizeRender%2B%25287%2529.jpg" width="320" /></a><a href="https://4.bp.blogspot.com/-SGbm6dxY1oE/VwK9U9T0aPI/AAAAAAAAHxA/9kh3i0lM_8EOcd2UiMUBzs_tjZPNgOfMg/s1600/FullSizeRender%2B%25289%2529.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;"><img border="0" height="319" src="https://4.bp.blogspot.com/-SGbm6dxY1oE/VwK9U9T0aPI/AAAAAAAAHxA/9kh3i0lM_8EOcd2UiMUBzs_tjZPNgOfMg/s320/FullSizeRender%2B%25289%2529.jpg" width="320" /></a></div>
<span style="font-family: &quot;trebuchet ms&quot; , sans-serif;"><b><br /></b></span>
<span style="font-family: &quot;trebuchet ms&quot; , sans-serif;"><b>TECHNOLOGY</b></span><br />
<span style="font-family: &quot;trebuchet ms&quot; , sans-serif;">Courtesy of my cool cat grandpa. Left; Original Sun600 Polaroid camera. Right; Olympus OM10, also with extra macro lens and flash. <a href="https://4.bp.blogspot.com/-4zLBRgYcJ1A/VwK9TfH5jJI/AAAAAAAAHwk/VNvJSNtyDLQqTjYcsAJTB66IxdNPkSD2w/s1600/FullSizeRender%2B%252810%2529.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;"><img border="0" height="320" src="https://4.bp.blogspot.com/-4zLBRgYcJ1A/VwK9TfH5jJI/AAAAAAAAHwk/VNvJSNtyDLQqTjYcsAJTB66IxdNPkSD2w/s320/FullSizeRender%2B%252810%2529.jpg" width="320" /></a><a href="https://4.bp.blogspot.com/-GEhm9BtqyDc/VwK9TRb5UcI/AAAAAAAAHww/nGa9oOF9WAQFpSgXogPe3poEY_QQ4ovJA/s1600/FullSizeRender%2B%252811%2529.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;"><img border="0" height="320" src="https://4.bp.blogspot.com/-GEhm9BtqyDc/VwK9TRb5UcI/AAAAAAAAHww/nGa9oOF9WAQFpSgXogPe3poEY_QQ4ovJA/s320/FullSizeRender%2B%252811%2529.jpg" width="320" /></a></div>
<span style="font-family: &quot;trebuchet ms&quot; , sans-serif;"><b><br /></b></span>
<span style="font-family: &quot;trebuchet ms&quot; , sans-serif;"><b>RECORDS</b></span><br />
<span style="font-family: &quot;trebuchet ms&quot; , sans-serif;">Left; Probably Fleetwood Mac's most famous and influential album, <i>Rumours</i>, typically sold nowadays for around &#163;45 - ta, dad. Right; Stevie Wonder's <i>Songs in the Key of Life </i>and <i>Innervisions.</i></span><br />
<div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
<a href="https://3.bp.blogspot.com/-YB7spsHMIkM/VwK9UwUJKYI/AAAAAAAAHxQ/Fqjge_3QaEk3LOUt5wQkmMa5YSZHXOMWQ/s1600/FullSizeRender%2B%25288%2529.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;"><img border="0" height="320" src="https://3.bp.blogspot.com/-YB7spsHMIkM/VwK9UwUJKYI/AAAAAAAAHxQ/Fqjge_3QaEk3LOUt5wQkmMa5YSZHXOMWQ/s320/FullSizeRender%2B%25288%2529.jpg" width="320" /></a><a href="https://2.bp.blogspot.com/-ZV0qk6i-Dy4/VwTdAUEnNdI/AAAAAAAAHxk/llnuH713KJMpcRpKrzlI24i1TIgWnaNFg/s1600/FullSizeRender.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;"><img border="0" height="320" src="https://2.bp.blogspot.com/-ZV0qk6i-Dy4/VwTdAUEnNdI/AAAAAAAAHxk/llnuH713KJMpcRpKrzlI24i1TIgWnaNFg/s320/FullSizeRender.jpg" width="320" /></a></div>

<ol>
<li><span style="font-family: &quot;trebuchet ms&quot; , sans-serif;">The colour coordinated array of toiletries, which is amongst the most satisfying features in my life right now. Alas, the display is mainly hand soap and lotion, but nevertheless, it's an aesthetic I appreciate.</span></li>
<li><span style="font-family: &quot;trebuchet ms&quot; , sans-serif;">Home accessories are also organised by colour, a somewhat sanative experience thanks to the gentle gradient of the glass vases and candles on display. </span></li>

Luckily, I stumbled across this Valentino cutie (below left) for &#163;29.99 reduced from &#163;100 (!!!!!!!!!!!) and this Karl Largerfeld holdall (below right) for &#163;99.99 down from &#163;199.99.<div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">

Expect to find Calvin Klein underwear sets for almost half the RRP, bed sheets by designers such as DKNY, discounted luxury kitchen and bathroom essentials and every travel 'necessity' you could possibly think of.</span></div>

<h2 class='date-header'><span>Saturday, 2 April 2016</span></h2>

          <div class="date-posts">
        
<div class='post-outer'>
<div class='post hentry' itemprop='blogPost' itemscope='itemscope' itemtype='http://schema.org/BlogPosting'>
<meta content='https://4.bp.blogspot.com/-jGQ1CCYbLrI/VwAqDelXFvI/AAAAAAAAHvQ/W8NqWfoQ2YMnsNDoFl7zuot_O6_8bs0bw/s320/batman-vs-superman-info-tag.jpg' itemprop='image_url'/>

<h3 class='post-title entry-title' itemprop='name'>
<a href='http://thischarmingyan.blogspot.com/2016/04/batman-vs-superman-dawn-of-justice.html'>Batman vs. Superman: Dawn of Justice</a>
</h3>
<div class='post-header'>
<div class='post-header-line-1'></div>
</div>
<div class='post-body entry-content' id='post-body-8576097658721972772' itemprop='description articleBody'>
<div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
<a href="https://4.bp.blogspot.com/-jGQ1CCYbLrI/VwAqDelXFvI/AAAAAAAAHvQ/W8NqWfoQ2YMnsNDoFl7zuot_O6_8bs0bw/s1600/batman-vs-superman-info-tag.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;"><img border="0" height="213" src="https://4.bp.blogspot.com/-jGQ1CCYbLrI/VwAqDelXFvI/AAAAAAAAHvQ/W8NqWfoQ2YMnsNDoFl7zuot_O6_8bs0bw/s320/batman-vs-superman-info-tag.jpg" width="320" /></a></div>
<div style="text-align: center;">
<span style="font-family: &quot;trebuchet ms&quot; , sans-serif; font-size: x-small;">Source: screencrush.com</span></div>
<div>
<br /></div>
<span style="font-family: &quot;trebuchet ms&quot; , sans-serif;">For some time, Marvel films have been ruling the roost, so when the first trailer for the live action adaptation of Frank Miller&#8217;s comic 'The Dark Knight Returns' was released, it seemed auspiciously gritty and thunderous. Then the second trailer came and things went south pretty fast. <i>Batman v Superman: Dawn of Justice</i> had to be staggeringly exceptional in order to live up to the high standard set by Christopher Nolan&#8217;s Dark Knight trilogy, because his solemn and hyper-realist spin paved the way for the latest additions to the superhero portfolio such as Netflix&#8217;s <i>Daredevil</i> and <i>Jessica Jones</i>. And despite the criticism, <i>Man of Steel</i> wasn&#8217;t terrible. The title characters are both iconic in their own right, and it was nigh on impossible to anticipate this film without a basic understanding of their profiles as outlined in the comic books and previous blockbusters. They are heroes that have transcended generations, and so this film was always going to be a big deal, regardless of director and casting choice.<br /><br />Firstly, the opening scene of a young Bruce Wayne and his parents is an overload of every death-related cliche possible for a 12A film: the reflective voice over, the slow motion macro shot of the bullet leaving the chamber, the silent scream blocked out by angelic music, the flagrant lack of wounds on the victims and blood at the scene of the crime. It&#8217;s grating, but it&#8217;s an accurate snapshot into the quality of the remainder of the film - poor.<br /><br />The first hour is essentially pointless. It provides context that either turns out to be completely irrelevant or could be trimmed and summarised more concisely in a ten minute sequence, and rehashes scenes from the climax of <i>Man of Steel</i> as a flashback then haphazardly references them throughout the rest of the film without marrying the two storylines together smoothly. The first time you see the Batmobile, it&#8217;s supposed to be a &#8220;woah!&#8221; moment because it combines the ingenious technology of a vigilante Inspector Gadget with a suave brutality. But when the Batmobile is revealed during a chase after Lex Luther&#8217;s cargo, it&#8217;s an ostentatious display of CGI that feels like an extremely long and tawdry commercial selling a racing video game. <br /><br />For a two and a half hour action film to be justified, especially in the superhero genre, it has to be filled with action. The moment Batman and Superman come head to head and finally have their pivotal battle is probably the most disappointing and lacklustre fistfight you&#8217;ll ever see on screen. Thereafter, the narrative of their squabble comes to an abrupt end (even though this should be the central theme of the film, as the title would suggest) and attention turns to Lex Luther&#8217;s Kryptonite-fuelled monster, which bares its chest and roars like a slimey Godzilla.</span><br />
<div>
<span style="font-family: &quot;trebuchet ms&quot; , sans-serif;"><br /></span>
<br />
<div>
<span style="font-family: &quot;trebuchet ms&quot; , sans-serif;">The perpetually controversial casting of Ben Affleck proves to be a blunder. Maybe it was the immense pressure weighing down on him, a) because he had big shoes to fill and b) because of that unforgettable <i>Daredevil</i> travesty, but he plays Batman with a frigidness best described as homage to Terminator. That is not a compliment. Affleck succeeds, to a certain extent, at reflecting the inherent rage Bruce Wayne simultaneously struggles with and thrives on. But Affleck is no Batman, no matter how many hours he racks up in the gym. And poor Jeremy Irons was set up to fail, because the Alfred character is now synonymous with his predecessor, Michael Caine, so his performance was ill-fated from the start.<br /><br />But one of the most upsetting flaws, of which there are many, is that Batman uses guns.  A defining trait of Batman&#8217;s moral conscience is that he is fundamentally against the use of guns. Though Batman did use firearms in the early days of Detective Comics, he has not been shown with one since 1939, when his origin story of his parent&#8217;s death was introduced. Fellow sceptics have been quick to point out that Batman is not explicitly against killing people, and has in fact killed in every live-action film adaptation, but in <i>Batman v Superman</i> he seems unperturbed about killing, keen almost, especially in regards to Superman. This may have been a deliberate decision to ameliorate Superman&#8217;s status as the ultimate good guy, but nevertheless, it dishonours and besmirches Batman&#8217;s persona.<br /><br />The only remaining factors that can salvage this wreckage is Hans Zimmer casting his magic with the music (he's since wiped his hands of it and officially retired from superhero films after this) and the subtle hints of Christopher Nolan&#8217;s touch as executive producer. Jesse Eisenberg was the perfect choice to play Lex Luther, and it&#8217;s not his fault he&#8217;s a talented actor cast into a promising role in a film ruined by a post-production massacre and tired script.</span><br />
<span style="font-family: &quot;trebuchet ms&quot; , sans-serif;"><br /></span></div>
<div>
<span style="font-family: &quot;trebuchet ms&quot; , sans-serif;">So there we have it. After a long wait, now we know that the credibility of the Batman franchise was resuscitated by Christopher Nolan, only to be tortured again by Zack Snyder.</span></div>
</div>
