<div dir="ltr" style="text-align: left;" trbidi="on">
<br />
<span style="font-family: Verdana, sans-serif;"><br /></span>
<span style="font-family: Verdana, sans-serif;"><b><span style="background-color: black; color: purple;"><br /></span></b>
<b><span style="background-color: black; color: purple;">AND ALSO THE TREES</span></b></span><br />
<b><span style="background-color: black; color: purple; font-family: Verdana, sans-serif;"><img alt="And Also the Trees" src="http://exystence.net/wp-content/uploads/2016/04/And-Also-the-Trees-300x300.jpg" height="320" width="320" /></span></b><br />
<b><span style="background-color: black; color: purple; font-family: Verdana, sans-serif;">BORN INTO THE WAVES - 2016</span></b><br />
<span style="font-family: Verdana, sans-serif;"><b><span style="background-color: black; color: purple;"><br /></span></b>
<b><span style="background-color: black; color: purple;"><br /></span></b>
<b><span style="background-color: black; color: purple;"><br /></span></b>
<b><span style="background-color: black; color: purple;"><br /></span></b>
</span><br />
<div style="margin-bottom: 1.967em; padding: 0px;">
<b><span style="background-color: black; color: purple; font-family: Verdana, sans-serif;"><br /><span style="line-height: 12.16px;">O</span><span style="line-height: 24.375px;">riginally from the small Worcestershire village of Inkberrow, And Also The Trees was formed in 1979 by two sets of brothers, Simon Huw (vocals) and Justin Jones (guitar) and Nick (drums) and Graham Havas (bass), although the latter had been replaced by Steve Burrows by the time they released their debut single, produced by Lol Tolhurst of The Cure, in 1983. The line up remained unchanged until 1997 when Paul Hill took over from Havas on drums while Burrows left in 2007 to be replaced by Ian Jenkins with the relatively recent addition of Emer Brizzolara on keyboards, dulcimer and melodic, that&#8217;s how it stands today, although the members are now scattered far and wide, both here and abroad.</span></span></b></div>
<div style="line-height: 24.375px; margin-bottom: 1.967em; padding: 0px;">
<b><span style="background-color: black; color: purple; font-family: Verdana, sans-serif;">I mention all this because the chances are that, despite their extensive back-catalogue, you&#8217;ll not have come across them before. They have a solid following in Europe, but, back home they remain very much a cult. However, their influence has not gone unmarked and I find it hard to imagine that they are unknown to either Esben and the Witch or Scott Walker, whose latterday experimental albums carry some strong echoes.</span></b></div>
<div style="line-height: 24.375px; margin-bottom: 1.967em; padding: 0px;">
<b><span style="background-color: black; color: purple; font-family: Verdana, sans-serif;">As this might suggest, they&#8217;re not on the immediately accessible side of the folk fence. Rather their music is the aural equivalent of those dank, cobweb hung English woods, a sort of folk with heady poetic gothic undertones filtered perhaps through the same blood that ran through the veins of Byron and Coleridge in their more existential moments. Basically, while they draw on the English landscape, they&#8217;re more Nick Cave than Nick Drake.</span></b></div>
<div style="line-height: 24.375px; margin-bottom: 1.967em; padding: 0px;">
<b><span style="background-color: black; color: purple; font-family: Verdana, sans-serif;">That said, the songs on the new album, inspired by recent tours to hitherto unvisited countries, are, for the most, relatively straightforward in that they&#8217;re themed around love, albeit less in the romantic sense and more as an emotion and a force, sometimes warm, sometimes cruel and dark, destructive as well as energising.</span></b></div>
<div style="line-height: 24.375px; margin-bottom: 1.967em; padding: 0px;">
<b><span style="background-color: black; color: purple; font-family: Verdana, sans-serif;">With Jones not so much singing as delivering the lyrics in a narcotic semi-spoken style, the album opens with the&nbsp;&nbsp; heady, almost Slavic folk feel of&nbsp;<em style="margin: 0px; padding: 0px;">Your Guess</em>, a number about the unfathomable nature of love and the way it can lift you up while still remaining a mystery. Things turn rather more disturbing on the pizzicato bass pulsing&nbsp;<em style="margin: 0px; padding: 0px;">Hawksmoor and the Savage</em>, Jones evoking Cave&#8217;s tightly coiled menace as he sings of a bleeding piece of meat, a head with a necklace of teeth and the souls of men &#8220;<em style="margin: 0px; padding: 0px;">growing with the yarrow</em>&#8221;, its winter imagery spilling over into the parted love of&nbsp;<em style="margin: 0px; padding: 0px;">Winter Sea</em>, clarinet complementing what sounds like (though it&#8217;s not credited) a balalaika, which would chime with the references to steppes and Moscow.</span></b></div>
<div style="line-height: 24.375px; margin-bottom: 1.967em; padding: 0px;">
<b><span style="background-color: black; color: purple; font-family: Verdana, sans-serif;"><span style="line-height: 0.76em;">T</span>he temperature rises for the summery images of good weather and hammocks on the lawn in the keyboards-pulsing&nbsp;<em style="margin: 0px; padding: 0px;">Seasons And Storms</em>, a moment in time snapshot of two lovers or perhaps a parent and child caught in the dawn&#8217;s light, Adam Sherry from A Dead Forest Index sharing the vocals. Simon trained as a photographer and continues to exhibit, and you can hear the shutter at work as he captures vignettes from life in the lyrics, particularly on the&nbsp;<em style="margin: 0px; padding: 0px;">Sleepers</em>&nbsp;as, the music shading from staccato rhythm to fairground waltzer, describing the titular characters entwined beneath the white bed sheets as &#8220;<em style="margin: 0px; padding: 0px;">joined at the hip joined at the mouth</em>.&#8221;</span></b></div>
<div style="line-height: 24.375px; margin-bottom: 1.967em; padding: 0px;">
<b><span style="background-color: black; color: purple; font-family: Verdana, sans-serif;">The music may be shrouded in swirling shadows, but the romanticism is unfettered on the metaphorically inclined&nbsp;<em style="margin: 0px; padding: 0px;">Bridges</em>, a distant cousin of Scott Walker&#8217;s&nbsp;<em style="margin: 0px; padding: 0px;">The Girls From The Streets</em>, with its talk of following through the alleyways and of being &#8220;<em style="margin: 0px; padding: 0px;">in your arms now high in the garrets above the town</em>&#8221;, striking a sexual note in the line &#8220;<em style="margin: 0px; padding: 0px;">my hand inside your head, between your legs</em>.&#8221;</span></b></div>
<div style="line-height: 24.375px; margin-bottom: 1.967em; padding: 0px;">
<b><span style="background-color: black; color: purple; font-family: Verdana, sans-serif;">Another moment in time is captured on the musically stark&nbsp;<em style="margin: 0px; padding: 0px;">The Bells Of Saint Christopher&#8217;s</em>, with just keyboard drone ceding to a pulsing tone behind the vocal as, rising from his shared bed, the narrator describes the scene and sounds from the window as the sky hangs &#8220;<em style="margin: 0px; padding: 0px;">like a ripped curtain above the town</em>.&#8221;</span></b></div>
<div style="line-height: 24.375px; margin-bottom: 1.967em; padding: 0px;">
<b><span style="background-color: black; color: purple; font-family: Verdana, sans-serif;">Following the six minute&nbsp;<em style="margin: 0px; padding: 0px;">Naito-Shinjuku</em>, an otherworldly instrumental named for an historic area of Tokyo penned by Justin Jones and Hill and built around tuned percussion, bass, guitar and vibraphone, we return to the sleepers and the night in the spidery&nbsp;<em style="margin: 0px; padding: 0px;">Boden</em>, a renaissance-like guitar line underpinning a lyric about folk-tale like figure that seems ambiguously either protector or predator.</span></b></div>
<div style="line-height: 24.375px; margin-bottom: 1.967em; padding: 0px;">
<b><span style="background-color: black; color: purple; font-family: Verdana, sans-serif;">Introduced with Andalucian coloured guitar and clarinet and founded upon Hill&#8217;s stuttering drums , the album ends on the intoxicating whirl of&nbsp;<em style="margin: 0px; padding: 0px;">The Skeins of Love</em>, about the billowing weave that binds us to one another, the land and the dance, the lyric of which provides the album title and includes almost certainly the only song to feature the word polyhedron.</span></b></div>
<div style="margin-bottom: 1.967em; padding: 0px;">
<div style="line-height: 24.375px;">
<b><span style="background-color: black; color: purple; font-family: Verdana, sans-serif;">It&#8217;s not an easy or immediate listen, but, like their equally enthralling (and I use the term literally) back-catalogue, like the tendrils, vines and roots of the earth upon which they draw, it wraps itself around your mind and germinates.</span></b></div>
<span style="font-family: Verdana, sans-serif;"><b style="background-color: black; color: purple; line-height: 24.375px;"><a href="http://www.folkradio.co.uk/author/mike-davies/">BY MIKE DEVIES</a> &nbsp;&nbsp;</b><b style="background-color: black; color: purple; letter-spacing: 1px; line-height: 17.996px; text-transform: uppercase;"> (www.folkradio.co.uk/)</b></span></div>
<div style="line-height: 24.375px; margin-bottom: 1.967em; padding: 0px;">

<h2 class='date-header'><span>Κυριακή, 3 Απριλίου 2016</span></h2>

          <div class="date-posts">
        
<div class='post-outer'>
<div class='post hentry uncustomized-post-template' itemprop='blogPost' itemscope='itemscope' itemtype='http://schema.org/BlogPosting'>
<meta content='https://f4.bcbits.com/img/a3621022240_16.jpg' itemprop='image_url'/>
<meta content='163206480004181803' itemprop='blogId'/>
<meta content='7169659086025490641' itemprop='postId'/>
<a name='7169659086025490641'></a>
<div class='post-header'>
<div class='post-header-line-1'></div>
</div>
<div class='post-body entry-content' id='post-body-7169659086025490641' itemprop='description articleBody'>
<div dir="ltr" style="text-align: left;" trbidi="on">
<br />
<br />
<span style="font-family: Verdana, sans-serif;"><br /></span>
<span style="font-family: Verdana, sans-serif;"><span style="color: purple; font-family: &quot;arial&quot; , &quot;helvetica&quot; , sans-serif;"><b style="background-color: black;"><br /></b></span>
<span style="color: purple; font-family: &quot;arial&quot; , &quot;helvetica&quot; , sans-serif;"><b style="background-color: black;">TREMBLING BELLS</b></span></span><br />
<span style="color: purple; font-family: Verdana, sans-serif;"><b style="background-color: black;"><img alt="Wide Majestic Aire cover art" height="320" src="https://f4.bcbits.com/img/a3621022240_16.jpg" width="320" /></b></span><br />
<span style="color: purple; font-family: Verdana, sans-serif;"><b style="background-color: black;">WIDE MAJESTIC AIRE - 2016</b></span><br />
<span style="font-family: Verdana, sans-serif;"><span style="color: purple; font-family: &quot;arial&quot; , &quot;helvetica&quot; , sans-serif;"><b style="background-color: black;"><br /></b></span>
<span style="color: purple; font-family: &quot;arial&quot; , &quot;helvetica&quot; , sans-serif;"><b style="background-color: black;"><br /></b></span>
<span style="color: purple; font-family: &quot;arial&quot; , &quot;helvetica&quot; , sans-serif;"><b style="background-color: black;"><br /></b></span>
</span><br />
<div style="border: 0px; line-height: 21px; margin-bottom: 1.5em; outline: 0px; padding: 0px; vertical-align: baseline;">
<span style="color: purple; font-family: Verdana, sans-serif;"><b style="background-color: black;"><span style="border: 0px; font-style: inherit; margin: 0px; outline: 0px; padding: 0px; vertical-align: baseline;">Trembling Bells</span>&nbsp;are in the grand tradition of folk musicians who roam far and wide in their influences and subsequent styles. Perhaps best known for collaborations with Mike Heron (of The Incredible String Band) and Will Oldham they take elements of traditional music, sixties and seventies experimental folk, classical music, psychedelia and Velvet Underground fuzz blending it all together in their particular alchemical pot. Their last album, The Sovereign Self, was Stuart Maconie&#8217;s favourite album of 2015; Maconie of course is the curator of Radio 6&#8217;s Freak Zone and therein lies the rub. It&#8217;s all too easy to consign the &#8216;Bells to the &#8220;weird&#8221; zone, labelled as a tough listen but folk who remember (or are catching up on) albums such as Shirley Collins&#8217; Anthems Of Eden, Kevin Ayers&#8217; Whatevershebringswesing, Richard Thompson&#8217;s Henry The Human Fly or The Albion Band&#8217;s Rising Like The Sun will find much here to comfort them.&nbsp;<span style="border: 0px; font-style: inherit; margin: 0px; outline: 0px; padding: 0px; vertical-align: baseline;">Wide Majestic Aire</span>&nbsp;doesn&#8217;t sound like any of these but the spirit of adventure and quest that informed works like these is very much alive here, the band unafraid to mix soaring guitar solos with pump organ sounds, unaccompanied voice and Baroque classical influences.</b></span></div>
<div style="border: 0px; line-height: 21px; margin-bottom: 1.5em; outline: 0px; padding: 0px; vertical-align: baseline;">
<span style="color: purple; font-family: Verdana, sans-serif;"><b style="background-color: black;"><span style="border: 0px; font-style: inherit; margin: 0px; outline: 0px; padding: 0px; vertical-align: baseline;">Wide Majestic Aire</span>&nbsp;is a seven song mini album, its feet firmly anchored in two of the band&#8217;s (or more properly leader Alex Neilson&#8217;s) homelands, Leeds, his birthplace, and Carbeth in Scotland. The opening title song revisits Neilson&#8217;s formative years living close to the river Aire, reading Lorca and Blake as he walks the river banks, the dismal council estate he comes from replaced by Elysian Fields. The song flows like the river, a grand romantic sweep that takes him to the dreaming spires of Oxford, the buildings golden brown and ochre as Lavinia Blackwall&#8217;s voice commands attention, her mannered delivery nicely set against a rickety instrumental break. It&#8217;s an inspiring song and one of two here which most recall the pomp years of classic folk rock, Sandy Denny on Liege &amp; Lief and solo recordings perhaps. The second song to do so is the closing&nbsp;<em style="border: 0px; margin: 0px; outline: 0px; padding: 0px; vertical-align: baseline;">Marble Arch</em>. Again the band cleave to a rock sound, a solid rhythm section backing Blackwall&#8217;s majestic voice, guitarist Mike Hastings adding a fuzzed up guitar screech throughout.</b></span></div>
<div style="border: 0px; line-height: 21px; margin-bottom: 1.5em; outline: 0px; padding: 0px; vertical-align: baseline;">
<span style="color: purple; font-family: Verdana, sans-serif;"><b style="background-color: black;">No such firm footing on the capricious&nbsp;<em style="border: 0px; margin: 0px; outline: 0px; padding: 0px; vertical-align: baseline;">England Was Aghast</em>, a song that veers from sonic rumblings with guitars and cymbals crashing to an electronic hornpipe of sorts as Blackwall declaims an England that is like a Hieronymus Bosch painting.&nbsp;<em style="border: 0px; margin: 0px; outline: 0px; padding: 0px; vertical-align: baseline;">Show Me A Hole (And I&#8217;ll Crawl In It)</em>&nbsp;musically recalls the Incredible string Band as a reedy organ dominates although there&#8217;s a decidedly medieval feel present. Lyrically obscure there are some fabulous images conjured.&nbsp; &#8220;<em style="border: 0px; margin: 0px; outline: 0px; padding: 0px; vertical-align: baseline;">There&#8217;s a line of beauty that starts at Roland&#8217;s wrist and ends at your mouth which I kissed and even Rodin showed an envy for that ruinous bliss&#8221;</em>&nbsp;while later, up on Dionysian hills,&nbsp;<em style="border: 0px; margin: 0px; outline: 0px; padding: 0px; vertical-align: baseline;">&#8220;a butterfly dashed itself against a riot shield</em>.&#8221; The organ pumps away until, towards the end, the band dash in in full prog folk glory, Celtic fuzz guitar and Keith Emerson keyboards to the fore.&nbsp;<em style="border: 0px; margin: 0px; outline: 0px; padding: 0px; vertical-align: baseline;">Swallows Of Carbeth</em>&nbsp;is a simpler affair, a seemingly straightforward paean to the bucolic attractions of this off grid haven, the band nicely rolling and tumbling along initially. However it&#8217;s a lost love song, the delights soured by a leaving and the music darkens and becomes more frenzied, the fiddle blazing away. There&#8217;s more travelogue on the appealing&nbsp;<em style="border: 0px; margin: 0px; outline: 0px; padding: 0px; vertical-align: baseline;">I Love Bute,</em>&nbsp;a lovely kenspeckled number that recalls the music that added so much to the original Whickerman. Finally Alex Neilson offers the unaccompanied voice song,&nbsp;<em style="border: 0px; margin: 0px; outline: 0px; padding: 0px; vertical-align: baseline;">The Day That Maya Deren Died</em>, inspired, he says, from seeing The Watersons. As befits this unique writer however he gathers obscure references (Deyer was an avant garde filmmaker), place names (Kelvinside) and surrealistic images (a self immolated soldier) and weaves them into a song which kind of defies description but is also self referencing as it ends with the singer stating he&#8217;ll write a song about London&#8217;s Marble Arch, that song following and closing the disc.</b></span></div>
<div style="border: 0px; line-height: 21px; margin-bottom: 1.5em; outline: 0px; padding: 0px; vertical-align: baseline;">
<span style="color: purple; font-family: Verdana, sans-serif;"><b style="background-color: black;"><a href="https://paulkerr.wordpress.com/">https://paulkerr.wordpress.com</a></b></span><br />
<span style="font-family: Verdana, sans-serif;"><span style="color: purple; font-family: &quot;arial&quot; , &quot;helvetica&quot; , sans-serif;"><br /></span>
<span style="color: purple; font-family: &quot;arial&quot; , &quot;helvetica&quot; , sans-serif;"><br /></span></span>

<span style="font-family: Verdana, sans-serif;"><span style="color: purple; font-family: &quot;arial&quot; , &quot;helvetica&quot; , sans-serif;"><b style="background-color: black;"><br /></b></span>
<span style="color: purple; font-family: &quot;arial&quot; , &quot;helvetica&quot; , sans-serif;"><b style="background-color: black;">ΚΩΣΤΑΣ ΚΟΥΚΟΥΤΑΡΑΣ &amp; ATZITEISIONS&nbsp;</b></span></span><br />
<span style="color: purple; font-family: Verdana, sans-serif;"><b style="background-color: black;"><img height="320" src="https://1.bp.blogspot.com/_Q2dNLQVEiAs/SSbN-XW_3qI/AAAAAAAADMQ/QHrmsPA110M/s320/koukotaras.jpg" width="320" /></b></span><br />
<span style="color: purple; font-family: Verdana, sans-serif;"><b style="background-color: black;">ΑΠΟ ΠΟΥ ΚΑΙ ΠΟΥ... - 1974</b></span><br />
<span style="font-family: Verdana, sans-serif;"><span style="color: purple; font-family: &quot;arial&quot; , &quot;helvetica&quot; , sans-serif;"><b style="background-color: black;"><br /></b></span>
<span style="color: purple; font-family: &quot;arial&quot; , &quot;helvetica&quot; , sans-serif;"><b style="background-color: black;"><br /></b></span>
<span style="background-color: #f2f0db; color: purple;"><b style="background-color: black;">Όταν μιλάμε για τον συγκεκριμένο δίσκο μιλάμε ίσως για μια από τις σπανιότερες βινυλιακές κυκλοφορίες στην Ελλάδα.Ο κώστας Κουκουτάρας ήταν μια από τις εμβληματικές και πρωτοπόρες φυσιογνωμίες στην Rock σκηνή της Λάρισας στα τέλη των 70's.Το 1974 ηχογραφεί μάζι με το συγκρότημά του Atziteisons το συγκεκριμένο album το οποίο τυπώνεται σε ελάχιστες κόπιες. Μουσικά ο δίσκος είναι μια μίξη 60's &amp; 70's psych/folk rock. Αντίθετα με το μουσικό ρεύμα της εποχής τους οι Atziteisons κινούνται πιο πολύ προς την Βρετανική freakbeat/psych σκηνή με μια προσέγγιση όμως πιο "βαρβαρική",πράγμα που οφείλεται στην πενιχρή παραγωγή του δίσκου. Σε κάποια κομμάτια του δίσκου διαπιστώνονται επίσης έντονες επιρροές από την Γαλλική σκηνή των αρχών του '70. Γενικά εάν θέλουμε να μιλήσουμε για την μουσική αξία αυτού του δίσκου θα μπορούσαμε να πούμε ότι είναι ενδεικτικός του κλίματος που επικρατούσε στην Ελληνική rock σκηνή (με την εξαίρεση των πιο pop συγκροτημάτων) και αν σκεφτούμε πως αυτό το συγκρότημα άνθησε κατά τη διάρκεια της Επταετίας αντιλαμβανόμαστε το πόσο δύσκολο ήταν να αναπτύξουν μια ιδιαίτερη κουλτούρα. Στιχουργικά ο δίσκος είναι επηρεασμένος από το κίνημα των χιπυς (το λεγόμενο flower power movement). Τα βαρύτονα φωνητικά του Κουκουτάρα δίνουν στο δίσκο μια ξεχωριστή μαγεία και δημιουργούν μια μυστηριακή ατμόσφαιρα. Το μοναδικό "πταίσμα" στον δίσκο είναι το ολίγον τι γελοίο εξώφυλλό του το οποίο παραπέμπει σε καλλιτέχνες άλλων μουσικών ειδών. Ο δίσκος επανεκδόθηκε το 2006 από την Αναζήτηση Records σε περιορισμένη κοπή μόλις 100 δίσκων (με σεβασμό στην σπανιότητά του).</b></span></span><br />

<h2 class='date-header'><span>Κυριακή, 27 Μαρτίου 2016</span></h2>

          <div class="date-posts">
        
<div class='post-outer'>
<div class='post hentry uncustomized-post-template' itemprop='blogPost' itemscope='itemscope' itemtype='http://schema.org/BlogPosting'>
<meta content='https://f1.bcbits.com/img/a0667683718_16.jpg' itemprop='image_url'/>
<meta content='163206480004181803' itemprop='blogId'/>
<meta content='1014373296084096874' itemprop='postId'/>
<a name='1014373296084096874'></a>
<div class='post-header'>
<div class='post-header-line-1'></div>
</div>
<div class='post-body entry-content' id='post-body-1014373296084096874' itemprop='description articleBody'>
<div dir="ltr" style="text-align: left;" trbidi="on">
<br />
<br />
<span style="font-family: Verdana, sans-serif;"><br /></span>
<span style="font-family: Verdana, sans-serif;"><span style="color: purple; font-family: &quot;arial&quot; , &quot;helvetica&quot; , sans-serif;"><b style="background-color: black;"><br /></b></span>
<span style="color: purple; font-family: &quot;arial&quot; , &quot;helvetica&quot; , sans-serif;"><b style="background-color: black;">KEVIN MORBY</b></span></span><br />
<span style="color: purple; font-family: Verdana, sans-serif;"><b style="background-color: black;"><img alt="Singing Saw cover art" height="320" src="https://f1.bcbits.com/img/a0667683718_16.jpg" width="320" /></b></span><br />
<span style="color: purple; font-family: Verdana, sans-serif;"><b style="background-color: black;">SINGING SAW - 2016</b></span><br />
<span style="font-family: Verdana, sans-serif;"><span style="color: purple; font-family: &quot;arial&quot; , &quot;helvetica&quot; , sans-serif;"><b style="background-color: black;"><br /></b></span>
<span style="color: purple; font-family: &quot;arial&quot; , &quot;helvetica&quot; , sans-serif;"><b style="background-color: black;"><br /></b></span>
</span><br />
<div class="tralbumData tralbum-about" itemprop="description" style="line-height: 15px; margin-top: 2em; word-wrap: break-word;">
<span style="color: purple; font-family: Verdana, sans-serif;"><b style="background-color: black;">'Singing Saw' is a record written simply and realized orchestrally. In it, Kevin Morby faces the reality that true beauty &#8211; deep and earned &#8211; demands a whole-world balance that includes our darker sides. It is a record of duality, one that marks another stage of growth for this young, gifted songwriter with a kind face and a complicated mind.<br /><br />In the Autumn of 2014, Kevin Morby moved to the small Los Angeles neighborhood of Mount Washington. The move would shape 'Singing Saw', Morby&#8217;s first album for new label Dead Oceans. Previous tenants at Morby&#8217;s new home happened to leave an upright piano behind, with a few mysterious pieces of sheet music and an introductory book of common chords stacked on top. Thankful to finally be in one place for an extended spell, Morby, a beginner at the piano, immediately sat at the new instrument and began composing the songs that would form 'Singing Saw'.<br /><br />Alongside, he began taking long walks through the winding hills and side streets of the neighborhood each night, glimpsing views of both the skyline&#8217;s sweeping lights and the dark, dried out underbrush of the LA flora. The duality of the city itself began to shape a set of lyrical ideas that he would refine with the sparse accompaniment of piano and acoustic guitar.<br /><br />What is a singing saw? It is an instrument that creates ethereal sounds, but it is also a tool: basic and practical while also being fearsome, even destructive. Morby watches the singing saw in its eponymous song; that instrument of eerie soft beauty cuts down the flowers in its path and chases after him, while his surroundings mock and dwarf him, Alice in Wonderland style. And in a singing saw, we can understand music as something more powerful than its inviting, delicate sound. No wonder Morby talks about a &#8220;songbook&#8221; in his head as something he needs to take up the hills so he can &#8220;get rid of it.&#8221; Heavy themes are nothing new for Morby, whose previous records (2013&#8217;s 'Harlem River' and 2014&#8217;s 'Still Life', both released on the Woodsist label) dealt with their own eerie visions and damning prophecies.<br /><br />Morby opens 'Singing Saw' with &#8220;Cut Me Down&#8221;, a song of tears, debts and a prescient vision of being reduced to nothing. &#8220;I Have Been to the Mountain&#8221;, &#8220;Destroyer&#8221; and &#8220;Black Flowers&#8221; continue to explore beauty and freedom, seizing upon the rot that seeps into even the supposedly safest of realms; peace, family and romantic love. By the end of the record on &#8220;Water&#8221;, Morby is literally begging to be put out once and for all, like a fire that might burn all the visions away.<br /><br />Travels beyond his mountain walks inform songs like &#8220;Dorothy&#8221;, which recounts a trip to Portugal, witnessing a fishing ritual and luxuriating in the aura of a bar light-tinged reunion with old friends The touching innocence of &#8220;Ferris Wheel&#8221; stands alone in stark simplicity amidst the lush sonic textures of the album. Here, the album is balanced by Morby&#8217;s signature sweetness and joie de vivre.<br /><br />The arrangements of 'Singing Saw' trace back to Morby&#8217;s experience playing in 'The Complete Last Waltz', a live recreation of The Band&#8217;s legendary last performance. There, Morby developed a fast friendship with producer/bandleader Sam Cohen (Apollo Sunshine, Yellow Birds), which led Morby to forgo recording in Los Angeles and take the nascent songs of 'Singing Saw' to Isokon Studios in Woodstock, New York. There, in a converted A-frame house, they set about creating a record that would bring a sonic balance, intricacy and depth to match these songs and all that inspired them.<br /><br />In the end, Morby fulfills the promise many heard on his first two albums, bringing his most realized effort of songwriting and lyricism to fruition. The songs of 'Singing Saw' reflect the clarity that comes from welcoming change and embracing duality, and the distillation of those elements into an entirely new vision.</b></span></div>
<h3 class="credits-label" style="font-stretch: normal; left: -10000px; line-height: 15px; margin: 0px 0px 7px; position: absolute; top: -10000px;">
<span style="background-color: black; color: purple; font-family: Verdana, sans-serif; font-size: small;">credits</span></h3>
<div class="tralbumData tralbum-credits" style="line-height: 15px; margin-top: 2em; word-wrap: break-word;">
<span style="color: purple; font-family: Verdana, sans-serif;"><b style="background-color: black;">releases April 15, 2016<br /><br />Sam Cohen added a multitude of instrumentation to the record (guitar, bass, drums and keyboard), and were joined by fellow Complete Last Waltz alum Marco Benevento on piano and keyboard, fleshing out Morby&#8217;s original compositions and upholding the vision for a cohesive piano sound that serves as a touchstone for the entire album. Backup vocalists Hannah Cohen, Lauren Balthrop and Alecia Chakor contribute soaring harmonies; Nick Kinsey (Elvis Perkins) adds drums and percussion; Justin Sullivan, a longtime Morby collaborator and staple of his live band, contributes drums; Oliver Hill and Eliza Bag lift numerous songs with string accompaniments, and Alec Spiegelman on saxophone and flute and Cole Kamen-Green on trumpet bring dramatic swells. Finally, John Andrews (Quilt) adds the eerie lilt of the album&#8217;s promise, providing saw on the &#8220;Cut Me Down&#8221; and &#8220;Singing Saw&#8221;.</b></span></div>

<span style="font-family: &quot;verdana&quot; , sans-serif;"><span style="font-family: &quot;verdana&quot; , sans-serif;"><br /></span>
<span style="font-family: &quot;verdana&quot; , sans-serif;"><b><span style="color: purple; font-family: &quot;verdana&quot; , sans-serif;"><span style="background-color: black;"><br /></span></span>
<span style="color: purple; font-family: &quot;verdana&quot; , sans-serif;"><span style="background-color: black;">ΝΙΚΟΣ ΚΥΠΟΥΡΓΟΣ</span></span></b></span></span><br />
<span style="color: purple; font-family: &quot;verdana&quot; , sans-serif;"><span style="background-color: black;"><b><img height="320" src="https://2.bp.blogspot.com/_pRLGgZyys_Y/Sv8qs8hNrvI/AAAAAAAAAxU/SIwhyE9wYVM/s320/kypou2.jpg" width="318" /></b></span></span><br />
<span style="color: purple; font-family: &quot;verdana&quot; , sans-serif;"><span style="background-color: black;"><b>ΝΙΚΟΣ ΚYΠΟΥΡΓΟΣ 2 - 1970</b></span></span><br />
<span style="font-family: &quot;verdana&quot; , sans-serif;"><b><span style="color: purple; font-family: &quot;verdana&quot; , sans-serif;"><span style="background-color: black;"><br /></span></span>
</b></span><br />
<span style="font-family: &quot;verdana&quot; , sans-serif;"><b><span style="background-color: #f8ffcc; color: #222222; line-height: 21.56px;"><br /></span>
<span style="font-size: xx-small;"><span style="background-color: #f8ffcc; color: #222222; line-height: 21.56px;"><br /></span>
</span></b></span><br />
<span style="color: purple; font-family: &quot;arial&quot; , &quot;helvetica&quot; , sans-serif;"><b style="background-color: black;"><span style="line-height: 21.56px;">Λίγοι γνωρίζουν, λιγότεροι έχουν ακούσει, κι ακόμη λιγότεροι κατέχουν, τους τρεις προσωπικούς δίσκους που κυκλοφόρησε, νεαρός ακόμη (κάτω από τα 20), ο&nbsp;</span><span style="line-height: 21.56px;">Νίκος Κυπουργός</span><span style="line-height: 21.56px;">&nbsp;την τριετία 1969-1971, στην εταιρία&nbsp;</span><span style="line-height: 21.56px;">Rod Strofes</span><span style="line-height: 21.56px;">, των Μ. &amp; Κώτιας Ροδιάδου.</span></b></span><br />
<span style="color: purple; font-family: &quot;arial&quot; , &quot;helvetica&quot; , sans-serif;"><b style="background-color: black;"><span style="line-height: 21.56px;">&nbsp;Τα άλμπουμ μπορεί να μην είναι από εκείνα που θα άλλαζαν τη μουσική ιστορία, έχουν όμως ενδιαφέρον και, σίγουρα, μερικές πολύ καλές στιγμές &#8211; για να μην πω απίθανες, και παρεξηγηθώ... Συγκριτικώς, το&nbsp;</span><span style="line-height: 21.56px;">&#8220;2&#8221;</span><span style="line-height: 21.56px;">&nbsp;είναι το &#171;καλύτερο&#187; από τα τρία. Είναι στηριγμένο σε ποιήματα των&nbsp;</span><span style="line-height: 21.56px;">Γιώργου Σεφέρη, Νικηφόρου Βρεττάκου, Μίλτου Σαχτούρη, Νίκου Καρύδη, Κοραλίας Θεοτοκά</span><span style="line-height: 21.56px;">&nbsp;και&nbsp;</span><span style="line-height: 21.56px;">Ματθαίου Μουντέ</span><span style="line-height: 21.56px;">&nbsp;(&#171;εικόνα&#187; για τον Κυπουργό εκείνα τα χρόνια) και περιέχει ένα τουλάχιστον ωραίο τραγούδι, τη&nbsp;</span><span style="line-height: 21.56px;">&#171;Ρόδινη κυρά&#187;</span><span style="line-height: 21.56px;">, σε στίχους Θεοτοκά. Ακόμη καλύτερα όμως είναι τα δύο ορχηστρικά. Το&nbsp;</span><span style="line-height: 21.56px;">&#171;Ταξίδι&#187;</span><span style="line-height: 21.56px;">... ταξίδι αληθινό, σε acid folk διαδρομές &#8211; είναι απίστευτο! &#8211;, καθώς και η&nbsp;</span><span style="line-height: 21.56px;">&#171;Υπόσχεση β&#8217;&#187;</span><span style="line-height: 21.56px;">, με ωραία μέρη βιολιού. Γενικώς, τα όργανα που ακούγονται στον δίσκο είναι ελάχιστα. Μια-δυο κιθάρες, το βιολί, και τα... κόλπα στο &#171;Ταξίδι&#187;.</span></b></span><br />
<a href="http://diskoryxeion.blogspot.gr/2009/11/strofes.html"><span style="color: purple; font-family: Verdana, sans-serif; font-size: x-small;"><b style="background-color: black;">http://diskoryxeion.blogspot.gr/2009/11/strofes.html</b></span></a><span style="background-color: black; line-height: 21.56px;"><span style="color: purple; font-family: &quot;arial&quot; , &quot;helvetica&quot; , sans-serif;"><b><br /></b></span></span>

<div dir="ltr" style="text-align: left;" trbidi="on">
<br />
<br />
<br />
<span style="color: purple; font-family: &quot;verdana&quot; , sans-serif;"><b style="background-color: black;"><br /></b></span>
<span style="color: purple; font-family: &quot;verdana&quot; , sans-serif;"><b style="background-color: black;">ΝΙΚΟΣ ΤΑΤΣΗΣ</b></span><br />
<span style="color: purple; font-family: &quot;verdana&quot; , sans-serif;"><b style="background-color: black;"><img height="320" src="https://3.bp.blogspot.com/-Xc3gu1ytxW8/VKxdvttsr1I/AAAAAAAACV8/YuvyrBXzu18/s320/%CE%95%CE%BE%CF%8E%CF%86%CF%85%CE%BB%CE%BB%CE%BF%2B(1).jpg" width="320" /></b></span><br />
<span style="color: purple; font-family: &quot;verdana&quot; , sans-serif;"><b style="background-color: black;">ΕΡΑΝΑ - 1978</b></span><br />
<span style="color: purple; font-family: &quot;verdana&quot; , sans-serif;"><b style="background-color: black;"><br /></b></span>
<span style="color: purple; font-family: &quot;verdana&quot; , sans-serif;"><b style="background-color: black;"><br /></b></span>
<span style="color: purple; font-family: &quot;verdana&quot; , sans-serif;"><b style="background-color: black;"><br /></b></span>
<br />
<div style="line-height: 19.404px; text-align: justify;">
<span style="background-color: black;"><span style="color: purple; font-family: &quot;verdana&quot; , sans-serif;"><b>Αυτός είναι ο πρώτος ολοκληρωμένος δίσκος του συνθέτη&nbsp;Νίκου Τάτση, ο οποίος μέχρι εκείνη τη στιγμή είχε ελάχιστη παρουσία στα μουσικά πράγματα. Ένα τραγούδι του με τίτλο&nbsp;<span style="font-style: italic;">"Ο σαραντάπηχος"</span>&nbsp;είχε ακουστεί στα μέσα της δεκαετίας από τον Γιώργο Νταλάρα. Λίγο αργότερα ακούστηκαν ορισμένα τραγούδια του από τη Χαρούλα Αλεξίου&nbsp;<span style="font-style: italic;">(Ανεστάκι, Λέλι γιαλέλι, Έλεγα)</span>, ενώ το 1985 κυκλοφόρησε ο δεύτερος δίσκος του με τη μουσική της ταινίας<span style="font-style: italic;">"Τι έχουν να δουν τα μάτια μου"</span>.&nbsp;</b></span></span></div>
<div style="line-height: 19.404px; text-align: justify;">
<span style="background-color: black;"><span style="color: purple; font-family: &quot;verdana&quot; , sans-serif;"><b>Στο δίσκο&nbsp;<span style="font-style: italic;">"Έρανα"</span>&nbsp;συνεργάστηκε με τον ποιητή και φίλο του&nbsp;Κώστα Παπαγεωργίου&nbsp;και το αποτέλεσμα είναι μια σειρά πρωτότυπα τραγούδια χαμηλών τόνων σε έντεχνο ύφος, αλλά με στέερες βάσεις πάνω σε δρόμους από την ελληνική παράδοση.<br />Το ιδιότυπο ορχηστρικό χρώμα των τραγουδιών, που παραπέμπει σε ήχους πρώιμου ethnic, προέκυψε από έναν πολύ ενδιαφέροντα συνδυασμό μουσικών οργάνων από Ανατολή και Δύση. Τα εκτελούν οι σημαντικότεροι δεξιοτέχνες της εποχής, όπως ο&nbsp;Δημήτρης Βράσκος&nbsp;(βιολί), οΔημήτρης Φάμπας&nbsp;(κιθάρα), ο&nbsp;Τάσος Διακογιώργης&nbsp;(σαντούρι), ο&nbsp;Ανδρέας Ροδουσάκης(κοντραμπάσο) και άλλοι.</b></span></span></div>
<div style="line-height: 19.404px; text-align: justify;">
<span style="background-color: black;"><span style="color: purple; font-family: &quot;verdana&quot; , sans-serif;"><b>Τα τραγούδια ερμηνεύουν ο&nbsp;Γιάννης Δημητράς&nbsp;και η&nbsp;Μαρία Κάτηρα, γνωστοί τότε από τη συμμετοχή τους σε δίσκους του&nbsp;Μάνου Χατζιδάκι, ο οποίος είναι και ο παραγωγός αυτού του δίσκου.</b></span></span></div>
<div style="text-align: justify;">
<span style="color: purple; font-family: &quot;verdana&quot; , sans-serif; font-size: xx-small;"><span style="background-color: black; line-height: 19.404px;"><b><a href="http://diskovolos58.blogspot.gr/">http://diskovolos58.blogspot.gr/</a></b></span></span></div>
<div style="text-align: justify;">
<span style="color: purple; font-family: &quot;verdana&quot; , sans-serif; font-size: x-small;"><span style="background-color: black; line-height: 19.404px;"><br /></span></span></div>
<div style="text-align: justify;">
<span style="color: purple; font-family: &quot;verdana&quot; , sans-serif; font-size: x-small;"><span style="background-color: black; line-height: 19.404px;"><br /></span></span></div>
<div style="text-align: justify;">

<span style="color: purple; font-family: &quot;verdana&quot; , sans-serif;"><b style="background-color: black;">ΔΩΡΟΣ ΓΕΩΡΓΙΑΔΗΣ</b></span><br />
<span style="color: purple; font-family: &quot;verdana&quot; , sans-serif;"><b style="background-color: black;"><img height="308" src="https://3.bp.blogspot.com/-l4G9XzVao1k/Vuo-CvT279I/AAAAAAAAFwQ/7611c_6iGZQksj6aVxUOnz3vP_5lJ0xTQ/s320/f.JPG" width="320" /></b></span><br />
<span style="color: purple; font-family: &quot;verdana&quot; , sans-serif;"><b style="background-color: black;">ΠΕΡΑΣΑΝ 2000 ΧΡΟΝΙΑ - 1976</b></span><br />
<span style="color: purple; font-family: &quot;verdana&quot; , sans-serif;"><b style="background-color: black;"><br /></b></span>
<span style="color: purple; font-family: &quot;verdana&quot; , sans-serif;"><b style="background-color: black;"><br /></b></span>
<span style="color: purple; font-family: &quot;verdana&quot; , sans-serif;"><b style="background-color: black;"><br /></b></span>
<br />
<div style="border: 0px; font-stretch: inherit; line-height: 22.4px; margin-bottom: 1.25rem; padding: 0px; text-align: justify;">
<span style="color: purple; font-family: &quot;verdana&quot; , sans-serif;"><b style="background-color: black;">O Δώρος Γεωργιάδης πρωτοεμφανίστηκε στις αρχές της δεκαετίας του 1970. Μεταξύ άλλων, πήρε δύο Α&#900; βραβεία στο Φεστιβάλ Θεσ/νίκης (το 1972 ερμηνεύοντας ο ίδιος το τραγούδι του &#171;Αν ήμουν πλούσιος&#187; και το 1977 με το τραγούδι &#171;Ας κάνουμε απόψε μιαν αρχή&#187;, με ερμηνεύτρια την Άννα Βίσσυ). Το 1979, με το τραγούδι του &#171;Σωκράτης&#187; (ερμηνευμένο από την Ελπίδα), εκπροσώπησε την Ελλάδα στον Διαγωνισμό Τραγουδιού της Γιουροβίζιον και κατέλαβε την 8η θέση.</b></span></div>
<div style="border: 0px; font-stretch: inherit; line-height: 22.4px; margin-bottom: 1.25rem; padding: 0px; text-align: justify;">
<span style="color: purple; font-family: &quot;verdana&quot; , sans-serif;"><b style="background-color: black;">O Σταύρος Σιδεράς είναι μουσικός, συνθέτης και σκηνοθέτης. &nbsp;Η καριέρα του στη μουσική ξεκίνησε το 1976 ως ποπ τραγουδιστής και συνθέτης. Επελέγη να τραγουδήσει μαζί με την Κωνσταντίνα και να εκπροσωπήσουν την Κύπρο στο Διαγωνισμό Τραγουδιού της Γιουροβίζιον το 1983. Με τη δική του σύνθεση &#171;Η Αγάπη Ζει Ακόμα&#187; το ντουέτο κατέλαβε τη 16η θέση.<br />Η καριέρα του Σιδερά ως συγγραφέα και σκηνοθέτη σε μιούζικαλ ξεκίνησε το 1979 με τη ροκ όπερα &#8220;The Archon&#8221;. Έκτοτε οι παραγωγές του έχουν παιχτεί σε διάφορες χώρες όπως Η.Π.Α., Ελλάδα και Νότιος Αφρική.&nbsp;</b></span></div>
<div style="border: 0px; font-stretch: inherit; line-height: 22.4px; margin-bottom: 1.25rem; padding: 0px; text-align: justify;">
<span style="color: purple; font-family: &quot;verdana&quot; , sans-serif;"><b style="background-color: black;">Οι δύο Κύπριοι καλλιτέχνες συνεργάστηκαν &nbsp;το 1976 στο άλμπουμ &#171;Πέρασαν 2000 Χρόνια&#187;, το οποίο αποτελεί ένα σπάνιο δείγμα χριστιανικού ποπ / ροκ με συμφωνικό χαρακτήρα και αραιά προοδευτικά ψήγματα.</b></span><br />
<b style="background-color: black; color: purple; font-family: verdana, sans-serif; line-height: 22.4px;"><a href="http://progrocks.gr/">http://progrocks.gr/</a></b></div>
<div class="separator" style="clear: both; font-size: 13px; line-height: 18.2px;">
