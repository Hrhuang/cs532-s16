<h2 class='date-header'><span>Wednesday, 30 March 2016</span></h2>

          <div class="date-posts">
        
<div class='post-outer'>
<div class='post hentry'>
<a name='7315151746319917472'></a>
<h3 class='post-title entry-title'>
<a href='http://didnotchart.blogspot.com/2016/03/grant-mclennan-tribute-gig-may-6-2016.html'>Grant McLennan tribute gig, May 6 2016, Bush Hall</a>
</h3>
<div class='post-header'>
<div class='post-header-line-1'></div>
</div>
<div class='post-body entry-content' id='post-body-7315151746319917472'>
This year marks the tenth anniversary of Grant McLennan's death. His music lives on - Streets Of Your Town influencing Courtney Barnett's Depreston, for example - and will continue to as long as there are music fans and musicians ready to embrace emotionally uncompromising, classic guitar pop.<br />
<br />
You might have been to any of the intimate afternoon tributes we've put on in the last 5 years. You'll have seen acts like Robert Forster, Allo Darlin, David Westlake and Luke Haines. You'll know those acoustic sets were brilliant.<br />
<br />
But this is the big one. May 6 2016 is exactly 10 years after the death of one of our favourites, one of the greats. So let's make this special. Let's do this in style. Let's plug in the guitars and do it on a Friday night.<br />
<br />
So we've got the grand Bush Hall. And we've got quite a line-up:<br />
<br />
Stewart Lee is hosting. He's written some new material. And he's playing some McLennan covers on the guitar accompanied by a violinist.<br />
<br />
Gerry Love ("The Go-Betweens have been with me all my adult life") is playing with a full band. And that band has pedigree because they've played with some brilliant bands before:<br />
<br />
<ul>
<li>Jim McCulloch (BMX Bandits, The Soup Dragons)</li>
<li>Dave McGowan (Kevin Ayers, Isobel Campbell, Arab Strap, Teenage Fanclub)</li>
<li>Noel O&#8217;Donnell (Attic Lights)</li>
</ul>
<br />
Pete Astor<br />
The Wave Pictures<br />
The Wolfhounds<br />
Bill Botting &amp; the Two Drink Minimums<br />
The Left Outsides<br />
<br />
Yes, there's still room for one more special guest. We're working on it, believe me.<br />
<br />
Some bands are doing all McLennan covers - Go-Betweens, solo and Jack Frost songs - and some are mixing them with their own material. And one band is going to thrash through Karen because that's where it all started. It's going to be one hell of a night.<br />
<br />
Doors open at 7pm. The music will start at around 7.45pm. There's a <a href="https://www.facebook.com/events/1685283051754022/" target="_blank">facebook event</a>. And most importantly <a href="http://www.wegottickets.com/event/352608" target="_blank">buy your ticket</a>.<br />
<br />
All money from the tickets goes to the bands after the venue costs are paid for.<br />

<h2 class='date-header'><span>Monday, 28 March 2016</span></h2>

          <div class="date-posts">
        
<div class='post-outer'>
<div class='post hentry'>
<a name='875790792091132234'></a>
<h3 class='post-title entry-title'>
<a href='http://didnotchart.blogspot.com/2016/03/this-weeks-new-soul-vinyl.html'>This week's new soul vinyl</a>
</h3>
<div class='post-header'>
<div class='post-header-line-1'></div>
</div>
<div class='post-body entry-content' id='post-body-875790792091132234'>
<b>Freakway - Hot Touch</b><br />
The spiel is Freakway are a Minneapolis funk band living in Tokyo. It smells a bit fishy to me, because this underground disco boogie peaked in Philly 79-80 and was last seen in the wild in 1982 before electro took over. Unless you were listening to Cameo. My gut tells me this is a repro, or a studio find, but whatever way you listen to it, full marks.<br />
<br />
<iframe seamless="" src="https://bandcamp.com/EmbeddedPlayer/album=789509766/size=large/bgcol=ffffff/linkcol=0687f5/tracklist=false/artwork=small/transparent=true/" style="border: 0; height: 120px; width: 100%;"><a href="http://starcreature.bandcamp.com/album/freakway-hot-touch-b-w-aint-gonna-last">Freakway - Hot Touch b/w Ain't Gonna Last by Freakway</a></iframe><br />
<br />
<b>Shateish - I'm Leavin'</b><br />
A clever nu-soul reinvention of Etta James's I'd Rather Go Blind. Sometimes this thing works - like&nbsp;Dimitri From Stoke On Trent's I Pay a Little Slayer or Featurecast's One Step - and this lockstep groove really works.<br />
<br />
I'm Leavin' has been doing the rounds as an MP3 for 4 years. This 7" is overdue, though, and I was happy to shell out.<br />
<br />
<iframe allowfullscreen="" frameborder="0" height="281" mozallowfullscreen="" src="https://player.vimeo.com/video/35792363" webkitallowfullscreen="" width="500"></iframe><br />
<a href="https://vimeo.com/35792363">Shateish "I'm Leavin" (prod by Nottz)</a> from <a href="https://vimeo.com/rawkoncept">Raw Koncept</a> on <a href="https://vimeo.com/">Vimeo</a>.<br />
<br />
<b>Liz Vice - There's A Light</b><br />
Not exactly new - the vinyl came out last year, but has just hit the UK import racks - but what a sound! What a voice! And her band sound like they were schooled in Sam Cooke soul and Stax electric keyboards.<br />
<br />
This is a gospel record every bit as good as Leon Bridges' Coming Home, also from 2015. Liz didn't get the same notices as Leon last year, although I know which I've played more. And I played Coming Home a lot.<br />
<br />
<iframe allowfullscreen="" frameborder="0" height="315" src="https://www.youtube.com/embed/sR1MoY3DCu8" width="500"></iframe>

<h2 class='date-header'><span>Sunday, 27 March 2016</span></h2>

          <div class="date-posts">

<h3 class='post-title entry-title'>
<a href='http://didnotchart.blogspot.com/2016/03/muncie-girls-from-caplan-to-belsize.html'>Muncie Girls - From Caplan To Belsize</a>
</h3>
<div class='post-header'>
<div class='post-header-line-1'></div>
</div>
<div class='post-body entry-content' id='post-body-5472986942045029955'>
This album fits right in with the UK DIY scene of bands like Personal Best and Shit Present - punky guitars, feminist lyrics, immediate hooks - but it's more commercial.<br />
<br />
That's not a criticism because this is a very good record. It's one that sounds like it was made for US radio stations, all chugging bass and powerpop punch, somewhere between Belly and The Go-Go's.<br />
<br />
I can imagine The Cars' Ric Ocasek producing them - like he did with Weezer and Guided By Voices - and Muncie Girls gunning for chart glory. Because, seriously, one of the UK's DIY bands is going to break through. This already sounds like a crossover hit.<br />
<br />
<iframe seamless="" src="https://bandcamp.com/EmbeddedPlayer/album=1187539988/size=large/bgcol=ffffff/linkcol=0687f5/tracklist=false/artwork=small/track=2690606415/transparent=true/" style="border: 0; height: 120px; width: 100%;"><a href="http://munciegirls.bandcamp.com/album/from-caplan-to-belsize">From Caplan To Belsize by Muncie Girls</a></iframe>

<div class='post-header'>

</div>
<div class='post-body entry-content' id='post-body-1696263221372756395'>

<h2 class='date-header'><span>Sunday, 20 March 2016</span></h2>

          <div class="date-posts">

<h3 class='post-title entry-title'>
<a href='http://didnotchart.blogspot.com/2016/03/magic-bullets-young-mans-fancy.html'>Magic Bullets - Young Man's Fancy</a>
</h3>
<div class='post-header'>
<div class='post-header-line-1'></div>
</div>
<div class='post-body entry-content' id='post-body-1384929804896823026'>
This unreleased album was recorded from 2007 to 2009, but 1980 to 1984 seems more accurate. Every reference point is a joy.<br />
<br />
There's the trembling vulnerability in the vocals of the young Edwyn Collins. Magic Bullets do Orange Juice's post-punk discotheque, all stuttering guitars and strangled emotions, on I Could Go. They sound just like St Christopher - bedsit balladry written for opera houses - on Television Won't Know.<br />
<br />
They even cover The Line's Nerve Pylon (Phil Wilson's favourite ever single, fact fans) to really set the early 80s scene. When you buy this, make sure you also pick up their Lives For Romance 12" - they're knocking them out for a mere 5 clams. Everyone's a winner.<br />
<br />
<iframe seamless="" src="https://bandcamp.com/EmbeddedPlayer/album=122726361/size=large/bgcol=ffffff/linkcol=0687f5/tracklist=false/artwork=small/transparent=true/" style="border: 0; height: 120px; width: 100%;"><a href="http://magicbullets.bandcamp.com/album/young-mans-fancy">Young Man's Fancy by Magic Bullets</a></iframe>

<h2 class='date-header'><span>Thursday, 17 March 2016</span></h2>

          <div class="date-posts">
        
<div class='post-outer'>
<div class='post hentry'>
<a name='4188834897937781672'></a>
<h3 class='post-title entry-title'>
<a href='http://didnotchart.blogspot.com/2016/03/spinning-coin-albany.html'>Spinning Coin - Albany</a>
</h3>
<div class='post-header'>
<div class='post-header-line-1'></div>
</div>
<div class='post-body entry-content' id='post-body-4188834897937781672'>
<div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
<a href="https://2.bp.blogspot.com/--V_4cshFl6w/Vur3hmu9MKI/AAAAAAAABMo/s2IMSeQ9ytoC4Kth4pVTtCskMr6BnIHwA/s1600/Spinning%2BCoin.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="clear: left; float: left; margin-bottom: 1em; margin-right: 1em;"><img border="0" height="200" src="https://2.bp.blogspot.com/--V_4cshFl6w/Vur3hmu9MKI/AAAAAAAABMo/s2IMSeQ9ytoC4Kth4pVTtCskMr6BnIHwA/s200/Spinning%2BCoin.jpg" width="200" /></a></div>
Albany is angular pop and rhythmic stuttering like Josef K's It's Kinda Funny. Live, Spinning Coin have three guitarists upfront, all west coast (Scotland or the USA, take your pick) harmonies and classic tunes. Yes, like Teenage Fanclub, only with a bit more dirt under their fingernails.<br />
<br />
<br />
<br />
<br />
Remember how Franz Ferdinand took the Josef K template and aimed to "make music that girls that can dance to"? Spinning Coin have decided, actually, maybe they'd rather make hesitant Josef K post-punk in a DIY style for people who can't dance but quite like the idea of it.<br />
<br />
With a record&nbsp;<i>this</i> good it's customary to pronounce it 'single of the year', But Albany might have to fight the new Sheer Mag single for that. So let's celebrate brilliance (because that's what this is) and enjoy its splendour of riches.<br />

<h2 class='date-header'><span>Tuesday, 15 March 2016</span></h2>

          <div class="date-posts">

<h3 class='post-title entry-title'>
<a href='http://didnotchart.blogspot.com/2016/03/grandstands-stranger.html'>Grandstands - Stranger</a>
</h3>
<div class='post-header'>
<div class='post-header-line-1'></div>
</div>
<div class='post-body entry-content' id='post-body-4222598665370505838'>
There's a countrified current in the Melbourne underground - Ciggie Witch, Lower Plenty, Velcro - which Grandstands add to with their own desolation and wide open spaces.<div>
<br /></div>
<div>
There are two singles (you know what I mean) from this tape,&nbsp;&#8216;Getting Out&#8217; and &#8216;Stranger (In A Sense), but there are many more potential hits.</div>
<div>
<br /></div>
<div>
Meditative - all sprung rhythms and sinewy folk-rock - sounds like a session track from Before Hollywood. Then there's Two and Five. Here, judge for yourself:</div>
<div>
<br /></div>
<div>
<iframe seamless="" src="https://bandcamp.com/EmbeddedPlayer/album=21695016/size=large/bgcol=ffffff/linkcol=0687f5/tracklist=false/artwork=small/track=857872188/transparent=true/" style="border: 0; height: 120px; width: 100%;"><a href="http://whalesmouth.bandcamp.com/album/stranger">Stranger by Grandstands</a></iframe></div>
<div>
<br /></div>
<div>
After listening to this album, I reached for Paul Kelly's cover of The Triffids' Raining Pleasure. Well, that's not quite true. I played Stranger again a few more times first.</div>
