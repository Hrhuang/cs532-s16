<meta content='http://2.bp.blogspot.com/-UKoZYheIFAY/VdhNf_PDzNI/AAAAAAAACCg/QesIs9CnKDI/s1600/imagesCAPGQXFO.jpg' itemprop='image_url'/>

<h3 class='post-title entry-title' itemprop='name'>
<a href='http://maggotcaviar.blogspot.com/2015/08/peelfest-5.html'>Peelfest! #5 </a>
</h3>
<div class='post-header'>
<div class='post-header-line-1'></div>
</div>
<div class='post-body entry-content' id='post-body-1971397558083993864' itemprop='description articleBody'>
<div align="center" class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
&nbsp;</div>
<div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
<a href="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/-UKoZYheIFAY/VdhNf_PDzNI/AAAAAAAACCg/QesIs9CnKDI/s1600/imagesCAPGQXFO.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;"><img border="0" src="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/-UKoZYheIFAY/VdhNf_PDzNI/AAAAAAAACCg/QesIs9CnKDI/s1600/imagesCAPGQXFO.jpg" /></a></div>
<div align="center" class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
&nbsp;</div>
<div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: left;">
&nbsp;Today would have been John Peel's 76th birthday, so I thought I'd pop back out from the woodwork from whence I originally came to&nbsp;enlighten&nbsp;the masses with another eclectic smorgasboard of sonic wonderment that is Peelfest #5.</div>
<div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: left;">
&nbsp;</div>
<div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: left;">
Since I announced my "retirement" last October, whereupon a nation mourned and wept uncontrollably, sparking off severe flood warnings far and wide across the land, I seem to have spent most of my leisure time clearing out my email inbox notifying of ever-increasing downloads from this blog. I've even acquired 7 or 8 new members in my absence. Hmmmm.... maybe I should just keep quiet.</div>
<div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: left;">
&nbsp;</div>
<div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: left;">
There was a time in the not so distant past when comebacks were frowned upon and were normally the reserve for burned out heavy metal bands who were forced into earning a living again due to unexpected tax bills or having their homes repossessed due to monies squandered on excessive drug usage.</div>
<div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: left;">
&nbsp;</div>
<div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: left;">
But after many sleepless nights pacing the floorboards and&nbsp;writhing feverishly in contemplation and indecision on sweat soaked sheets, my decision was swayed when one night a bearded&nbsp;apparition swathed in golden light appeared at the foot of my bed.</div>
<div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: left;">
&nbsp;</div>
<div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: left;">
"Young fawn!", the spirit asserted. "I hearby command you to post the Mandrake Paddle Steamer session. I realise it received a limited vinyl released a few years back, but here in the afterlife there are no turntables. I've not heard the session snce the first night of broadcast and will not rest until I have done so. Failure to comply and I will quite happily - and, for that matter,&nbsp;mercilessly - unleash the ghosts of my dead dogs upon ye!"</div>
<div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: left;">
&nbsp;</div>
<div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: left;">
"Y-y-y-yes, Your Greatness", I stammered as the incandescent figure&nbsp;dematerialised into the windswept, crepuscular night.</div>
<div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: left;">
&nbsp;</div>
<div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: left;">
So here he have a whopping ten sessions, two from each decade of Uncle John's broadcasting career,&nbsp;that are 100% guaranteed to eat up precious disk space on your hard drive.&nbsp;And if that wasn't more than enough, at the bottom of the post you will find ten links to upgraded sessions&nbsp;- including, at long last, a pristine-ish upgrade of Bogshed's fourth session from 1987 and Big Flames debut from 1984. For zydeco fans, the John Delafose 1992 session for Andy Kershaw is now complete with all six tracks.</div>
<div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: left;">

&nbsp;</div>
<div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
<a href="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/-vDGRTqDqrUc/VEuETU-x8RI/AAAAAAAAB_s/FEuJH6-bqMk/s1600/10726308_10152509486832903_403801538_n.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;"><img border="0" src="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/-vDGRTqDqrUc/VEuETU-x8RI/AAAAAAAAB_s/FEuJH6-bqMk/s1600/10726308_10152509486832903_403801538_n.jpg" height="215" width="320" /></a></div>
<div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
&nbsp;</div>
<div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: left;">
&nbsp;</div>
<div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: left;">
It doesn't seem possible that a whole decade has elapsed since the passing of John Peel. While his former colleagues are being exposed as paedos and pervs, Peel's legacy remains undiminished and will&nbsp;quite possibly inspire future generations of musicians with the misfortune of being too young&nbsp;to have had the pleasure of listening to his shows first time around.</div>
<div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: left;">
&nbsp;</div>
<div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: left;">
So here we have the fourth - and final - Peelfest!, kicking off with two&nbsp;much-bootlegged-but-still-commercially-unreleased Syd-era Pink Floyd sessions&nbsp;from 1967, Peel's first year of broadcasting at Radio 1. Ideally,&nbsp;it would have&nbsp;been fitting&nbsp;to end this post with a session from 2004, but&nbsp;all I&nbsp;had&nbsp;from that year was The Magic Band's set which is already available @ <a href="http://lifehassurfacenoise.blogspot.co.uk/2012/02/john-peel-magic-band-070704.html" target="_blank">Life Has Surface Noise</a>&nbsp;(along with the <a href="http://lifehassurfacenoise.blogspot.co.uk/2011/12/john-peel-magic-band-atp-050403.html" target="_blank">ATP set from April 2003)</a>, so it seems pointless duplicating it here.</div>
<div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: left;">
&nbsp;</div>
<div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: left;">
And so it seems&nbsp;appropriate to call time on my life as a blogger and consign Maggot Caviar to the flotsam of cyberspace, until rediscovered a hundred years from now by a futuristic music obsessive commuting to work by jetpack, whose genetically-modified cerebellum with built in Wi-Fi accidently stumbles across these archaic but perfectly preserved pages.</div>
<div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: left;">
&nbsp;</div>
<div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: left;">
Nostalgia be damned!</div>
<div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: left;">
&nbsp;</div>
<div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: left;">
&nbsp;But as Shaw Taylor used to say on Police 5: Keep 'em Peeled!</div>

<h3 class='post-title entry-title' itemprop='name'>
<a href='http://maggotcaviar.blogspot.com/2014/10/rig-veeda-twins-tale-of-man-with.html'>Rig Veeda & The Twins - The Tale of the Man With The Toothpaste Head (LP 1986)</a>
</h3>
<div class='post-header'>
<div class='post-header-line-1'></div>
</div>
<div class='post-body entry-content' id='post-body-766515483288644993' itemprop='description articleBody'>
<div align="center">
<a href="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/-d6iuRH4UX6k/VEQ44ZzzZCI/AAAAAAAAB-M/wb4DySJg2uE/s1600/front.png" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;"><img border="0" src="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/-d6iuRH4UX6k/VEQ44ZzzZCI/AAAAAAAAB-M/wb4DySJg2uE/s1600/front.png" /></a></div>
<div align="center">
&nbsp;</div>
<div align="center">
&nbsp;</div>
<div align="center">
&nbsp;</div>
<div style="text-align: left;">
Trawling through the extensive and comprehensive Cale archives over the windy weekend, I stumbled upon this here cult classic curio.&nbsp;&nbsp;Despite the name giving the impression of a mystic frontman backed by identical siblings, they were in fact two different groups: The Twins featured Paul Rixon from The McTells and&nbsp;Bi-Joopiter co-founder&nbsp;Gillian Elam; Rig Veeda were the aforementioned duo&nbsp;accompanied by the surname-secretive David and Lesley. </div>
<div style="text-align: left;">
&nbsp;</div>
<div style="text-align: left;">
No mere McTells side project(s) for rejected ideas in the group proper, Rig Veeda issued their debut cassette on Bi-Joopiter&nbsp;as far back as 1983. The Twins issued to two cassette-only albums - A Banana Fish On The Moon and Boy Saves Hedgehog - in 1986 and '87 respectively. In between those releases this vinyl album came out on Illusion Production, a French label with a roster that included The Legendary Pink Dots and DDAA, and whose sporadic releases continue to this day.</div>
<div style="text-align: left;">
&nbsp;</div>
<div style="text-align: left;">
The McTells performed at the C86 Launch Party in London back in June, and with that ongoing revival largely being documented by Cherry Red, it is tempting to fantasise about a Bi-Joopiter back catalogue box set materialising in physical form(at) one day in the not too distant future.</div>
<div style="text-align: left;">
&nbsp;</div>
<div style="text-align: left;">
Until then, give this album a virtual spin and marvel at more innocent times when lo-fi was being created before the genre was indelibly tagged.</div>
<div style="text-align: left;">
&nbsp;</div>
<div style="text-align: left;">
&nbsp;</div>
<div align="center">

<div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">

<a href='http://maggotcaviar.blogspot.com/2014/09/the-wolfhounds-1988-peel-session-upgrade.html'>The Wolfhounds - 1988 Peel Session Upgrade</a>
</h3>
<div class='post-header'>
<div class='post-header-line-1'></div>
</div>
<div class='post-body entry-content' id='post-body-145743951520319849' itemprop='description articleBody'>
<div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
<a href="http://1.bp.blogspot.com/-VPYhq81VvFg/VCGheip2mFI/AAAAAAAAB8Q/dDSGMnORoKQ/s1600/untitled.png" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;"><img border="0" src="http://1.bp.blogspot.com/-VPYhq81VvFg/VCGheip2mFI/AAAAAAAAB8Q/dDSGMnORoKQ/s1600/untitled.png" /></a></div>
<div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
&nbsp;</div>
<div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
.....Or 75% upgrade to be exact.</div>
<div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
&nbsp;</div>
<div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: left;">
&nbsp;</div>
<div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: left;">
The Wolfhounds third (and final) session was Peel was aired recently on Marc Riley's 6 Music show, and yours truly was on hand to capture the sonic delights for posterity. IN-FUUUR-REEE-A-TING-LEEEE&nbsp;Non-Specific Song wasn't played,&nbsp;so we're stuck with the tinny file&nbsp;from the original post.</div>
<div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: left;">
&nbsp;</div>
<div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: left;">
Retail therapy is bestowed upon Wolfhounds fans worldwide this winter with the release of the group's first album since 1990. Middle Aged Freaks, a 13 track cd to be issued by Oddbox in October, collects all of the recordings the group have made since reuniting, including all tracks from the three limited edition 7" singles. Available to pre-order now from <a href="https://oddboxrecords.bandcamp.com/album/middle-aged-freaks-2" target="_blank">Oddbox.</a></div>
<div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: left;">
&nbsp;</div>
<div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: left;">
And if that wasn't enough spontaneous auto combustion-inducing excitement for you, 1987 debut Unseen Ripples From A Pebble receives the expanded deluxe reissue treatment this November. Available on cd for the first time ever, there will also be a double vinyl version, each format featuring 15 bonus tracks. Details from <a href="http://opticnerverecordings.com/unseen-ripples-from-pebble-p-49.html?osCsid=euqufkkd53ddnd8m1a3v6qcdn6" target="_blank">Optic Nerve.</a></div>
<div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: left;">

<a href="https://app.box.com/s/xxqjzj8u8pkghhbyegsf" target="_blank">Peel 1/2/1988</a></div>
<div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
