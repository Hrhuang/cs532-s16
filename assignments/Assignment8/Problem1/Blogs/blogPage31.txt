<h3 class="review-author headline">
            <span style="font-family: Georgia,&quot;Times New Roman&quot;,serif;"><span style="font-size: small;">AllMusic Review
                            by <span itemprop="author">Linda Woods</span>&nbsp;</span><a class="read-more-headline" href="http://www.allmusic.com/album/tilt-o-whirl-mw0000063234#"></a></span></h3>
<span style="font-family: Georgia,&quot;Times New Roman&quot;,serif;">

        </span><div class="text" itemprop="reviewBody" style="padding-bottom: 35px;">
<span style="font-family: Georgia,&quot;Times New Roman&quot;,serif;">
                            </span>
                    <span style="font-family: Georgia,&quot;Times New Roman&quot;,serif;"><a class="album-link" href="http://www.allmusic.com/album/tilt-o-whirl-mw0000063234">Tilt O' Whirl</a> exposes <a class="name-link" href="http://www.allmusic.com/artist/luke-doucet-mn0000264727">Luke Doucet</a>
 for what he really is: a guitar god. Long before he saddled up and 
said, "Aloha, Manitoba," he was the backbone of Vancouver-based band <a class="name-link" href="http://www.allmusic.com/artist/veal-mn0000314168">Veal</a>. There are indicators that <a class="name-link" href="http://www.allmusic.com/artist/luke-mn0000264727">Luke</a>
 has a more folky side to him on this record, namely the acoustic 
"Buttercloud." The follow-up to its initial release, Hot Loser, <a class="album-link" href="http://www.allmusic.com/album/tilt-o-whirl-mw0000063234">Tilt O' Whirl</a> is when <a class="name-link" href="http://www.allmusic.com/artist/veal-mn0000314168">Veal</a>
 honed the band's distinctive sound. Very representative of their live 
show, the album is packed with energy and hooky singalong songs. Three 
standout tracks punctuate the album: "Spiderman," "Skid," and "Pinkos." 
"Spiderman"'s chorus is one of the best hooks on the record -- this, 
along with the fabulous guitar riffs make this the number one radio hit 
that everyone missed. The only thing detracting from this song is the 
questionable production at the end, which may lead listeners to believe 
that their CD player is having a conniption fit. Worry not. "Skid" is 
another single that radio overlooked -- a great summertime song. Playful
 lyrics on the subject of seeking revenge on someone for name calling --
 apparently no one in the band has heard the old "sticks and stones" 
adage. It's a good thing -- otherwise you would have missed out on this 
song that brings a smile to the face of anyone who has ever wanted to 
smash a cell phone. "Pinkos" sounds a bit like what it would sound like 
if <a class="name-link" href="http://www.allmusic.com/artist/david-byrne-mn0000197364">David Byrne</a> and <a class="name-link" href="http://www.allmusic.com/artist/the-rheostatics-mn0000890350">the Rheostatics</a> collaborated on a song. The latter can likely be contributed to the fact that <a class="name-link" href="http://www.allmusic.com/artist/michael-phillip-wojewoda-mn0000468204">Michael Phillip Wojewoda</a> produced this record, after having produced five <a class="name-link" href="http://www.allmusic.com/artist/rheostatics-mn0000890350">Rheostatics</a> records. A great overall listen -- this record shows why <a class="name-link" href="http://www.allmusic.com/artist/veal-mn0000314168">Veal</a> was considered the West Coast's "buzziest" band shortly after this album's release.&nbsp;</span><br />

        <b><span style="color: #3d85c6;"></span></b></span><br />
<section class="biography" itemprop="review" itemscope="" itemtype="http://schema.org/Review"><div class="text" itemprop="reviewBody">
<span style="font-family: &quot;georgia&quot; , &quot;times new roman&quot; , serif;">
                            </span>
                    
                    <span style="font-family: &quot;georgia&quot; , &quot;times new roman&quot; , serif;"><a class="name-link" href="http://www.allmusic.com/artist/see-saw-mn0001266873">See Saw</a> (also known as <a class="name-link" href="http://www.allmusic.com/artist/sea-saw-mn0001266873">Sea Saw</a>) was the four-track indie-pop project of singer/songwriter <a class="name-link" href="http://www.allmusic.com/artist/trevor-kampmann-mn0001832793">Trevor Kampmann</a>,
 a former teen actor who made guest appearances on television programs 
including Little House on the Prairie, Who's the Boss? and Charles in 
Charge. After flirting with a career as a professional skateboarder, 
Kampmann drifted across the country before settling in Washington, D.C. 
and landing work as a computer graphics designer. Upon recording his 
first batch of songs, Kampmann released <a class="name-link" href="http://www.allmusic.com/artist/see-saws-mn0001266873">See Saw's</a> 1995 debut <a class="album-link" href="http://www.allmusic.com/album/and-its-head-popped-off-mw0000972966">...And its head popped off</a> on the Arlington, Virginia-based indie label Simple Machines. Following the release of 1996's <a class="album-link" href="http://www.allmusic.com/album/magnetophone-mw0000984421">Magnetophone</a>, issued under the name <a class="name-link" href="http://www.allmusic.com/artist/sea-saw-mn0001266873">Sea Saw</a>, Kampmann announced that future releases would appear under the Magnetophone aegis, although he instead recorded primarily as <a class="name-link" href="http://www.allmusic.com/artist/holland-mn0000958916">hollAnd</a>.&nbsp;</span><br />

<h3 class="headline">
            <span style="font-family: Georgia,&quot;Times New Roman&quot;,serif;"><span style="font-size: small;">Artist Biography
                            by <span itemprop="author">Alex Ogg</span></span>
                    </span></h3>
<span style="font-family: Georgia,&quot;Times New Roman&quot;,serif;">

        
                            </span>
                                            <br />
<div class="inline-image odd">
                            <span style="font-family: Georgia,&quot;Times New Roman&quot;,serif;"><a class="album-cover-link crop-image-borders" href="http://www.allmusic.com/album/formaldehyde-mw0000382427" style="height: 200px; width: 200px;" title="Formaldehyde"><img alt="Formaldehyde" class="lazy" data-original="http://cps-static.rovicorp.com/3/JPG_250/MI0001/771/MI0001771030.jpg?partner=allrovi.com" height="201" src="http://cps-static.rovicorp.com/3/JPG_250/MI0001/771/MI0001771030.jpg?partner=allrovi.com" style="display: inline;" width="201" /></a></span>                        </div>
<span style="font-family: Georgia,&quot;Times New Roman&quot;,serif;">
                    
                    Alongside <a class="name-link" href="http://www.allmusic.com/artist/new-model-army-mn0000333766">New Model Army</a> and a fertile bhangra scene, <a class="name-link" href="http://www.allmusic.com/artist/terrorvision-mn0000022698">Terrorvision</a>

capturing a U.K. number one single in 1999, there was every reason for 
them to be a source of local pride. After abandoning the name Spoilt 
Bratz in 1991, <a class="name-link" href="http://www.allmusic.com/artist/terrorvision-mn0000022698">Terrorvision</a> (named after a cult B-movie) formed around vocalist <a class="name-link" href="http://www.allmusic.com/artist/tony-wright-mn0001270346">Tony Wright</a>, guitarist <a class="name-link" href="http://www.allmusic.com/artist/mark-yates-mn0000630051">Mark Yates</a>, bass player <a class="name-link" href="http://www.allmusic.com/artist/leigh-marklew-mn0000629706">Leigh Marklew</a>, and drummer <a class="name-link" href="http://www.allmusic.com/artist/shutty-mn0002308807">Shutty</a>.
 A single demo tape was enough to convince EMI Records to offer them not
 only a deal, but their own imprint, Total Vegas. A succession of albums
 followed, melding pop hooks with metal guitars (akin to a harsher <a class="name-link" href="http://www.allmusic.com/artist/cheap-trick-mn0000105354">Cheap Trick</a>). These included <a class="album-link" href="http://www.allmusic.com/album/formaldehyde-mw0000382427">Formaldehyde</a> (1992), <a class="album-link" href="http://www.allmusic.com/album/how-to-make-friends-and-influence-people-mw0000546592">How to Make Friends and Influence People</a> (1994), and <a class="album-link" href="http://www.allmusic.com/album/regular-urban-survivors-mw0000377797">Regular Urban Survivors</a>
 (1996). These spawned a series of chart appearances for singles such as
 "New Policy One," "Pretend Best Friend," the excellent "Alice, What's 
the Matter?" and "Perseverance." The latter took them into the U.K. Top 
Five, illustrating their broadening commercial appeal. That was 
confirmed with the release of 1998's <a class="album-link" href="http://www.allmusic.com/album/shaving-peaches-mw0000709865">Shaving Peaches</a>, a heady mix of whacked-out pop and rock piledrivers with a real dumb charm that some compared to <a class="name-link" href="http://www.allmusic.com/artist/the-ramones-mn0000490004">the Ramones</a>.                 
                            </span>
                                            <br />
<div class="inline-image even">
                                                    </div>
<span style="font-family: Georgia,&quot;Times New Roman&quot;,serif;">
                    
                    Despite attendant single "Tequila" topping the charts, <a class="name-link" href="http://www.allmusic.com/artist/terrorvision-mn0000022698">Terrorvision</a>
 were nevertheless dropped by EMI before the end of the millennium. 
However, their own label scored a working relationship with Papillon in 
May 2000. A sixth appearance on the Never Mind the Buzzcocks Christmas 
special soon followed, prior to the band returning to the studio the 
next year. Fifth album <a class="album-link" href="http://www.allmusic.com/album/good-to-go-mw0000967474">Good to Go</a>, which sparked the hit single "D'Ya Wanna Go Faster?," appeared in February 2001. Sadly, after a string of spring shows, <a class="name-link" href="http://www.allmusic.com/artist/terrorvision-mn0000022698">Terrorvision</a>'s
 13-year career came to a halt when the band disbanded in June. A small 
farewell tour in the U.K. was scheduled for late fall to coincide the 
release of the retrospective <a class="album-link" href="http://www.allmusic.com/album/whales-dolphins-mw0000464879">Whales &amp; Dolphins</a> in September. The band re-formed for a smattering of live shows between 2005 and 2009, and in 2011 <a class="name-link" href="http://www.allmusic.com/artist/tony-wright-mn0001270346">Tony Wright</a> announced that <a class="name-link" href="http://www.allmusic.com/artist/terrorvision-mn0000022698">Terrorvision</a> would be releasing a new album in 2011.</span><b><span style="font-family: Georgia, &quot;Times New Roman&quot;, serif;"> </span></b></div>

        <h3 class="headline">
            <span style="font-family: Georgia,&quot;Times New Roman&quot;,serif;"><span style="font-size: small;">Artist Biography
                            by <span itemprop="author">Stephen Cramer</span></span>
                    </span></h3>
<span style="font-family: Georgia,&quot;Times New Roman&quot;,serif;">

        </span><div class="text" itemprop="reviewBody">
<span style="font-family: Georgia,&quot;Times New Roman&quot;,serif;">
                            </span><span style="font-family: Georgia,&quot;Times New Roman&quot;,serif;">
                    
                    Calgary, Alberta's <a class="name-link" href="http://www.allmusic.com/artist/hot-little-rocket-mn0000701495">Hot Little Rocket</a> were a melodic yet abrasive power pop/punk band. Guitarist <a class="name-link" href="http://www.allmusic.com/artist/aaron-smelski-mn0001300058">Aaron Smelski</a> and bassist <a class="name-link" href="http://www.allmusic.com/artist/mark-macarthur-mn0001312169">Mark MacArthur</a>, veterans in the Calgary scene, recruited guitarist <a class="name-link" href="http://www.allmusic.com/artist/andrew-wedderburn-mn0001297070">Andrew Wedderburn</a> in a classified ad. Drummer <a class="name-link" href="http://www.allmusic.com/artist/joel-nye-mn0001617532">Joel Nye</a>
 already had a long history of drumming for various bands in 
Saskatchewan, even before he joined the band. Their debut, Laika, came 
out in March 2000. Recorded by Marc Schroder and mixed by <a class="name-link" href="http://www.allmusic.com/artist/dave-alcock-mn0000958010">Dave Alcock</a>, the disc was well received in their homeland and abroad. The follow-up, <a class="album-link" href="http://www.allmusic.com/album/danish-documentary-mw0000517954">Danish Documentary</a>,
 was unveiled in September 2001. Recorded by Tylor Scollon, the album 
cemented their strong position in the Calgary music community. Chris 
Vail added keyboards and extra vocals, while Barb Noad added extra 
vocals as well.</span><br />

<h3 class='post-title entry-title'>
<a href='http://ihatethe90s.blogspot.com/2016/03/lisa-germano-geek-girl-1994.html'>LISA GERMANO Geek the Girl 1994</a>
</h3>
<div class='post-header'>
<div class='post-header-line-1'></div>
</div>
<div class='post-body entry-content' id='post-body-3183340657235488540'>
<div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
</div>
<div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
</div>
<div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
<a href="https://4.bp.blogspot.com/-EJQ-fS5cZnA/Vvwb7X5RGiI/AAAAAAAAMpE/lQZY0BhfPXg_gWQBaykRig8eJiZ-0uA3w/s1600/cover.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;"><img border="0" height="320" src="https://4.bp.blogspot.com/-EJQ-fS5cZnA/Vvwb7X5RGiI/AAAAAAAAMpE/lQZY0BhfPXg_gWQBaykRig8eJiZ-0uA3w/s320/cover.jpg" width="320" /></a></div>
<br />
<br /><a href="https://www.discogs.com/Lisa-Germano-Geek-The-Girl/release/1082305" target="_blank"><span style="font-family: Georgia,&quot;Times New Roman&quot;,serif;"><b>Discogs</b></span></a><br />
<br />
<h3 class="headline">
            <span style="font-family: Georgia,&quot;Times New Roman&quot;,serif;"><span style="font-size: small;">Artist Biography
                            by <span itemprop="author">MacKenzie Wilson</span></span>
                    </span></h3>
<span style="font-family: Georgia,&quot;Times New Roman&quot;,serif;">

        
                            </span>
                    
                    <span style="font-family: Georgia,&quot;Times New Roman&quot;,serif;"><a class="name-link" href="http://www.allmusic.com/artist/lisa-germano-mn0000299500">Lisa Germano</a>
 is one of those artists who's never belonged to one core crowd. Doing 
the big rock thing wasn't important to her, although time spent playing 
with <a class="name-link" href="http://www.allmusic.com/artist/john-mellencamp-mn0000224186">John Mellencamp</a> in the late '80s and early '90s opened her eyes to the ever-changing ways of the music business. <a class="name-link" href="http://www.allmusic.com/artist/germano-mn0000299500">Germano</a> worked her craft without becoming a conformist; music is her soul and writing is her fuel.                </span><br />
<span style="font-family: Georgia,&quot;Times New Roman&quot;,serif;">
                            </span><span style="font-family: Georgia,&quot;Times New Roman&quot;,serif;">
                    
                    Born in Mishawaka, Indiana, at the dawn of 1958, <a class="name-link" href="http://www.allmusic.com/artist/germano-mn0000299500">Germano</a>
 was the middle of six kids. Her parents were teachers and musicians who
 encouraged their children to play an instrument until they turned 18. 
At the age of seven, she wrote her first piece of music, a 15-minute 
opera on the piano and, in due time, learned the violin, an instrument 
that would ultimately guide her to a professional career in music. She 
made her debut as John Mellencamp's violinist and fiddle player on his 
1987 hit album Lonesome Jubilee. Germano would spend the next seven 
years with Mellencamp, shaping a smooth, fearless playing style. 
Additional tours and recording sessions with Simple Minds and the Indigo
 Girls motivated Germano to do something on her own. She found her voice
 at the age of 30 and developed a quirky sound rooted in folk-rock and 
poetry. The lo-fi, shadowy On the Way Down from the Moon Palace was 
issued on her own Major Bill label in 1991, marking Germano's proper 
introduction as a solo artist.                 </span><br />
<span style="font-family: Georgia,&quot;Times New Roman&quot;,serif;">
                            </span><span style="font-family: Georgia,&quot;Times New Roman&quot;,serif;">
                    
                    Happiness was much more melancholy and acerbic when 
it appeared two years later on Capitol. Sales were fair, but Germano 
didn't care for the major-label buzz, so she signed with Ivo 
Watts-Russell's 4AD in 1994 and reissued a new version of the album. 
(4AD eventually included the fantastically dramatic Inconsiderate Bitch 
EP with its re-release in 1999.) Geek the Girl, which also appeared 
before the year's end, captured Germano's secrets of a wavering 
self-concept and her disgust for social ignorance. This particular album
 of sexual conflicts earned Germano her biggest praise from the press to
 date. People started to connect with her, and that's exactly what 
Germano wanted. Excerpts from a Love Circus was gushing in adoration 
when it arrived in 1996, but critics didn't take to this glossy body of 
work. The emotional double-cross of Slide (1998) didn't do as well, 
either, so Germano took some time off. Within months, she lost her deal 
with 4AD. She questioned her place in music and basically vowed to never
 make another record.                 </span><br />
<span style="font-family: Georgia,&quot;Times New Roman&quot;,serif;">
                            </span>
                                            <br />
<div class="inline-image odd">
                            <span style="font-family: Georgia,&quot;Times New Roman&quot;,serif;"><a class="album-cover-link crop-image-borders" href="http://www.allmusic.com/album/lullaby-for-liquid-pig-mw0000592707" style="height: 200px; width: 200px;" title="Lullaby for Liquid Pig"><img alt="Lullaby for Liquid Pig" class="lazy" data-original="http://cps-static.rovicorp.com/3/JPG_250/MI0001/577/MI0001577430.jpg?partner=allrovi.com" height="201" src="http://cps-static.rovicorp.com/3/JPG_250/MI0001/577/MI0001577430.jpg?partner=allrovi.com" style="display: inline;" width="201" /></a></span>                        </div>
<span style="font-family: Georgia,&quot;Times New Roman&quot;,serif;">
                    
                    A move to Hollywood for a simple kind of life gave <a class="name-link" href="http://www.allmusic.com/artist/germano-mn0000299500">Germano</a>
 a much needed break. She took a job as a clerk at Book Soup and enjoyed
 her anonymity. Songwriting remained an integral part of her life, but 
having it be a means to making a living wasn't a concern. Joining other 
artists such as <a class="name-link" href="http://www.allmusic.com/artist/david-bowie-mn0000531986">David Bowie</a>, <a class="name-link" href="http://www.allmusic.com/artist/anna-waronker-mn0000484120">Anna Waronker</a>, and <a class="name-link" href="http://www.allmusic.com/artist/neil-finn-mn0000393768">Neil Finn</a> on their respective projects kept <a class="name-link" href="http://www.allmusic.com/artist/germano-mn0000299500">Germano</a> connected. Luckily for fans, <a class="name-link" href="http://www.allmusic.com/artist/germano-mn0000299500">Germano</a> inked a deal with the ARTISTdirect imprint Ineffable in 2002. <a class="name-link" href="http://www.allmusic.com/artist/germano-mn0000299500">Germano</a>'s classic, twisted sense of humor was at its finest on <a class="album-link" href="http://www.allmusic.com/album/lullaby-for-liquid-pig-mw0000592707">Lullaby for Liquid Pig</a>, which was issued in April 2003. Her music found another new home, <a class="name-link" href="http://www.allmusic.com/artist/michael-gira-mn0000465387">Michael Gira</a>'s Young God label, where <a class="name-link" href="http://www.allmusic.com/artist/germano-mn0000299500">Germano</a> released <a class="album-link" href="http://www.allmusic.com/album/in-the-maybe-world-mw0000425306">In the Maybe World</a> in summer 2006. After touring in support of the album, <a class="name-link" href="http://www.allmusic.com/artist/germano-mn0000299500">Germano</a> took a break, then began recording her second Young God album, <a class="album-link" href="http://www.allmusic.com/album/magic-neighbor-mw0000825096">Magic Neighbor</a>, which arrived in fall 2009. Around that time, <a class="name-link" href="http://www.allmusic.com/artist/germano-mn0000299500">Germano</a> also collaborated with <a class="name-link" href="http://www.allmusic.com/artist/neil-finn-mn0000393768">Neil Finn</a> on his <a class="name-link" href="http://www.allmusic.com/artist/7-worlds-collide-mn0001591715">7 Worlds Collide</a> project, which also featured members of <a class="name-link" href="http://www.allmusic.com/artist/soul-coughing-mn0000034899">Soul Coughing</a>, <a class="name-link" href="http://www.allmusic.com/artist/radiohead-mn0000326249">Radiohead</a>, and <a class="name-link" href="http://www.allmusic.com/artist/wilco-mn0000254215">Wilco</a>. <a class="album-link" href="http://www.allmusic.com/album/no-elephants-mw0002475616">No Elephants</a>, <a class="name-link" href="http://www.allmusic.com/artist/germano-mn0000299500">Germano</a>'s 11th studio album, arrived in early 2013.                 </span><br />

<section class="biography" itemprop="review" itemscope="" itemtype="http://schema.org/Review">

        <h3 class="headline">
            <span style="font-family: Georgia,&quot;Times New Roman&quot;,serif;"><span style="font-size: small;">Artist Biography
                            by <span itemprop="author">Stewart Mason</span></span>
                    </span></h3>
<span style="font-family: Georgia,&quot;Times New Roman&quot;,serif;">

        </span><div class="text" itemprop="reviewBody">
<span style="font-family: Georgia,&quot;Times New Roman&quot;,serif;">
                            </span><span style="font-family: Georgia,&quot;Times New Roman&quot;,serif;">
                    
                    Less a true band than a loose collective of 
experimental improvisers and sonic explorers, Kent, OH-based Fuzzhead 
has no permanent members, save leader/philosopher Bill Weita. Since the 
mid-'80s, Weita has led an ever-shifting lineup (the sleeve photo of one
 album features a group photo of literally dozens of people) through an 
occasionally bizarre mlange of Krautrock-style hypnotic rhythms, 
tuneful psych-folk, and blistering noise, like some sort of cross 
between Can, Glenn Branca, and Pearls Before Swine.                </span><br />
<span style="font-family: Georgia,&quot;Times New Roman&quot;,serif;">
                            </span><span style="font-family: Georgia,&quot;Times New Roman&quot;,serif;">
                    
                    Although primarily an improvisatory live act, 
Fuzzhead released several albums, including 1988's Fuzzhead Is Love 
(with an utterly warped reworking of Jonathan Richman's already odd 
"Pablo Picasso"), 1991's Technicolor Soul, 1993's What's Going On?, 
1995's El Saturn, and the excellently titled I Saw the Best Minds of My 
Generation Rock. Following that 1996 release, Fuzzhead retreated to 
their live-performances-only roots and Weita began releasing solo albums
 (featuring some members of Fuzzhead's ensemble), including 1997's 
Substance D and 2000's Subsidized Time.&nbsp;</span><br />

<h2 class='title'>REQUESTS How To NEW RULES</h2>
<div class='widget-content'>
Please email to ihatethe90s [at] gmail [dot] com to make your request or use the dropdown box below to email me. One request per month, please. Before making a request, please make sure that the release hasn't already been posted. Use the search box at the top left of the page. No religious music please. Contributors can request up to 5 per month. All requests must be for releases between 1990 and 1999. Music must originally be released between 1990 and 1999. Other requests for releases beyond these years will no longer be honored. Do not ask me to bend or break the rules. Thanks.<br />Contributors must contribute releases that are <span =""  style="color:#cc0000;"><b>not</b></span> posted on other blogs and preferably are from the CD wishlist located to your right. No fake emails. You don't have to enter your email in the Chatbox unless you want, but don't enter in fake email addresses.
