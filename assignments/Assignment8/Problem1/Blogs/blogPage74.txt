<div class='post hentry uncustomized-post-template' itemprop='blogPost' itemscope='itemscope' itemtype='http://schema.org/BlogPosting'>
<meta content='http://2.bp.blogspot.com/-Nqa-OfLxvho/U1UbhEroFeI/AAAAAAAACZc/dZwBrHjnSXM/s200/toddterje+cover.jpg' itemprop='image_url'/>

<h3 class='post-title entry-title' itemprop='name'>
<a href='http://sixeyes.blogspot.com/2014/04/todd-terje-its-album-time.html'>Todd Terje 'It's Album Time'</a>
</h3>
<div class='post-header'>
<div class='post-header-line-1'></div>
</div>
<div class='post-body entry-content' id='post-body-3783296882754908779' itemprop='description articleBody'>
<div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
<a href="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/-Nqa-OfLxvho/U1UbhEroFeI/AAAAAAAACZc/dZwBrHjnSXM/s1600/toddterje+cover.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="clear: left; float: left; margin-bottom: 1em; margin-right: 1em;"><img border="0" src="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/-Nqa-OfLxvho/U1UbhEroFeI/AAAAAAAACZc/dZwBrHjnSXM/s200/toddterje+cover.jpg" /></a></div>
If any of you have wondered why this blog has slipped away into one of the millions of dusty corners of the internet, today I came upon a prime example of 'what' caused this... Pitchfork's current golden boy, Todd Terje.<br />
<br />
&nbsp;All electronic, all disconnected... his songs off of &nbsp;<i>It's Album Time&nbsp;</i>exhibit a lack of nature, a lack of skin and bone, of fingers hitting, plucking, striking an instrument of wood and skin...they are disconnected from nature.

The copper wires, circuits and binary codes only strengthen the wall between natural and unreal. Oh, yes, there are rhythms and melodies, but no matter how sharp and clear they still carry a sterility borne of science's clinical heart and the cradle of technology from which they came.

There are more than enough robots and computerized circuits in our lives today, just let music breathe on its own.<br />
<br />
Will someone just pull the plug on this binary music and see if it can breathe on its own... <i>Please?</i><br />
<br />
Todd Terje's album is the sound of kidnapped acoustic music trapped in an iron lung of motherboards and drivers. Sounds to make the robots dance... I'll sit this one out.<br />
<br />

<h3 class='post-title entry-title' itemprop='name'>
<a href='http://sixeyes.blogspot.com/2014/04/alice-boman-new-song-what-with-video.html'>Alice Boman: New Song "What" with Video</a>
</h3>
<div class='post-header'>
<div class='post-header-line-1'></div>
</div>
<div class='post-body entry-content' id='post-body-8645071074105040627' itemprop='description articleBody'>
<a href="http://1.bp.blogspot.com/-C8d_Yte4eFw/U0vsxxKs-UI/AAAAAAAACYE/novAf2wLZMk/s1600/AliceBoman7_JohannaAttesson.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;"><img border="0" src="http://1.bp.blogspot.com/-C8d_Yte4eFw/U0vsxxKs-UI/AAAAAAAACYE/novAf2wLZMk/s1600/AliceBoman7_JohannaAttesson.jpg" /></a><br />
<br />
<div style="text-align: left;">
&nbsp;It's fantastic to hear new Alice Boman music, fantastic as it hasn't lost it's unique personality while still being a more 'hi-tech record' (she's moved on from intimate home recordings to a cabin in the woods).</div>
<div style="text-align: left;">
<br /></div>
<div style="text-align: left;">
&nbsp;Alice came to us as a soft baby bird you gather in your hands, watching it easily settle into it's new nest of fingers, skin and bone. That soft baby bird is still there, you can almost feel the feathers blossoming from her lips with a voice of quiet aching and wanting. Buttressed by an everpresent piano, her new song,"What",&nbsp;<i>will</i> give you shivers, blue shivers, up and down your willing spine. And feathers to tickle your blues away, or keep it company until the sun comes up.</div>
<br />
<br />
<div style="text-align: center;">
<object height="281" width="500"><param name="movie" value="//www.youtube.com/v/cgvVoG4FjD4?version=3&amp;hl=en_GB"></param>

<div style="text-align: center;">
<br /></div>
<div style="text-align: left;">
&nbsp;Alice's new EP, titled simply <i>EP II</i>, will be available June 3 via The Control Group in North America, Adrian Recordings in Scandinavia and Happy Death throughout the rest of Europe.</div>

</h3>
<div class='post-header'>
<div class='post-header-line-1'></div>
</div>
<div class='post-body entry-content' id='post-body-6783936059422520823' itemprop='description articleBody'>
<div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
<a href="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/-bTK4mFQHkYU/UcyOetr2FaI/AAAAAAAACLI/2nOYBxJ2FIk/s480/The_Bed-Low_Skies.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;"><img border="0" src="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/-bTK4mFQHkYU/UcyOetr2FaI/AAAAAAAACLI/2nOYBxJ2FIk/s1600/The_Bed-Low_Skies.jpg" /></a></div>
<br />
There is something odd about Low Skies' Christopher Salvater's voice&#8212;there seems to be a sad moan buried just beneath the surface. It claws it way closer to the light when in lower registers and when it climbs up from it's blue-bladed grave into falsetto, the weeping willows sway. Driven by a big and ragged guitar, Low Skies, "Sad Hymn" turns blue in all the right places.<br />
<br />
