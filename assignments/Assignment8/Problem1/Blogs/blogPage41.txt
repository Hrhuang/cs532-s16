<h3 class='post-title entry-title' itemprop='name'>
<a href='http://kidchair.blogspot.com/2015/12/top-25-songs-of-2015.html'>Top 25 Songs of 2015</a>
</h3>
<div class='post-header'>
<div class='post-header-line-1'></div>
</div>
<div class='post-body entry-content' id='post-body-8939874001977538676' itemprop='description articleBody'>
<div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
</div>
<div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
<a href="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/-fnZ98UArQQA/Vogh-ABSMvI/AAAAAAAADgI/r9Czjn7aHOE/s1600/banner2015.png" imageanchor="1" style="clear: left; float: left; margin-bottom: 1em; margin-right: 1em;"><img border="0" src="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/-fnZ98UArQQA/Vogh-ABSMvI/AAAAAAAADgI/r9Czjn7aHOE/s1600/banner2015.png" /></a></div>
<span style="font-size: large;"><b><i><br /></i></b></span>
<span style="font-size: large;"><b><i><br /></i></b></span><br />
<span style="font-size: large;"><b><i>Well, here come my favorite songs again. </i></b></span><br />
<br />
This year was chock full of tunes that really kicked me in the feel-goods. And lots of Beach House. I can't remember the last time I had such a hard time narrowing down what songs I should include. Which is interesting, because from an album perspective this wasn't the most impressive year to me. But individual songs kicked all sorts of azz. <br />
<br />
I mentioned Beach House. You'll notice they've got quite a few tunes here. That's what happens when you put out two FANTASTIC albums in a year. Some of you duders may not like when an artist has multiple entries on a list like this, but...I list what I truly loved. So suck it.<br />
<br />
That's about all I have to say about it I suppose. <br />
<br />
<b>Each song links to a video of it. Dig in.<br /><span style="font-size: large;">(Here's the rest if you want: <a href="http://kidchair.blogspot.com/2016/01/favorite-songs-of-2015-50-26.html" target="_blank">50-26</a>)</span></b><br />
<br />
<div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
</div>
<div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
</div>
<div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
<span style="clear: left; float: left; margin-bottom: 1em; margin-right: 1em; text-align: left;"><span style="font-size: x-large;"><img border="0" src="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/-fTguaiHCpTc/VoYVGRsSQaI/AAAAAAAADfI/HHCkk3VsIuE/s1600/25.jpg" />25\\<a href="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=rClUOdS5Zyw" target="_blank">Want to Want Me</a>\\by Jason Derulo</span><br />I was actually a little mad at how much I liked this song when I first heard it. How dare Jason Derulo make such a sugary sweet throwback nugget! And when he hits them high notes? It's just too much. I give up.</span></div>
<div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: left;">
<a href="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/-FY84wd8B1K8/VoYVBk7MRfI/AAAAAAAADe8/99pk5AyTH4Q/s1600/24.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="clear: left; float: left; margin-bottom: 1em; margin-right: 1em;"><img border="0" src="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/-FY84wd8B1K8/VoYVBk7MRfI/AAAAAAAADe8/99pk5AyTH4Q/s1600/24.jpg" /></a></div>
<div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: left;">
<span style="font-size: x-large;">24\\<a href="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=FVbupT0o0gQ" target="_blank">Ewok</a></span><span style="font-size: x-large;">\</span><span style="font-size: x-large;">\</span><span style="font-size: x-large;">by Kidkanevil ft. Ocean Wisdom</span></div>
<div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: left;">
Kidkanevil came out of nowhere to provide some of my favorite music last year after I found his album in the Tokyo Tower Records. I've been following his moves since then, and was blessed with this rambunctious number. I know nothing about Ocean Wisdom, but his raps over this nasty beat managed to impress in a year full of impressive rap. Plus, Star Wars.</div>
<br />
<div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: left;">
<br /></div>
<div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: left;">
<a href="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/-wsnBx3sCTGg/VoYU8LBVWEI/AAAAAAAADew/sV7nWoLy6zI/s1600/23.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="clear: left; float: left; margin-bottom: 1em; margin-right: 1em;"><img border="0" src="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/-wsnBx3sCTGg/VoYU8LBVWEI/AAAAAAAADew/sV7nWoLy6zI/s1600/23.jpg" /></a><span style="font-size: x-large;"><br /></span></div>
<div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: left;">
<span style="font-size: x-large;">23\\<a href="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=jpXSwIXA6YA" target="_blank">Dear Skorpio Magazine</a>\\by Neon Indian</span></div>
In an album full of Sega Genesis grooves, this one was surely the most funky. Sneaky sly lyrics, and them shimmery synths make for a helluva good time. I think I'll go play me some Sonic the Hedgehog now.<br />
<br />
<br />
<br />
<div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: left;">
<a href="http://1.bp.blogspot.com/-EdnjuyFGTLM/VoYU5PrgizI/AAAAAAAADek/U0avHUtGQ8Q/s1600/22.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="clear: left; float: left; margin-bottom: 1em; margin-right: 1em;"><img border="0" src="http://1.bp.blogspot.com/-EdnjuyFGTLM/VoYU5PrgizI/AAAAAAAADek/U0avHUtGQ8Q/s1600/22.jpg" /></a><span style="font-size: x-large;"><br /></span></div>
<div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: left;">
<span style="font-size: x-large;">22\\<a href="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=FIIZnZ-pgDI" target="_blank">In the Heat of the Moment</a>\\by Noel Gallagher's High Flying Birds</span></div>
Uncle Noel always seems to hit me where it counts. A prime example of the elder Gallagher's melodic prowess and straight forward goodness, this one just rolls right along like it's existed forever. And his voice has rarely sounded better. Another notch on the man's belt, for sure.<br />
<br />
<br />
<br />
<div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: left;">

When Daniel Johns first started sharing his first new music in 8 years, I'm not quite sure what I expected. Something along the lines of St. Vincent's "Birth In Reverse" would've made sense to me after Silverchair. But what we got instead were a few morsels of silky post-R&amp;B goodness like this. "Surrender" was the third track he shared from the&nbsp;<i>Aerial Love</i> EP, a sampling of what to expect on his first full-length,&nbsp;<i>Talk</i>. And though I was pleasantly surprised by the first two offerings, it was this one that truly had me spinning. Soulful and smoove, groovy and chill. It was hard to believe this was the same person. "Maybe I was ready all along" he repeats to the end. It's grand, it's inspiring, and yes... it's epic.<br />

<a href="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/-BS25fC16KA8/VoYTicq4LJI/AAAAAAAADas/7McILySC2bU/s1600/2.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="clear: left; float: left; margin-bottom: 1em; margin-right: 1em;"><img border="0" src="http://2.bp.blogspot.com/-BS25fC16KA8/VoYTicq4LJI/AAAAAAAADas/7McILySC2bU/s1600/2.jpg" /></a><span style="font-size: x-large;"><br /></span></div>
<div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: left;">
<span style="font-size: x-large;">2\\<a href="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=mMKP2Vcc6wA" target="_blank">Eugene</a>\\by Sufjan Stevens</span></div>
This song is a hymn. A subtle, honest reflection of the soul and small memories of summers in Eugene, Oregon. It's not all sad, mentions of a swim teacher calling Sufjan "Subaru" always makes me chuckle. But every line of "I just want to be near you" breaks my heart. Within its short length, we hear moments of nostalgia, sadness, doubt, fear, loneliness, regret, and praying to what he cannot see. This is a man I've never met, I don't know anything about him other than what I've read online or heard in interviews. And yet, every time I hear "Eugene," I want to cry right along with him. That's rare for me in music, to be so emotionally affected. And it's even more heartbreaking to hear that last line, and know we're not his intended audience: "What's the point of singing songs, if they'll never even hear you?"<br />
<br />
<br />
<br />
<br />
<div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: left;">
<a href="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/-VtNf2uQy4tw/VoYTcGym4SI/AAAAAAAADag/8qHbRgoEs0c/s1600/1.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="clear: left; float: left; margin-bottom: 1em; margin-right: 1em;"><img border="0" src="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/-VtNf2uQy4tw/VoYTcGym4SI/AAAAAAAADag/8qHbRgoEs0c/s1600/1.jpg" /></a><span style="font-size: x-large;"><br /></span></div>
<div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: left;">
<span style="font-size: x-large;">1\\<a href="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=WjNssEVlB6M" target="_blank">Gosh</a></span><span style="font-size: x-large;">\\by Jamie xx</span></div>
It starts pretty plainly. "Nothing too fancy about this thing..." I thought. For about two minutes you're wondering if this will go anywhere. And suddenly it does. The bass rumbles in, and a high pitched squeal drops from the sky like a meteor. And what initially seemed pretty plain and eventless slowly morphs into the most eventful thing all year. Suddenly you can see the cosmos. You can feel humanity. You can dance with God. Or is it "Gosh?" Suddenly a word to censor perceived blasphemy takes on a new meaning. "Oh my gosh! Easy, easy!" chants the samples. Apart from the out of body experience, my first thought upon hearing this tune was that I wanted to see it live. It's certainly rare that an electronic song can evoke such a reaction. But "Gosh" comes across as very alive, very organic, and very communal. When I hear it, I want nothing more than to join mankind in unified bliss and dance the night away. If you're gonna sample that link, do me a favor and make sure your headphones and/or speakers are worthy. Forever and ever, amen. May Gosh bless you.

<b><span style="font-size: small;">23 <br /> <span style="font-size: large;">The Smashing Pumpkins</span></span></b><i><span style="font-size: large;"><b><br /></b></span><span style="font-size: small;"><span style="font-size: large;">Monuments to an Elegy</span></span></i><br />
I have to admit I was a tad underwhelmed with <i>Monuments</i>. It&#8217;s adequately enjoyable, but I prefer my Pumpkins to be a tad more EPIC. I really loved <i>Oceania</i> and this just sort of feels like its b-sides. Still, every album Corgan puts out that isn&#8217;t &#8220;bad&#8221; is a sigh of relief these days. Keep it up! <br />
--&nbsp;<a href="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=UHMSDYtxsu4" target="_blank">Tiberius</a><br />

<br />
<br />

<b><span style="font-size: small;">22 <br /> <span style="font-size: large;">Coldplay</span></span></b><i><span style="font-size: large;"><b><br /></b></span><span style="font-size: small;"><span style="font-size: large;">Ghost Stories</span></span></i><br />
Hard to pinpoint this one. I felt like every other song was incredible, among the best they&#8217;ve made. Stuff like &#8220;<a href="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=BQeMxWjpr-Y" target="_blank">Midnight</a>&#8221;&nbsp; branched into new territory, while &#8220;<a href="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=cLhAoW8LiIE" target="_blank">Oceans</a>&#8221; was a blissful and nostalgic return to the <i>Parachutes</i> intimacy of yesterdecade. So, at least half of it was fantastic. Which is enough to merit a #22, I suppose. <br />
--&nbsp;<a href="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=gKM15TaKLUI" target="_blank">Ink</a><br />
<br />
<br />
<div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">

<b><span style="font-size: small;">21 <br /> <span style="font-size: large;">Hercules and Love Affair</span></span></b><i><span style="font-size: small;"><span style="font-size: large;"><br />Feast of the Broken Heart</span></span></i><br />
After hinting at it on the previous two, Andy Butler and friends finally offer an album full of 90&#8217;s house goodness. Not as euphoric as Disclosure&#8217;s romp the year before, but definitely a worthwhile party in the same vein. And just a little more gay. <br />
--&nbsp;<a href="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=JozUoRIbsEE" target="_blank">Do You Feel the Same?</a><br />
<br />
<br />
<br />
<div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
<a href="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/-5mqceksclSo/VNRqqKhq2OI/AAAAAAAADLg/DSZoBijN1zQ/s1600/nothing.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="clear: left; float: left; margin-bottom: 1em; margin-right: 1em;"><img border="0" src="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/-5mqceksclSo/VNRqqKhq2OI/AAAAAAAADLg/DSZoBijN1zQ/s1600/nothing.jpg" height="200" width="200" /></a></div>
<b><span style="font-size: small;">20 <br /> <span style="font-size: large;">Nothing</span></span></b><i><span style="font-size: large;"><b><br /></b></span><span style="font-size: small;"><span style="font-size: large;">Guilty of Everything</span></span></i><br />
Juiced up shoegaze goodness is always welcome in my house. A superbly fuzzy guitar album, filling some of that geetar void in my life. <br />
--&nbsp;<a href="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=H9UIdlXhrBQ" target="_blank">Dig</a><br />
<br />
<br />
<br />
<div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
<a href="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/-EyBq29UHtOI/VNRqxVP48TI/AAAAAAAADLo/pkE5C4KtQsk/s1600/lost-in-the-dream-300.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="clear: left; float: left; margin-bottom: 1em; margin-right: 1em;"><img border="0" src="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/-EyBq29UHtOI/VNRqxVP48TI/AAAAAAAADLo/pkE5C4KtQsk/s1600/lost-in-the-dream-300.jpg" height="200" width="200" /></a></div>
<b><span style="font-size: small;">19<br /> <span style="font-size: large;">The War On Drugs</span></span></b><i><b><span style="font-size: small;"><span style="font-size: large;"><br /></span></span></b><span style="font-size: small;"><span style="font-size: large;">Lost in the Dream</span></span></i><br />
The album meanders a bit more than I&#8217;d like, but I&#8217;ll be damned if the vibe put off on this record isn&#8217;t chill as chill could be. The power of its Springsteenian bliss didn&#8217;t quite hit until I put it on while strollin&#8217; Newport Beach in California. Definitely a good setting for its bright melancholia. <br />
--&nbsp;<a href="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=vkLOg252KRE" target="_blank">Under the Pressure</a><br />
<br />
<div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
<a href="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/-oH2xn660KiM/VNRq5_zj5bI/AAAAAAAADLw/L-5FNQbWxz4/s1600/Pains-of-Being-Pure-at-Heart-new-album-cover-300x300.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="clear: left; float: left; margin-bottom: 1em; margin-right: 1em;"><img border="0" src="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/-oH2xn660KiM/VNRq5_zj5bI/AAAAAAAADLw/L-5FNQbWxz4/s1600/Pains-of-Being-Pure-at-Heart-new-album-cover-300x300.jpg" height="200" width="200" /></a></div>
<b><span style="font-size: small;">18<br /> <span style="font-size: large;">The Pains of Being Pure At Heart</span></span></b><i><b><span style="font-size: small;"><span style="font-size: large;"><br /></span></span></b><span style="font-size: small;"><span style="font-size: large;">Days of Abandon</span></span></i><br />
I dig the pop leanings from the band this time around. Especially the female led tracks like the shimmering &#8220;<a href="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=_c8fkmzd8vY" target="_blank">Kelly</a>.&#8221; I&#8217;d take a full album of those! Good jorb, kids.<br />
--&nbsp;<a href="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=XTy3bSofqTM" target="_blank">Simple and Sure</a><br />
<br />
<br />
<br />
<div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
<a href="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/-hb_BQk-MZbo/VNRrKKeMGEI/AAAAAAAADL4/8xfuYd_bzTU/s1600/St_-Vincent-Album-Art.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="clear: left; float: left; margin-bottom: 1em; margin-right: 1em;"><img border="0" src="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/-hb_BQk-MZbo/VNRrKKeMGEI/AAAAAAAADL4/8xfuYd_bzTU/s1600/St_-Vincent-Album-Art.jpg" height="200" width="200" /></a></div>
<b><span style="font-size: small;">17<br /> <span style="font-size: large;">St. Vincent</span></span></b><i><span style="font-size: large;"><b><br /></b></span><span style="font-size: small;"><span style="font-size: large;">St. Vincent</span></span></i><br />
This album grooves and also rocks a little more than I was used to from her, and I totally dug it. Listening to it now, I realize I probably didn&#8217;t listen to it as much as I should have. Songs like &#8220;<a href="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=TAdARF4rGcQ" target="_blank">Birth In Reverse</a>&#8221; remind of what I imagine Daniel Johns would do solo. I&#8217;d love to hear those two tackle a song together. Stand back, David Byrne!<br />
--&nbsp;<a href="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=mVAxUMuhz98" target="_blank">Digital Witness</a><br />

The kind of fist pumping melodic guitar jams you can&#8217;t help but sing along to at the top of your lungs while driving down the street. Listen to those riffs on &#8220;<a href="http://youtu.be/3fkt91iye4c" target="_blank">Breaking Up</a>.&#8221;&nbsp; Or how about the Ramones rip &#8220;<a href="http://youtu.be/7H89R9K75eo" target="_blank">London Queen</a>?&#8221; Hell, &#8220;<a href="http://youtu.be/bZxyS_CAHnI" target="_blank">Hanging Around</a>&#8221; is the best Weezer song in ages. And don&#8217;t even get me started on the epic that is &#8220;<a href="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=5f5A4DnGtis" target="_blank">Famous</a>.&#8221; That jangly intro, that soaring chorus&#8230;you kidding me?!? FUHGETTABOUTIT. Sure, the lyrics are a tad empty. Purposely so, just like many a fantastic rock jam of the past where the goal is simply having a good time. And, sure, it&#8217;s a pop album at heart (&#8220;<a href="http://youtu.be/Km3agvlyRmM" target="_blank">Doing It</a>&#8221; is a spot-on Madonna by way of Haim tribute). But good, ol' fashioned rock hasn&#8217;t had the best showing the last few years. If it takes a chick like Charli to get it goin', I ain't complaining.<br />
<br />
<br />
<br /></div>
<br />
<div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
<a href="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/-Y794eTThUYY/VNT9mNj744I/AAAAAAAADOc/WbwyFs6GndA/s1600/FKA-twigs-LP1-artwork.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="clear: left; float: left; margin-bottom: 1em; margin-right: 1em;"><img border="0" src="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/-Y794eTThUYY/VNT9mNj744I/AAAAAAAADOc/WbwyFs6GndA/s1600/FKA-twigs-LP1-artwork.jpg" height="580" width="580" /></a></div>
<br />
<div>
<b>2</b><br />
<b><span style="font-size: small;"><span style="font-size: x-large;">FKA twigs &#8226;&nbsp;</span></span></b><span style="font-size: x-large;"><i>LP1</i></span></div>
<div>
<br />
<div class="p1">
I&#8217;m not the smartest man, but two things I know are true in this world: #1.) Some albums simply must be heard on a system with enough bass to shake your bones. #2.) as a regular consumer of music, there is no greater joy than hearing something new that completely takes your breath away on first listen. Listening to <i>LP1</i> reminded me of these very truths. #2 is no easy feat. It doesn&#8217;t happen as often as I&#8217;d like, that&#8217;s for sure. But this is easily one of the most fabulously produced albums I think I&#8217;ve ever heard. My immediate thought was that of a female James Blake (&#8220;Jane Blake?&#8221;). Aesthetically, there are many similarities between the two; the spine tingling quiet-but-emotive vocal presentation, the flawless production, a reverence for the space between the noise (and the impact it can have), and an uncanny ability of injecting warmth and soul into an otherwise cold and mechanical sound. Of course, Blake mostly produces his own stuff, while Twigs had some help from a few super-powered collaborators (a fact that shouldn&#8217;t be overlooked, cuz those guys made some magic here). But, the most distinct connection between the two is the feeling of hearing pop from the future. Like some big headed space goon beaming sounds into my brain exclaiming, &#8220;This is how we do in 3014.&#8221; It&#8217;s is the kind of album that you can&#8217;t come back from. I hear the crap on the radio and it sounds like the poundings of cave men compared to this.<br />
--&nbsp;<a href="http://youtu.be/O8yix8PZKlw" target="_blank">Pendulum</a>&nbsp; <br />
--&nbsp;<a href="http://youtu.be/iHp3FP4wbxQ" target="_blank">Hours</a><br />
--&nbsp;<a href="http://youtu.be/3yDP9MKVhZc" target="_blank">Two Weeks</a><br />
<br />
<br />
<br /></div>
<br />
<div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
<a href="http://1.bp.blogspot.com/-b8WKOh1ZGQw/VNT9nfeHXJI/AAAAAAAADO4/xaVKTz5VF78/s1600/wyeoak_shriek_cover.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="clear: left; float: left; margin-bottom: 1em; margin-right: 1em;"><img border="0" src="http://1.bp.blogspot.com/-b8WKOh1ZGQw/VNT9nfeHXJI/AAAAAAAADO4/xaVKTz5VF78/s1600/wyeoak_shriek_cover.jpg" height="580" width="580" /></a></div>
<br />
<div>
<b>1</b><br />
<b><span style="font-size: small;"><span style="font-size: x-large;">Wye Oak &#8226;&nbsp;</span></span></b><span style="font-size: x-large;"><i>Shriek</i></span></div>
<div>
<div class="p1">
Someone I don&#8217;t even know once told me &#8220;If you like Beach House, you&#8217;ll enjoy Wye Oak.&#8221; That person was correct. And though it&#8217;s practically a funkier <i>Teen Dream</i>, I was still sort of surprised <i>Shriek</i> ended up here. I had pegged <i>LP1</i> as my sure-fire #1 for much of the year, and was convinced it would take quite a behemoth to topple it. Yet, slow and steady, Wye Oak crept their way up the ladder as 2014 went on. The little album that could! Wasn&#8217;t until it came time to crown my favorites that I realized, &#8220;Waitaminutte, I think <i>Shriek</i> is actually my favorite album of the year&#8230; how &#8216;bout that!&#8221; The only reason it surprised me was because there really isn&#8217;t anything significantly mind-blowing about this album. There&#8217;s no new sounds that warp my brain, or life-affirming themes that change my world. It&#8217;s no <i>Bloom, </i>that&#8217;s for sure. But therein lies its true power&#8211;it&#8217;s simply the most immediately approachable, and endlessly enjoyable album I heard this year. I don&#8217;t have to wrap my head around how good this is, I can just hear it. More often than not, when I&#8217;d get in the car and pull out my iPod with nothing specific in mind to listen to, I found myself putting on <i>Shriek</i>. And it was always a good choice. The lyrics are understated but poetic (&#8220;<a href="http://youtu.be/tMOcpGKTh84" target="_blank">And in the telling of the story/I lose my way inside a prepositional phrase/I read his lips and I see glory/but what I hear is &#8220;be afraid&#8221;</a>), the instrumentation is minimal but lush, the grooves are understated but memorable, and Jenn Wasner&#8217;s voice is oh so pleasant. I think one of the album&#8217;s secret weapons are Wasner&#8217;s perfectly placed bass lines. Right away that bass hooks you with opening track &#8220;<a href="http://youtu.be/6j1HRBgboNQ" target="_blank">Before</a>,&#8221; and then flows right alongside a cloudy piano in the <a href="http://youtu.be/9Wz4rXraAgU" target="_blank">title-track</a> that follows. Of course, the synths are obviously integral as well. They bounce to perfection in &#8220;<a href="http://youtu.be/U1UUqrGn2B0" target="_blank">The Tower</a>.&#8221; But it&#8217;s when &#8220;<a href="http://youtu.be/k39M2TN5bTs" target="_blank">Sick Talk</a>&#8221; hits that every element involved seems to click in flawless unison. Definitely the overlooked centerpiece of the record, and an instant classic.&nbsp;</div>
<div class="p2">
<br /></div>
After all is said and done, the sounds captured here seem as though they were fine-tuned to my very liking. As pleasant as pleasant can be. Many people lamented the change in direction and lack of guitars. I had actually never listened to <i>Civilian</i> before this, but after falling for <i>Shriek</i>, I decided to go back and take a gander. And it&#8217;s ok I guess. But there&#8217;s too much guitar. And not enough euphoric, blissful, heavenly awesome. Shriek, FTW! Now, if only we can get these guys and Beach House to tour together&#8230;hot damn.</div>
</div>
</div>
</div>
