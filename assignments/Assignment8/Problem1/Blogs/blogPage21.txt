<a href='http://encorenorthernireland.blogspot.com/2016/03/ep-review-dusk-and-dawn-hurdles.html'>ep review ~ 'dusk and dawn' ~ hurdles</a>
</h3>
<div class='post-header'>
<div class='post-header-line-1'></div>
</div>
<div class='post-body entry-content' id='post-body-8028586238047512190' itemprop='description articleBody'>
<div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
<a href="https://3.bp.blogspot.com/-BEfANerss5U/VvJz4gORPfI/AAAAAAAACGk/aT5o0bQK6zg5VtYDIwY1rDZk0qNgQSewA/s1600/hurdles%2Bdusk.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;"><img border="0" height="400" src="https://3.bp.blogspot.com/-BEfANerss5U/VvJz4gORPfI/AAAAAAAACGk/aT5o0bQK6zg5VtYDIwY1rDZk0qNgQSewA/s400/hurdles%2Bdusk.jpg" width="398" /></a></div>
<br />
<span style="background-color: white; font-family: &quot;arial&quot; , &quot;tahoma&quot; , &quot;helvetica&quot; , &quot;freesans&quot; , sans-serif; font-size: 13px; line-height: 18.2px;"><i><b><span style="font-family: inherit;">Hurdles</span></b></i></span><br />
<span style="background-color: white; font-family: &quot;arial&quot; , &quot;tahoma&quot; , &quot;helvetica&quot; , &quot;freesans&quot; , sans-serif; font-size: 13px; line-height: 18.2px;"><i><b><span style="font-family: inherit;">For fans of<span style="color: #141823; line-height: 19.32px;">&#8286;&#9658; Two Door Cinema Club, Chic, Peace</span></span></b></i></span><br />
<span style="background-color: white; font-family: &quot;arial&quot; , &quot;tahoma&quot; , &quot;helvetica&quot; , &quot;freesans&quot; , sans-serif; font-size: 13px; line-height: 18.2px;"><span style="color: #141823; line-height: 19.32px;"><span style="font-family: inherit;"><i><b><a href="https://www.facebook.com/hurdlesofficial/">Facebook</a>&nbsp;/ <a href="http://twitter.com/WeAreHurdles">Twitter</a></b></i></span></span></span><br />
<span style="background-color: white; font-family: &quot;arial&quot; , &quot;tahoma&quot; , &quot;helvetica&quot; , &quot;freesans&quot; , sans-serif; font-size: 13px; line-height: 18.2px;"><span style="color: #141823; line-height: 19.32px;"><span style="font-family: inherit;"><i><br /></i></span></span></span>
<span style="background-color: white;"><span style="font-family: inherit;"><span style="color: #141823;"><span style="line-height: 19.32px;">The slick,&nbsp;</span></span><span style="color: #141823;"><span style="line-height: 19.32px;">infectious</span></span><span style="color: #141823;"><span style="line-height: 19.32px;">&nbsp;indie pop which first saw Hurdles slide into our stream of &nbsp;consciousness back in 2012 has remained at the core of their sound, even if it has experienced a face-lift or two along the way. New EP <i style="font-weight: bold;">'Dusk &amp; Dawn' </i>has the look and feel of a band ready to take on the next stage of their musical careers.</span></span></span></span><br />
<span style="background-color: white;"><span style="font-family: inherit;"><span style="color: #141823;"><span style="line-height: 19.32px;"><br /></span></span></span></span>
<span style="color: #141823;"><span style="background-color: white; line-height: 19.32px;">Catchy opener (they always start strong!)<b style="font-style: italic;"> 'Closer' </b>offers a brief glimpse back into their more guitar centered past, before those Summer-night synth's get their first outing of the record. It's a carefully crafted sound, each layer more intricate than the last. When it all comes together, as it so often does throughout <b style="font-style: italic;">'Dusk &amp; Dawn', </b>you begin to feel that this is the sound Hurdles have always been building towards.&nbsp;</span></span><br />
<span style="color: #141823;"><span style="background-color: white; line-height: 19.32px;"><br /></span></span>
<span style="color: #141823;"><span style="background-color: white; line-height: 19.32px;">Those richly textured indie-pop riffs perhaps work best on the title track, sounding straight out of Bombay Bicycle Club's <b style="font-style: italic;">'So Long, See You Tomorrow'.</b><i><b> 'Wake'</b></i> too, sounds smooth and natural, even with it's new found level of groove.&nbsp;</span></span><br />
<span style="color: #141823;"><span style="background-color: white; line-height: 19.32px;"><br /></span></span>
<br />
<div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
<a href="https://3.bp.blogspot.com/-RehsVA3X-Hg/VvJ8TEQ1-rI/AAAAAAAACG0/yobxbuRPKio7n3Mi9nrVD-KuetehsJ-5A/s1600/hurdles.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;"><img border="0" height="640" src="https://3.bp.blogspot.com/-RehsVA3X-Hg/VvJ8TEQ1-rI/AAAAAAAACG0/yobxbuRPKio7n3Mi9nrVD-KuetehsJ-5A/s640/hurdles.jpg" width="452" /></a></div>
<br />
<span style="color: #141823;"><span style="background-color: white;"><span style="line-height: 19.32px;">Deliberate or not, </span><b style="font-style: italic; line-height: 19.32px;">'Dusk &amp; Dawn' </b><span style="line-height: 19.32px;">feels like a chronological guide to Hurdles history, building to it's crescendo, the mammoth </span><b style="font-style: italic; line-height: 19.32px;">'France'. &nbsp;</b><span style="line-height: 19.32px;">First debuted on the Oh Yeah Centre's 'Scratch My Progress' talent program/label, this dancey single has already notched up nearly 30,000 soundcloud plays, a remarkable amount for any young band. From the first listen </span><b style="font-style: italic; line-height: 19.32px;">'France' </b><span style="line-height: 19.32px;">remains firmly implanted in your head and trust us, it will stay there. It may not have the 'indie-upstart' charm of earlier singles &nbsp;</span><span style="line-height: 19.32px;"><i style="font-weight: bold;">'Kaleidoscope'</i>, or<i style="font-weight: bold;"> 'Pictures'</i>,<i style="font-weight: bold;">&nbsp;</i>but it is clear that this is not quite the same band anymore.</span></span></span><br />
<span style="color: #141823;"><span style="background-color: white;"><span style="line-height: 19.32px;"><br /></span></span></span>
<span style="color: #141823;"><span style="background-color: white; line-height: 19.32px;">A mature, confident release from a band still made for the festival circuit and long, hot Summers.</span></span><br />
<br />
<div style="text-align: right;">
<b><i>Taylor Johnson</i></b></div>

          <div class="date-posts">
        
<div class='post-outer'>
<div class='post hentry uncustomized-post-template' itemprop='blogPost' itemscope='itemscope' itemtype='http://schema.org/BlogPosting'>
<meta content='https://2.bp.blogspot.com/-nlqZ73OFJzs/VvE7BsomsjI/AAAAAAAACGE/HgeaidtTuQsNU1ui1JyqT_fLbB4_Cnrpg/s640/new%2Batlas.jpg' itemprop='image_url'/>

<h3 class='post-title entry-title' itemprop='name'>
<a href='http://encorenorthernireland.blogspot.com/2016/03/single-review-young-hearts-new-atlas.html'>single review ~ 'young hearts' ~ new atlas</a>
</h3>
<div class='post-header'>
<div class='post-header-line-1'></div>
</div>
<div class='post-body entry-content' id='post-body-6846890000229493887' itemprop='description articleBody'>
<div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
<a href="https://2.bp.blogspot.com/-nlqZ73OFJzs/VvE7BsomsjI/AAAAAAAACGE/HgeaidtTuQsNU1ui1JyqT_fLbB4_Cnrpg/s1600/new%2Batlas.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;"><img border="0" height="430" src="https://2.bp.blogspot.com/-nlqZ73OFJzs/VvE7BsomsjI/AAAAAAAACGE/HgeaidtTuQsNU1ui1JyqT_fLbB4_Cnrpg/s640/new%2Batlas.jpg" width="640" /></a></div>
<span style="background-color: white; font-family: &quot;arial&quot; , &quot;tahoma&quot; , &quot;helvetica&quot; , &quot;freesans&quot; , sans-serif; font-size: 13px; line-height: 18.2px;"><i><b><span style="font-family: inherit;">New Atlas</span></b></i></span><br />
<span style="background-color: white; font-family: &quot;arial&quot; , &quot;tahoma&quot; , &quot;helvetica&quot; , &quot;freesans&quot; , sans-serif; font-size: 13px; line-height: 18.2px;"><i><b><span style="font-family: inherit;">For fans of<span style="color: #141823; line-height: 19.32px;">&#8286;&#9658; The 1975, Barcelona, Swim Deep</span></span></b></i></span><br />
<span style="background-color: white; font-family: &quot;arial&quot; , &quot;tahoma&quot; , &quot;helvetica&quot; , &quot;freesans&quot; , sans-serif; font-size: 13px; line-height: 18.2px;"><span style="color: #141823; line-height: 19.32px;"><span style="font-family: inherit;"><i><b><a href="https://www.facebook.com/NewAtlasOfficial/">Facebook</a></b></i></span></span></span><br />
<span style="background-color: white; font-family: &quot;arial&quot; , &quot;tahoma&quot; , &quot;helvetica&quot; , &quot;freesans&quot; , sans-serif; font-size: 13px; line-height: 18.2px;"><span style="color: #141823; line-height: 19.32px;"><span style="font-family: inherit;"><i><br /></i></span></span></span>
It may be a bit premature to proclaim <i><b>'Young Hearts'</b></i>, the debut single from Armagh-born, Liverpool-based, two piece New Atlas, the best they've ever done. As despite their new image, new colour scheme and bold new sound, the emotive-pop noise of guitarist/frontman Eoghan O'Hagan and pianist Luc McParland is not an entirely new prospect. Indeed, under previous moniker 'His New Atlas', the lads mournful ballads marked them out as one of this countries most promising young outfits. but all things must change and along with the haircuts, so has the sound.<br />
<i style="font-weight: bold;"><br /></i>
Part of what made 'His New Atlas' so enchanting was O'Hagan's palpable pain behind the microphone. Songs like 'His Young' dragged you into the songwriters troubled mind, and often left you there long after he'd left the stage.&nbsp;<i style="font-weight: bold;">'Young Hearts' </i>manages<i style="font-weight: bold;">&nbsp;</i>to convey that same emotion, but in very different ways. If <b style="font-style: italic;">'His New Atlas' </b>led you into the dark, <b style="font-style: italic;">'New Atlas' </b>feels like an escape back into the light.<br />
<br />
<div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
<a href="https://4.bp.blogspot.com/-PeYQL8B-Qck/VvFCzGOufJI/AAAAAAAACGU/JsL7BtGulWcnYDwLa9f9Bo3TcjoJoUgaw/s1600/new%2Batlas.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;"><img border="0" height="426" src="https://4.bp.blogspot.com/-PeYQL8B-Qck/VvFCzGOufJI/AAAAAAAACGU/JsL7BtGulWcnYDwLa9f9Bo3TcjoJoUgaw/s640/new%2Batlas.jpg" width="640" /></a></div>
<br />
Shimmering synth work from McParland paints 'Young Hearts' dreamy pop landscape, whilst his bandmate pulls another big chorus out of nowhere. This was par for the course on his slower work, but bringing out the big guns on a single like this is a totally different matter all together. They nail it here.<br />
<br />
For all of 'Young Hearts' big production and instant melody, there remains some of the shadows from his previous work. <i><b>'If my Mother and Father had&nbsp;</b></i><b style="font-style: italic;">stayed together, maybe we could have too..."</b>, suggesting that we won't be hearing them cover 'Love Me Do' anytime soon.<br />
<b style="font-style: italic;"><br /></b>
A bold new direction, illustrative pop-critiques on the human condition and a festival worthy chorus. A certain Matty Healy may well approve. Extremely promising.<br />
<br />
<div style="text-align: right;">
<i><b>Taylor Johnson</b></i></div>

<h2 class='date-header'><span>Monday, 29 February 2016</span></h2>

          <div class="date-posts">
        
<div class='post-outer'>
<div class='post hentry uncustomized-post-template' itemprop='blogPost' itemscope='itemscope' itemtype='http://schema.org/BlogPosting'>
<meta content='https://4.bp.blogspot.com/-1Fjna6zCpwE/Vs7euVcpQoI/AAAAAAAACFI/aDh7BztcTIU/s640/fionns%2Bgig.jpg' itemprop='image_url'/>
<meta content='7422745402798072222' itemprop='blogId'/>
<meta content='5487680453807297846' itemprop='postId'/>
<a name='5487680453807297846'></a>
<h3 class='post-title entry-title' itemprop='name'>
<a href='http://encorenorthernireland.blogspot.com/2016/02/gig-review-blackstaff-music-presents.html'>gig review ~ blackstaff music presents... fionn crossan, search party, fox colony & red house</a>
</h3>
<div class='post-header'>
<div class='post-header-line-1'></div>
</div>
<div class='post-body entry-content' id='post-body-5487680453807297846' itemprop='description articleBody'>
<div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
<a href="https://4.bp.blogspot.com/-1Fjna6zCpwE/Vs7euVcpQoI/AAAAAAAACFI/aDh7BztcTIU/s1600/fionns%2Bgig.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;"><img border="0" height="640" src="https://4.bp.blogspot.com/-1Fjna6zCpwE/Vs7euVcpQoI/AAAAAAAACFI/aDh7BztcTIU/s640/fionns%2Bgig.jpg" width="452" /></a></div>
<br />
<h2>
<span style="background-color: white; color: #444444; font-family: &quot;calibri&quot; , sans-serif; font-size: 15px; line-height: 21.3px;"><i><b>This week our reporter Connor Martin headed down to Alexander's Bar, Lisburn, to check out the latest Blackstaff Promotions &nbsp;showcase...</b></i></span></h2>
<span style="background-color: white; font-family: &quot;calibri&quot; , sans-serif; font-size: 15px; line-height: 21.3px;">On a blustery night here in Lisburn opening act Red House continued their upward trajectory &nbsp;with an electric set. Their first song was a new one, <i><b>'Scorpion Woman'</b></i>, but it immediately slotted in with their growing body of original material. The standard of songwriting and musicianship in this band continues to amaze, particularly considering their age.&nbsp;</span><br />
<span style="background-color: white; font-family: &quot;calibri&quot; , sans-serif; font-size: 15px; line-height: 21.3px;"><br /></span>
<span style="background-color: white; font-family: &quot;calibri&quot; , sans-serif; font-size: 15px; line-height: 21.3px;">I have to say, despite a rather subdued crowd, I moshed myself silly, only enhancing this young band's</span><span style="background-color: white;"><span style="color: #444444; font-family: &quot;calibri&quot; , sans-serif;"><span style="font-size: 15px; line-height: 21.3px;">&nbsp;reputation for producing a fun, energetic live show. The sheer joy of making music shines through in every note they&nbsp;play. Second on the bill were Fox Colony, a three piece from Belfast. These relative newcomers impressed with their hooky and atmospheric pop punk. Like Modern Baseball, their songs seem to create a little world of fuzzy guitars, clattering drums and an angsty solidarity for the audience to really get in to.</span></span></span><br />
<span style="background-color: white;"><span style="color: #444444; font-family: &quot;calibri&quot; , sans-serif;"><span style="font-size: 15px; line-height: 21.3px;"><br /></span></span></span>
<iframe frameborder="no" height="450" scrolling="no" src="https://w.soundcloud.com/player/?url=https%3A//api.soundcloud.com/tracks/242448977&amp;auto_play=false&amp;hide_related=false&amp;show_comments=true&amp;show_user=true&amp;show_reposts=false&amp;visual=true" width="100%"></iframe>



<span style="background-color: white;"><span style="color: #444444; font-family: &quot;calibri&quot; , sans-serif;"><span style="font-size: 15px; line-height: 21.3px;"><br /></span></span></span>
<span style="background-color: white; color: #444444; font-family: &quot;calibri&quot; , sans-serif; font-size: 15px; line-height: 21.3px;"><br /></span><br />
<span style="background-color: white; font-family: &quot;calibri&quot; , sans-serif; font-size: 15px; line-height: 21.3px;">Highlight of the set came through debut single<i><b> 'The Weekend'.</b></i> I look forward to seeing what happens as this band continues to grow in confidence... there is so much promise here!</span><br />
<span style="background-color: white; font-family: &quot;calibri&quot; , sans-serif; font-size: 15px; line-height: 21.3px;"><br /></span>
<span style="background-color: white; font-family: &quot;calibri&quot; , sans-serif; font-size: 15px; line-height: 21.3px;">Next up was punk-new comers Search Party. Lead singer Sean McDonnell broke a string during the first song from sheer enthusiasm. This band (who have just released the excellent EP<i><b> 'Life is Art'</b></i>) have improved leaps and bounds since I last saw them. They are tighter, faster, leaner. Sean's voice has found new&nbsp;</span><span style="background-color: white; font-family: &quot;calibri&quot; , sans-serif; font-size: 15px; line-height: 21.3px;">power, and new lead guitarist Ethan Murphy consistently manages to push things further than before.&nbsp;</span><br />
<span style="background-color: white; font-family: &quot;calibri&quot; , sans-serif; font-size: 15px; line-height: 21.3px;"><br /></span>
<span style="background-color: white; font-family: &quot;calibri&quot; , sans-serif; font-size: 15px; line-height: 21.3px;">Bass player Ryan Pendleton has also come on, contributing a rock solid low end thump, particularly evident on the awesome <i><b>'Coming Alive'.</b></i> Special mention must go, however, to drummer David Malone. A lesser musician might have phoned in such a sparsely attended show, whilst David did not. Put simply, he beat the hell out of the kit and I commend him for it. As Sean said at the opening of&nbsp;</span><span style="background-color: white; font-family: &quot;calibri&quot; , sans-serif; font-size: 15px; line-height: 21.3px;">Search Party's set,<i><b> "We may not have gotten all of Lisburn out tonight"</b></i>, but it was, frankly, their loss.&nbsp;</span><br />
<span style="background-color: white; color: #444444; font-family: &quot;calibri&quot; , sans-serif; font-size: 15px; line-height: 21.3px;"><br /></span>
<br />
<div style="text-align: center;">
<iframe seamless="" src="https://bandcamp.com/EmbeddedPlayer/album=233964279/size=large/bgcol=ffffff/linkcol=0687f5/tracklist=false/transparent=true/" style="border-style: initial; border-width: 0px; height: 470px; width: 350px;"></iframe></div>
<div style="text-align: center;">
<br /></div>
<div style="text-align: left;">
Finally came headliner Fionn Crossan, remarkably composed and confident beyond his eighteen years. In a set which ranged between tranquil fragility and blustering folk, Crossan's alluring vocals and relaxed demeanor endeared him to the small crowd before a note had even been sung. Opening number <b style="font-style: italic;">'Siblings' </b>and penultimate tale <b style="font-style: italic;">'Chased By Wolves' </b>may have been set highlights, but Crossan's carefully chosen covers also made for some wonderful listening, particularly with his total reinvention of the Springsteen classic <b style="font-style: italic;">'I'm On Fire'.&nbsp;</b><br />
<b style="font-style: italic;"><br /></b>
<br />
<div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
<a href="https://1.bp.blogspot.com/-xD62LWmaVls/VtRZCjn-nJI/AAAAAAAACFc/fC1F0oA_pmQ/s1600/fionn.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;"><img border="0" height="640" src="https://1.bp.blogspot.com/-xD62LWmaVls/VtRZCjn-nJI/AAAAAAAACFc/fC1F0oA_pmQ/s640/fionn.jpg" width="640" /></a></div>
<br />
As a debut headline show the Mallusk teenager could do no more, making it all the more frustrating that there weren't more people there to enjoy his maudlin tales. From this performance, a future of attentive crowds in much bigger venues looks almost a certainty; and with a debut &nbsp;EP not too far into the distance, Fionn Crossan may just get there sooner, rather than later.<br />
<br />

<i><b>Connor Martin</b></i></div>

<h3 class='post-title entry-title' itemprop='name'>
<a href='http://encorenorthernireland.blogspot.com/2016/02/single-review-beluga-autocratic.html'>single review ~ 'beluga' ~ the autocratic </a>
</h3>
<div class='post-header'>
<div class='post-header-line-1'></div>
</div>
<div class='post-body entry-content' id='post-body-70801834266062122' itemprop='description articleBody'>
<div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
<a href="https://1.bp.blogspot.com/-QBs8gbhh85Q/Vs2WCqQi5gI/AAAAAAAACEk/yjc5JsvMHWk/s1600/beluga.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;"><img border="0" height="640" src="https://1.bp.blogspot.com/-QBs8gbhh85Q/Vs2WCqQi5gI/AAAAAAAACEk/yjc5JsvMHWk/s640/beluga.jpg" width="640" /></a></div>
<span style="background-color: white; font-family: &quot;arial&quot; , &quot;tahoma&quot; , &quot;helvetica&quot; , &quot;freesans&quot; , sans-serif; font-size: 13px; line-height: 18.2px;"><i><b><span style="font-family: inherit;">The Autocratic</span></b></i></span><br />
<span style="background-color: white; font-family: &quot;arial&quot; , &quot;tahoma&quot; , &quot;helvetica&quot; , &quot;freesans&quot; , sans-serif; font-size: 13px; line-height: 18.2px;"><i><b><span style="font-family: inherit;">For fans of<span style="color: #141823; line-height: 19.32px;">&#8286;&#9658; Muse, Arctic Monkeys, The Killers</span></span></b></i></span><br />
<span style="background-color: white; font-family: &quot;arial&quot; , &quot;tahoma&quot; , &quot;helvetica&quot; , &quot;freesans&quot; , sans-serif; font-size: 13px; line-height: 18.2px;"><span style="color: #141823; line-height: 19.32px;"><span style="font-family: inherit;"><i><b><a href="https://www.facebook.com/theautocraticmusic">Facebook</a>/<a href="https://soundcloud.com/theautocratic">Soundcloud</a></b></i></span></span></span><br />
<br />
The North Coast's reputation for producing riff-bashing three pieces is much like Barcelona's for producing world class midfielders. Pretty good. So the arrival of North Coast, riff-bashing, three piece The Autocratic should really come as no surprise.<br />
<br />
A young band just starting to find their feet in the local music scene, already they have amassed a decent northern following; throwing themselves head first into those halcyon days of pub gigs and house parties. It's an initiation all the best have had to go through, and with a few headline shows already under their belts, the arrival of debut single <i><b>'Beluga'</b></i> is a welcome one indeed.<br />
<br />
Taking their lead from the likes of Axis Of and A Northern Light,<i><b> 'Beluga'</b></i>s big chorus and punk-tinged verse's keep things upbeat; and whist the riff which draws you in evokes <i><b>'Favorite Worst Nightmare'</b></i> era Arctic Monkeys, frontman Connall Ennis' distinctive vocal ensure the band's authentic sound remains just that.<br />
<br />
As a debut release, <i><b>'Beluga'</b></i> sounds bold and confident. The drumming is tight, the chorus is memorable, and the solo running into the outro hints at much bigger things to come. For a young band right at the start of their musical careers, all the signs point skyward for The Autocratic.<br />
<br />
Just as <i><b>Axis Of </b></i>look to hang up their guitars for the foreseeable future, The Autocratic's arrival ensures the musical circle of life continues. The North Coast scene is alive and kicking.<br />
<div style="text-align: right;">
<i><b><br /></b></i></div>
<div style="text-align: right;">
<i><b>Taylor Johnson</b></i></div>

<h3 class='post-title entry-title' itemprop='name'>
<a href='http://encorenorthernireland.blogspot.com/2016/02/track-of-week-galway-rain-son-of-hound.html'>track of the week ~ 'galway rain' ~son of the hound</a>
</h3>
<div class='post-header'>
<div class='post-header-line-1'></div>
</div>
<div class='post-body entry-content' id='post-body-6327306821394949924' itemprop='description articleBody'>
<div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
<a href="https://3.bp.blogspot.com/-vmN8GfJpmF8/VsxmWxd6A2I/AAAAAAAACEE/XQz7Zms6zZc/s1600/galway%2Brain.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;"><img border="0" height="640" src="https://3.bp.blogspot.com/-vmN8GfJpmF8/VsxmWxd6A2I/AAAAAAAACEE/XQz7Zms6zZc/s640/galway%2Brain.jpg" width="640" /></a></div>
<br />
<span style="background-color: white; font-family: Arial, Tahoma, Helvetica, FreeSans, sans-serif; font-size: 13px; line-height: 18.2px;"><i><b><span style="font-family: inherit;">Son of the Hound</span></b></i></span><br style="background-color: white; font-family: Arial, Tahoma, Helvetica, FreeSans, sans-serif; font-size: 13px; line-height: 18.2px;" /><span style="background-color: white; font-family: Arial, Tahoma, Helvetica, FreeSans, sans-serif; font-size: 13px; line-height: 18.2px;"><i><b><span style="font-family: inherit;">For fans of<span style="color: #141823; line-height: 19.32px;">&#8286;&#9658; The Pogues, Van Morrison, Bruce Springsteen</span></span></b></i></span><br style="background-color: white; font-family: Arial, Tahoma, Helvetica, FreeSans, sans-serif; font-size: 13px; line-height: 18.2px;" /><span style="background-color: white; font-family: Arial, Tahoma, Helvetica, FreeSans, sans-serif; font-size: 13px; line-height: 18.2px;"><span style="color: #141823; line-height: 19.32px;"><span style="font-family: inherit;"><i><b><a href="http://www.facebook.com/sonofthehound">Facebook</a>/<a href="http://www.twitter.com/sonofhound">Twitter</a></b></i></span></span></span><br />
<br />
Michael McCullagh is a story-teller at heart. A troubadour in every sense, whether regaling us with tales of &nbsp;doomed sea-captains or Native American wanderers, a closer look will reveal the dedication at the heart of Son Of The Hound's music; a dedication that his won him more than a few admirers in both Northern Ireland and beyond.<br />
<br />
New single <b style="font-style: italic;">'Galway Rain' </b>is a happier departure from last single <b style="font-style: italic;">'Pilgrim'. </b>There's still that blue-grass celtic charm which has carried on from his Meb Jon Sol days, but there's a breezier edge to the guitarist's words here, a lighter touch. He sounds, dare we say it, <b style="font-style: italic;">happy?&nbsp;</b><br />
<br />
In a genre so&nbsp;often littered with pain and suffering, political rebellion, or even just a good old fashioned punch-up (cheers for all that Shane MacGowan), <b style="font-style: italic;">'Galway Rain' </b>treads the road less traveled, and sounds all the better for it. Lyrically it's more Van Morrison than anything else, and that's not just because of the chorus' mention of <b style="font-style: italic;">"Brown eyes shining in the Galway rain". </b>McCullagh sounds at peace here, flowing through his folk-pop melodies with ease, as if he's running on pure instinct.<br />
<br />
With each new release Son of the Hound continues to thrive.<b style="font-style: italic;">&nbsp;</b><b style="font-style: italic;">'Galway Rain' </b>is a song to dance to, singalong at the top of your voices and ultimately, to remember the good times.<br />
<i><b><br /></b></i>
<i><b>We'll drink to that.</b></i><br />
<br />
<div style="text-align: right;">
<i><b>Taylor Johnson</b></i></div>

<a href='http://encorenorthernireland.blogspot.com/2016/02/news-jessica-hopper-announced-as.html'>news ~ jessica hopper announced as keynote speaker 'women's work festival' in belfast</a>
</h3>
<div class='post-header'>
<div class='post-header-line-1'></div>
</div>
<div class='post-body entry-content' id='post-body-3956566613775194008' itemprop='description articleBody'>
<div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
<a href="https://4.bp.blogspot.com/-8vU7jlDvA3o/VroMhjV0wBI/AAAAAAAACDk/yazwi0KS9Zw/s1600/DCS-JHopp-bookpicpreview-54270002%2B%25281%2529.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;"><img border="0" height="630" src="https://4.bp.blogspot.com/-8vU7jlDvA3o/VroMhjV0wBI/AAAAAAAACDk/yazwi0KS9Zw/s640/DCS-JHopp-bookpicpreview-54270002%2B%25281%2529.jpg" width="640" /></a></div>
<br />
<h3>
One of the most important voices in modern music journalism today has been announced as the keynote speaker at an upcoming Belfast based festival.</h3>
The <i><b>"Women's Work Festival"</b></i> is a series of events designed to celebrate women's crucial role within the music industry and to encourage a new generation of girls to pursue careers within music. This year's keynote speaker, Jessica Hopper, is the current editorial director for music at MTV, having previously held roles at Pitchfork and Rookie Magazine.<br />
<br />
Her unmissable talk will take place in the <i><b>Oh Yeah Music Centre, at 3pm on the 6th of March</b></i>, and is set to cover important issues such as&nbsp;<span style="background-color: white; line-height: 18.46px;"><span style="font-family: inherit;">the international conversation around inclusivity in the music industry and &nbsp;what is changing within it.&nbsp;</span></span><br />
<span style="background-color: white; line-height: 18.46px;"><span style="font-family: inherit;"><br /></span></span>
<span style="background-color: white; line-height: 18.46px;"><span style="font-family: inherit;">The festival will kick off with a unique evening on Friday 4th with broadcasting pioneer Annie Nightingale, who recently celebrated 50 years in music and will end with a music showcase featuring some of the most promising acts of 2016 including Saint Sister, Jealous of The Birds and Katharine Philippa.&nbsp;</span></span><br />
<span style="background-color: white; font-family: inherit; line-height: 18.46px;"><br /></span>
<span style="background-color: white; font-family: inherit; line-height: 18.46px;"><br /></span>
<span style="background-color: white; font-family: inherit; line-height: 18.46px;">Further details can be found at; &nbsp;</span><a href="http://www.womensworkni.com/" style="background-color: white; color: #0068cf; cursor: pointer; font-family: inherit; line-height: 18.46px;" target="_blank">www.womensworkni.com</a><br />
<span style="background-color: white; line-height: 18.46px;"><span style="font-family: inherit;"><br /></span></span>

<h3 class='post-title entry-title' itemprop='name'>
<a href='http://encorenorthernireland.blogspot.com/2016/02/news-jealous-of-birds-to-release-debut.html'>news ~ jealous of the birds to release debut album </a>
</h3>
<div class='post-header'>
<div class='post-header-line-1'></div>
</div>
<div class='post-body entry-content' id='post-body-2260709054824365602' itemprop='description articleBody'>
<div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;">
<a href="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/-ALHa4qXD8kM/VrIki9l2h4I/AAAAAAAACDU/SweKHmIyhhc/s1600/jotb.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;"><img border="0" height="480" src="http://4.bp.blogspot.com/-ALHa4qXD8kM/VrIki9l2h4I/AAAAAAAACDU/SweKHmIyhhc/s640/jotb.jpg" width="640" /></a></div>
<br />
Well, the cat is well and truly out of the bag now. One of Northern Ireland's best kept secrets looks just about ready for SOAK-styled admiration beyond these shores, and after the way teenage songwriting-wildflower Naomi Hamilton has handled her musical career since the release of lo-fi debut<i><b> 'Capricorn'</b></i> last year, you'd swear she's been craving it all her life. Except, she hasn't; and one listen through the sepia-toned wanderlust of debut single<b style="font-style: italic;"> 'Goji Berry Sunset' </b>confirms this.<br />
<br />
Jealous of the Birds is not singing for anyone other than the girl in her bedroom, lost in her journal. Or the awkward new kid, desperate for that last period maths class to finish so they can get home. This is thoughtful music, inquisitive and bold. It's a nostalgic sound, with the details left blank. A colouring book that only you can fill in. <i><b>"Your name, fizzes on my tongue...damn that name".</b></i><br />
<br />
When you then get down to the actual music, Jealous of the Birds again leaves you warm and starry eyed. Gentle folk blends smoothly with that haunting whistling refrain. On Capricorn it sounded great, here, as the lead single from her debut album <b style="font-style: italic;">'Parma Violets' </b>it sounds better. There hasn't been a lot added or taken away from the original, but nor did it need it.<b style="font-style: italic;"> 'Goji Berry Sunset' </b>feels like a very personal letter from an old friend.<br />
<br />
Parma Violets is out April 8th. What an extraordinary journey this young artist has ahead of her.<br />
<br />
<div style="text-align: right;">
<i><b>Taylor Johnson&nbsp;</b></i></div>
