<meta content='http://3.bp.blogspot.com/-3nN1jZRyQL4/Von0wmZ9IpI/AAAAAAAABtg/6_5YjrOdo9s/s320/50cover.jpg' itemprop='image_url'/>

<h3 class='post-title entry-title' itemprop='name'>
<a href='http://thetremagazine.blogspot.com/2016/01/50-favorite-songs-of-2015.html'>50 Favorite Songs of 2015</a>
</h3>
<div class='post-header'>
<div class='post-header-line-1'></div>
</div>
<div class='post-body entry-content' id='post-body-4914768594797103460' itemprop='description articleBody'>
<div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;"><a href="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/-3nN1jZRyQL4/Von0wmZ9IpI/AAAAAAAABtg/6_5YjrOdo9s/s1600/50cover.jpg" imageanchor="1" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;"><img border="0" src="http://3.bp.blogspot.com/-3nN1jZRyQL4/Von0wmZ9IpI/AAAAAAAABtg/6_5YjrOdo9s/s320/50cover.jpg" /></a></div><br />
Hello friends, another year has come and gone and it's time for my annual version of The Tremagazine Favorite Songs of the year. I went about this a little bit differently this year as I setup a playlist back in January and then tried to filter songs into it throughout the year, some made the final cut and others dropped out along the way.  Over the last month or so I&#8217;ve dug deeper into lists from friends or others I&#8217;ve seen online and found some more songs that I may have missed over the year.<br />
<br />
With each song posted, I'll list some of my favorite lyrics along with some personal thoughts on the song and/or artist, if I got to see that band this year I'll be sure to mention a note about the show.  Once again, I've added a spotify playlist so those of you that use it can just stream the songs in one place.<br />
<br />
For those of you that have read through these lists previously, I hope you enjoy as much as before.  For the newcomers, I hope you find something in here that you like and will stop by again in 2016. In no particular order, here are my fifty favorite songs of 2015, enjoy.  <br />
<br />
<br />
<a href="https://play.spotify.com/user/tremags33/playlist/7iAn4J37XKvJohI4g75EIK"></a><br />
<br />
<br />
<b>"Love Like Ghosts" - Lord Huron</b><br />
<br />
Lord Huron's album <i>Strange Trails</i> came out early in the year but I found it in constant rotation right up to 2016.  This was definitely one of my favorites of 2015 and hard to narrow down the tracks I loved most.  It's rare nowadays to buy an album and let it run through but this is one of those that is enjoyable from start to finish.  I got to see them perform at Terminal 5 this year and had a great time at the show.  Highly recommend not only this album but their previous work as well. <br />
<br />
<b><i>I don't feel it 'til it hurts sometimes<br />
So go on baby, hurt me tonight.<br />
I want ours to be an endless song.<br />
Baby, in my eyes you do no wrong.</i></b><br />
<br />
<iframe width="420" height="315" src="https://www.youtube.com/embed/Q_nQd7Z8WEw" frameborder="0" allowfullscreen></iframe><br />
<br />
<br />
<b>"In Its Infancy (The Waterfall)" - My Morning Jacket</b><br />
<br />
Another favorite album from 2015, My Morning Jacket's <i>The Waterfall</i> had a bunch of songs that could have made this list.  I always enjoy MMJ's studio work but their songs take on a new life whenever I see them live.  I got the chance to see them a few times this year, both in a club and festival setting and they are one of the few bands that just puts on an "A" show every time I've seen them.  The new tracks sounded even better than I would have imagined and this was ones that stood out to me. <br />
<br />
<b><i>Again I stop the waterfall by simply thinking<br />
Again I stop the waterfall before my breathing<br />
Again I stop the waterfall by finally feeling<br />
Again I stop the waterfall by just believing</i></b><br />
<br />
<iframe width="560" height="315" src="https://www.youtube.com/embed/baRtFUiKAEU" frameborder="0" allowfullscreen></iframe><br />
<br />
<br />
<b>"New Skin" - Torres</b><br />
<br />
Throughout this list there are many artists that I just heard for the first time this year, Torres would fall into that category.  This track was the one that jumped out at me and reminded me of something I listened to back in the 90s.  I've yet to see her live but I love this clip of her below, you get a real sense in the passion that's behind her lyrics.<br />
<br />
<i><b>The darkness fears<br />
What darkness knows<br />
But if you&#8217;ve never known the darkness<br />
Then you&#8217;re the one who fears the most</b></i><br />
<br />
<iframe width="560" height="315" src="https://www.youtube.com/embed/z2IT4zGRvoM" frameborder="0" allowfullscreen></iframe><br />
<br />
<br />
<b>"No, No, No" - Beirut</b><br />
<br />
I'm a big fan of this band, chances are if they've put out an album in a given year at least one of the songs will find it's way to this list.  Lead singer Zach Condon seems to consistently surround himself with talented musicians which was one of the reasons I chose this live clip to share.  I've seen Beirut numerous times and highly recommend checking them out if you get the chance. <br />
<br />
<b><i>Don't know the first thing about who you are<br />
My heart is waiting, taken in from the start<br />
If we don't go now, we won't get very far<br />
Don't know the first thing about who you are</i></b><br />
<br />
<iframe width="560" height="315" src="https://www.youtube.com/embed/XZLluvDZOBs" frameborder="0" allowfullscreen></iframe><br />
<br />
<br />
<b>"Right On Time" - Dawes</b><br />
<br />
I've written numerous times about Dawes, one of my favorite bands that have come out in recent years.  From other lists I've seen, it seems like the song that everyone loves off their latest release is <a href="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=I74C2hClAsA">"All Your Favorite Bands" </a> featuring cameos from other artists like Matt Vazquez (Delta Spirit), John McCauley (Deer Tick), Brandon Flowers (The Killers) and My Morning Jacket.  It's indeed a good song but "Right On Time" is the standout track that jumped out to me.  <br />
<br />
<b><i>If these walls could talk<br />
I'd defer to the furniture<br />
The piano, the table, the bed that we both sleep in<br />
These constant, silent messengers</i></b><br />
<br />
<iframe width="560" height="315" src="https://www.youtube.com/embed/pOldBR-iHDI" frameborder="0" allowfullscreen></iframe><br />
<br />
<br />
<b>"S.O.B." - Nathaniel Rateliff & The Night Sweats</b><br />
<br />
Nathaniel Rateliff & The Night Sweats were simply my favorite discovery of 2015.  I first heard S.O.B. driving down at the Jersey shore over the summer and I raced home to find out more about the band.  This whole album is incredible, I had to hold myself back from putting about four songs on this list.  If I were ranking a top five songs for the year I'd easily have this in there.  A few months ago I got to see the band in Brooklyn and was really impressed with the performance.  An extremely tight live band, you can tell they've been at it awhile and are completely in sync with one another.  One other thing that struck me was how the entire band is involved in vocals, that's not something you see often.  Take a look at the video below and you'll get a sense of how good a live show these guys put on.  <br />
<br />
<b><i>I'm gonna need someone to help me<br />
I'm gonna need somebody's hand<br />
I'm gonna need someone to hold me down<br />
I'm gonna need someone to care</i></b><br />
<br />
<iframe width="560" height="315" src="https://www.youtube.com/embed/2_pqOJt7gE8" frameborder="0" allowfullscreen></iframe><br />
<br />
<br />
<b>"I Got You" - The White Buffalo</b><br />
<br />
I wasn't familiar with White Buffalo until earlier this year when a friend pointed me in his direction.  Great voice and some strong songs on this album.  Fans of the show <i>Sons of Anarchy </i>are probably familiar with him as I understand his music is featured in several episodes of the show. <br />
<br />
<i><b>But I got you<br />
In my veins, in my blood<br />
I got you<br />
Make me insane and boil my blood<br />
Like there ain't no other love<br />
Hearts aren't always red<br />
They're black and blue<br />
But I got you</b></i><br />
<br />
<iframe width="560" height="315" src="https://www.youtube.com/embed/U2l95cgTVR8" frameborder="0" allowfullscreen></iframe><br />
<br />
<br />
<b>"Southern Belles" - Langhorne Slim</b><br />
<br />
I think Langhorne Slim says it best in the video below... "This is a bit of a smoother shuffle, you might feel it in your thighs and perhaps your ass.  If you feel it in those places, shake it out." <br />
<br />
<b><i>It's been a while since I've been alright <br />
I try to smile but it just don't come off right </i></b><br />
<br />
<iframe width="420" height="315" src="https://www.youtube.com/embed/7nlQ5RRy_40" frameborder="0" allowfullscreen></iframe><br />
<br />
<br />
<b>"Went Looking for Warren Zevon's Los Angeles" - Lucero</b><br />
<br />
Lucero has been around for quite awhile now but for some reason I never listened to them much.  A coworker of mine is a big fan and I dug into them a bit a few months ago and really enjoyed their latest album.  Just seeing Warren Zevon's name in the title made me hope that I'd love this song and I can say that it's my favorite from this release.  <br />
<br />
<i><b>Fell in love but the love ran out<br />
At Lucy's El Adobe by the Paramount<br />
Margaritas under black palm trees<br />
I've got time, no one is waiting for me</b></i><br />
<br />
<iframe width="560" height="315" src="https://www.youtube.com/embed/xuAFJt-FHss" frameborder="0" allowfullscreen></iframe><br />
<br />
<br />
<b>"Mr. E. Mann" - Titus Andronicus</b><br />
<br />
I don't remember when or how exactly I came across this song earlier this year but it's a damn good rock song.  Love the vocals from lead singer Patrick Stickles. <br />
<br />
<i><b>I was crying as I tried to get high<br />
I've been dying all the time to get by<br />
And along came a guy who said "Looking on the bright side's alright<br />
It's alright<br />
It's alright"</b></i><br />
<br />
<iframe width="560" height="315" src="https://www.youtube.com/embed/XNHSrEAYMW4" frameborder="0" allowfullscreen></iframe><br />
<br />
<br />
<b>"You Don't Care for Me Enough to Cry" - John Moreland</b><br />
<br />
John Moreland is another newcomer to the list this year.  If I were to try to narrow down this list to only five songs, this track would have been included.  Incredibly powerful song lyrically and one of the most beautiful musically that I heard in 2015.  Moreland will head out on tour opening for Jason Isbell overseas early in 2016, that's a hell of a pairing to see live.  <br />
<br />
<b><i>And I'm the kind of love it hurts to look at<br />
maybe we should take it as a sign<br />
when I'm strung out on leaving<br />
exhaulting all my demons<br />
and you don't care for me enough to cry</i></b><br />
<br />
<iframe width="560" height="315" src="https://www.youtube.com/embed/VwIWj0uNVCo" frameborder="0" allowfullscreen></iframe><br />
<br />
<br />
<b>"Palmetto Rose" - Jason Isbell</b><br />
<br />
Speaking of Jason Isbell......one of my favorite albums from 2015, this is the first of two songs that will appear on the list.  Over the last few years I've become a huge fan of Jason and especially his live show.  I got to see him a couple times in 2015 and was fortunate to make a trip down to Nashville to see him perform in the legendary Ryman Auditorium (if you haven't seen what this place looks like, take a look <a href="http://thesoundopinion.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/07/Ryman-Auditorium.jpg">HERE</a>).  Imagine seeing one of your favorite performers in a church, that's the closest I can come to describing it.  A phenomenal experience, one of my favorite memories and best shows I saw this year.<br />
<br />
<i><b>At night, after everything closes<br />
I follow my own free will<br />
And I take in my fill<br />
I take in my fill </b></i><br />
<br />
<iframe width="560" height="315" src="https://www.youtube.com/embed/z-dh3YyjH3Q" frameborder="0" allowfullscreen></iframe><br />
<br />
<br />
<b>"River" - Saintseneca</b><br />
<br />
You never quite know how you're going to come across music that appeals to you.  I found Saintseneca through a thread on a basketball messageboard that I spend some time on.  Liked this song and video a lot and kept it in my rotation most of the year.  <br />
<br />
<iframe width="560" height="315" src="https://www.youtube.com/embed/q5oVY221wxE" frameborder="0" allowfullscreen></iframe><br />
<br />
<br />
<b>"Paul Is Alive" - El Vy</b><br />
<br />
El Vy is a project featuring Matt Berninger from The National with Brent Knopf of Menomena.  If you hadn't heard them before but listened to this song, you'd be able to place Berninger's vocals pretty quickly if you are a National fan.  This was my favorite track from their 2015 release.   <br />
<br />
<b><i>Beatlemania made my mother<br />
Think the way she does<br />
She always said<br />
"Don&#8217;t waste your life wishing everything was how it was"</i></b><br />
<br />
<iframe width="560" height="315" src="https://www.youtube.com/embed/vFCZGqPx4Gg" frameborder="0" allowfullscreen></iframe><br />
<br />
<br />
<b>"Hotline Bling" - Drake</b><br />
<br />
One thing you'll find going through this list is that I have a pretty wide range in songs that I listen to.  This is one of the catchiest tunes I heard this year, I don't think I've ever skipped it when it comes on my playlist.  Drake puts out some absolutely massive hits and Hotline Bling falls into that category.  <br />
<br />
<b><i>You should just be yourself<br />
Right now, you're someone else<br />
</i></b><br />
<br />
<iframe width="560" height="315" src="https://www.youtube.com/embed/uxpDa-c-4Mc" frameborder="0" allowfullscreen></iframe><br />
<br />
<br />
<b>"The Eye" - Brandi Carlile</b><br />
<br />
One downside to living in a city is that I no longer have my car so I don't get a lot of access to radio.  I recently had a rental car for a meeting upstate and got a chance to listen to one of my favorite stations, WFUV.  They had a tremendous playlist for my morning drive and one of the songs I heard was this one.  I knew some of Brandi Carlile's music but had never heard this one, such a beautiful song.  Luckily I was able to find a great live version from WFUV to share with you as well. <br />
<br />
<b><i>I wrapped your love around me like a chain<br />
But I never was afraid that it would die<br />
You can dance in a hurricane<br />
But only if you&#8217;re standing in the eye</i><br />
</b><br />
<br />
<iframe width="560" height="315" src="https://www.youtube.com/embed/P8OJaBYoBWI" frameborder="0" allowfullscreen></iframe><br />
<br />
<br />
<b>"Bad Art & Weirdo Ideas" - Beach Slang</b><br />
<br />
It's too bad that the Grantland website was shutdown earlier this year.  I enjoyed reading articles on various subjects and the site introduced me to some writers I've enjoyed following.  One of those was <a href="https://twitter.com/Steven_Hyden">Steven Hyden</a> who led me to some great new music this year.  Two of the bands that will be featured on this list were his recommendations during the year.  I absolutely love this Beach Slang album, reminds me a lot of the last Japandroids album which was a favorite of mine as well.  Hoping to see these guys live in 2016.<br />
<br />
<b><i>We are not alone. <br />
We are not mistakes. <br />
Don&#8217;t whisper now. <br />
We&#8217;re allowed to be loud. </i></b><br />
<br />
<iframe width="560" height="315" src="https://www.youtube.com/embed/i7nXvOTqyCY" frameborder="0" allowfullscreen></iframe><br />
<br />
<br />
<b>"Ship to Wreck" - Florence + The Machine</b><br />
<br />
I've never gotten the chance to see this band live but every time I see a live video from them it looks like they put on a great show.  I love Florence Welch's voice, it's incredible and she has a real knack for being a lead.  I love this clip from SNL, we've all seen some poor tv performances as well as some good ones but they knock it out of the park here. <br />
<br />
<i><b>And oh my love remind me, what was it that I did?<br />
Did I drink too much?<br />
Am I losing touch?<br />
Did I build this ship to wreck?</b></i><br />
<br />
<iframe width="560" height="315" src="https://www.youtube.com/embed/HgY7nzGxkD4" frameborder="0" allowfullscreen></iframe><br />
<br />
<br />
<b>"When You're Smiling and Astride Me" - Father John Misty</b><br />
<br />
I've enjoyed both of Father John Misty's albums to date.  Last year I listed "Bored In The USA" as a favorite, which also came off this same album but was released as a single late in 2014.  As you can see in the clip below, along with having a great voice, FJM puts on quite the show.  <br />
<br />
<i><b>When you're smiling and astride me<br />
I can hardly believe I've found you and I'm terrified by that</b></i><br />
<br />
<iframe width="560" height="315" src="https://www.youtube.com/embed/UXXfXhF8SQQ" frameborder="0" allowfullscreen></iframe><br />
<br />
<br />
<b>"Depreston" - Courtney Barnett</b><br />
<br />
Courtney Barnett is another newcomer to the list this year.  If you've read some other top of 2015 lists, you've probably seen her name mentioned.  She seems to be a favorite for many people this year and she's got a great sound.  This is my favorite off her album, reminds me of an old Lemonheads song. <br />
<br />
<i><b>If you've got a spare half a million<br />

Hello friends, another year has come and gone and it's time for my annual version of The Tremagazine Favorite Songs of the year.  I spent about two months compiling this list, starting a bit earlier than I usually do as I had a lot of material to review and wanted to share the best that I could find.  <br />
<br />
As I typically do, I'll list some of my favorite lyrics along with some personal thoughts on the song and/or artist. I was fortunate enough to see several of these acts live and I'll include some thoughts on those experiences as well.  One thing I tried to incorporate this year was finding as many live versions of the songs that I could share.  Many of these artists & groups are excellent performers so I wanted to capture that best I could.  Additionally, I've added a spotify playlist so those of you that use it can just stream the songs in one place.  <br />
<br />
I enjoyed putting this together and I hope you can find some new favorites that you may have missed during the year.  As always, I appreciate any feedback and thoughts regarding the list by either commenting here or shooting me an email.  A lot of the fun in putting this list together is getting the chance to share it with all of you. Without further ado, in no particular order, here's my fifty favorite songs of 2014..... hope you enjoy!  <br />

<b>"Slow Coming" - Benjamin Booker (from the album, <i>Benjamin Booker</i>)</b><br />
<br />
I've seen this album show up on a couple "best of 2014" lists but I still think overall it deserves to be on more.  I spent many hours listening to this record during the year and it only grew on me with every listen.  I was able to see Benjamin Booker a couple times in different settings, first at Austin City Limits (my first trip, which was amazing by the way) and then at Mercury Lounge in NYC.  Completely different venues but both performances were met with a ton of energy and passion from Benjamin and his band mates.  This is the first of two songs that made my list but I could have easily included a few more, wonderful debut album. <br />

Sometimes schedules get put together early for festivals and then the bands just kind of explode in popularity, which I think is what we saw here.  Anyways, it's well deserved as Shakey Graves is a great live act and it's fun seeing that many people rally around an up and coming artist.  <br />

I think Kendrick is extremely talented, both lyrically and performance-wise.  I don't think there's many artists that could pull of what he does in the video below.  <br />
<br />
<i>Dreams of reality's peace<br />
Blow steam in the face of the beast<br />
The sky could fall down, the wind could cry now<br />
The strong in me, I still smile</i><br />
<br />
<iframe width="560" height="315" src="//www.youtube.com/embed/sop2V_MREEI" frameborder="0" allowfullscreen></iframe><br />
<br />
<br />
<b>"Houston" - Robert Ellis (from the album, <i>Songs From The Chemical Plant</i>)</b><br />
<br />
Another good one from Robert Ellis.  I couldn't make this performance as I had a conflict that night but happy this was recorded, excellent version.  <br />
<br />
<i>When I came here I was a boy<br />
And now I'm leaving a man<br />
When I came here I was lost<br />
But now I know who I am</i><br />
<br />
<iframe width="560" height="315" src="//www.youtube.com/embed/VofZEu-d-Zw" frameborder="0" allowfullscreen></iframe><br />
<br />
<br />
<b>"Head Underwater" - Jenny Lewis (from the album, <i>The Voyager</i>)</b><br />
<br />
Jenny Lewis played on the main stage during the day at the Newport Folk Festival and I got to watch her entire set, great stuff.  Love this song off her latest album.  <br />
<br />
<i>I put my head underwater baby,<br />
I held my breath until it passed<br />
Crossed my fingers and concentrated<br />
I closed my eyes and I was free at last<br />
<i></i></i><br />
<br />
<iframe width="560" height="315" src="//www.youtube.com/embed/Hv5GE107sXY" frameborder="0" allowfullscreen></iframe><br />
<br />
<br />
<b>"It Kills" - Afghan Whigs (from the album, <i>Do To The Beast</i>)</b><br />
<br />
This band has been making great music for many, many years. I was surprised how much I liked this album but they're still going strong. <br />
<br />
<i>Over and over I get to know myself.<br />
Over and over there ain't nobody else<br />
Imperceptible the lines I draw to you.<br />
It kills to watch you love another</i><br />
<br />
<iframe width="560" height="315" src="//www.youtube.com/embed/H06Dwj_IOCc" frameborder="0" allowfullscreen></iframe><br />
<br />
<br />
<b>"I Thought I Heard You Screaming" - Benjamin Booker (from the album, <i>Benjamin Booker</i>)</b><br />
<br />
This song kills me.  Fell in love with it the first time it hit my ears.  <br />
<br />
<i>Set yourself on fire so high so high<br />
I could feel your heart was speeding<br />
Did you love me or was it the drugs?<br />
<br />
And the life we&#8217;re living now will surely bury me</i><br />
<br />
<iframe width="560" height="315" src="//www.youtube.com/embed/Z-iGGnTwZTw" frameborder="0" allowfullscreen></iframe><br />
<br />
<br />
<b>"Would You Fight For My Love" - Jack White (from the album, <i>Lazaretto</i>)</b><br />
<br />
Confession, I've never been a huge White Stripes fan.  I was never negative on them, just never clicked for me.  Jack White headlined Newport Folk this year on the second night and man, he was phenomenal.  I really, really enjoyed his set.  Super talented guy and great backing band.  I've written a bit about how Newport is such a special and unique festival and if you haven't seen it, I think you should watch <a href="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=cEdQbEcGO0I">THIS VIDEO</a>.  From what I've read and heard, this festival had really strong impact on him and I think you can see that in his eyes towards the end of the song.... almost brought to tears. Call me a fan, I will certainly be seeing him again. <br />
<br />
<i>But I'm afraid of being hurt that's true<br />
But not afraid of any physical pain<br />
Just as I am always scared of water<br />
But not afraid of standing out in the rain</i><br />
<br />
<iframe width="560" height="315" src="//www.youtube.com/embed/_t-rHNlsACY" frameborder="0" allowfullscreen></iframe><br />
<br />
<br />
<b>"Wild Animals" - Trampled By Turtles (from the album, <i>Wild Animals</i>)</b><br />
<br />
I've seen this band at a couple festivals and I've really enjoyed their live performances.  They put out a really solid studio album in 2014 and I think this is one of the better tracks.  <br />
<br />
<i>I see the better part of you<br />
I see the better part of you<br />
But I'm a monster just like you<br />
Wild animals it's true</i><br />
<br />
<iframe width="560" height="315" src="//www.youtube.com/embed/3_ae4-XQZr4" frameborder="0" allowfullscreen></iframe><br />
<br />
<br />
<b>"Break The Cycle" - You+Me (from the album, <i>Rose Ave.</i>)</b><br />
<br />
Since this album was in my rotation most of the year I decided to include a second track.  <br />
<br />
<i>Circles and cycles and seasons<br />
For everything there's always reason<br />
But it's never good<br />
Never turns out as it should</i><br />
<br />
<iframe width="560" height="315" src="//www.youtube.com/embed/mXmqfcyrnJU" frameborder="0" allowfullscreen></iframe><br />
<br />
<br />
<b>"Stayin Out All Night" - Wiz Khalifa (from the album, <i>Blacc Hollywood</i>)</b><br />
<br />
I completely dig Wiz Khalifa, I like most of his material.  I think the performance below is really unique.  <br />
<br />
<i>You work hard for what you got<br />
they could never take it from you<br />
All you need is a little patience<br />
let me demonstrate</i><br />
<br />
<iframe width="560" height="315" src="//www.youtube.com/embed/fIak8qW7FOo" frameborder="0" allowfullscreen></iframe><br />
<br />
<br />
<b>"Fever" - The Black Keys (from the album, <i>Turn Blue</i>)</b><br />
<br />
Talk about a band that's exploded, unbelievable.  I cannot believe how big these guys are now.  I sat in the upper deck at the Barclays Center a few months back looking around in disbelief at a packed stadium.  Just a great live band. <br />
<br />
<i>Now if the cold pale light in your eyes<br />
Reaches those horizon lines<br />
You know not to leave her</i><br />
<br />

<br />
<br />
<b>"Burning" - The War On Drugs (from the album, <i>Lost In The Dream</i>)</b><br />
<br />
Another great one from the war on drugs, this performance is really cool.  <br />
<br />
<i>Wide awake<br />
I rearrange the way I listen in the dark<br />
Dreaming, starting up again</i><br />
<br />
<iframe width="560" height="315" src="//www.youtube.com/embed/Wo9GwYnZPCc" frameborder="0" allowfullscreen></iframe><br />
<br />
<br />
<b>"Bad Self Portraits" - Lake Street Dive (from the album, <i>Bad Self Portraits</i>)</b><br />
<br />
How great is Rachel Price's voice?  Listen to that video and tell me it doesn't knock you on your ass?  Amazing. <br />
<br />
<i>I've spent my life<br />
so lost on lovin'<br />
I could have been a painter or a president<br />
But after 25 years<br />
I should be good at something<br />
Gone are the days of me being so innocent</i><br />
<br />
<iframe width="560" height="315" src="//www.youtube.com/embed/l2aZTYVnY0Y" frameborder="0" allowfullscreen></iframe><br />
<br />
<br />
<b>"Close Your Eyes (And Count to Fuck)" - Run The Jewels feat. Zack De La Rocha (from the album, <i>Run The Jewels 2</i>)</b><br />
<br />
Run the Jewels AND Zack De La Rocha?!?!?  I am a huge fan of Rage Against The Machine.  Saw them many times back in the day, one of the greatest live bands ever assembled.  Again not the best sound quality in the video below but a chance to see Zack on stage sold me on it.<br />
<br />
<i>It's De La on the cut,liftin' 6 on your snitchin' crew<br />
I'm miles ahead of you, you can sip my bitches brew<br />
My battle status is burnin' mansions from Dallas to Malibu<br />
Check my résumé, your residence is residue</i><br />
<br />
<iframe width="560" height="315" src="//www.youtube.com/embed/tyGxlhMbJLQ" frameborder="0" allowfullscreen></iframe><br />
<br />
<br />
<b>"Stars" - Angel Olsen (from the album, <i>Burn Your Fire For No Witness</i>)</b><br />
<br />
This was one of my last entries into the list but really a beautiful song.  I love the title of her album too, phenomenal.  <br />
<br />
<i>To scream the stars out of our universe<br />
To scream at all back into nothingness<br />
To scream the feeling til there's nothing left</i><br />
<br />
<iframe width="560" height="315" src="//www.youtube.com/embed/3u9tcPiSKg4" frameborder="0" allowfullscreen></iframe><br />
<br />
<br />
<b>"Take Shelter" - Delta Spirit (from the album, <i>Into The Wide</i>)</b><br />
<br />
The only band that had three songs on my list but it was my favorite album of 2014 so I felt it was worth it. <br />
<br />
<i>Hold your head up to the light, wait till it's over now. <br />
Take my hand and hold it tight, wait till it's over now. <br />
Everything will be alright, wait till it's over now.</i><br />
<br />
<iframe width="560" height="315" src="//www.youtube.com/embed/642quSiWM_E" frameborder="0" allowfullscreen></iframe><br />
<br />
<br />
<b>"White Gardenias" - Justin Townes Earle (from the album, <i>Single Mothers</i>)</b><br />
<br />
With every Justin Townes Earle release, you're guaranteed to find at least one gem within the track listing.  This was the song for me, stunning. <br />
<br />
<i>So if you see her out tonight,<br />
Yeah just look her in the eyes<br />
You might not know it now,<br />
But you'll know it when you see her<br />
<br />
White dress, white shoes, and white gardenias</i><br />
<br />
<iframe width="560" height="315" src="//www.youtube.com/embed/b0IKMldcfes" frameborder="0" allowfullscreen></iframe><br />
<br />
<br />
<b>"When I Get My Hands On You" - The New Basement Tapes (from the album, <i>Lost On The River</i>)</b><br />
<br />
Second entry from this collection of musicians.  Very sweet & sentimental lyrically.  <br />
<br />
<i>When I come home to you<br />
Gonna take you down to the riverside<br />
When I come home to you<br />
Hold you in my arms all night</i><br />
<br />
<iframe width="560" height="315" src="//www.youtube.com/embed/daDceKdhRWU" frameborder="0" allowfullscreen></iframe><br />
<br />
<br />
<b>"XO" - John Mayer (from the single, <i>XO</i>)</b><br />
<br />
Obviously a cover of the Beyonce hit but I thought it was a really unique take on a popular song that almost sounds like an original. <br />
<br />
<i>In the darkest night hour (in the darkest night hour)<br />
I'll search through the crowd (I'll search through the crowd)<br />
Your face is all that I see<br />
I'll give you everything</i><br />
<br />
<iframe width="420" height="315" src="//www.youtube.com/embed/JVH6OkUT7Dc" frameborder="0" allowfullscreen></iframe><br />
<br />
<br />
<b>"Homesick" - Catfish & The Bottlemen (from the album, <i>Kathleen and the Other Three</i>)</b><br />
<br />
One more from Catfish & The Bottlemen... I'm pretty sure we'll be hearing more about these guys in 2015.<br />
<br />
<i>I got misled, mistook, discard<br />
Anything that I said<br />
See I'm not the type to call you up drunk<br />
But I've got some lies to tell<br />
</i><br />
<br />
<iframe width="560" height="315" src="//www.youtube.com/embed/vsrUD6Y2qgk" frameborder="0" allowfullscreen></iframe><br />
<br />
<br />
<b>"Betray My Heart" - D'Angelo and The Vanguard (from the album, <i>Black Messiah</i>)</b><br />
<br />
The last entry into the list!  Just made it in by the surprise 2014 release of this album.  Somebody had to get bumped but it's worth it for a song this good.  Welcome back, D'Angelo. <br />
<br />
<i>Like the breeze that blows in June<br />
I will steady keep you cool<br />
This I swear with all that's true<br />
I'll take nothing in place of you</i><br />

<br />
