   By [145]Patrick Colorcrayons on July 2, 2015
   A great twist to the Heroscape franchise. Now offering hand management
   and deck building along with the already familiar army building found
   in Heroscape.
   The game concentrates on your planeswalker and a keystone for your
   force, similar to how casters operate in warmachine/hordes.
   A light miniatures wargame that offers surprising depth for the content
   provided.
   This isn't meant to replace or augment the magic the gathering CCG, but
   rather, offer a new way to explore the ideas illustrated in the
   original game using the proven game engine found in Heroscape.
   Recommended.
   The plastic tiles provided in this game, do indeed, allow you to
   incorporate your older heroscape tiles. So let's put that argument to
   rest.
   All pieces are stored within a great insert in the box. And while the
   card stock is not the same quality as the card stock found in the M:tG
   CcG, they work well enough considering how unnecessary it is to shuffle
   the cards compared to its ccg counterpart.
   The art quality on the cards is what you would expect for a product
   using the M:tG brand. Much of which is recycled art for CCG cards, with
   a few new pieces just for this set.
   The game is assembled quickly with the new cardboard base tiles. Each
   cardboard tile mimics two 24 hex tiles in heroscape, with great art
   design on each. Sadly, the tiles are not double sided with terrain, and
   instead, illustrate planeswalker artwork on the reverse of each tile.
   But again, the game is assembled and disassembled so much more quickly
   than heroscape ever was. So it does have that as a positive for some
   people, while drastically cutting the cost to produce and sel the game.

   By [153]Andrew J. Riemer on October 6, 2015
   FULL DISCLOSURE: I'm an avid player and collector of Magic: the
   Gathering (the collectible card game) and of Heroscape (the original
   version of this re-branded miniatures-based strategy game).
   BOTTOM LINE?
   It's a fun game, but it can be pretty involved. It takes some time to
   get familiar with the rules and flow of the game, but once you pick it
   up, the game moves well from turn to turn. I give it only three stars
   because I was disappointed with some of the packaging (see below) and
   the construction/finish of the various squad figures (also detailed
   below).
   I was also disappointed with the re-branding. I believe they could have
   introduced Magic: the Gathering themed rules and sets to the original
   Heroscape environment, just as Hasbro/Wizards of the Coast had
   introduced Dungeons & Dragons themed sets before Heroscape was
   discontinued. In fact, 99% of the rules mirror the Heroscape rules,
   with only minor changes.
   THE PRODUCT ITSELF
   As to the game itself, I have mixed feelings. The game is fun. The
   spell cards add a new dimension. The cost of this initial set has a
   MSRP of $29.99 --not inexpensive, but significantly cheaper than its
   predacessor (Heroscape master sets were introduced at $39.99), though
   you get far less for that price. The painted Planeswalker figures are
   beautifully painted. The other squad figures are, however, hugely
   disappointing.
   They are plain, colored plastic--no details painted on them at all.
   After seeing how awesome the painted figures look, the rest look like
   cheap junk thrown in. The detail of the painted figures was one of the
   first things that drew me to the original Heroscape game. They made the
   experience of playing the game more full and rich--more

   [159]4.0 out of 5 stars[160]A Good Light Skirmish Game with Sorcery!
   By [161]Cody Carlson [162]TOP 1000 REVIEWER[163]VINE VOICE on August
   21, 2015
   Vine Customer Review of Free Product ( [164]What's this? )
   So... Confession time. I have never played Magic: The Gathering. Oh,
   I've seen the multitudes at the local game store queuing up on
   tournament nights, smacking cards down on the table, wearing that smug
   sense of self-satisfaction on their big stupid faces... But I digress.
   My point is that I didn't know exactly what I was getting into with
   Magic: The Gathering Arena of the Planeswalkers. I had heard it was a
   re-theming of Heroscape, another game I haven't played that has a wide
   following. I just thought the minis looked cool, and the epic mage
   fights sounded awesome.
   I'm pleased to report that Magic: The Gathering Arena of the
   Planeswalkers is s really fun game. Two to five players can play in a
   free-for-all, or there are scenarios for a 2v2 game in the book. After
   setting up the board- complete with some 3D terrain- each player gets
   his own Planeswalker, which represents things like fire, nature,
   necromancy, etc... Players then take army cards which depict squads
   that can be summoned and put into play. On a player's turn they draw a
   card (sorcery for one off effects, enchantments for ongoing bonuses),
   then select which army card to activate, move with, and/or attack.
   Combat is pretty simple: the attacker rolls a number of dice and
   however many crossed sword icons come up is counted as a hit. The
   defender rolls the same dice, and however many shield icons come up
   negate the crossed sword hits. Players continue taking turns until they
   have reached the number of turns for the specific scenario (players add
   up units still on the board to determine score), or if only one
   Planeswalker remains.
   The game is essentially a light combat skirmish game, but it really
   works and plays well.[165]Read more ›
