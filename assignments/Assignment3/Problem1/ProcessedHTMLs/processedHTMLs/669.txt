   [157]4.0 out of 5 stars[158]"A Killer Game Where the Fun Never Dies"!
   By [159]Happy Reader [160]TOP 500 REVIEWER[161]VINE VOICE on October 4,
   2014
   Package Type: Standard Packaging
   I just purchased this game to put out at our Halloween party this year.
   "Zombie-Opoly" is a visually colorful game with a currently popular
   motif. As you'd expect, it plays just like "Monopoly", but everything
   has different names.
   The "Chance" and "Community Chest" cards are now called "Crawl" and
   "Stagger". You may get a card that says, "You are crowned Miss Rage
   Zombie! Collect $100", or, "You ate too much hamstring! Lose one turn",
   both accompanied by suitably zombie illustrations.
   The properties around the board are more like personalities, with names
   like "Goodbye Kitty", "Zombarista" and "Chef Boyzombie". In a nice
   touch, the backs of the property cards have zombie trivia or jokes. For
   example, one starts, "Researchers in tropical rainforests have
   identified a new species of brain-controlling fungal parasites. These
   fungi infect carpenter ants and turn them into zombies...."
   Money is called Blood Money, so the Bank is, of course, the Blood Bank.
   In place of Houses and Hotels, you have gray squares = boxes of flesh,
   and screaming heads = screaming humans. You buy these and place them on
   your personality/properties just as in regular Monopoly, with one
   Screaming Head = 4 Boxes of Flesh. This is how you become a property
   magnate in Zombieland. I am personally fond of the screaming heads - a
   great touch!
   According to the box, play is for 2-6 players, age 8 and up. The six
   player pieces are made of metal, which is nice. They are a shovel, ear,
   bone and hammer, which can fit the zombie motif. The 5th is a boot,
   which is a stretch. And the 6th is a child in pajamas and holding a
   teddy bear. Its zombie significance escapes me.
   In short, this is a fun variation of Monopoly. If we only bring it out
   at Halloween, we shouldn't get tired of it.[162]Read more ›
