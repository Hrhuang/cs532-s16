<div id="blox-story-text" class="entry-content">
    <div id="paging_container" class="container">
        <div class="content">
            
                
                    
                    
                    
                        
                            <span class="first-paragraph">
                        
                        <span class="paragraph-0"><p>The most coveted hunt to obtain through the Oklahoma Department of Wildlife Conservations controlled hunts program is undoubtedly the once-in-a-lifetime elk hunt in the Wichita Mountains Wildlife Refuge.</p></span>
                        
                            </span>
                            
                        
                    
                        
                
                    
                    
                    
                        
                        <span class="paragraph-1"><p>The chance of being drawn for this hunt is minute. This year, over 35,000 hunters applied for the 180 available elk tags, leaving each applicant with a 0.005 percent chance of acquiring a tag.</p></span>
                        
                        <div class="p402_hide no-images no-quotes yes-pagination"></div>
                    
                        
                
                    
                    
                    
                        
                        <span class="paragraph-2"><p>Tahlequah native Blake Stephens, age 54, was one of the hunters whose name was drawn.</p></span>
                        
                    
                        
                
                    
                    
                    
                        
                        <span class="paragraph-3"><p>Blake, a lifelong outdoorsman who has experience harvesting a wide range of different game, had entered his name in the lottery-type elk hunt drawing for 29 years before finally being selected. And much to his surprise, his son, 29-year-old Joshua Stephens, was also drawn for this years prestigious elk hunt after applying for the past 11 years.</p></span>
                        
                    
                        
                
                    
                    
                    
                        
                        <span class="paragraph-4"><p>We were elated about being drawn out, Blake said. My son, Joshua, called me and said he had been selected for the elk hunt, and I thought he was pulling my leg. It just sounded too good to be true for one of us to be that fortunate.</p></span>
                        
                    
                        
                
                    
                    
                    
                        
                        <span class="paragraph-5"><p>Turns out, Joshua and Blake were the fortunate ones. After his phone conversation with his son, Blake checked the ODWC website and found that he, too, had been drawn for the hunt.</p></span>
                        
                    
                        
                
                    
                    
                    
                        
                        <span class="paragraph-6"><p>I immediately called Joshua back and told him the good news, Blake said. We were really fired up and excited. The odds of one person drawing out for a hunt in their lifetime is small enough, but for a man and his son to draw out in the same year, thats basically like winning the lottery.</p></span>
                        
                    
                        
                
                    
                    
                    
                        
                        <span class="paragraph-7"><p>As advertised, the Wichita Mountains elk hunt is truly a once-in-a-lifetime event. Once a hunter draws out, he or she will never be eligible for the hunt again. That is true whether the hunter harvests an elk, does not harvest an elk or is unable to make the trip.</p></span>
                        
                    
                        
                
                    
                    
                    
                        
                        <span class="paragraph-8"><p>You know that youll never get to do it again, Blake said. About seven or 10 years ago was when they made it a lifetime hunt. So, up until then, if you drew out, you could put your name into the drawing again. But thats not the case anymore.</p></span>
                        
                    
                        
                
                    
                    
                    
                        
                        <span class="paragraph-9"><p>Hunters who are lucky enough to be drawn also get to see parts of the Wichita Mountains refuge not open to the public. Unlike most whitetail deer hunting where you sit in a stand and wait, in the estimated 5,000-acre designated hunting area in the Wichitas, you have to spot and stalk the elk.</p></span>
                        
                    
                        
                
                    
                    
                    
                        
                        <span class="paragraph-10"><p>This years bunch of selected hunters were divided into two separate groups for a 2.5-day hunt in December and a 2.5-day hunt in January. Blake was slotted for the December hunt, while Joshua was scheduled for January.</p></span>
                        
                    
                        
                
                    
                    
                    
                        
                        <span class="paragraph-11"><p>Each hunter was allowed to being a helper to help skin, quarter and pack the harvested elk. Joshua and Blake chose each other to be their appointed helpers, giving them an opportunity to experience the prestigious event twice over the course of a little more than one month.</p></span>
                        
                    
                        
                
                    
                    
                    
                        
                        <span class="paragraph-12"><p>Blake noted that while the Wichita Mountains features a beautiful and scenic landscape, the rough terrain can be physically taxing.</p></span>
                        
                    
                        
                
                    
                    
                    
                        
                        <span class="paragraph-13"><p>Youre walking around on mountainous, rough terrain stalking elk all day and it gets tiring, so it makes sense to have a helper with you to help harvest your elk, Blake said. It was pretty neat to have Joshua with me for my hunt and for me to help him out on his.</p></span>
                        
                    
                        
                
                    
                    
                    
                        
                        <span class="paragraph-14"><p>Both Blake and Joshua harvested their elk on the first day of their respective hunts, and coincidentally, both of them acquired their bulls at the same time of 4:10 p.m. Blakes bull (harvested on December 8) measured out at 6-by-6 and field dressed at 476 pounds, while Joshua also acquired a 6-by-6 bull (harvested on January 12).</p></span>
                        
                    
                        
                
                    
                    
                    
                        
                        <span class="paragraph-15"><p>The 29-year anticipation of what my hunt would someday be is now a memory, Blake said. I felt blessed to be able to share my experience with my son. The U.S. Fish and Wildlife Service and the Oklahoma Department of Wildlife Conservation partner in making this one of the best hunts in the country. The staff goes above and beyond to make sure the hunters enjoy their lifetime hunt.</p></span>
                        
                    
                        
                
                    
                    
                    
                        
                        <span class="paragraph-16"><p>The elk herd in the Wichita Mountains refuge is estimated to be around 1,000 before the hunts. Rod Smith, the Southwest Region Supervisor for the refuge, reported that 63 bulls and cows were harvested in the December hunt this year, leaving a 90 percent success rate for bull hunters and a 75 percent success rate for cow hunters. In the January hunt, there were 62 bulls and cows harvested. The overall success rate was 81 percent for both hunts.</p></span>
