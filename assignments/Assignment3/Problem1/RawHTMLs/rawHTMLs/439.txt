forward Colin Wilson has been removed from injured reserve and is expected to return Tuesday against the Capitals.</p><p>"Yeah, I think that's the plan right now," Wilson said after fully practicing Monday.</p><p>Lower-body injuries previously sidelined Wilson for five games in December, but he declined to specify Monday if his most recent ailment was related to his previous ones. The Predators canceled their morning skate Tuesday, but based on Monday's practice, Wilson could start on a line with forwards Mike Fisher and Craig Smith.</p><p>Wilson's season has undoubtedly been disappointing. He scored a career-high 20 goals last season, but has not been able to overcome the inconsistency that has defined his NHL career. He has four goals this season, once enduring a 21-game goalless drought that spanned two months, and his 5.3 shooting percentage is well below his 12.3 career average.</p><div id="module-position-O0LRKzoNnvI" class="story-asset oembed-asset"><div class="story-oembed-wrap "><div
