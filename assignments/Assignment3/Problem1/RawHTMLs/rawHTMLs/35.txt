<p class="entry-meta"><time class="entry-time" itemprop="datePublished" datetime="2013-12-13T09:00:31+00:00">December 13, 2013</time> by <span class="entry-author" itemprop="author" itemscope itemtype="http://schema.org/Person"><a href="http://www.castleviewacademy.com/author/crystalmcclean/" class="entry-author-link" itemprop="url" rel="author"><span class="entry-author-name" itemprop="name">Crystal McClean</span></a></span> <span class="entry-comments-link"><a href="http://www.castleviewacademy.com/christmas-tree-game-toddlers-preschoolers/#comments">16 Comments</a></span></p></header><div class="entry-content" itemprop="text"><div class='shareaholic-canvas' data-app-id='7873674' data-app='share_buttons' data-title='Christmas Tree Game for Toddlers &amp; Preschoolers' data-link='http://www.castleviewacademy.com/christmas-tree-game-toddlers-preschoolers/' data-summary=''></div><p>Our Christmas Tree Game for toddlers and preschoolers is a fun and fast-paced learning game.  We&#8217;ve used it for helping our children learn their shapes and colours.  It can also be adapted for primary aged kids for other subjects.  This post contains affiliate links for your convenience.</p>
<p><a href="http://www.castleviewacademy.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/12/PicMonkey-Collage.jpg"><img class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-9012" src="http://www.castleviewacademy.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/12/PicMonkey-Collage.jpg" alt="Christmas Tree Race Game for learning fun" width="600" height="600" srcset="http://www.castleviewacademy.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/12/PicMonkey-Collage-150x150.jpg 150w, http://www.castleviewacademy.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/12/PicMonkey-Collage-300x300.jpg 300w, http://www.castleviewacademy.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/12/PicMonkey-Collage-120x120.jpg 120w, http://www.castleviewacademy.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/12/PicMonkey-Collage.jpg 600w" sizes="(max-width: 600px) 100vw, 600px" /></a></p>
<p><span style="color: #008000;">What supplies will you need for the Christmas Tree Game?</span></p>
<ul>
<li>Green construction paper or<a href="http://www.amazon.com/gp/search?ie=UTF8&amp;camp=1789&amp;creative=9325&amp;index=arts-crafts&amp;keywords=cardstock&amp;linkCode=ur2&amp;tag=crysstinytrea-20" onclick="__gaTracker('send', 'event', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.amazon.com/gp/search?ie=UTF8&amp;camp=1789&amp;creative=9325&amp;index=arts-crafts&amp;keywords=cardstock&amp;linkCode=ur2&amp;tag=crysstinytrea-20', ' card stock');" title="AA cardstock"  target="_blank"> card stock</a></li>
<li>scrap bits of paper in various colours</li>
<li>scissors and/or <a href="http://www.amazon.com/gp/search?ie=UTF8&amp;camp=1789&amp;creative=9325&amp;index=arts-crafts&amp;keywords=craft%20punches&amp;linkCode=ur2&amp;tag=crysstinytrea-20" onclick="__gaTracker('send', 'event', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.amazon.com/gp/search?ie=UTF8&amp;camp=1789&amp;creative=9325&amp;index=arts-crafts&amp;keywords=craft%20punches&amp;linkCode=ur2&amp;tag=crysstinytrea-20', 'craft punches');" title="AA craft punch"  target="_blank">craft punches</a></li>
<li>laminator or clear <a href="http://www.amazon.com/gp/search?ie=UTF8&amp;camp=1789&amp;creative=9325&amp;index=arts-crafts&amp;keywords=sticky%20backed%20plastic&amp;linkCode=ur2&amp;tag=crysstinytrea-20" onclick="__gaTracker('send', 'event', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.amazon.com/gp/search?ie=UTF8&amp;camp=1789&amp;creative=9325&amp;index=arts-crafts&amp;keywords=sticky%20backed%20plastic&amp;linkCode=ur2&amp;tag=crysstinytrea-20', 'sticky-backed plastic');" title="sticky backed paper"  target="_blank">sticky-backed plastic</a></li>
</ul>
<h2><span style="color: #008000;">How to assemble the Christmas Tree Game.</span></h2>
<p>I cut out the shape of a Christmas tree from a piece of green construction paper and laminated it.</p>
<p>The first year Tristan and I played this he was learning about colours and shapes.  I cut out many shapes from various colours of paper.  some I cut free-hand and others I used a paper punch for.  I laminated and cut out the little shape decorations.</p>
<p><a href="http://www.castleviewacademy.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/12/IMG_1599-1.jpg"><img class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-9008" src="http://www.castleviewacademy.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/12/IMG_1599-1.jpg" alt="Christmas Tree Race Game for kids, Christmas learning games,  learning shapes, learning colours, photo" width="600" height="377" srcset="http://www.castleviewacademy.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/12/IMG_1599-1-300x188.jpg 300w, http://www.castleviewacademy.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/12/IMG_1599-1.jpg 600w" sizes="(max-width: 600px) 100vw, 600px" /></a></p>
<p>I used some fun-tak to hold the trees up on the window (thus they double as window decorations when not in use).  However, I wouldn&#8217;t advise having them on a window for the game itself.  We have a small home so I&#8217;ve used the window as a demonstration as our hall is too small to take photos in (and in case of an accident during the photos our glass is toughened to industry standard for childminding).</p>
<p>There was a little bit of fun-tak on the back of each shape decoratoion.  We put them on the back on our <a title="10 LITTLE SNOWMEN" href="http://www.castleviewacademy.com/10-little-snowmen/">10 Little Snowman Cards</a> and placed them across the room from the trees.</p>
<h2><span style="color: #008000;">How we play the Christmas Tree Game.</span></h2>
<p><img class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-8950" src="http://www.castleviewacademy.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/11/IMG_1605-1.jpg" alt="Christmas race, Christmas games for daycares, learning games for nurseries, Christmas games for childminders, photo" width="600" height="522" srcset="http://www.castleviewacademy.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/11/IMG_1605-1-300x261.jpg 300w, http://www.castleviewacademy.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/11/IMG_1605-1.jpg 600w" sizes="(max-width: 600px) 100vw, 600px" /></p>
<p>The kids stood behind the cards on the floor and I called out a shape or a colour and they raced to pick up the correct item from their card and ran to put it on their tree.</p>
<p><a href="http://www.castleviewacademy.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/12/IMG_1606-1.jpg"><img class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-9010" src="http://www.castleviewacademy.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/12/IMG_1606-1.jpg" alt="Indoor games for Christmas, Crystal's Tiny Treasures, Christmas learning games, photo" width="600" height="307" srcset="http://www.castleviewacademy.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/12/IMG_1606-1-300x153.jpg 300w, http://www.castleviewacademy.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/12/IMG_1606-1.jpg 600w" sizes="(max-width: 600px) 100vw, 600px" /></a></p>
<p>The first one to get their item on the tree won that round.  Or you can mix up the decorations and play until one player has an empty card.</p>
<p>This game will grow with your children as you can write numbers, letters, or words on the decorations.  And when they&#8217;re a bit older yet, they could pick up the answer to math questions: 19-4 (6) for example.</p>
<p><a href="http://www.castleviewacademy.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/12/IMG_1653-1.jpg"><img class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-9011" src="http://www.castleviewacademy.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/12/IMG_1653-1.jpg" alt="Christmas learning fun for kids, frugal Christmas games for kids, photo" width="600" height="423" srcset="http://www.castleviewacademy.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/12/IMG_1653-1-300x211.jpg 300w, http://www.castleviewacademy.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/12/IMG_1653-1.jpg 600w" sizes="(max-width: 600px) 100vw, 600px" /><br />
</a></p>
<p>If you have only one child, you can race against them yourself or use a timer to see how full they can make the tree before their time is up.</p>
