It had the motivation. It had the talent.</p><p>Oh boy, that talent.</p><p>The Warriors featured two Parade All-Americans, defensive back Jerimy Finch and running back Darren Evans, the IndyStar Mr. Football. Dullaghan and Peyton Manning. Dullaghan, the former Ben Davis coach, told Tutsie the '06 Warren Central was the best team he had ever seen.</p><p>"That says a lot," Tutsie said.</p><p>It said a lot that even after three successive state championships, Warren Central aimed higher. At the end of the season, the Warriors were ranked fourth nationally by ESPN and USA Today, and a case could be made that they were underrated.</p><p>Their Game 2 victory was by 61-12 over Maine South, a suburban Chicago school coming off a runner-up finish in Illinois' largest class (8A).</p><p>Among Tutsie's ploys was to make "212 degrees" a team motto. Water is hot at 211. It boils at 212.</p><p>Warren Central boiled over against Ben Davis, underscoring the fact that this was a force unlike anything seen before in this state. Evans scored six touchdowns against the Giants — half the times he touched the ball — and rushed for 243 yards. His TDs included runs of 65, 55 and 51 yards.</p><p>"We (have) seen how good we could be," Evans said. "I think we wanted to match that every week."</p><p>Evans rushed for 201 yards and three touchdowns in the climactic 35-14 victory over Carmel in the championship game at the RCA Dome.</p><p>He rushed for 61 touchdowns that season — then No. 4 in U.S. history and still No. 6 — and 2,806 yards. And that was against opponents in the Metropolitan Interscholastic Conference, one of the nation's strongest leagues and featuring schools with the largest enrollments. Contemporaries included future NFL players such as MarQueis Gray and Tandon Doss, both of Ben Davis, and North Central running back Chad Spann.</p><p>The Warriors recorded five shutouts in an era when scoring was much higher than a half-century ago. As spectacular as Warren Central's offense was, Tutsie said, the defense might have been as good. Carmel quarterback Andrew Durbin could attest to that.</p><p>"They just keep coming at you, and there's nothing you can do about it," Durbin said after the championship game.</p><p>Warren Central passed infrequently, so wide receiver Adrien Robinson had modest statistics that year: 28 catches. But he averaged 31 yards on those receptions.</p><p>Robinson scored his first NFL touchdown on a 1-yard pass from New York Giants quarterback Eli Manning against the Dallas Cowboys last Nov. 23. Other alumni in the NFL were Evans (Virginia Tech), an Orange Bowl MVP who was briefly with the Colts and Tennessee Titans; running back Jewell Hampton (Iowa/Southern Illinois), San Francisco 49ers; offensive lineman Andrew McDonald (Indiana), whose five teams included the Colts, and tight end Ed Hazelett (Western Kentucky), an undrafted free agent signed by the Oakland Raiders.</p><p>Life after Warren Central has been bumpy for some.</p><p>Evans, 26, is a customer service associate for Essco Indy, a supplier of equipment for those handling hazardous waste. Injuries contributed to an early end to his football career, and he speculated that concussions have affected him. He said he would play with headaches and take Tylenol later.</p><p>"It hasn't played a big part in my life," Evans said. "But I can tell a difference."</p><p>McDonald has undergone surgery for testicular cancer. He was identified as one of those harassed in the Miami Dolphins' bullying scandal, although his agent later refuted the report.</p><p>Finch was never the same player after he broke his leg early in his career at Florida. He transferred to Indiana and then to Marian University. In December, he was sentenced to a year of probation in a plea agreement after being arrested by Beech Grove police offers in connection with a liquor store burglary. The burglary charge was dismissed.</p><p>Back in that magical fall, the Warriors could do no wrong. So many students were behind the team, Tutsie said, that fights at school diminished. Burchett, now an assistant to the superintendent, said football success influenced achievement in other sports, performing arts, and elsewhere.</p><p>"It galvanized a good beginning to that particular year," Burchett said.</p><p>There was pressure on the Warriors ahead of the championship game — 20 consecutive victories, three straight titles, a national ranking — but they overran that as they did so many opponents. Finch was in on 10 tackles, Semiaj Wright intercepted two passes, and defensive end Michael Bell blocked a punt to set up a clinching touchdown.</p><p>Afterward, Tutsie was prescient when he asserted that the accomplishment would be more special 10 or 12 years later. It could be many more years before there is a team worthy to be mentioned in the same conversation with '06 Warren Central.</p><p>"It's almost 10 years later, and people are still talking about it," Evans said.
