		<p><a href="http://www.rotoworld.com/player/nba/1870/jimmy-butler" target="_blank">Jimmy Butler</a> is <a href="http://nba.nbcsports.com/2016/02/09/bulls-all-star-jimmy-butler-out-3-4-weeks-with-strained-knee/">out of the NBA All-Star Game in Toronto this weekend due to a strained knee</a>. Which sucks, because he earned that spot, and while the fans didn&#8217;t vote him in the coach&#8217;s did.</p>
<p>Butler&#8217;s teammate <a href="http://www.rotoworld.com/player/nba/766/pau-gasol" target="_blank">Pau Gasol</a> will replace him.</p>
<p>NBA Commissioner Adam Silver gets to make the call on a replacement, and he stayed in Chicago, but he added a front court player to replace a guard. Keeping a Bulls representative might have been part of the thinking. The coaches&#8217; votes on replacement players has been weighed in the past (Gasol may have been high on that list, coaches love him). Also, the East roster has a lot of wings and was light on bigs (<a href="http://www.rotoworld.com/player/nba/1965/andre-drummond" target="_blank">Andre Drummond</a>, <a href="http://www.rotoworld.com/player/nba/1316/paul-millsap" target="_blank">Paul Millsap</a> and <a href="http://www.rotoworld.com/player/nba/930/chris-bosh" target="_blank">Chris Bosh</a> are the only real bigs), so this gives coach Tyronn Lue some flexibility up front.</p>
<p>The East leading Cleveland Cavaliers remain with just one representative, <a href="http://www.rotoworld.com/player/nba/927/lebron-james" target="_blank">LeBron James</a> (voted in by the fans).</p>
<p>Gasol is averaging 17 points, 10.9 rebounds, and a couple blocksa game, and is the only thing close to a consistent performer the Bulls have. Besides the injured Butler.</p>

		<p>Next season, the Kings are moving into a new arena in downtown Sacramento, a state of the art building.</p>
<p>For the team&#8217;s six &#8220;Friday night flashback&#8221; games the rest of this season  plus the final contest  the team will play on a retro court designed to look almost exactly like the court at the Arco Arena when the team first moved to Sacramento in 1985. The court will have the Kings logo at center court, the original baby blue framing, and the &#8220;SACRAMENTO&#8221; on the baseline will be in the original font.</p>
<p>Our final season at Sleep Train Arena has been filled with opportunities to look back at our rich history, said Kings President Chris Granger in a statement. This court is a special way to salute all of the fans, players and coaches who have helped us build toward a proud future in Sacramento.</p>
<p>While there is always nostalgia, nobody is going to miss Arco/Sleep Train that much, it&#8217;s one of the more outdated buildings in the league. Plus the new arena looks fantastic. But I love this idea to celebrate the old look on the way out. Those baby blue uniforms are some of my favorite throwbacks.</p>

			<div class="article-sidebar-modules">

		<p>A report earlier this season said <a href="http://www.rotoworld.com/player/nba/1041/dwight-howard" target="_blank">Dwight Howard</a> <a href="http://nba.nbcsports.com/2015/12/15/rumor-dwight-howard-unhappy-taking-backseat-to-james-harden-with-rockets/">didn&#8217;t like playing second fiddle</a> to <a href="http://www.rotoworld.com/player/nba/1597/james-harden" target="_blank">James Harden</a> on the Rockets. <a href="http://nba.nbcsports.com/2015/12/16/dwight-howard-on-rumors-hes-unhappy-people-make-up-lies-and-rumors/">Howard denied it</a>, but here&#8217;s more evidence of the players&#8217; discord.</p>
<p><a href="http://www.nba.com/2016/news/features/fran_blinebury/02/11/houston-rockets-reaching-out-to-teams-to-see-about-deal-for-dwight-howard/">Fran Blinebury of NBA.com</a> (hat tip: <a href="https://www.reddit.com/r/nba/comments/46als8/blinbebury_it_was_only_days_after_damian_lillards/">reddit user Longh0rns</a>):</p>
<blockquote><p>Howard and Harden have been an oil-and-water mix since they came together for the 2012-13 season. It was only days after <a href="http://www.rotoworld.com/player/nba/1957/damian-lillard" target="_blank">Damian Lillard</a>&#8216;s 3-point dagger eliminated them in the first round of their first playoff series together when both were sending out messages and maneuvering to get the other one traded.</p>
<p>Harden is a difficult teammate to play with, dominating the ball and so much of every shot clock and Howard too often felt ignored.</p></blockquote>
<p>[<strong>Correction</strong>: That Rockets-Trail Blazers series occurred in 2014.]</p>
<p>Plenty could have changed since 2014, and Howard and Harden clicked well enough last season to lead the Rockets to the Western Conference finals.</p>
<p>But it&#8217;s hard to watch their disharmony  and, by extension, the Rockets&#8217; disharmony  this season and believe there are no lingering issues. It&#8217;s easier to get over those problems when the team is winning. When it&#8217;s losing, personal issues can be both a cause and symptom, snowballing on themselves.</p>
<p>To whatever extent Howard and Harden are still battling, <a href="http://nba.nbcsports.com/2016/02/11/report-rockets-working-with-dwight-howards-agent-on-trade/">Harden is winning</a>. That&#8217;s only logical considering Harden is better, four years younger and on a more favorable contract.</p>
<p>If Howard wants to stay in Houston (<a href="http://nba.nbcsports.com/2016/02/12/dwight-howard-says-he-hasnt-asked-rockets-for-trade-im-not-running/">he says he does</a>)and play without Harden (much more questionable), Howardshould have campaigned better twoyears ago.</p>

<p>After heading to <a href="http://nba.nbcsports.com/2015/06/22/report-charlotte-gets-2017-nba-all-star-game/" target="_blank">Charlotte</a> next year, the All-Star game appears likely for another warm destination in 2018.</p>

<blockquote><p>According to two people with knowledge of the situation, the NBA and Los Angeles officials have made significant progress toward finalizing a partnership for the event. The deal is not done, but the prospect of All-Star weekend returning to Los Angeles for the first time since 2010 is very strong.</p></blockquote>
<blockquote><p>Indianapolis and Miami were also known to be on the leagues short list of possible cities for 2018. Sacramento officials had hoped to land the 2018 event as a fitting reward for the expected October opening of the Kings Golden 1 Center in the downtown area, but have since shifted their focus to a 2019 bid.</p></blockquote>
<p><a href="http://www.cleveland.com/cavs/index.ssf/2016/02/los_angeles_lakers_opposed_to.html" target="_blank">Chris Haynes of Cleveland.com</a>:</p>
<blockquote><p>Sources say the league feels Brooklyn is deserving of its own All-Star Weekend in the near future since it shared the event with New York in 2015.</p></blockquote>
<p>Toronto and New York are excellent cities.</p>
<p>In February, though, Los Angeles is an upgrade.</p>

		<p>The Nets reportedly interviewed Sean Marks twice for their general-managing vacancy. Then, they reportedly <a href="http://nba.nbcsports.com/2016/02/16/report-nets-offer-gm-job-to-spurs-sean-marks/" target="_blank">offered him the job</a>.</p>
<p>But with Marks reportedly <a href="http://nba.nbcsports.com/2016/02/17/report-sean-marks-leaning-toward-rejecting-nets-offer/" target="_blank">leaning toward rejecting it</a>, Nets owner Mikhail Prokhorov <a href="http://nba.nbcsports.com/2016/02/17/mikhail-prokhorov-nets-havent-offered-anyone-gm-job-need-another-week/" target="_blank">says no offer has been made</a> and he needs another week.</p>
<p>If extending this search past the trade deadline weren&#8217;t embarrassing enough, Prokhorov digs his hole even deeper.</p>
<p>Andy Vasquez of The Record:</p>
<div class="embed-twitter">
<blockquote class="twitter-tweet" data-width="500">
<p lang="en" dir="ltr">When asked specifically about Sean Marks, Prokhorov said: &quot;I have never heard this name before.&quot;</p>
<p>&mdash; Andy Vasquez (@andy_vasquez) <a href="https://twitter.com/andy_vasquez/status/700034863454363648">February 17, 2016</a></p></blockquote>
<p><script async src="//platform.twitter.com/widgets.js" charset="utf-8"></script></div>
<p>Prokhorov is either clueless or outrageously bitter. The latter is far more likely, but either look tarnishes him more than anyone else.</p>
<p>I think we can safely assume Marks is out of the running. The next question: Will this make any other candidates leery of taking the job?</p>
