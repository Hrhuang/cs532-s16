                    <p>There it is, in bold white letters on a black bar, over the top of the shocking-pink Leet.gg website: “Kill your friends and take their money.” </p>

<p class="S06StoryIndent00StoryStyles">Don’t call 911. Call your investment manager.</p>

<p class="S06StoryIndent00StoryStyles">The cheeky slogan is on a website that takes Bitcoin payment for multi-player, cash-prize tournaments for video games like Counter-Strike, League of Legends and Minecraft. Last August, Eilers Research published its <i>eSports Betting: It’s Real, and Bigger Than You Think</i> white paper that projected US$250 million in gambling on competitive video gaming in 2015, ballooning to US$23 billion by 2020. </p>

<p class="S06StoryIndent00StoryStyles">“E-sports and gambling are converging,” University of California, Los Angeles gambling studies Prof. Brett Abarbanel told the BC Lottery Corp.’s (BCLC) New Horizons in Responsible Gambling Conference on February 2. </p>

<p class="S06StoryIndent00StoryStyles">“These industries are meeting, and they’re absolutely exploding. We have multiple gambling variants, we have multiple gaming variants. We have combinations of crossovers: the gaming components are in the gambling games, the gambling components are in the gaming games.”</p>

<p class="S06StoryIndent00StoryStyles">Eilers concluded that “e-sports is an extremely hospitable environment [technologically, logistically, culturally and demographically] for real-money online wagering.”</p>

<p class="S06StoryIndent00StoryStyles">Betting by the gamer community and growth of betting on e-sports “is beginning to benefit from tailwinds generated by positive macro trends for both e-sports and the broader online gambling industry.”</p>

<p class="S06StoryIndent00StoryStyles">Indeed, the bandwagon is filling up, as video game players emerge from basements to become stars online, at stadium stages and on traditional broadcast networks. </p>

<p class="S06StoryIndent00StoryStyles">In January, Activision Blizzard (Nasdaq:ATVI) bought Major League Gaming for US$46 million. </p>

<p class="S06StoryIndent00StoryStyles">The company’s senior vice-president, Mike Sepso, told <i>Wired</i> that the mandate was to “build the ESPN of e-sports.” </p>

<p class="S06StoryIndent00StoryStyles">ESPN added e-sports news and scores to its website last year. Seattle e-sports startup Unikrn has attracted investment from the Dallas Mavericks’ billionaire owner Mark Cuban and actor Ashton Kutcher.</p>

<p class="S06StoryIndent00StoryStyles">Abarbanel said e-sports bears all the hallmarks of traditional major sports leagues, with ticket sales, event organization, commentary, merchandising and media sales. </p>

<p class="S06StoryIndent00StoryStyles">“The core of it is all about entertainment generated by spectating a competition.”</p>

<p class="S06StoryIndent00StoryStyles">It is biggest in the United States and China, but South Korea and Canada are also hotbeds. E-sports gambling is in a grey area, an unregulated product that is a game of skill, yet some of the players on which wagers are placed are only “tweens.” </p>

<p class="S06StoryIndent00StoryStyles">The top players in the sport are gaining profile. Vancouver 22-year-old Kurtis “Aui_2000” Ling helped the Evil Geniuses team win the $6.6 million International Dota 2 Championships last summer in Seattle.</p>

<p class="S06StoryIndent00StoryStyles">Evil Geniuses also are the odds-on favourite at the February 24 Dota 2 Shanghai Major, according to BCLC’s e-sports page on <i>PlayNow.com</i>. The Crown corporation reported $109.1 million in e-gaming revenue in 2014-15, but did not break down the segments.</p>

<p class="S06StoryIndent00StoryStyles">“These professional teams will live together in one house, and they won’t do anything except eat, sleep and practise,” Abarbanel said. </p>

<p class="S06StoryIndent00StoryStyles">“They are actively training much in the same way that a traditional athlete has to train his muscle memory.” </p>

<p class="S06StoryIndent00StoryStyles">Not surprisingly, they use special equipment to improve their posture and circulation, because the sport requires such intense concentration and sitting for long periods. In a data-driven atmosphere, players are rated by efficient actions per minute. It’s not just how fast they can click, she said, “but how fast you can click usefully.” </p>

<p class="S06StoryIndent00StoryStyles">Abarbanel added that the sport is also facing growing pains that range from allegations of performance-enhancing drug use to match fixing. There is no single global governing organization for e-sports, and the gambling component leaves it open to potential addiction. </p>

<p class="S06StoryIndent00StoryStyles">E-sports will be coming to a theatre near you. Cineplex (TSX:CGX) announced the US$10 million purchase of WorldGaming last September and said it planned to invest US$5 million to create a competitive gaming league. That came on the heels of last August’s live showing of the ESL One Cologne 2015 Counter-Strike: GO Tournament.</p>

<p class="S06StoryIndent00StoryStyles">E-sports is exploding among millennials who are entering campus tournaments. University of British Columbia and Simon Fraser University teams went head-to-head in January on a tournament that was webcast live by <i>Twitch.tv</i>, the Amazon-owned website that shows matches around the clock and allows participants to trash-talk each other in real time. &nbsp;<span class="00QuioscoBullet">•</span></p>
