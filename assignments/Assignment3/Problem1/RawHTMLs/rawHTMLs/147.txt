<h1 id="post-43698" class="post-43698 post type-post status-publish format-standard hentry category-moda tag-bleacher-report-front-page tag-ifttt" title="Jimmy Butler Injury Update: Bulls Star Ruled out of 2016 NBA All-Star Game">Jimmy Butler Injury Update: Bulls Star Ruled out of 2016 NBA All-Star Game</h1>
	
<div class="blog-block single-page">	
			 
		 <p class="ui-droppable"><img class="slot" src="/images/pixel.gif" alt="">The <a href="http://ift.tt/12b1j2Y">Chicago Bulls</a> <a href="http://ift.tt/1o0Cvr8">announced</a> Tuesday that <a href="http://ift.tt/1aqQpZd">Jimmy Butler</a> will be out three to four weeks while recovering from the <a href="http://ift.tt/1PHvshi">knee injury</a> he suffered this past weekend. &nbsp;</p>
<p>As a result, Butler will miss Sunday&#8217;s NBA All-Star Game. The league has yet to confirm who will replace the swingman in the Eastern Conference&#8217;s reserve unit.</p>
<p>Commissioner Adam Silver could opt for a representative from the conference&#8217;s best team, the <a href="http://ift.tt/ZINSpm">Cleveland Cavaliers</a>. Both <a href="http://ift.tt/17rHr92">Kyrie Irving</a> and Kevin Love have strong cases to make the All-Star reserves. <a href="http://ift.tt/1jeRUcQ">Charlotte Hornets</a> point guard Kemba Walker and <a href="http://ift.tt/13nmYo2">Boston Celtics</a> forward Jae Crowder are two additional deserving candidates. Silver could also look no further than the Bulls roster and opt for <a href="http://ift.tt/19jwWLK">Pau Gasol</a>.</p>
<p>No matter in which direction the commissioner goes, he&#8217;s bound to upset the fans of those players overlooked for the All-Star Game for what will be the third time.</p>
<p>Far more important than who replaces Butler is the fact the Bulls now have a timetable for how long they could be without their leading scorer. Whereas this was once Derrick Rose&#8217;s team, Butler is now the unquestioned leader.</p>
<p>He&#8217;s also one of the best 2-guards in the league, per ESPN Stats &amp; Info:</p>
<p><img class="slot" src="/images/pixel.gif" alt=""></p>
<p>Chicago is only a game and a half ahead of the <a href="http://ift.tt/1h2hCDn">Hornets</a>, who are ninth in the Eastern Conference, and only four games separate the Bulls from the <a href="http://ift.tt/Y0z6nh">Orlando Magic</a> and <a href="http://ift.tt/V98moR">Washington Wizards</a>, the 10th and 11th seeds.</p>
<p>The Bulls could easily slip out of the top eight in Butler&#8217;s absence. In their first two games since his injury, they lost to the Hornets and <a href="http://ift.tt/Xis8g1">Minnesota Timberwolves</a>.</p>
<p>Silver doesn&#8217;t have an enviable task ahead as he fills out the East roster for the All-Star Game, but Bulls head coach Fred Hoiberg is in a much tougher position as he looks ahead to the next month with his best player out of action.</p>
