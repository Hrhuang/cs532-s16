		<p>What you&#8217;re looking at is an architectural rendering of TCF Bank Stadium for the upcoming outdoor game between the Minnesota Wild and Chicago Blackhawks on Sunday, Feb. 21.</p>
<p>The current forecast for that day in Minneapolis is a high of 27 F and a low of 19 F, with only a 20 percent possibility of precipitation, i.e. snow.</p>
<p>Which is to say, that guy in the Toews jersey is gonna be cold.<span style="line-height:1.5;">At least roll up your sleeves, man. Don&#8217;t be a hero.</span></p>
<p>The game, scheduled for 3:30 p.m. ET, will be broadcast live on NBC as part of Hockey Day in America, while Hockey Night In Canada and TVA Sports will have the action for the folks up north.</p>

		<p>NHL commissioner Gary Bettman has <a href="https://www.nhl.com/news/wideman-suspension-upheld/c-278917928" target="_blank">upheld Dennis Wideman&#8217;s 20-game suspension</a> for abuse of officials.</p>
<p>Wideman hadappealed<a href="http://nhl.nbcsports.com/2016/02/03/breaking-wideman-suspended-20-games-for-hitting-linesman/" target="_blank">the suspension</a> that was originally assessed by the NHL&#8217;s senior executive VP of hockey operations, Colin Campbell.</p>
<p><a href="http://www.nhl.com/nhl/en/v3/ext/pdfs/NHL-Wideman-Order.PDF" target="_blank">Click here</a>to read Bettman&#8217;s decision. In it, the commissioner wrote that he was &#8220;left with the firm view that a significant suspension is appropriate&#8221; and that he was &#8220;troubled by Mr. Wideman&#8217;s total failure to accept any responsibility for his actions.&#8221;</p>
<p>Widemanis expected to appeal further to an independent arbitrator.</p>

		<p>Patrick Roy has shaken up his lines ahead of tonight&#8217;s visit from the Montreal Canadiens.</p>
<p>The hope is more goals. Roy&#8217;sAvalanche have scored just 14 times in their last sevengames.</p>
<p>From <a href="https://www.nhl.com/news/montreal-canadiens-colorado-avalanche-preview/c-278882112?tid=278837326" target="_blank">NHL.com</a>:</p>
<p style="padding-left:30px;"><em>&#8230;Roy is moving <a href="http://www.rotoworld.com/player/nhl/2980/matt-duchene" target="_blank">Matt Duchene</a>, who had been playing right wing, back to his natural center position to skate with <a href="http://www.rotoworld.com/player/nhl/4012/mikhail-grigorenko" target="_blank">Mikhail Grigorenko</a> and <a href="http://www.rotoworld.com/player/nhl/631/jarome-iginla" target="_blank">Jarome Iginla</a>. <a href="http://www.rotoworld.com/player/nhl/4102/nathan-mackinnon" target="_blank">Nathan MacKinnon</a> will center <a href="http://www.rotoworld.com/player/nhl/816/alex-tanguay" target="_blank">Alex Tanguay</a> and <a href="http://www.rotoworld.com/player/nhl/1800/jack-skille" target="_blank">Jack Skille</a>, and <a href="http://www.rotoworld.com/player/nhl/2600/john-mitchell" target="_blank">John Mitchell</a> will center <a href="http://www.rotoworld.com/player/nhl/2447/cody-mcleod" target="_blank">Cody McLeod</a> and <a href="http://www.rotoworld.com/player/nhl/4965/andreas-martinsen" target="_blank">Andreas Martinsen</a>. The line of <a href="http://www.rotoworld.com/player/nhl/3670/gabriel-landeskog" target="_blank">Gabriel Landeskog</a>, <a href="http://www.rotoworld.com/player/nhl/2033/carl-soderberg" target="_blank">Carl Soderberg</a> and <a href="http://www.rotoworld.com/player/nhl/1983/blake-comeau" target="_blank">Blake Comeau</a> remains intact.</em></p>
<p>Colorado&#8217;s coming off a 4-1 loss in Buffalo on Sunday &#8212; a game in which the Avs actually outshot the Sabres, 35-21, only to be thwarted 34 times by goalie <a href="http://www.rotoworld.com/player/nhl/3093/robin-lehner" target="_blank">Robin Lehner</a>.</p>
<p>&#8220;We felt like that was our best game of the road trip, and that just goes to show you where this game is sometimes. You have your best effort and you feel you&#8217;re dominating, but you still lose 4-1,&#8221; Landeskog <a href="http://avalanche.nhl.com/club/news.htm?id=869344" target="_blank">told the club&#8217;s website</a>. &#8220;We got to make sure that we take that offensive game with us that we played in Buffalo, where we felt like we created a lot of scoring chances. It&#8217;s just a matter of putting the puck in the back of the net.&#8221;</p>
<p>Of note, the Avs have some winnable games coming up. They get the Canadiens tonight, then travel to Edmonton to play the Oilers on Saturday. The day after that, they take on the Canucks in Vancouver.</p>
<p>For now, theyhold the second wild-card spot in the West. But Arizona and Minnesota are only four points back, and each have three games in hand.</p>

		<p>If Thatcher Demko doesn&#8217;t sign with the Vancouver Canucksby Aug. 15, 2017, hecan becomean unrestricted free agent and join whichever NHL team he chooses.</p>
<p>Canucks fans are naturally nervous about this, because the 20-year-old Boston College netminder has been outstanding this season. Demko, a junior, is 20-4-4 with a .935 save percentage for the <a href="http://www.ncaa.com/rankings/icehockey-men/d1" target="_blank">No. 2team in the nation</a>. Recently, the 36th overall pick in the 2014 draft earned his ninth shutoutin <a href="http://bceagles.com/news/2016/2/8/MIH_0208160244.aspx" target="_blank">the Beanpot final</a>, a 1-0 victory over Boston University.</p>
<p>So, what&#8217;s his plan?</p>
<p>&#8220;My plan right now is to win a championship here at Boston College,&#8221; Demko <a href="https://soundcloud.com/tsn-radio-vancouver/thatcher-demko-my-numbers-are-due-to-a-good-team" target="_blank">told TSN 1040 today</a>. &#8220;I&#8217;m not sure what this summer&#8217;s going to look like. I do have another year (of eligibility) here at Boston College, so I&#8217;m trying to stay in the present here, not look ahead, because it can be a distraction. We&#8217;ll see what this summer has in store for me.&#8221;</p>
<p>Would he consider signing this summer and starting his pro career?</p>
<p>&#8220;To be honest, I haven&#8217;t even talked to the Canucks about it,&#8221; he said. &#8220;Obviously, it&#8217;s a two-way street, so they&#8217;ve got to talk to me about it before it can even be an option. I think once the season ends, I&#8217;ll just touch base with them and see where they&#8217;re at and we&#8217;ll have a discussion about it.&#8221;</p>
<p>The Canucks would surely loveto get Demko under contract ASAP. He could play next season in the AHL, with an eye on joining the big club in 2017-18. (<a href="http://www.rotoworld.com/player/nhl/1327/ryan-miller" target="_blank">Ryan Miller</a>, remember, is only signed through 2016-17.)</p>
<p>Individually, Demko has little left to prove at the college level. So Canucks fans should probably be cheering for the Eagles when the Hockey East playoffs begin next month. For Demko, a national title would be a nice way to end his college career. Anything less and he might be tempted to stay.</p>
