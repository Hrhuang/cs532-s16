received a face-lift half a century after it opened.</p><p>The Roundhouse in Marshalltown has been a source of community pride that it appears on the city's logo on signs as visitors drive into town.</p><p>It made its debut in 1965 and was Iowa's largest high school gym.</p><p>There's a different look after a 15-month, $8.5 million project aimed at preserving tradition while improving and expanding the facility.</p><p>"I always tell our student-athletes that your community wants to make an investment in you," Marshalltown activities director Craig Huegel said. "Hopefully it lasts another 50 years."</p><p>The project restores and expands this crown jewel of Iowa high school athletics.</p><p>There's a new, gleaming court that will be dedicated to former boys' basketball coach and activities director George Funk on Friday.</p><p>The bleachers have been replaced  — while the wood from the old ones have been repurposed into coat racks, tables and even a birdhouse that are being sold.</p><p>New locker rooms, a weight room, a team meeting room and equipment storage facilities are part of the upgrade. A "hall of pride" that has trophies honors the past.</p><p>"It's an icon, it's something you don't see every day," said Mark Hauser, a 1989 graduate who played basketball and now serves as a coach at the school.</p><p>Physical education classes use the court for soccer and other activities. The weight room, which includes $100,000 of new equipment, also benefits students and athletes, too. There's air conditioning now to keep annual graduation ceremonies cool.</p><p>The gym has long been a place to play basketball that opponents love to visit.</p><p>"They come in here and they always look up," said Aaron Hauser, a basketball player and son of Mark. "They say, 'I like your Roundhouse.' "</p><p>The funding came from a bond issue and community donations.</p><p>Aaron said he noticed the differences in the new court when he first practiced on it.</p><p>"It's not as slick," Hauser said. "If you were making cuts (in the past), you'd fall down three times, at least."</p><p>An addition to the building is expected to be constructed on the north side of the Roundhouse. Phase 2 will include a wrestling room and scoreboards.That portion of the project is expected to start this year and will be paid for by $3.85 million in private fundraising.</p><p>The gym can seat 3,800, a bit smaller than the previous capacity of about 5,000.</p><p>"It's a legacy throughout the process," Aaron Hauser said.</p><h2 class="presto-h2">Wrestling contributor remembered </h2><p>Wayne Endres, who helped develop an iconic piece of equipment found in many of the state's high school wrestling rooms, has passed away. Endres had a key role in producing the ADAM Wrestling Takedown Machine. Former Iowa State wrestler Carl Adams and Endres worked together to build the machines, which are mounted on walls to help athletes improve their takedown technique.</p><h2 class="presto-h2">Bowling rolling</h2><p>The state coed bowling meet will be held Feb. 22-24. All competition will be held at Plaza Lanes in Des Moines. The Class 1A meet will be contested Feb. 22. Class 2A is Feb. 23 and 3A is Feb. 24. Last year's girls' team champs: Johnston in 3A, Keokuk in 2A and Camanche in 1A. Last year's boys' winners were Marshalltown, Fort Dodge and Camanche.</p><h2 class="presto-h2">State swimming splash</h2><p>District meets used to determine the state boys' swimming field were held Saturday. The state meet will be held Feb. 13 at the Marshalltown YMCA-YWCA. Races start at 12:30 p.m. <a href="/story/sports/high-school/2016/01/23/high-school-insider-iowa-city-west-swim-team-mark-mcglaughlin-will-scott-aidan-keen/79223414/">Iowa City West will try to win a third consecutive team championship.</a> Muscatine is the last school to win three in row. The Muskies were champions from 2011-13. West had the fastest times in all three relays entering district competition. Seniors Mark McGlaughlin and Aiden Keen both have won eight state titles.</p><h2 class="presto-h2">Mark your calendars</h2><p>Postseason competition is coming up for basketball and wrestling. Girls' basketball teams start regional play Thursday in Class 1A, Feb. 13 in 2A and 3A, and Feb. 17 in 5A and 4A. The state tournament starts Feb. 29 at Wells Fargo Arena. Boys' basketball teams will kick off Feb. 15 in 1A and 2A, Feb. 22 in 3A and 4A. The state tournament begins March 7. Wrestling sections began Saturday. Regional dual meets will be contested Tuesday in 2A and 1A and Wednesday in 3A. Districts are Feb. 13. The state dual meet is Feb. 17, followed by the traditional meet Feb. 18-20.</p><p><em>Connect with John: @JohnNaughton on Twitter. Got a story idea to share?
