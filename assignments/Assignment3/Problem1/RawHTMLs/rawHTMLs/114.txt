				<a class="author-link" href="http://hellogiggles.com/author/sammy-nickalls/">Sammy Nickalls</a><span class="entry-date"> / February 9, 2016 11:12 am</span>			</div>
			<div class="entry-share"><a class="share-popup share-change" href="http://www.facebook.com/sharer/sharer.php?u=http://hellogiggles.com/diverse-models-sports-illustrated/" alt="Facebook"><i class="icon-facebook"></i> </a><a class="share-popup share-change" href="http://twitter.com/intent/tweet?url=http://hellogiggles.com/diverse-models-sports-illustrated/&text=These amazing, diverse models will be in the Sports Illustrated Swimsuit Issue&via=hellogiggles" alt="Tweet"><i class="icon-twitter"></i> </a><a class="share-popup share-change" href="http://tumblr.com/widgets/share/tool?canonicalUrl=http://hellogiggles.com/diverse-models-sports-illustrated/&title=These amazing, diverse models will be in the Sports Illustrated Swimsuit Issue" alt="Tumblr"><i class="icon-tumblr"></i> </a><a class="share-change email" href="mailto:?subject=These amazing, diverse models will be in the Sports Illustrated Swimsuit Issue&body=Check out this story: %20%0D%0A%20%0D%0AThese amazing, diverse models will be in the Sports Illustrated Swimsuit Issue%20%0D%0Ahttp://hellogiggles.com/diverse-models-sports-illustrated/%20%0D%0A%20%0D%0A via hellogiggles.com" alt="E-Mail"><i class="icon-mail"></i> </a><a class="share-popup share-change" href="http://www.pinterest.com/pin/create/button/?url=http://hellogiggles.com/diverse-models-sports-illustrated/&media=http://s3-us-west-2.amazonaws.com/images.hellogiggles.com/uploads/2016/02/09020723/Nicola-Solo-HI-RES-e1455041272738-500x375.jpg&description=These amazing, diverse models will be in the Sports Illustrated Swimsuit Issue" alt="Pin it"><i class="icon-pinterest"></i> </a></div>			</header>
	
	
	<section class="entry-content">
		
				
				

		
		
		<p><a href="http://hellogiggles.com/sports-illustrated-plus-size-model/">This time last year</a>, we were thrilled about the #CurvesInBikinis movement, which led to the very first plus-sized model — Ashley Graham — to be featured in a <em>Sports Illustrated Swimsuit Issue</em>. Started by swimsuitsforall, #CurvesInBikinis encouraged women of all body types to feel beautiful just the way they are in any swimsuit.</p>
<p>Now, SwimsuitsForAll is at it again with their new groundbreaking campaign, #SwimSexy. The campaign, which features three &#8220;curvilicious women,&#8221; will again appear in the <em>SI Swimsuit Issue</em>, but this time, the campaign is taking it a step further. Featuring Graham, <span class="s1">British Ghanaian model Philomena Kwao, and 56-year-old model Nicola Griffin (the oldest woman to appear on the pages of the <em>Swimsuit Issue</em>), #SwimSexy embraces body diversity for women of any shape, size, age, or ethnicity. </span><em>HelloGiggles</em> had the chance to chat with Graham, Kwao, and Griffin about their participation in the campaign and what body positivity means to them.</p>
<p>Graham, who was discovered as a model at age 12, is no stranger to trailblazing in the modeling industry. She did, after all, coin the term &#8220;body activist.&#8221; &#8220;Last year’s campaign broke barriers within the fashion industry and successfully empowered women to feel sexy at any size,&#8221; Graham, pictured below, told us. &#8220;I was thrilled to be asked to be in the #SwimSexy campaign this year to keep the momentum going. It is my personal mission to help women embrace their curves and boost confidence, and SwimsuitsForAll does this with elegance.&#8221;</p>
<p><img class="aligncenter wp-image-447959 size-full" src="https://s3-us-west-2.amazonaws.com/images.hellogiggles.com/uploads/2016/02/09015619/SI_Ashley_Ad-e1455040629595.jpg" alt="SI_Ashley_Ad" width="600" height="800" /><figcaption><cite class="img-credits">SwimsuitsForAll</cite></figcaption></p>
<p>The shoot itself was an &#8220;incredible experience,&#8221; Kwao, pictured below, explained. &#8220;We shot on an island in Puerto Rico, with beautiful white sands and turquoise blue waters surrounding us,&#8221; she said. &#8220;Being in such a stunning environment calmed any nerves I had during the shoot. The team was also incredible. Everyone on set was so friendly and relaxed that it made the entire shoot feel like a holiday.&#8221;</p>
<p><img class="aligncenter wp-image-447958 size-full" src="https://s3-us-west-2.amazonaws.com/images.hellogiggles.com/uploads/2016/02/09015610/SI_Philomena_Ad-e1455040671221.jpg" alt="SI_Philomena_Ad" width="600" height="800" /><figcaption><cite class="img-credits">SwimsuitsForAll</cite></figcaption></p>
<p>Kwao, whose family is from Ghana though she was born and raised in London, described the campaign as &#8220;revolutionary&#8221;:</p>
<blockquote><p>&#8220;For so long, there’s been a false idea that the only bodies that are beach-worthy are the ones that appear in magazines. The <em>Sports Illustrated Swimsuit Issue</em> is a cultural phenomenon and sets the standard for swimsuit modeling each year. By publishing this campaign in its biggest issue of the year, <em>Sports Illustrated</em> is demolishing those traditional standards of swimsuit modeling and shows that everybody is beach-worthy. How could I not be part of such a phenomenal movement?&#8221;</p></blockquote>
<p>Griffin, who just started modeling three years ago when she was 53, is also &#8220;thrilled&#8221; to be featured in the campaign. &#8220;Wearing a bikini, too!&#8221; Griffin, pictured below, told HG. &#8220;And why ever not — I&#8217;m sexy, I&#8217;m healthy, and I feel it&#8217;s about time we celebrated women all sizes and shapes and ages. . . I want to help motivate women to have the confidence to wear a bikini if they wish, and to show off their bodies and be accepted in our society.&#8221;</p>
<p><img class="aligncenter wp-image-447961 size-full" src="https://s3-us-west-2.amazonaws.com/images.hellogiggles.com/uploads/2016/02/09015628/Nicola-Solo-HI-RES-e1455040736687.jpg" alt="Nicola Solo HI RES" width="600" height="800" /><figcaption><cite class="img-credits">SwimsuitsForAll</cite></figcaption></p>
<p><span style="line-height: 1.5;">&#8220;We are beautiful, no matter what size and whatever age we are,&#8221; Griffin, a mother of twin daughters, told </span><em style="line-height: 1.5;">HG</em><span style="line-height: 1.5;">. &#8220;. . . </span>It&#8217;s been the most exciting thing to happen to me and I&#8217;m honored to be part of it. It was great fun to go on the shoot in a beautiful part of the world with some amazing people. I made some lovely friends.&#8221;</p>
<p>But even though they were busy having fun, the models knew that what they were doing was groundbreaking. &#8220;The whole time we knew we were on the cusp of something big,&#8221; Graham said.</p>
<p>Plus-size has come to the forefront of the fashion conversation as of late, and major retailers have been stepping up. Take Forever21, for example, which <a href="http://hellogiggles.com/forever21-plus-size-activewear/">recently came out with their new plus-size fashion line</a> (starring Graham!). &#8220;I think that 2015 was a pivotal year for fashion brands and retailers accepting that women come in different shapes and sizes,&#8221; Graham explained. &#8220;There have been many retailers who have transformed their &#8216;plus size&#8217; offerings into trendy, fashion forward collections. We always encourage flaunting our curves, not hiding them.&#8221;</p>
