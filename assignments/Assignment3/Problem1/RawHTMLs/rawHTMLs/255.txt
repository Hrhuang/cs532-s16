</P><P><strong>PROVIDENCE, R.I. (AAC/SMU) &ndash; </strong>The SMU swimming and diving program swept the final American Athletic Conference weekly awards, accounting for almost half of the season&rsquo;s honors, league officials announced Tuesday. SMU is the only program to win all four honors in the same week, doing so the opening week of awards as well.<br /><br /><strong>Male Swimmer of the Week: </strong><A HREF="http://www.smumustangs.com/sports/m-swim/mtt/bartosz_krzyzaniak_926299.html">Bartosz Krzyzaniak</A> <br /><strong>Male Diver of the Week: </strong><A HREF="http://www.smumustangs.com/sports/m-swim/mtt/daniel_pitts_828628.html">Daniel Pitts</A><br /><br /><strong>Female Swimmer of the Week:</strong> <A HREF="http://www.smumustangs.com/sports/w-swim/mtt/hannah_rogers_977146.html">Hannah Rogers</A> <br /><strong>Female Diver of the Week: </strong><A HREF="http://www.smumustangs.com/sports/w-swim/mtt/lauren_lamb_860826.html">Lauren Lamb</A><br /><br />Krzyzaniak posted a career-best and NCAA B cut in the 200-yard freestyle to post the top time at the Mustangs&rsquo; Pre-Conference Time Trial event over the weekend. The sophomore turned in a 1:37.76, giving him the second fastest time in the conference so far. Krzyzaniak also anchored SMU&rsquo;s 200-yard medley relay team, posting a 20.29 split to help SMU to the top spot. This is his second award (Nov. 24) of the season.<br /><br />He currently holds the conference&rsquo;s top times in the 100- and 200-yard backstroke, while sitting top three in both the 100- and 200-yard freestyles. In relay action, SMU has turned in the fastest time in four of five relay events, all of which Krzyzaniak has led off.<br /><br />Pitts, who won the first award of the season, picked up his second nod after his performance at the pre-conference event. He registered an NCAA Zone score of 310.35 in the 1-meter, while also turning in the highest total in 3-meter.<br /><br />The senior will travel to College Station, Texas, in March to compete at the Zone D Championship.<br /><br />Rogers&rsquo; first award as a Mustang comes after a total of five event leading performances between the dual meet with North Texas and the pre-conference event. In both events, Rogers won the 50-yard freestyle and was part of SMU&rsquo;s 200-yard medley relay team that finished the fastest. She also led off the winning 200-yard freestyle relay at the meet against UNT.<br /><br />Rogers currently has an NCAA B standard in the 50 free with a season-best time of 22.79.<br /><br />Lamb posted event leading scores in both the 1- and 3-meter at the pre-conference event, while also earning runner-up honors in the Mustangs&rsquo; dual meet with UNT to help secure the victory.<br /><br />The full program returns to action Feb. 17-20 for The American Swimming and Diving Championship hosted by Houston at the CRWC Natatorium. The women won the title a season ago, while the men finished as the event runner-up. For more information on the Championship, visit the AAC&rsquo;s Championship Central <a href="http://theamerican.org/sports/2016/1/6/SD2016.aspx?path=swim">page</a>.<br /><br /><strong>Mustangs on Social<br /> Facebook: </strong>SMUSwimDiveW | SMUSwimDiveM<strong><br /> Twitter: </strong>@SMUSwimmingW | @SMUSwimmingM</P>
