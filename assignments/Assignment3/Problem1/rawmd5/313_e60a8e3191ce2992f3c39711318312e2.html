Buster men look to finish strong, host region game

  • By J. Levi Burnfin lburnfin@gctelegram.com

    Levi Burnfin

  • Feb 8, 2016
  • Comments

Three games is all that separates the Garden City mens basketball team from fourth place in the KJCCC. But a 13th-place finish is just one-and-a-half games away for the currently ninth-place Busters with five games to play in the regular season.

But for now, finishing in the top eight is the biggest goal for the Busters because that guarantees a home game for the first round of the Region VI tournament, an advantage the Busters have not been able to enjoy in each of head coach Brady Trenkles first two seasons.

The result each of those seasons? A loss and a quick elimination from regionals.

The Busters (14-11, 6-8) are tied for eighth in the conference, but drop to ninth on a head-to-head tiebreaker with Butler, which edged the Busters, 81-75, way back on Dec. 2, the first game of the conference season.

You always want to finish as high as you, Trenkle said. With five games to go now, theres a lot of space to move. We were just in seventh a week ago.

But after losses to Hutchinson and Barton the latter on a tip-in at the buzzer the Busters are now facing the the unenviable task of going on the road for the region first round once again.

Two years ago, the Busters were on the wrong end of a four-team tiebreaker for third place in the Jayhawk West, forcing them to travel to Neosho County, where they lost 74-59.

Last year, they finish seventh in the unbalanced Jayhawk West, and traveled to Hutchinson, where the Busters nearly pulled off the upset, falling 70-68. Hutchinson went on to win the region.

Garden City is not the only team that has struggled on the road in region play, with home teams going 17-4 in the last three seasons. Though, last year was an outlier as home teams went just 3-3.

But Trenkle and the Busters cant worry about that now theyve got to focus on the five games in front of them, with three of them at home. Protect home court and steal one or maybe two on the road, and the Busters likely will have one more home date at the Perryman Athletic Complex.

Weve just got to worry about Dodge City, Trenkle said of the Busters next opponent, the fourth-place Conqs who come to Garden City for a 7:30 p.m. tip-off Wednesday. Weve just got to take them one at a time, and at the end of it all, you want to win five in a row and finish 11-8.

The Busters also are taking one surgery at a time during a season with perhaps more than their fair share of injuries. The most recent were a couple of surgeries for guard Jamar Gilbert and Dionte Jones.

Gilbert is out indefinitely for now, and Jones is a game-time decision for Wednesday.

Its just been one of those years, Trenkle said.

Gilbert was playing 20.4 minutes per game, scoring 6.8 points and was the Busters leader in assists per game at three. Jones was averaging 8.1 points and 6.1 rebounds per game, leading the Busters in the latter category.

After the Conqs (9-5) who the Busters beat, 74-71, at the Dodge City Civic Center on Jan. 20 the Busters travel to Colby Saturday. The seventh-place Trojans (7-7) and Busters tangled in a high-intensity game on Jan. 24, with the Busters getting the best of the scoreboard, 72-65.

Then, the Busters return home to host sixth-place Seward County on Feb. 17. The Saints (7-6) won the previous meeting with Garden City, 92-87, on Jan. 27 at the Greenhouse. The Busters final road game of the regular season is a long trip to Concordia and last-place Cloud County on Feb. 20. The T-Birds (2-14) snapped a nine-game losing streak with a 76-74 win over Seward County Saturday.

In their previos meeting with the Busters, Garden City won a close one, 73-70, on Jan. 30. To close the regular season, the Busters host first-place Hutchinson. The Dragons (12-3) smacked the Busters on Feb. 1 in an 83-64 win.

Levi Burnfin
