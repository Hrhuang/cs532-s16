Pharrell Williams Owns A Chunk of G-Star RAW Now

Matt Sebra
February 9, 2016
Facebook
Twitter
Email

Get ready for "the definitive jeans brand of the 21st century."

It was announced today, via a press release from G-Star RAW and on Pharrell Williams's Instagram account, that the music mega-producer, The Voice judge, Billionaire Boys Club and Ice Cream owner, children's book author, Adidas sneaker designer, movie producer, and philanthropist (are we leaving anything out?) entered into a "strategic partnership" with the Dutch denim brand.

Loading

View on Instagram

Financial rewards (and risks) aside, Williams will be "immersed in the company on many creative levels, from collections, to advertising and business strategy." And while that general outline doesn't dictate just how involved he'll be in the design process of each collection, it's safe to assume that this new arrangement will build on Williams's previous endeavors with G-Star, most notably his Bionic Yarn recycled denim program. But for the hyperbole-happy Pharrell, the partnership represents more than just a savvy business move or the chance to weigh in on denim's newest trends—it offers him the chance to build, what he believes, "will be the definitive jeans brand of the 21st century." After all, Williams does wear a lot jeans (like, more than most boldface names we can think of), so maybe that promise is more than just a positive pull quote.
