Maria Eagle on why football fans must not be priced out of the game they love

    • 17:49, 9 Feb 2016
    • Opinion
    • by MariaEagle

The gulf between fans and the owners of many clubs is growing, particularly in clubs owned by individuals without a connection to the local community

  •   Comments

As the banners held up at Anfield last weekend said: “Football without fans is nothing”.

Full credit must go to the Liverpool supporters who walked out of the game against for Sunderland for putting this issue firmly on the agenda.

As a lifelong Liverpool fan, I am angry and disappointed by the proposals to raise the prices to £77 a seat.

How can season tickets breaking the £1,000 barrier be justified?

Clubs cannot be allowed cynically to abuse fans’ loyalty. I can understand why they are furious.

    Fans must not be priced out of the game they love by Premier League clubs just as they reap the rewards of a bumper TV deal.

    The gulf between fans and the owners of many clubs is growing, particularly in clubs owned by individuals without a connection to the local community.

    Working people have already been hit hard in the pocket by almost six years of Tory Government.

    Read more: Liverpool fans have been pushed too far

    The real wages of ordinary people have fallen since David Cameron became Prime Minister, yet the wealth of Premier League clubs and players’ salaries have gone through the roof in recent years.

    The Premier League has more than trebled its domestic revenue from TV rights since 2008.

    So why haven’t any savings been passed on to the fans, and why are clubs now squeezing more and more from their own fans?

    Why is so little of this money making its way down to the grass roots?

    Click to play

    Talk of a £30 price cap for away tickets is a good start, but I want to see clubs really get behind the Football Supporters’ Federation’s ‘Twenty is Plenty’ campaign.

    It cannot be right that the most loyal fans sometimes have to fork out well over £50 just for an away ticket, and that is before you add on the cost of travelling.

    Labour has long campaigned to give fans and supporters’ trusts a place on club boards.

    But the Government only last month turned their backs on giving fans a seat in every boardroom.

    Click to play

    We need to see more leadership from the Government, more leadership from the Premier League and more leadership from the clubs themselves.

    The Premier League is the best league on earth. But it is about time clubs started treating fans with the respect they deserve.
