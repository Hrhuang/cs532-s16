<div class="twitter-share"><a href="https://twitter.com/intent/tweet?url=http%3A%2F%2Fthebruns.ca%2F2016%2F02%2F03%2Fbrunswickan-sports-panel-performance-enhancers-to-rebound-from-injury%2F&#038;via=Brunswickan" class="twitter-share-button">Tweet</a></div>
<p>&#8220;Based on recent allegations against Denver Bronco&#8217;s Quarterback Peyton Manning, do you believe it is unethical for athletes to use performance enhancers to help return from injury?&#8221;</p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p><img class="alignright size-medium wp-image-13761" src="http://thebruns.ca/wp-content/uploads/2015/11/Rob-Trites-Headshot1-300x300.jpg" alt="Rob Trites Headshot" width="300" height="300" srcset="http://thebruns.ca/wp-content/uploads/2015/11/Rob-Trites-Headshot1-150x150.jpg 150w, http://thebruns.ca/wp-content/uploads/2015/11/Rob-Trites-Headshot1-300x300.jpg 300w, http://thebruns.ca/wp-content/uploads/2015/11/Rob-Trites-Headshot1-600x600.jpg 600w, http://thebruns.ca/wp-content/uploads/2015/11/Rob-Trites-Headshot1.jpg 1000w" sizes="(max-width: 300px) 100vw, 300px" />Rob Trites (Sports Editor): Last year Steven Stamkos of the Tampa Bay Lightning, recovered from a broken tibia/fibula in just under two months. In my opinion he clearly used enhancers to recover, as it would normally take much longer to fully recover from that type of injury. Stars like Stamkos, being out of the game for long periods of time only hurts the game. If athletes can use enhancers to safely return to the game more quickly but stop when they are back, I have no problem with it.</p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p><img class="size-medium wp-image-13760 alignleft" src="http://thebruns.ca/wp-content/uploads/2015/11/Tyler-Belyea-271x300.jpg" alt="Tyler Belyea" width="271" height="300" srcset="http://thebruns.ca/wp-content/uploads/2015/11/Tyler-Belyea-271x300.jpg 271w, http://thebruns.ca/wp-content/uploads/2015/11/Tyler-Belyea-600x665.jpg 600w, http://thebruns.ca/wp-content/uploads/2015/11/Tyler-Belyea.jpg 1000w" sizes="(max-width: 271px) 100vw, 271px" />Tyler Belyea (Former Mediocre Athlete): First off, I want to put it out there that I am a Peyton Manning fan. Second, Iâll go out on a limb and say that Peyton will come up clean. Third, I might be of the unpopular opinion and do not believe it is unethical. We as fans want the best player on the field to watch, and theyâre bound to need some help coming back from gruesome injuries. More importantly, go Broncos!</p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p><img class="alignright size-medium wp-image-13537" src="http://thebruns.ca/wp-content/uploads/2015/11/John-Robb-300x266.jpg" alt="John Robb" width="300" height="266" srcset="http://thebruns.ca/wp-content/uploads/2015/11/John-Robb-300x266.jpg 300w, http://thebruns.ca/wp-content/uploads/2015/11/John-Robb-600x532.jpg 600w, http://thebruns.ca/wp-content/uploads/2015/11/John-Robb.jpg 1000w" sizes="(max-width: 300px) 100vw, 300px" />John Robb (V-Reds Fan): I think everything in the NFL is unethical. On this specific issue, HGH wasn&#8217;t tested for when Peyton allegedly used it, so to test him now seems farcical at best. There were tests in 2013 that could have found HGH, but the NFL didn&#8217;t use them. In a league where your career can be over in one play and a team can cut you at any time, I won&#8217;t hold it against players attempting any treatment to get back to playing shape.</p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p><img class="size-medium wp-image-13538 alignleft" src="http://thebruns.ca/wp-content/uploads/2015/11/Nathan-Delong-Headshot-266x300.jpg" alt="Nathan Delong Headshot" width="266" height="300" srcset="http://thebruns.ca/wp-content/uploads/2015/11/Nathan-Delong-Headshot-266x300.jpg 266w, http://thebruns.ca/wp-content/uploads/2015/11/Nathan-Delong-Headshot-908x1024.jpg 908w, http://thebruns.ca/wp-content/uploads/2015/11/Nathan-Delong-Headshot-600x677.jpg 600w" sizes="(max-width: 266px) 100vw, 266px" />Nathan DeLong (Staff Reporter): It isnât right to use performance-enhancing drugs to recover faster from injuries, which Peyton Manning denies while Shawn Horcoff of the NHLâs Anaheim Ducks admitted to doing it upon receiving a 20-game suspension. Itâs also unethical to use substances to gain an edge (pun intended) over opponents instead of playing fair. But I can understand the temptation when on the mend, considering the physical toll of sports and potential salary implications for lost playing time.</p>
