<div class="text combinedtext parbase section">
<p itemprop="articleBody">To the eyes of several trained arborists, the<a href="http://www.thestar.com/sports/leafs/2016/02/03/brendan-shanahan-pleased-at-reaction-to-new-maple-leafs-logo.html"> logo unveiled earlier </a>this week by Maple Leaf Sports & Entertainment is not, strictly speaking, as much Maple Leaf as much as it is Toronto maple-ish.</p>
</div>
<div class="text combinedtext parbase section">
<p itemprop="articleBody">The logo revealed Tuesday night is laden with symbolism &#x2014; including 13 veins at the top of the leaf to mark the team&#x2019;s 13 Stanley Cup titles &#x2014; but it does not appear to follow closely to any obvious tree biology. </p>
</div>
<div class="text combinedtext parbase section">
<p itemprop="articleBody">&#x201c;It doesn&#x2019;t look like any specific leaf, that&#x2019;s for sure,&#x201d; said Joe Outram, co-ordinator of the arboriculture and urban forestry programs at Sir Sandford Fleming College. &#x201c;But it does resemble some sort of cross-breeds between several maple leafs.&#x201d;</p>
</div>
<div class="text combinedtext parbase section">
<p itemprop="articleBody">He suggested the new logo, which will be stitched onto the jerseys next season, appears to be a blend of the sugar maple and silver maple though there are traces of red maple, as well. </p>
</div>
<div class="text combinedtext parbase section">
<p itemprop="articleBody">&#x201c;You could make an argument that it looks more like a sycamore maple leaf,&#x201d; said Mark Isselhardt, who specializes in the study of maple trees at the University of Vermont. </p>
</div>
<div class="text combinedtext parbase section">
<p itemprop="articleBody">The new leaf on Toronto&#x2019;s logo does not look exactly like the leaf from a sugar maple, he suggested, because those leaves do not have serrations &#x2014; outcroppings that look like teeth on a handsaw &#x2014; on their lobes (the three main branches rising from the base.)</p>
</div>
<div class="text combinedtext parbase section">
<p itemprop="articleBody">&#x201c;The geometry of it doesn&#x2019;t really reflect what a leaf would look like, regardless of the species,&#x201d; Isselhardt said. &#x201c;It just looks a little too long and drawn out, oddly pointed.&#x201d;</p>
</div>
<div class="text combinedtext parbase section">
<p itemprop="articleBody">&#x201c;I will say we did not consult an arborist,&#x201d; said Shannon Hosford, vice-president, marketing and communications with MLSE. &#x201c;We wanted to try and capture the winningest leafs that this club wore between the 1940s and &#x2019;60s, going back to our roots.&#x201d;</p>
</div>
<div class="text combinedtext parbase section">
<p itemprop="articleBody">Carla Antonation, an arborist who co-owns Trilogy Tree Services Ltd. in Winnipeg, said that while it is not an exact replica of a maple leaf, the new logo is &#x201c;similar enough that it definitely says &#x2018;Canada.&#x2019; &#x201d;</p>
</div>
</div>
