<article id="post-4014" class="regular post-4014 post type-post status-publish format-standard hentry category-booz-allen category-data-science">
	
	<div class="post-content">
		
				
		<div class="content-inner">
			
						
						
		   
			<p>Booz Allen does not just have a data science team. Yes, we are proud of our industry leading, 600+ member group of data scientists; but that team is not evidence of our firm simply checking a box in the technology market. Our data science capabilities, in contrast, are indicative of our diagnostic fascination with finding new, better ways of answering our worlds oldest questions.<span id="more-4014"></span></p>
<p>To celebrate data science as a discipline against the backdrop of our Data Science Bowl, we have pulled together a selection of a few of our favorite problems solved by analytics. Heres to growing this list in 2016 and beyond:</p>
<p><strong><em>Predicting pass or run in the NFL</em></strong></p>
<p>Last spring, we unveiled our <a href="http://www.forbes.com/sites/davidlariviere/2015/02/12/play-prediction-analytics-could-be-coming-soon-to-the-nfl/">BlitzD application for the Microsoft Surface Pro 3</a>, capable of predicting the next play of an NFL game in real-time. The platform awed audiences at the MIT Sloan Sports Analytics Conference, and we hope to one day bring this technology to a sideline near you.</p>
<p><a href="http://www.datasciencebowl.com/wp-content/uploads/2016/01/batters-box.png"><img class="alignnone size-full wp-image-4024" src="http://www.datasciencebowl.com/wp-content/uploads/2016/01/batters-box.png" alt="batters box" width="853" height="569" /></a></p>
<p><strong><em>From the boardroom to the batters box</em></strong></p>
<p>Also from our Sports Analytics team, <a href="http://www.fastcolabs.com/3033324/how-sports-data-analytics-is-upsetting-the-game-all-over-again">this model is capable of predicting the next pitch</a> in an MLB game with an average of 74.5 percent accuracy. The initial framework encompassed 400 pitchers and officially launched our work in the sports world.</p>
<p><strong><em>Wait, where should I park?</em></strong></p>
<p>We analyzed open, public data from the San Francisco Police Departments CABLE system and were able to pinpoint when and where it is most likely for your car to be stolen in the city; an incredible use case of data sciences direct ability to fight crime.</p>
<p><a href="http://www.datasciencebowl.com/wp-content/uploads/2016/01/digital-disease.png"><img class="alignnone size-full wp-image-4023" src="http://www.datasciencebowl.com/wp-content/uploads/2016/01/digital-disease.png" alt="digital disease" width="837" height="467" /></a></p>
<p><strong><em>Digital Disease Detection</em></strong></p>
<p>Our Epidemico teams <a href="http://www.healthmap.org/en/">HealthMap</a> platform uses public data to provide real-time, population health tracking around the world. This technology has become a trusted asset for global healthcare experts, international travelers and everyone looking to stay healthier, all year long.</p>
<p><strong><em>Book your flights yesterday</em></strong></p>
<p>Our analytics team works deeply within the airline industry and, last year, <a href="http://www.boozallen.com/media-center/press-releases/2015/08/flying-high--booz-allen-hamilton-highlights-what-2015s-record-br">discovered that booking behavior has shifted by one week</a>, resulting in earlier consumer competition for seats.</p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
