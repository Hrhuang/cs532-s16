<div style="clear:both; height:20px;"></div><p class="p1"><span class="s1">After putting out a two-hour notice for help, A-Game's Alliance Volleyball had nearly 200 parents and supporters laying down red and blue squares atop the covered dirt floor in the Williamson County Ag Expo Center on Monday. </span></p>
<p class="p1"><span class="s1">The effort came in partnership with Williamson County Mayor Rogers Anderson, who allowed the estranged A-Game tenant to use the facility while they remain in limbo over the use of their previous home on 215 Gothic Court in Franklin. </span></p>
<p class="p1"><span class="s1">"They are bringing the floors up today because of the situation that's occurred in the last week," Anderson said. "They asked if they could use the facility in conjunction of the tournament this weekend. We agreed to work with them so they could have some practice time and whatever else they need."</span></p>
<p class="p1"><span class="s1">Parents strung up black nets on posts, while others sat on the ground yanking white tape from their rolls to outline the court on the floor. Seven of the eight courts were almost finished Monday night after only three hours of work. Each of the courts are just more than 110 feet in length and under 50 feet in width. </span></p>
<p class="p1"><span class="s1">The courts shipped in from Austin, Texas, five days in advance of the President's Day Volleyball Tournament. </span></p>
<p class="p1"><span class="s1">"It usually takes an hour a court," Alliance Volleyball Director Jeff Wismer said. "These families got it done in less than three hours. Our community rallied. Mayor Anderson gets the credit for this. I am stupefied by it. It's amazing."</span></p>
<p class="p1"><span class="s1">The Expo Center will handle seven of the 18 courts needed for the weekend's upcoming tournament. The Franklin Field House will help on two of the three days of the event. The athletic director of Williamson County Parks and Rec also offered some solutions, along with potential for Brentwood High School to pitch in some help with the space. </span></p>
<p class="p1"><span class="s1">With a pair of work gloves in his hand, parent Randy Sanders said he thought of everyone's effort as a reflection of what their girls could learn when life takes an unexpected turn. </span></p>
<p class="p1"><span class="s1">"It is frustrating, certainly," he said. "But with every challenge comes an opportunity. So, if life is an easy path or straight line, it would be kind of boring. Anytime you get thrown a curve like this, you want your kids to develop the ability and reason to say, 'Hey, I am not going to let this defeat me. I am going to find a solution and continue doing whatever my goal is.'"</span></p>
<p class="p1"><span class="s1">On the flip side, the upcoming hockey tournament this weekend will face cancellation, according to a newsletter email sent out by MyHockey Tournaments:</span></p>
<p class="p1" style="padding-left: 30px;"><span class="s1">"A-Game Sportsplex has been unable to resolve their legal issues and cannot reach an agreement which will allow the rink to open.</span></p>
<p class="p1" style="padding-left: 30px;"><span class="s1">"Our intention has been to re-schedule the tournament based on available ice-time at Ford Ice Center. We began sending revised schedules to the teams this afternoon but the majority of the teams that received the revised schedules have withdrawn from the tournament. We no longer have enough teams in any division to hold the tournament.</span></p>
<p class="p1" style="padding-left: 30px;"><span class="s1">"Unfortunately, the entire tournament is cancelled. We are very sorry for this situation but it is very much out of our control."</span></p>
<p class="p1"><span class="s1">How the legal and A-Game ownership proposal battle will turn out remains undetermined, while its <strong><a href="http://franklinhomepage.com/a-game-tenants-mulling-over-latest-proposal-from-ownership-cms-23881#.VrlhF5MrJsM">leasing tenants review the potential agreement</a> </strong>provided to them on Saturday. </span></p>
<p><strong><a href="http://franklinhomepage.com/sports-groups-go-back-to-court-over-sudden-a-game-closure-cms-23846#.VrZm-JMrLR0">On Friday afternoon,</a></strong> legal representatives for volleyball and hockey/figure skating filed an expedited motion for a judge to reverse the previous decision and to review their leasing agreements.</p>
<p>The group had a judge lift a temporary restraining order against itself <strong><a href="http://franklinhomepage.com/a-game-volleyball-hockey-looking-at-legal-options-against-owners-cms-23813#.VrZn3JMrLR2">last Tuesday.</a> <a href="http://franklinhomepage.com/a-game-users-said-they-were-surprised-by-abrupt-closure-cms-23817#.VrZnu5MrLR1">Two days later,</a></strong> Alliance Volleyball and MDG Managment, which handles hockey, had to retrieve their personal belongings from A-Game after finding it abruptly locked on Thursday morning.</p>
<p><strong><a href="http://franklinhomepage.com/kids-parents-chant-and-march-in-support-of-maintaining-a-game-cms-23827#.VrUQ-JMrLVo">A-Game users protested for its opening Thursday</a> </strong>night in a march from the CoolSprings Galleria mall to the complex at 215 Gothic Court, waving signs at cars in exchange for honks of support.</p>
<p>Alliance and MDG have legally enforceable leases which expire in September 2017 and December 2019. They asked a judge to expedite that motion and hear it no later than tomorrow, Feb. 9.</p>
