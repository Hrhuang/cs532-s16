            <div class="story-text">
                 <strong>ORLANDO, Fla.</strong> - North Florida women's tennis begins its five-game road swing at UCF on Wednesday at 2 p.m. The Ospreys are currently 2-1 on the season, while the Knights are 3-1.<br><br>UNF is coming off a 7-0 sweep of Flagler on Friday, Feb. 5. Three players currently own two victories in singles play with freshman <dfn><a href="/roster.aspx?rp_id=8435" rel="smarttag" rev="8435">Rafaela Gomez</a></dfn> sitting at 2-0 in the No. 5 spot. Doubles partners <dfn><a href="/roster.aspx?rp_id=8169" rel="smarttag" rev="8169">Noemi Hemmerich</a></dfn> and <dfn><a href="/roster.aspx?rp_id=8166" rel="smarttag" rev="8166">Quetzali Vazquez</a></dfn> are each 2-1 in the No. 1 and No. 3 singles positions, respectively. In doubles play, Gomez and <dfn><a href="/roster.aspx?rp_id=8164" rel="smarttag" rev="8164">Luise Intert</a></dfn> are 3-0 in No. 2 matches, while <dfn><a href="/roster.aspx?rp_id=8167" rel="smarttag" rev="8167">Hannah Fluhrer</a></dfn> and <dfn><a href="/roster.aspx?rp_id=8165" rel="smarttag" rev="8165">Michelle Valdez</a></dfn> own the same mark in No. 3.<br><br>The Knights' Sofia Baptista and Emilia Granstrom are each 4-0 in No. 2 and No. 3 singles, respectively, while two others have three victories. UCF opened with a Quad Tournament with Rice, North Texas and FIU before hosting Troy in its first dual match. The Knights lone loss is to Atlantic Sun Conference foe FGCU.<br><br>North Florida owns a 5-4 record in the last nine meetings against UCF, but stand at an even 2-2. In the last two seasons, the Ospreys have edged the Knights, 4-3. UCF last won in 2013 by the same score.<br><br>After UCF, the Ospreys continue on with a trip to Alabama at Auburn and Troy on February 20 and 21, respectively.<div class='print-friendly-story-button'><a onclick='window.open(this.href,"_blank");return false;' href='/news/2016/2/9/WTEN_0209162825.aspx?print=true'>Print Friendly Version</a></div>
