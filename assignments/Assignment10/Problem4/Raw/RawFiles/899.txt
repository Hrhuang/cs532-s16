						<div class="articleBody" itemprop="articleBody"><p>Co-main-event winner Roy Nelson ($125,000) and main-card victor Joseph Benavidez ($118,000) were the top earners at this past weekend&#8217;s UFC Fight Night 82 event.</p>
<p>MMAjunkie today requested and obtained the list of official disclosed payouts from the Nevada State Athletic Commission.</p>
<p>UFC Fight Night 82 took place Saturday at MGM Grand Garden Arena in Las Vegas. It aired on FOX Sports 1 following early prelims on UFC Fight Pass.</p>
<p>In the headliner, welterweight contender Stephen Thompson (12-1 MMA, 7-1 UFC) upset former champion Johny Hendricks (17-4 MMA, 12-4 UFC) via <a href="http://mmajunkie.com/2016/02/ufc-fight-night-82-results-stephen-thompson-dominates-johny-hendricks-for-first-round-ko">dominant first-round knockout</a>. Thompson earned $48,000 (half of which was a win bonus), and Hendricks pocketed $100,000.</p>
<p>Nelson (21-12 MMA, 8-8 UFC), who <a href="http://mmajunkie.com/2016/02/ufc-fight-night-82-results-roy-nelson-tops-jared-rosholt-for-first-decision-win-since-2007">scored a unanimous-decision victory</a> over fellow heavyweight Jared Rosholt (14-3 MMA, 6-2 UFC) in the co-headliner, earned $75,000 to show and $50,000 as a win bonus. Rosholt picked up $33,000.</p>
<p>In addition to Benavidez, Mike Pyle ($106,000) and Ovince Saint Preux ($102,000) also pocketed six-figure disclosed paydays.</p>
<p>The total disclosed payroll for the event was $1,054,000.</p>
<p>The full list of UFC Fight Night 82 payouts included:</p>
<p>Stephen Thompson: $48,000 (includes $24,000 win bonus)<br />
def. Johny Hendricks: $100,000</p>
<p>Roy Nelson: $125,000 (includes $50,000 win bonus)<br />
def. Jared Rosholt: $33,000</p>
<p>Ovince Saint Preux: $102,000 (includes $51,000 win bonus)<br />
def. Rafael Cavalcante: $42,000</p>
<p>Joseph Benavidez: $118,000 (includes $59,000 win bonus)<br />
def. Zach Makovsky: $19,000</p>
<p>Misha Cirkunov: $24,000 (includes $12,000 win bonus)<br />
def. Alex Nicholson: $10,000</p>
<p>Mike Pyle: $106,000 (includes $53,000 win bonus)<br />
def. Sean Spencer: $17,000</p>
<p>Josh Burkman: $90,000 (includes $45,000 win bonus)<br />
def. K.J. Noons : $34,000</p>
<p>Derrick Lewis: $50,000 (includes $25,000 win bonus)<br />
def. Damian Grabowski: $17,000</p>
<p>Justin Scoggins: $34,000 (includes $17,000 win bonus)<br />
def. Ray Borg: $18,000</p>
<p>Diego Rivas: $20,000 (includes $10,000 win bonus)<br />
def. Noad Lahat: $17,000</p>
<p>Mickey Gall: $20,000 (includes $10,000 win bonus)<br />
def. Mike Jackson: $10,000</p>
<p>Alex White: $24,000 (includes $12,000 win bonus)<br />
def. Artem Lobov: $13,000</p>
<p>Now, the usual disclaimer: The figures do not include deductions for items such as insurance, licenses and taxes. Additionally, the figures do not include money paid by sponsors, including the official <a href="http://mmajunkie.com/2016/02/ufc-fight-night-82-athlete-outfitting-pay-four-fighters-earn-15k-each-of-140k-total">UFC Athlete Outfitting sponsorship program pay</a>. They also do not include any other “locker room” or special discretionary bonuses the UFC oftentimes pays.</p>
<p>For example, as previously reported, UFC officials handed out additional $50,000 <a href="http://mmajunkie.com/2016/02/ufc-fight-night-82-bonuses-wonderboy-gets-50000-for-biggest-win-of-career">UFC Fight Night 82 fight-night bonuses</a> to Thompson, Rivas, Pyle and Spencer. Thompson and Rivas earned &#8220;Performance of the Night&#8221; awards while Pyle and Spencer picked up &#8220;Fight of the Night&#8221; honors.</p>
<p>In other words, the above figures are simply base salaries reported to the commission and do not reflect entire compensation packages for the event.</p>
