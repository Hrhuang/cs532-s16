	<strong>Number of Players: </strong>2 - 6<br>
	<strong>Duration:</strong> 10min<br>

	<strong>Rules:</strong><em> Coming soon! (please check below for an overview)</em></p><p style="text-align: center;"><em>Available for pre-order through our store - Estimated delivery time: May 2016</em><br><em></em>
</p><p style="text-align: center;"><strong>THIS IS NOT AN EXPANSION. IT IS A STAND-ALONE GAME</strong></p><p style="text-align: center;">
	<strong>Description:</strong> The circus show went really really bad and now the clowns are blaming each other...by throwing pies!
	<br>
	Clowns' code dictates that whoever remains clean the longest, will win the argument.
</p><p style="text-align: center;"><em><strong data-redactor-tag="strong">Clown Standoff is an implementation of the game Pen T'es Mort designed by Ludovic Barbe.</strong></em></p><p style="text-align: center;"><em><strong data-redactor-tag="strong"><br></strong></em><img src="http://store.artipiagames.com/images/companies/1/CLOWN%203_Painted.jpg?1455044523950"></p><p style="text-align: center;">
	<em><strong data-redactor-tag="strong">Gameplay:</strong></em>
	The game is separated into two phases.
	<br>
	Phase one is a trick-taking phase with simultaneous card selection. First we check for ties. The tie numbers are gone and then the highest card wins. Cards are 1 to 6. Four times per card.
	<br>
	If after the first check we have a 1 card and a 6, then the 1 card wins the trick.
	<br>
	So now all keep the winning trick cards and we are ready for phase 2.
	<br>
	In Phase 2 we have 6 cards (Pie Throw cards) in the middle of the table, 5 "Dodge" cards and a "Hit" card.
	<br>
	On your turn (beginning with the player with the most cards from phase one): 
	<br>
	 You can draw a card or you can discard one card from the ones you collected in Phase one, to shuffle all open Pie Throw cards back together and then draw one, or you can discard cards equal to the number of players who are still in the game to pass your turn to the next player.
	<br>
	If a player draws a Dodge card, nothing happens and the remaining cards move to the next player. If a player draws a Hit card, that player is out of the game. All Pie Throw cards are again shuffled to form a deck and the player with the most cards from Phase one starts a new turn.
</p><p style="text-align: center;">
	Last man standing is the winner!
</p></div>
