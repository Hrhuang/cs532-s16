                            <h2>
                                IceDogs vs. Bulldogs
                            </h2>
                                <small>PHOTO BY FRANCIS MORASIEWICZ</small>

                        <small class="color printable-newspaper">Niagara This Week - St. Catharines</small>

                <div class="bHeader">
            <span>By</span>
            <a href="/niagara-author/matt-young/f8617f76-d4db-4e59-9169-657c60179923" class="color printable-author">Matt Young</a>
                <a href="mailTo:mattyoung71@gmail.com">

                <div itemprop="articleBody">
                        <p class="printable-text">
                            HAMILTON&mdash;The Niagara IceDogs (26-18-3-3) begin the action this week with an unusual 10:30 a.m. puck drop at First Ontario Centre on Wednesday where they will visit the Bulldogs (19-27-5-0) in front of thousands of school children.
                        </p>                        
                        <p class="printable-text">
                            The Bulldogs are expecting more than 13,000 students from Grades 4-6.
                        </p>                        
                        <p class="printable-text">
                            Hamilton defeated Niagara 5-2 at the Meridian Centre in St. Catharines in their last meeting.
                        </p>                        
                        <p class="printable-text">
                            In a league typically dominated by the home team, the road team has managed to win each game in the series this season; a trend the IceDogs hope to continue on Wednesday morning.
                        </p>                        
                        <p class="printable-text">
                            Hamilton leads the series 2-1.
                        </p>                        
                        <p class="printable-text">
                            Goaltender Connor Hicks has dominant the IceDogs this season by posting a .924 save percentage.
                        </p>                        
                        <p class="printable-text">
                            In their last meeting, Hicks stole the game for Hamilton with 47 saves.
                        </p>                        
                        <p class="printable-text">
                            Welland&rsquo;s Niki Petti has elevated his game versus the IceDogs.
                        </p>                        
                        <p class="printable-text">
                            Petti notched a goal and two assists against Niagara and was the best player on the ice aside from Hicks.
                        </p>                        
                        <p class="printable-text">
                            He has five points in the three games versus Niagara.
                        </p>                        
                        <p class="printable-text">
                            Niagara will also need to generate traffic in front of the Hamilton goal in this game as Hicks has proven he is capable of stifling Niagara shooters when he is able to see the puck.
                        </p>                        
                        <p class="printable-text">
                            Hamilton is on the verge of capturing the last playoff spot in the Eastern Conference.
                        </p>                        
                        <p class="printable-text">
                            Niagara travels to London to face the Knights (36-11-2-1) on Friday at Budweiser Gardens.
                        </p>                        
                        <p class="printable-text">
                            In their only meeting of the season, Niagara defeated London 3-2.
                        </p>                        
                        <p class="printable-text">
                            It will have only been a week since these two teams squared off, and some bad blood may exist from the last game with Matthew Tkachuk slashing Jordan Maletta as the horn blew to end the game. Maletta wasn&rsquo;t seriously hurt on the play, yet it was the type of move that should result in suspension.
                        </p>                        
                        <p class="printable-text">
                            The key to this game will be for the IceDogs to stay out of the penalty box.  London has a lot of offensive weapons at their disposal and it is unlikely the IceDogs can win another game that has London on the power play eight times.  Niagara must play disciplined, physical hockey against the Knights to capture these two big points in the standings.  
                        </p>                        
                        <p class="printable-text">
                            Toronto Maple Leafs&rsquo; prospect Mitch Marner picked up an assist in last week&rsquo;s game, but was relatively invisible throughout the game due to an equipment issue.  Marner has 82 points in just 39 games this season and will play a significantly bigger role in this week&rsquo;s re-match with Niagara.
                        </p>                        
                        <p class="printable-text">
                            While Marner attracts most of the headlines by virtue of being Maple Leafs property, Christian Dvorak was once again the best Knight on the ice last week.
                        </p>                        
                        <p class="printable-text">
                            Dvorak scored one of London&rsquo;s two goals on the power play and now has 40 goals in 41 games this season.
                        </p>                        
                        <p class="printable-text">
                            Dvorak is on pace for 128 points this season which would crush his career high of 109 set last season playing with fellow Arizona Coyotes rookie Max Domi.
                        </p>                        
                        <p class="printable-text">
                            London rebounded with a pair of victories to close out the weekend.
                        </p>                        
                        <p class="printable-text">
                            Although the Knights play in the Western Conference, the IceDogs always seem to get up for games against head coach Dale Hunter.
                        </p>                        
                        <p class="printable-text">
                            The IceDogs have a three-game winning streak against London (dating back to last season), and you can bet the Knights will want to end that losing streak in front of their home fans this week.
                        </p>                        
                        <p class="printable-text">
                            This has the makings of a great game on paper, however the IceDogs need to keep their emotions in check and avoid the frustration they encountered last week.
                        </p>                        
                        <p class="printable-text">
                            The IceDogs close out the week by playing the Mississauga Steelheads (25-24-1-3) at the Meridian Centre on Sunday afternoon in a Valentine&rsquo;s Day matinee. 
                        </p>                        
                        <p class="printable-text">
                            While the details are sketchy, the IceDogs are promising &ldquo;that history will be made during the second intermission of this game.&rdquo;
                        </p>                        
                        <p class="printable-text">
                            While we have no idea what this means, it should give fans another reason to attend this game against their division rival.  
                        </p>                        
                        <p class="printable-text">
                            In their last meeting, Steelheads goaltender Jack Flinn shutout the IceDogs at the Hershey Centre; a 5-0 thrashing back on Jan. 22.
                        </p>                        
                        <p class="printable-text">
                            Flinn&rsquo;s shutout in that game was his league-leading seventh of the season, and was the latest display of his stellar play against Niagara this season.
                        </p>                        
                        <p class="printable-text">
                            Flinn has a .922 save percentage against the IceDogs this season, and his play in goal has been a difference-maker for Mississauga in their four contests.
                        </p>                        
                        <p class="printable-text">
                            Alexander Nylander and Michael McLeod are both eligible for the 2017 NHL Entry Draft, and both have turned in dominant performances against Niagara this season.  
                        </p>                        
                        <p class="printable-text">
                            Nylander has scored four goals and added four assists in four games against Niagara, while McLeod has six points of his own over the stretch.
                        </p>                        
                        <p class="printable-text">
                            The key to the game for Niagara will be finding a way to stop the Steelheads&rsquo; dynamic duo, something the rest of the league has yet to do.
                        </p>                        
                        <p class="printable-text">
                            Nylander&rsquo;s 61 points leads all OHL rookies and has him sitting 15th overall in league scoring, while McLeod sits 20th with 56 points.  
                        </p>                        
                        <p class="printable-text">
                            Marty Williamson will likely want to use the strategy they utilize against the Knights when they play Mississauga on Sunday.
                        </p>                        
                        <p class="printable-text">
                            The IceDogs will want to beat Mississauga&rsquo;s offensive stars to their blue line in order to avoid giving them any additional time and space in the Niagara zone.
                        </p>                        
                        <p class="printable-text">
                            If the IceDogs manage to use this strategy and the benefit of having the last change to their advantage, they should send the Meridian Centre faithful home happy this Sunday.  
                        </p>                        
                        <p class="printable-text">
                            With only 18 games left in the season, the IceDogs must continue to win the majority of their games as they compete for a home-ice advantage in the playoffs.
                        </p>                        
                        <p class="printable-text">
                            There are only four points separating the third place and seventh place teams in the East, so every game is important as the season comes to a close.  
                        </p>                        
                        <p class="printable-text">
                            This Week&rsquo;s Prediction: The IceDogs will capture four of six points to make it back-to-back winning weekends.
                        </p>                        
                </div>

      var info = new_item.find('.ownlocal-business-popup-info');
      var info_html = biz.city + ", " + biz.state;
      var website;

      new_item.find('.ownlocal-business-link').attr('href', biz.link).text(biz.name);
      list.append(new_item);
      info.find('h4 a').attr('href', biz.link).text(biz.name);

      if (biz.address) info_html = biz.address + "<br/>" + info_html;
      if (biz.website) {
        website = biz.website.replace('http://', '');
        info_html += " | <a target='_blank' href='http://" + website +"'>"+ website +"</a>";
      }

      info.find('p').html(info_html);
	  if (biz.ad_image) {

			info.append('<a target="_blank" href="'+ biz.ad_url +'"><img src="https://'+biz.ad_full_image.replace("https://", "")+'"/></a>');
		else
			info.append('<a target="_blank" href="'+ biz.ad_url +'"><img src="http://'+biz.ad_full_image.replace("http://", "")+'"/></a>');

			info.append('<a target="_blank" href="'+ biz.link +'"><img src="http://'+biz.image.replace("http://", "")+'"/></a>');
      }
    });
  });
}
