                                <div class="subscriber-preview"><p>Nebraska senior forward Shavon Shields is resting comfortably and will continue to go through concussion protocol before he can return to the basketball court, according to the mens basketball teams head athletic trainer, R.J. Pietig.</p></div><div class="subscriber-preview"><p>Hes doing better, Pietig said Monday night on the Nebraska Basketball Radio Show. Hes at home, resting comfortably. Hes in the best hands in Lincoln right now, as his mother is staying with him.</p></div><div class="subscriber-only"><p>The 6-foot-7, 220-pound Shields suffered a serious fall in Nebraskas 87-63 victory against Rutgers on Saturday in Lincoln that sent him to a hospital. Shields banged his head and neck on the court and was knocked unconscious.</p></div><div class="tnt-ads-container text-center hidden-sm hidden-md hidden-lg"><div class="tnt-ads dfp-ad" data-dfp-adunit="/8438/journalstar.com/sports/huskers/mens-basketball" data-dfp-custom-pos="fixed-big-ad-top, atf" id="fixed-big-ad-top-mobile" data-dfp-size="[300,250]"></div></div>
    <div class="subscriber-only"><p>Shields regained consciousness after about a minute and had feeling and movement in his extremities as he was taken off the court. A CT scan was negative.</p></div><div class="subscriber-only"><p>Nebraska (13-11, 5-6 Big Ten Conference) plays at Wisconsin (14-9, 6-4) on Wednesday. Shields, who averages 15.7 points and five rebounds, second on the team in both categories, has made 106 consecutive starts. Dave Hoppen holds the school record for consecutive starts at 111, set from 1983-86.</p></div><style>
