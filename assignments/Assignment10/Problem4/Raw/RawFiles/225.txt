<div class="rich-v2 standard no-sidebar clearfix" data-module="article-rich" data-title="Eagles Look For Strong Pitching Staff To Lead The Way" data-url="/sports/bsb/2015-16/releases/20160208nezz8i">
  

	
      <h1 class="article-title">Eagles Look For Strong Pitching Staff To Lead The Way</h1>
  

	

<div class="article-body clearfix">
	
	  	
				
	<div class="article-image">
      <div class="image">
        <img src="/sports/bsb/2015-16/photos/The_Pitchers.jpg?max_width=450" alt="Eagles Look For Strong Pitching Staff To Lead The Way" />
  
          
          
              </div>
	</div>
  
	
			
	
<div class="article-text">
	
<div class="article-date">
      <div class="date">Feb 09, 2016</div>
    
  	<div class="like-buttons" data-module="share-buttons">
    	  <div class="fb-like-btn"><a class="addthis_button_facebook_like" fb:like:layout="button_count"></a></div>
        	  <div class="tweet-btn"><a class="addthis_button_tweet"></a></div>
    	</div>
  </div>

	
<p><strong>Wednesday: The Catchers</strong></p>
<p><strong>BRIDGEWATER, Va.</strong> &ndash; The Bridgewater College pitching staff could certainly be the strengths of the team during the upcoming season.</p>
<p>The Eagles return third-team all-conference selections <a href="/sports/bsb/2015-16/bios/leake_ryan_m4vm">Ryan Leake</a> and <a href="/sports/bsb/2015-16/bios/woerner_steven_31pv">Steven Woerner</a> to headline the 2016 staff. Leake returns to his starting slot on the weekend while Woerner will assume a new role this season&nbsp;at the back end of games.</p>
<p>Leake led the team in innings pitched and finished with a 5-4 record while posting a sterling 2.02 earned run average. The senior relies on a good fastball and a plus changeup, giving head coach Curt Kendall one of the top lefties in the conference.</p>
<p>Woerner will move to the bullpen as the team's "finisher."</p>
<p>"We're not calling him a closer, he'll be a finisher," said Kendall. "Some days he could pitch the ninth, some days we might ask him to go three innings. We feel like it's a role he is well-suited for and it's a way for us to utilize him more."</p>
<p>Joining Leake on the weekend could be fellow senior <a href="/sports/bsb/2015-16/bios/petock_justin_z9d3">Justin Petock</a>. The righthander has pitched 106 innings during his first three seasons and has a 7-3 record with six saves and a solid 4.08 ERA. "Justin gives us a very experienced option on the mound and he's going to be in the starting mix."</p>
<p>Also looking to nail down rotation spots are lefty <a href="/sports/bsb/2015-16/bios/cockerham_brady_qsgw">Brady Cockerham</a> and sophomores <a href="/sports/bsb/2015-16/bios/lenz_chad_8a9c">Chad Lenz</a>, <a href="/sports/bsb/2015-16/bios/lavoie_johnny_a8v6">Johnny LaVoie</a> and <a href="/sports/bsb/2015-16/bios/mayes_ian_c1wu">Ian Mayes</a>. Each started at least two games a year ago and each turned in at least one stellar start.</p>
<p>Cockerham, a southpaw, made 15 appearances with two starts and both starts were gems. Cockerham pitched a seven-inning shutout in a 4-0 win over Penn St.-Harrisburg. He worked into the ninth inning in a game against Wesley where he finished by allowing three runs in 8.1 innings in the Eagles' 4-3 victory.</p>
<p>Lenz, another lefty,&nbsp;made seven starts with two of his best coming in conference play. He allowed one earned run in five innings in a win over Eastern Mennonite and allowed zero earned runs in seven innings&nbsp;during a win over Hampden-Sydney.</p>
<p>LaVoie made three starts with his best coming against a strong Mary Washignton team when&nbsp;he gave up just four hits in seven shutout innings in a no-decision.</p>
<p>Mayes struggled for much of his first season, but he finished strong. He pitched seven innings and allowed seven hits and three earned runs in a late-season non-conference victory over N.C. Wesleyan.</p>
<p>In addition to that group, Kendall also thinks freshman <a href="/sports/bsb/2015-16/bios/daywalt_tristan_n97f">Tristan Daywalt</a> could figure into the starting mix.</p>
<p>"I really feel comfortable in the fact I have six or seven different guys who can start," said Kendall. "The ones who don't end up starting will certainly be valuable pieces of our bullpen."</p>
<p>Another wildcard in the rotation could be lefty <a href="/sports/bsb/2015-16/bios/showalter_jordan_ohqc">Jordan Showalter</a>. After turning in a strong freshman season in 2014, Showalter missed the 2015 season due to arm surgery. Kendall believes the lefty could be at full strength for the stretch run this season.</p>
<p>In addition to Woerner, Kendall will have another side-armer at his disposal as sophomore <a href="/sports/bsb/2015-16/bios/jones_kyle_yyjm">Kyle Jones</a> returns after a solid first year. Jones made 13 appearances and finished with a 4-1 record. "Kyle dropped down to sidearm for the first time last year and he really pitched well. We threw him to the wolves in some tough situations and he did the job. He's another experienced guy we can go to."</p>
<p>Sophomores <a href="/sports/bsb/2015-16/bios/lasam_josh_tv1l">Josh Lasam</a> and <a href="/sports/bsb/2015-16/bios/horton_jarrett_fzm4">Jarrett Horton</a> also threw some quality innings for the Eagles a year ago. "Lasam is a guy who can come in and be pretty effective for an inning or two in relief. Horton's another guy who, when healthy, can be effective," said Kendall.</p>
<p>In addition to Daywalt, the Eagles have several more first-year arms capable of contributing on the mound. "<a href="/sports/bsb/2015-16/bios/imeson_samuel_7g2r">Samuel Imeson</a> has een throwing the ball well and <a href="/sports/bsb/2015-16/bios/berry_andrew_tux4">Andrew Berry</a> has been a pleasant surprise. Both those guys are lefties and then you have <a href="/sports/bsb/2015-16/bios/early_jeff_qkli">Jeff Early</a> and <a href="/sports/bsb/2015-16/bios/mason_ryan_4sb5">Ryan Mason</a> from the right side. All those guys are talented arms."</p>
<p>Kendall believes the staff is set up to perform well not only this season, but into the future.</p>
<p>"We have some pretty talented arms in the freshmen and sophomore class," said Kendall. "Overall this season, I feel like we have a strong and deep staff. We have a lot of depth and we should be able to keep our arms fresh throughout the season."</p>
<p>Bridgewater opens the season this weekend with a pair of games at Christopher Newport. The Eagles open their home schedule on Feb. 16 against Averett University.</p>
