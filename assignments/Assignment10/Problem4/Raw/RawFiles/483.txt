        <div class="bw-article-main-image" style="display: block; clear: both; text-align: center; margin: 0 auto; max-width: 600px; overflow:auto; " ><a href="http://www.brentwoodhomepage.com/a-game-nonprofits-mulling-over-latest-proposal-from-ownership-cms-25142"><img class="" src="https://bw-0dd6049f5fa537d41753be6d37859430-bwcore.s3.amazonaws.com/articles/AGameProtestERW61454964734.jpg" style="display: block; clear: both; text-align: center; margin: 0 auto; max-width: 600px; width: 600px"></a><div class="bw-article-main-caption" style="max-width: 100%">HOME PAGE MEDIA GROUP FILE PHOTO</div></div><p>A-Game's leasing tenants said they will closely vet a proposal ownership <strong><a href="http://franklinhomepage.com/a-game-owners-create-proposal-to-keep-facility-open-through-marchs-end-cms-23864#.VrkAb5MrLVo">presented late Saturday afternoon</a> </strong>that could keep the facility open through the end of March.</p>
<p>Alliance Volleyball Director Jeff Wismer said the group's legal team at Butler | Snow will closely examine what ownership presented. In the offer proposed by Sports Land Group, the owners would like to meet with the nonprofits' leaders before Wedesday, Feb. 10 at 5 p.m. SLG said they wish to work out a deal that would allow ownership to stop losing money and allow volleyball and hockey to finish their seasons in the facility while also preparing to find a new home.</p>
<p>Hockey season finishes around March and volleyball doesn't end until the third week of June.</p>
<p><span class="s2">Part of the deal would enclose the nonprofits receiving some money back in return as the facility shuts down. </span></p>
<p><span class="s2">Upon the closing of the sale, SLG would deposit $2 million of the closing proceeds into an escrow account ($1 million for the benefit of MDG and $1 million for the benefit of Alliance). The escrowed funds would be available to fund any legally permissible damages to which MDG and/or Alliance may be entitled arising from the lawsuit currently pending in the Williamson County Chancery Court (Case No. 44784). </span><span class="s2">Recoveries would not be limited to the amount of the escrowed funds.</span></p>
<p>"<span class="s1">They are concerned of the perplexity of the group," he said. "They are looking at the escrow accounts and the validity that they can give that money."</span></p>
<p><strong><a href="http://franklinhomepage.com/sports-groups-go-back-to-court-over-sudden-a-game-closure-cms-23846#.VrZm-JMrLR0">On Friday afternoon,</a></strong> legal representatives for volleyball and hockey/figure skating filed an expedited motion for a judge to reverse the previous decision and to review their leasing agreements. Alliance and MDG have legally enforceable leases which expire in September 2017 and December 2019. They asked a judge to expedite that motion and hear it no later than tomorrow, Feb. 9.</p>
<p>The group had a judge lift a temporary restraining order against itself <strong><a href="http://franklinhomepage.com/a-game-volleyball-hockey-looking-at-legal-options-against-owners-cms-23813#.VrZn3JMrLR2">last Tuesday.</a> <a href="http://franklinhomepage.com/a-game-users-said-they-were-surprised-by-abrupt-closure-cms-23817#.VrZnu5MrLR1">Two days later,</a></strong> Alliance Volleyball and MDG Managment, which handles hockey, had to retrieve their personal belongings from A-Game after finding it abruptly locked on Thursday morning.</p>
<p><strong><a href="http://franklinhomepage.com/kids-parents-chant-and-march-in-support-of-maintaining-a-game-cms-23827#.VrUQ-JMrLVo">A-Game users protested for its opening Thursday</a> </strong>night in a march from the CoolSprings Galleria mall to the complex at 215 Gothic Court, waving signs at cars in exchange for honks of support.</p>
<p>In the meantime, Alliance Volleyball will lay down hardwood in the Williamson County Ag Expo center starting Monday night for players to use during the next five days. During the weekend, Harpeth Hall opened up their gyms to let them practice.</p>
<p>"I<span class="s1">t's an unbelievable story," Wismer said. "For those who are questioning city and county, they are the ones opening up their doors and for them to make this viable and to get those courts here. We also have families willing to volunteer to get these courts up."</span></p>
