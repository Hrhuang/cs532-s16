                                          <h1 class="headline">A 56-Year-Old Model Will Be an Ad Star in Sports Illustrated's 2016 Swimsuit Issue</h1>
                              <span class="subheadline">Swimsuits for All brand pushes the envelope again</span>
                                            <span class="byline">By <a href="/contributor/robert-klara">Robert Klara</a></span>
                          </div>

            <ul class="meta">
              <li>February 9, 2016, 1:41 PM EST</li>
              <li><a href="/advertising-branding">Advertising & Branding</a> </li>
              <li class="sharebar"></li>
            </ul>
          </div>

          <div class="cols">
            <div class="cols colsAB left-article-2" data-print="news_article">
                    <div class="article article-single page1">
        <script>
            var twitterLoaded = [];
        </script>

                    
                
  <div class="graph left-article-2">
    <img src="http://www.adweek.com/files/imagecache/node-detail/2016_Feb/Nicola-Solo-HI-RES.jpg" alt="" title=""  class="imagecache imagecache-node-detail" width="652" height="869" />          <p class="caption">Aged 56, model Nicola Griffin cuts a memorable figure with, in her own words, "white hot hair."               </p>
      </div>

            
            
                    
                <!--paging_filter--><p class="google_elide">
	When you break open Sports Illustrated&#39;s Swimsuit Issue on Feb. 16, you&#39;ll see plenty of eye-popping stuff, including Justin Bieber&#39;s ex-girlfriend Barbara Palvin wearing a schoolbus-yellow bikini and UFC star Ronda Rousey wearing even less than that (<a href="http://www.si.com/swim-daily/2016/01/05/ronda-rousey-body-paint-si-swimsuit-2016" target="_blank">just body paint</a>, in fact).</p>
<p class="google_elide">
	But the biggest surprise this year is actually on the advertising pages. Clad in a reflective gold bikini, Nicola Griffin will demonstrate that swimsuit models are not always stick figures&mdash;and they&#39;re not always twentysomethings, either. At 56-years-old, Griffin is the oldest women to appear in SI&#39;s fleshy showcase since its debut in 1964.</p>
<p class="google_elide">
	&quot;People think you lose your sex appeal as you get older&mdash;but that&#39;s a myth,&quot; Griffin said in a statement. &quot;I&#39;ve never felt sexier.&quot;</p>
<p class="google_elide">
	Griffin will appear on a three-page ad buy from <a href="http://www.swimsuitsforall.com" target="_blank">Swimsuits for All</a>, a Web merchant that specializes in sizes 10-34 and bills itself as &quot;the leader in swimwear for curvy women.&quot; The brand&#39;s other two ad pages will feature supermodel Ashley Graham and British Ghanaian model <a href="http://www.philomenakwao.com/about" target="_blank">Philomena Kwao</a> who, like Griffin, embody &quot;a shift in the traditional notions of a bikini body,&quot; according to the company.</p>
<p class="google_elide">
	&quot;Young and skinny is typical,&quot; Swimsuits for All CEO Moshe Lanaido told Adweek, &quot;but we&#39;re saying it can also be curvy and older women.&quot;</p>
<div class="news-article-image" style="margin: 15px 0px 15px 15px; float: right;">
	<img src="http://www.adweek.com/files/2016_Feb/SI_Ashley_Ad.jpg" />
	<p class="caption">
		Ashley Graham shook things up in last year&#39;s Swimsuit Issue, too</p>
</div>
<p class="google_elide">
	Lanaido said as much in last year&#39;s Swimsuit Issue, too, <a href="http://www.adweek.com/adfreak/sis-swimsuit-issue-finally-includes-plus-size-model-least-its-advertising-162786" target="_blank">when its &quot;Curves in Bikinis&quot; campaign debuted model Ashley Graham (size 16)</a> wearing a black string bikini and knocking a skinny guy in a suit into a swimming pool. (New York shop <a href="http://www.kraftworksnyc.com/" target="_blank">KraftWorks</a> created both this year&#39;s and last year&#39;s campaigns.) Even though size-12 model Robyn Lawley was also featured in the same 2015 issue, Graham&#39;s appearance created a media sensation and landed everywhere from local news broadcasts to <a href="http://stylenews.peoplestylewatch.com/2015/02/04/sports-illustrated-swimsuit-issue-plus-size-model-ashley-graham/" target="_blank">People&#39;s style pages</a>.</p>
<p class="google_elide">
	&quot;We thought, &#39;Wow, we hit a nerve,&#39;&quot; Lanaido said, &quot;and I thought this may become an annual point of view for us.&quot;</p>
<p class="google_elide">
	For 2016, instead of simply adding ever-curvier models, Lanaido decided to push the envelope again by adding an older model. Griffin isn&#39;t just 56; she sports luxurious tresses of gray hair. &quot;I feel quite unique in the modeling world,&quot; Griffin says on her <a href="http://www.nicolagmodel.com/#!about/c786" target="_blank">website</a>. &quot;Mature models have come a long way in the industry, and we now have a place to represent women of all ages.&quot;</p>
<p class="google_elide">
	Swimsuits for All&#39;s effort comes amid a slow but undeniable shift away from decades-old notions of what a &quot;beautiful&quot; figure looks like. On Jan. 28, Mattel announced the expansion of its Fashionistas line of Barbie dolls, <a href="http://www.adweek.com/adfreak/barbie-suddenly-looks-more-normal-right-line-marketing-zeitgest-169254" target="_blank">featuring new body types</a> that included petite and curvy, in addition to &quot;a variety of skin tones.&quot; And late last year, the famous <a href="http://www.adweek.com/news/advertising-branding/24-hours-pirellis-calendar-showed-how-quickly-brand-can-modernize-its-image-168381" target="_blank">Pirelli Tire calendar</a> made headlines when it broke with its tradition of showing slender and busty models in favor of portraying women of achievement including Amy Schumer, Serena Williams and Yoko Ono&mdash;who is now 82.</p>
<p class="google_elide">
	&quot;There&#39;s this idea of age that&#39;s been floating around in the popular culture,&quot; Lanaido said. &quot;Jennifer Aniston said she&#39;s going to rock a bikini when she&#39;s 80, and that really sticks for us. We know consumers are shifting away from what my mom and grandmother thought of as being beautiful and sexy. There&#39;s a different take on it, and we feel we&#39;re trailblazers within it.&quot;</p>
<div class="news-article-image" style="margin: 15px 0px 15px 15px; float: right;">
	<img src="http://www.adweek.com/files/2016_Feb/SI_Philomena_Ad.jpg" />
	<p class="caption">
		Phenomena Kwao was Essence magazine&#39;s &quot;curvy model of the month&quot; in March 2014</p>
</div>

      <div id="print-content" style="display: none;">
        <p>
	When you break open Sports Illustrated&#39;s Swimsuit Issue on Feb. 16, you&#39;ll see plenty of eye-popping stuff, including Justin Bieber&#39;s ex-girlfriend Barbara Palvin wearing a schoolbus-yellow bikini and UFC star Ronda Rousey wearing even less than that (<a href="http://www.si.com/swim-daily/2016/01/05/ronda-rousey-body-paint-si-swimsuit-2016" target="_blank">just body paint</a>, in fact).</p>
<p>
	But the biggest surprise this year is actually on the advertising pages. Clad in a reflective gold bikini, Nicola Griffin will demonstrate that swimsuit models are not always stick figures&mdash;and they&#39;re not always twentysomethings, either. At 56-years-old, Griffin is the oldest women to appear in SI&#39;s fleshy showcase since its debut in 1964.</p>
<p>
	&quot;People think you lose your sex appeal as you get older&mdash;but that&#39;s a myth,&quot; Griffin said in a statement. &quot;I&#39;ve never felt sexier.&quot;</p>
<p>
	Griffin will appear on a three-page ad buy from <a href="http://www.swimsuitsforall.com" target="_blank">Swimsuits for All</a>, a Web merchant that specializes in sizes 10-34 and bills itself as &quot;the leader in swimwear for curvy women.&quot; The brand&#39;s other two ad pages will feature supermodel Ashley Graham and British Ghanaian model <a href="http://www.philomenakwao.com/about" target="_blank">Philomena Kwao</a> who, like Griffin, embody &quot;a shift in the traditional notions of a bikini body,&quot; according to the company.</p>
<p>
	&quot;Young and skinny is typical,&quot; Swimsuits for All CEO Moshe Lanaido told Adweek, &quot;but we&#39;re saying it can also be curvy and older women.&quot;</p>
<div class="news-article-image" style="margin: 15px 0px 15px 15px; float: right;">
	<img src="http://www.adweek.com/files/2016_Feb/SI_Ashley_Ad.jpg" />
	<p class="caption">
		Ashley Graham shook things up in last year&#39;s Swimsuit Issue, too</p>
</div>
<p>
	Lanaido said as much in last year&#39;s Swimsuit Issue, too, <a href="http://www.adweek.com/adfreak/sis-swimsuit-issue-finally-includes-plus-size-model-least-its-advertising-162786" target="_blank">when its &quot;Curves in Bikinis&quot; campaign debuted model Ashley Graham (size 16)</a> wearing a black string bikini and knocking a skinny guy in a suit into a swimming pool. (New York shop <a href="http://www.kraftworksnyc.com/" target="_blank">KraftWorks</a> created both this year&#39;s and last year&#39;s campaigns.) Even though size-12 model Robyn Lawley was also featured in the same 2015 issue, Graham&#39;s appearance created a media sensation and landed everywhere from local news broadcasts to <a href="http://stylenews.peoplestylewatch.com/2015/02/04/sports-illustrated-swimsuit-issue-plus-size-model-ashley-graham/" target="_blank">People&#39;s style pages</a>.</p>
<p>
	&quot;We thought, &#39;Wow, we hit a nerve,&#39;&quot; Lanaido said, &quot;and I thought this may become an annual point of view for us.&quot;</p>
<p>
	For 2016, instead of simply adding ever-curvier models, Lanaido decided to push the envelope again by adding an older model. Griffin isn&#39;t just 56; she sports luxurious tresses of gray hair. &quot;I feel quite unique in the modeling world,&quot; Griffin says on her <a href="http://www.nicolagmodel.com/#!about/c786" target="_blank">website</a>. &quot;Mature models have come a long way in the industry, and we now have a place to represent women of all ages.&quot;</p>
<p>
	Swimsuits for All&#39;s effort comes amid a slow but undeniable shift away from decades-old notions of what a &quot;beautiful&quot; figure looks like. On Jan. 28, Mattel announced the expansion of its Fashionistas line of Barbie dolls, <a href="http://www.adweek.com/adfreak/barbie-suddenly-looks-more-normal-right-line-marketing-zeitgest-169254" target="_blank">featuring new body types</a> that included petite and curvy, in addition to &quot;a variety of skin tones.&quot; And late last year, the famous <a href="http://www.adweek.com/news/advertising-branding/24-hours-pirellis-calendar-showed-how-quickly-brand-can-modernize-its-image-168381" target="_blank">Pirelli Tire calendar</a> made headlines when it broke with its tradition of showing slender and busty models in favor of portraying women of achievement including Amy Schumer, Serena Williams and Yoko Ono&mdash;who is now 82.</p>
<p>
	&quot;There&#39;s this idea of age that&#39;s been floating around in the popular culture,&quot; Lanaido said. &quot;Jennifer Aniston said she&#39;s going to rock a bikini when she&#39;s 80, and that really sticks for us. We know consumers are shifting away from what my mom and grandmother thought of as being beautiful and sexy. There&#39;s a different take on it, and we feel we&#39;re trailblazers within it.&quot;</p>
<div class="news-article-image" style="margin: 15px 0px 15px 15px; float: right;">
	<img src="http://www.adweek.com/files/2016_Feb/SI_Philomena_Ad.jpg" />
	<p class="caption">
		Phenomena Kwao was Essence magazine&#39;s &quot;curvy model of the month&quot; in March 2014</p>
</div>
