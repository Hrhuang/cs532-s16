		<p><a href="http://www.rotoworld.com/player/nba/1870/jimmy-butler" target="_blank">Jimmy Butler</a> is <a href="http://nba.nbcsports.com/2016/02/09/bulls-all-star-jimmy-butler-out-3-4-weeks-with-strained-knee/">out of the NBA All-Star Game in Toronto this weekend due to a strained knee</a>. Which sucks, because he earned that spot, and while the fans didn&#8217;t vote him in the coach&#8217;s did.</p>
<p>Butler&#8217;s teammate <a href="http://www.rotoworld.com/player/nba/766/pau-gasol" target="_blank">Pau Gasol</a> will replace him.</p>
<p>NBA Commissioner Adam Silver gets to make the call on a replacement, and he stayed in Chicago, but he added a front court player to replace a guard. Keeping a Bulls representative might have been part of the thinking. The coaches&#8217; votes on replacement players has been weighed in the past (Gasol may have been high on that list, coaches love him). Also, the East roster has a lot of wings and was light on bigs (<a href="http://www.rotoworld.com/player/nba/1965/andre-drummond" target="_blank">Andre Drummond</a>, <a href="http://www.rotoworld.com/player/nba/1316/paul-millsap" target="_blank">Paul Millsap</a> and <a href="http://www.rotoworld.com/player/nba/930/chris-bosh" target="_blank">Chris Bosh</a> are the only real bigs), so this gives coach Tyronn Lue some flexibility up front.</p>
<p>The East leading Cleveland Cavaliers remain with just one representative, <a href="http://www.rotoworld.com/player/nba/927/lebron-james" target="_blank">LeBron James</a> (voted in by the fans).</p>
<p>Gasol is averaging 17 points, 10.9 rebounds, and a couple blocks a game, and is the only thing close to a consistent performer the Bulls have. Besides the injured Butler.</p>

		<p>INDIANAPOLIS (AP) <a href="http://www.rotoworld.com/player/nba/1731/paul-george" target="_blank">Paul George</a> scored 21 points, <a href="http://www.rotoworld.com/player/nba/2466/myles-turner" target="_blank">Myles Turner</a> added 15 and the Indiana Pacers beat the Toronto Raptors 101-83 on Friday night to force a Game 7 of their series.</p>
<p>That will be played Sunday in Toronto, and the winner will advance to the Eastern Conference semifinals.</p>
<p>Indiana scored 18 straight points in the second half to pull away from the second-seeded Raptors, who haven&#8217;t won a postseason series since the first round in 2001.</p>
<p><a href="http://www.rotoworld.com/player/nba/1479/demarre-carroll" target="_blank">DeMarre Carroll</a> and <a href="http://www.rotoworld.com/player/nba/1878/cory-joseph" target="_blank">Cory Joseph</a> each had 15 points for the Raptors.</p>
<p><a href="http://www.rotoworld.com/player/nba/1269/kyle-lowry" target="_blank">Kyle Lowry</a> (4 for 14) and <a href="http://www.rotoworld.com/player/nba/1610/demar-derozan" target="_blank">DeMar DeRozan</a> (3 for 13) struggled again, and now the Raptors will head home and hear again about their troubled playoff problems: a Game 7 loss at home to Brooklyn in 2014, Washington&#8217;s four-game sweep last season and no series wins in a seven-game series. And there 15-year victory drought is the longest active streak in the league.</p>
<p>Indiana trailed by as much as 12 early, never led until early in the third quarter and had to fend off a late third-quarter charge from the Raptors before blowing it open in the fourth.</p>
<p>After Toronto cut the deficit to 65-64, Indiana responded by scoring the last six points of the third and the first 12 of the fourth to take an 83-64 lead.</p>
<p>The Raptors never recovered.</p>
<p>For Indiana, it was a dramatic turnabout.</p>
<p>Three days after blowing a 13-point, fourth-quarter lead, they came out flat. The Pacers struggled to make baskets, struggled to defend and played catch-up the entire first half after Toronto took an 18-6 lead just 6 1/2 minutes into the game.</p>
<p>But once the Pacers got righted, they took control and pulled away.</p>
<p>Indiana rebounded from a 44-40 halftime deficit with a 10-2 run to take its first lead, 49-48 on <a href="http://www.rotoworld.com/player/nba/1157/ian-mahinmi" target="_blank">Ian Mahinmi</a>&#8216;s tip-in with 9:33 left. They extended the lead to 63-55 before Toronto charged back to get within 65-64 in the final two minutes of the quarter.</p>
<p>Indiana scored the next 18 points to seal it.</p>
<p>GOLDEN CROWD</p>
<p>Indiana handed out gold T-shirts to fans at the game and it caused a bit of a stir because the wording on the shirts read: United State of Basketball, We The Gold. It was a twist on Toronto&#8217;s motto &#8211; We The North. Raptors coach Dwane Casey didn&#8217;t seem to mind that Indiana stole the idea. &#8220;I know that they have a great home court. You know you&#8217;re in Pacers territory because of all the gold shirts,&#8221; he said. &#8220;But what happens between the lines for 48 minutes is what I&#8217;m concerned about.&#8221;</p>
<p>TIP-INS</p>
<p>Raptors: The Raptors wound up getting outrebounded 44-40 after dominating the post in the first half. &#8230; <a href="http://www.rotoworld.com/player/nba/1832/jonas-valanciunas" target="_blank">Jonas Valanciunas</a> had 14 points, Lowry had 10 and DeRozan finished with eight. &#8230; <a href="http://www.rotoworld.com/player/nba/1834/bismack-biyombo" target="_blank">Bismack Biyombo</a> grabbed 10 rebounds. &#8230; Toronto gave up 20 points on 17 turnovers. &#8230; The last time the Raptors led a series 3-2 was in 2014 against Brooklyn. But the Nets won 97-83 on their home court before clinching the series with a 104-103 victory in Game 7 at Toronto.</p>
<p>Pacers: Turner blocked four shots, giving him 19 in the series to break Antonio Davis&#8217; previous franchise rookie record in a six-game series. &#8230; Indiana started the second half by making five of its first seven shots. &#8230; Actor-comedian Mike Epps, who lives in Indy, attended the game. &#8230; The Pacers have won four straight elimination games on their home court.</p>

		<p>&#8220;It&#8217;s a make-or-miss league&#8221; has been a worn-out cliche in the NBA for years, but it was never more apt than Friday night. <a href="http://www.rotoworld.com/player/nba/931/dwyane-wade" target="_blank">Dwyane Wade</a>, a career 28.4 percent three-point shooter who hadn&#8217;t hit a shot from beyond the arc in the calendar year of 2016, knocked down two threes late to help the Miami Heat hold on and beat the Charlotte Hornets, 97-90, to force a Game 7 on Sunday.</p>
<p>Wade finished with 23 points on 10-for-20 shooting along with 6 rebounds and 4 assists to lead Miami, and had a key block in the closing minute to stave off a Charlotte comeback. The Heat held off <a href="http://www.rotoworld.com/player/nba/1822/kemba-walker" target="_blank">Kemba Walker</a>&#8216;s 37-point explosion, which kept Charlotte in the game for much of the second half even as they never led in the third or fourth quarters.</p>
<p>Neither team got much out of their benches, and Miami overcame a rough night from <a href="http://www.rotoworld.com/player/nba/1538/goran-dragic" target="_blank">Goran Dragic</a> (6-for-17 from the field) and <a href="http://www.rotoworld.com/player/nba/1727/hassan-whiteside" target="_blank">Hassan Whiteside</a> fouling out in just 28 minutes of action. A three by Walker cut Miami&#8217;s lead to 90-88, the closest the Hornets got in the second half.</p>
<p>The two teams will now play a Game 7 in Miami on Sunday, with the winner facing the winner of that same day&#8217;s Game 7 between the Raptors and Pacers.</p>

		<p>Less than a week after firing head coach Byron Scott, the Lakers have their man to replace him: Luke Walton, who is currently associate head coach with the Warriors and coached the team to a 39-4 start to the season while Steve Kerr was out recovering from offseason back surgery.</p>
<p>The Lakers announced the hiring of Walton in a press release on Friday evening:</p>
<blockquote><p>Earlier today, the Los Angeles Lakers and Luke Walton reached an agreement on a multi-year contract for Walton to become the next Head Coach of the team, it was announced by General Manager Mitch Kupchak. Walton will begin his new duties at the conclusion of the Golden State Warriors season.<br />
&#8220;We&#8217;re excited to bring Luke back to Los Angeles, where we feel he&#8217;s going to start an outstanding coaching career,&#8221; said Kupchak. &#8220;He&#8217;s one of the brightest young coaching minds in the game and we feel fortunate that he&#8217;ll be leading the on-court future of our team.&#8221;</p></blockquote>
<p>The Warriors released statements from Kerr and GM Bob Myers after the Lakers&#8217; announcement:</p>
<blockquote><p>&#8220;We are thrilled for Luke,” said Warriors GM Bob Myers.  “This is a tremendous opportunity for him to return home and serve as head coach for one of the most storied franchises in sports.   Luke has done an incredible job during his two-year stint with our team and has played a significant role in our success, including last year’s championship and again this season under some very unique circumstances.  We are certainly going to miss Luke after we conclude our playoff run this season, but we wish him the very best with the Lakers.”</p>
<p>“I’m incredibly happy for Luke,” said Warriors Head Coach Steve Kerr.  “As we witnessed earlier this season, he has all of the intangibles necessary to be an outstanding head coach in this league, including a terrific understanding of the game the ability to communicate with a wide range of people.   He’s certainly ready for this opportunity and I’m confident he’ll do a great job with the Lakers once our season is complete.”</p></blockquote>
<p>Walton had long been rumored to be the Lakers&#8217; top target, given his successful run with the Warriors and his ties to the organization, where he played for nine seasons. It&#8217;s a solid hire, not a big name but someone with a good reputation around the league.</p>
