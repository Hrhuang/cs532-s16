<p>NASHVILLE, Tenn. (AP) &#8212; The <a href="https://sports.yahoo.com/nfl/teams/ten/">Tennessee Titans</a> have released safety <a href="https://sports.yahoo.com/ncaab/players/134275/">Michael Griffin</a> after nine seasons in their first step toward improving their secondary.</p>
<p>The Titans announced the move Tuesday.</p>
<p>A first-round draft pick in 2007 at No. 19 overall out of Texas, Griffin was a two-time Pro Bowl pick in 2008 and 2010 whose 141 games played tied <a href="https://sports.yahoo.com/nfl/players/8422/">Corey Graham</a> of Buffalo for most games played by a safety between 2007 and 2015. Griffin had 25 interceptions and also became the fifth player in franchise history with 900 career tackles and the most by a safety.</p>
<p>Griffin wrote on Twitter that he knew his time would come.</p>
<p>He wrote, &#8221;I cherished every minute of it. It&#8217;s been real Tennessee. Thanks for everything. (hash)nextchapter.&#8221;</p>
