Hundreds swarm the streets during the 817th Atherstone Ball Game

     * 19:50, 9 Feb 2016
     * By [155]Antonia Bannister

The strange tradition has been held on Shrove Tuesday for 817 years

   There was a melee on the streets of Atherstone this afternoon for the
   [158]817th Shrovetide Ball Game.

   The rough-and-tumble event, which dates back to the 12th century, was
   as popular as ever with [159]hundreds of people chasing a 4lb ball
   around the streets of the town for two hours.

   The [160]winner is the one with the ball in their hands at the end of
   the contest.

   This year’s winner was Jonathan Slessor, of Atherstone, who had the
   help of the 'Grendon Lads'.

   Stores boarded up doors and windows, with traffic being diverted to
   allow teams a free run of the streets.

   The game was started at Barclays Bank by Tamworth-born footballer Marc
   Albrighton, currently earning rave reviews [161]playing for Premier
   League table toppers Leicester City.

   Police presence was at an all time low this year with officers using
   CCTV to keep an eye on any off-the-ball trouble.

   Other activities that the crowd took part in were a raffle, which
   raised £906 for charity, a pancake race and golden penny ball game.
