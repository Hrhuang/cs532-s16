Blackhawks goalie Scott Darling extends charity to man in Arizona

     * [126]Email
     * comment

   Feb 9, 2016
     * ESPN.com news services

     *
     *
     *

     * [127]Email
     * [128]print
     * comment

   [129]Chicago Blackhawks goalie [130]Scott Darling confirmed on Monday
   that he recently helped a needy man in Arizona during a road trip.

   Aspiring goaltender Kane Van Gate tweeted on Sunday night a description
   of Darling's actions:

     Here's another example of hockey players being awesome people...
     [131]pic.twitter.com/mR1eNNB97F

     — Kane Van Gate (@KVanGate38) [132]February 8, 2016

   Darling told the Chicago Tribune that he met the man on Wednesday in
   Scottsdale, Arizona, when the Blackhawks were in the area to play the
   [133]Arizona Coyotes. He said he was surprised by the reaction he's
   received.

   "I just started talking to him, was interested in his life, seemed like
   a good guy and just needed a helping hand," Darling told the Tribune.
   "That's how it happened."

   Darling has spoken openly about his struggles with addiction. He said
   his past experiences gave him empathy for the man he ultimately helped.

   "I definitely have a soft spot in my heart for that situation -- people
   battling stuff that other people don't understand," Darling told the
   Tribune. "He seemed like a really nice person. I'm in the position to
   help somebody like that. I'm happy to do it."
