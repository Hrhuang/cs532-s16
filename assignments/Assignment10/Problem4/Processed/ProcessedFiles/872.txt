99Games alongwith Yash Raj Films to release game about SRK-starrer movie
'FAN'

   Shashwati Shankar, ET Bureau Feb 9, 2016, 04.55AM IST
     *

   (A second version of the game…)

   MUMBAI: Indian gaming studio 99Games has partnered with Yash Raj Films
   on a multi-year deal to launch their product, 'FAN: The Game', prior to
   the release of the film 'Fan' in mid-April. 'FAN: The Game', which will
   be released at the end of February, will be an online game for iOS and
   Android users with 75 levels in the first version, enabling the player
   to get closer to actor Shah Rukh Khan.

   After spending five months building the product, 99Games will track
   individual game play behaviour, since they have "built in a lot of
   social elements enabling the end-user to make personal choices, without
   trying to be very intrusive," said 99Games CEO Rohith Bhat. He
   estimates that they will see 20 million downloads and 3 billion minutes
   of gameplay within the first three months.

   "Since it requires a lot of server transaction, our team has built a
   system in place, which can handle over 20 million transactions on a
   daily basis," he added. According to the gaming company, findings from
   their previous game 'Star Chef ', indicated that players would spend an
   average of 42 minutes a day. 99Games is estimating a similar amount of
   time to be spent on this launch.

   A second version of the game with 45 levels will be launched on the
   release date of the film in April. Looking to Bollywood as both a
   customer as an acquisition strategy as well as to popularise gaming,
   99Games plans to create more localised Indian celebritythemed games for
   the end-user. "We are hoping that in the years to come, it will become
   a big-value proposition.

   We don't want to look at the gaming business as a marketing tactic but
   as a strategic product that we are creating and will continue to invest
   in," said Manan Mehta, vice-president of marketing and merchandise at
   Yash Raj Films. Mehta remained tightlipped about investment figures.
