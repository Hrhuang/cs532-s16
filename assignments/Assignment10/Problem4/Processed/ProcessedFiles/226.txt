Jimmy Butler Injury Update: Bulls Star Ruled out of 2016 NBA All-Star Game

   The [18]Chicago Bulls [19]announced Tuesday that [20]Jimmy Butler will
   be out three to four weeks while recovering from the [21]knee injury he
   suffered this past weekend.

   As a result, Butler will miss Sunday’s NBA All-Star Game. The league
   has yet to confirm who will replace the swingman in the Eastern
   Conference’s reserve unit.

   Commissioner Adam Silver could opt for a representative from the
   conference’s best team, the [22]Cleveland Cavaliers. Both [23]Kyrie
   Irving and Kevin Love have strong cases to make the All-Star reserves.
   [24]Charlotte Hornets point guard Kemba Walker and [25]Boston Celtics
   forward Jae Crowder are two additional deserving candidates. Silver
   could also look no further than the Bulls roster and opt for [26]Pau
   Gasol.

   No matter in which direction the commissioner goes, he’s bound to upset
   the fans of those players overlooked for the All-Star Game for what
   will be the third time.

   Far more important than who replaces Butler is the fact the Bulls now
   have a timetable for how long they could be without their leading
   scorer. Whereas this was once Derrick Rose’s team, Butler is now the
   unquestioned leader.

   He’s also one of the best 2-guards in the league, per ESPN Stats &
   Info:

   Chicago is only a game and a half ahead of the [27]Hornets, who are
   ninth in the Eastern Conference, and only four games separate the Bulls
   from the [28]Orlando Magic and [29]Washington Wizards, the 10th and
   11th seeds.

   The Bulls could easily slip out of the top eight in Butler’s absence.
   In their first two games since his injury, they lost to the Hornets and
   [30]Minnesota Timberwolves.

   Silver doesn’t have an enviable task ahead as he fills out the East
   roster for the All-Star Game, but Bulls head coach Fred Hoiberg is in a
   much tougher position as he looks ahead to the next month with his best
   player out of action.
